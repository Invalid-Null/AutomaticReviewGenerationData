The document discusses Oxidative Propane Dehydrogenation (ODH) using various metal oxide catalysts supported on alumina. The key enhancements are:

- The Galinstan alloy oxide showed improved direct CO2 activation compared to Ga oxide alone due to its lower Tamman temperature enabling oxygen mobility.

- In oxide showed no deactivation during reverse water gas shift reaction due to the redistribution of the active metal, enhancing stability.

The catalysts were tested using a feed ratio of 1:1 propane:CO2 at 400-700°C and achieved 55-99% propylene selectivity. The conversion ranged from 2-53% and stability from 0.17 h^-1. The major scientific contribution is understanding how oxygen mobility via the Tamman temperature correlates with improved CO2 activation.
