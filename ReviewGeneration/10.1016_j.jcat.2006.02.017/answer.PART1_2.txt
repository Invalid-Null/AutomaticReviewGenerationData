3. Yes, the article proposes that propane dehydrogenation proceeds through a heterolytic dissociation pathway over the supported Ga2O3 catalysts, similar to pure Ga2O3. It describes a mechanistic sequence where propane dissociatively chemisorbs on Ga2O3 sites to form gallium hydride and alkoxide species. The alkoxide then decomposes to give the alkene product, with the dehydrogenation rate enhanced by proton transfer from the support's acidic sites to the alkoxide intermediate.
