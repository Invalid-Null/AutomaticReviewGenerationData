Answer: Yes, the article proposes a mechanistic scenario for propane dehydrogenation over the studied zinc-modified H-MFI zeolite catalysts. The key steps described are:

1) Dissociative adsorption of propane over zinc oxide species to form zinc propyl species and ZnOH groups. 

2) Dehydrogenation of the zinc propyl species to form propene and dihydrogen. 

3) Recombinative desorption of the dihydrogen.

4) Propene evolution and further oligomerization over acidic sites.

The relative rates of hydrogen evolution vs hydrogenolysis to form ethane and methane depend on the partial pressure.
