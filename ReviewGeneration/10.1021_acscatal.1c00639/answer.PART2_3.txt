Answer: The article investigates electronic and geometric effects of Sn as the secondary metal in the Pt-Sn bimetallic alloy catalyst. Specifically, it shows that the incorporation of relatively larger Sn atoms expands the lattice fringe spacings and shifts XRD peaks to lower angles (Quotes 2-3), indicating a geometric effect. It also shows that alloying Pt with Sn causes an electronic effect, evident by the negative shift in Pt's XPS binding energy due to electron transfer from Sn (Quote 6). The geometric effect helps disperse Pt nanoparticles and minimize sintering (Quote 4).
