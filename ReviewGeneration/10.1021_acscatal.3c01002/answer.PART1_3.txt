[1] DFT calculations indicate that the introduction of Ga into the framework of ZrO2 alters the CH bond activation pathway from a stepwise mechanism to a concerted mechanism involving simultaneous cleavage of two CH bonds in propane, leading to a superior CH bond activation ability and a lower reaction barrier than state-of-the-art metal oxide catalysts.

[2] We compared the Gads of iso-C3H7 and H over different adsorption sites and established that the adsorption of propane on the GaO2cI site (C2) requires the lowest energy (1.19 eV, Table S7). The first methylene CH activation barrier is calculated to be 0.97 eV. 

[3] Subsequently, two CH bonds in the adsorbed propane are simultaneously cleaved, resulting in adsorbed propene, and two H atoms bonded to Ga and O (D2; Figures 5 and S16). It is worth noting that the formation of the GaH intermediate is proved by the in situ DRIFTS experiment. The reaction barrier is determined to be 1.5 eV, which is much lower than that of the stepwise mechanism (1.50 vs 2.65 eV).
