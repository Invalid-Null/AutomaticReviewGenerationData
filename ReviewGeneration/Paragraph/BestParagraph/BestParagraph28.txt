Based on the detailed analysis of the references provided, most of the articles do not evaluate different reactor types for propane dehydrogenation. The majority of studies focus on catalyst synthesis, characterization, and testing using fixed bed reactors. 

A few references provide some insights on reactor types:

- Reference [10.1016/j.cej.2004.11.005] discusses commercially available reactors like Oleflex, Houdry Catofin, Phillips STAR, and Linde processes as well as two-zone fluidized bed reactors (TZFBR) and internal circulating fluidized bed reactors (ICFBR) for propane dehydrogenation over Cr2O3/Al2O3 catalysts. Performance comparisons are made between TZFBR, ICFBR and other configurations.  

- Reference [10.1016/S1383-5866(01)00085-5] evaluates silica membrane reactors to improve propene yields by selective hydrogen removal during propane dehydrogenation. Results show higher yields than fixed beds but faster catalyst deactivation.

- Reference [10.1016/j.cej.2009.06.037] combines two-zone fluidized bed reactors (TZFBR) with Pd membrane reactors for the first time. The membrane increased conversion but also coking rates over PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts.

Overall, most studies utilize fixed bed reactors with only a few examples of fluidized beds and membrane reactors for propane dehydrogenation found in the literature. Opportunities likely exist to further explore the performance of these and other novel reactor configurations.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reveal fixed bed reactors remain the dominant choice for propane dehydrogenation, while some initial forays have been made applying fluidized beds and membranes. Considering these advancements, future undertakings might focus on optimization of multi-zone fluidized beds and selective membranes tailored for propane feeds to address issues like faster deactivation. Moreover, intensified continuous flow reactors with precise tuning of heat and mass transfer could be rewarding. Broader horizons may also emerge from transplantation of propane dehydrogenation into emerging areas like photo-/electro-catalysis.
