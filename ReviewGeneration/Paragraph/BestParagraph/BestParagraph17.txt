These lessons could be further extended to other Pt-based bimetallic catalysts, though the choice of secondary metal (e.g. Cu, In, Zn, Ga instead of Sn) may influence the optimal size of the Pt sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548].

The article [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671] investigates the electronic and geometric effects of Sn as a secondary metal in supported PtSn bimetallic clusters. It demonstrates that Sn forms strong bonds with Al2O3 support oxygens at the cluster-support interface. Sn also exhibits a stabilization effect on the Pt clusters [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671].

Increasing the P content is shown to reduce the Ru ensemble size, decreasing the hydrogenolysis rate (geometric effect). Ru-P bond formation also shifts the Ru XPS and XANES spectra to higher energies, suggesting decreased Ru 4d orbital energies that weaken metal-adsorbate bonds (electronic effect) [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667].

It shows ordered intermetallic nanoparticles can be formed that are not possible with traditional oxide or carbide supports. The different MXene compositions allow tuning of the catalyst properties and performance [10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5].
