Based on the details extracted from the references, catalysts facilitate propane dehydrogenation by providing active sites that can selectively activate C-H bonds in propane over C-C bonds with lower activation barriers [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01374]. Platinum-based catalysts in particular are widely employed, leveraging Pt's ability to break C-H bonds while minimizing cracking reactions [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019, 10.1021/acscatal.5b01423]. The key mechanisms described involve hydrogen abstraction to form metal-carbon bonds on coordinatively unsaturated sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00939, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01924]. 

Alternative catalysts are also being explored, including metal oxides like ZrO2 and Ga2O3 which can provide oxygen vacancies or Lewis acid sites [10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]. Zeolites exchanged with metal cations can similarly donate electron density to adsorbed propane [10.1002/advs.202207756]. Support effects are additionally important, with mesoporous silicas offering opportunities to tune selectivity and activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999].

This understanding may guide development of optimal single-atom Pt catalysts, building on demonstrated synergy between theory and experiment [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01924]. Stabilizing coordinatively unsaturated metal sites could also be a fruitful direction, whether via confinement in zeolites/mesoporous supports or surface organometallic frameworks. More broadly, shale gas availability renders propane dehydrogenation increasingly viable, necessitating accelerated innovation in selective, stable and scalable catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.025, 10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726].
