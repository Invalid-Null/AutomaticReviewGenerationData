Based on the details extracted from the literature, here is a comprehensive review section on the proposed mechanistic scenarios for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts:

Several articles describe Mars-van Krevelen type mechanisms operating during propane dehydrogenation over metal oxide catalysts like VOx/ZrO2 [10.1006/jcat.1999.2510], CrOx/ZrO2 [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.01.023], and MoVO mixed oxides [10.1021/jacs.9b09235]. In this pathway, lattice oxygen initiates the reaction by abstracting hydrogen from propane. The resulting alkyl species then lead to propene, while the reduced metal oxide surface gets re-oxidized by O2 or CO2 to complete the catalytic cycle. 

Isolated metal cations with unsaturated coordination (designated Mcus) are identified as active sites on several catalysts. On CrOx/ZrO2, Zrcus sites adjacent to oxygen vacancies catalyze C-H bond cleavage [10.1021/acscatal.9b05063]. Isolated Zn2+ on ZrO2 [10.1021/acssuschemeng.1c03074] and Ga+/H+ pairs on Ga/H-ZSM-5 [10.1021/acscatal.1c01497] also demonstrate high activity. The proximity of these Mcus sites to acid/base sites contributes to subsequent reaction steps like hydride transfer.

Several articles highlight the role of redox cycles Mx+ ↔ M(x-1)+ (x = 3-6) in activating propane on Cr [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999] and V [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.02.028] oxides. Optimal valency states like Cr3+ or V3+ balance activity and resistance to over-reduction. Tetrahedral Ga3+ sites are noted to selectively activate C-H bonds on Ga2O3 via heterolytic dissociation without changing oxidation state [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001].
