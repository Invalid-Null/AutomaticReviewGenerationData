Propylene is a crucial chemical feedstock utilized for producing a diverse range of products, including polypropylene, acrylonitrile, propylene oxide, acetone, etc. \[10.1021/acscatal.0c01455, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01374\]. Traditional production routes via steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking struggle to meet the rapidly rising demand, estimated to reach 191 megatons by 2030 \[10.1002/advs.202207756\]. Hence, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has emerged as an innovative and economically competitive technology by leveraging abundant supplies of propane from shale gas \[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889\]. Multiple studies have highlighted PDH's effectiveness in addressing propylene's supply deficit in an eco-friendly manner \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331\].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering propylene's indispensable role and PDH's ecological and financial viability, this technology retains strong potential for scaling up. Exploring novel catalysts and processes to boost propylene yield and selectivity appears particularly promising \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00939, 10.1021/jacs.2c03941\]. Researchers might also examine integrating PDH with other chemical production pathways in a bio- or electro- refinery setup for maximal resource utilization. Overall, by building upon recent advancements, PDH could cement its position in enabling large-scale, sustainable propylene generation.
