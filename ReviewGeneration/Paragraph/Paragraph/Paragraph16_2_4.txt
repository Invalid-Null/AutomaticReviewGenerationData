Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-based catalysts have demonstrated exceptional capability for activating C-H bonds in alkane dehydrogenation reactions. However, monometallic Pt catalysts suffer from drawbacks like fast deactivation due to coking and lower selectivity [1]. Alloying Pt with metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn has been extensively explored as a strategy to enhance the catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) [2]. 

Pt-Sn alloy catalysts are among the most widely studied systems for PDH. The addition of Sn serves to increase the selectivity towards propylene while also improving the stability by reducing coke formation and sintering [3] [4]. For example, a PtSn/Al2O3 catalyst achieved 40-45% propane conversion in a commercial PDH process, representing a major advancement over earlier catalytic systems [5]. Detailed studies reveal that incorporating Sn modulates the electron density of Pt sites, enabling more selective C-H bond activation over C-C bond cleavage [6]. Sn can also facilitate removal of coke precursors from Pt sites through a "drain-off" effect [7]. The optimal Sn/Pt ratio is typically around 1-1.5, with higher Sn content lowering activity [8]. 

Investigations into Pt-Zn catalysts demonstrate Zn helps disperse and isolate Pt sites, forming single-site PtZn4-6 structures that excel in both activity and stability [9] [10]. Dealuminated zeolite Beta (DeAlBEA) with grafted SiOZn-OH groups serves as an optimal support, stabilizing Pt atoms through anchoring interactions. Such atomically dispersed PtZn catalysts approach nearly 100% propylene selectivity at 550°C. The isolated PtZn4-6 sites induce specific geometric and electronic alterations beneficial for selective alkane activation [11].

Advancements with Pt-Ga catalysts underline exceptional durability stemming from suppressed side reactions and resilience towards coking. The formation of intermetallic Pt-Ga phases reinforces thermal stability while restricting the ensemble size of Pt sites [12]. A GaPt alloy confined in mesoporous silica reached 92.1% propylene selectivity and 45.9% propane conversion at 600°C [13]. The individual strengths of Ga and Pt are unified in an alloy structure mitigating respective deficiencies.

In summary, alloying tuning through Sn, Zn, and Ga promoters enables optimization of Pt catalysts for commercially competent PDH reaction rates and propylene yields. While trade-offs exist between activity and stability, innovative synthetic and characterization techniques now elucidate explicit descriptors and structure-performance correlations to be exploited for cutting-edge catalytic designs [14].

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

基于Pt的催化剂由于能够出色地激活C-H键,在烷烃脱氢反应中展现出了卓越的潜力。但是,单组分Pt催化剂存在快速失活(炭化)和较低选择性等缺点[1]。采用Sn、Ga和Zn等金属与Pt合金化的策略已经被广泛研究,以提高丙烷脱氢(PDH)的催化性能[2]。

Pt-Sn合金催化剂是PDH中研究最广泛的体系之一。添加Sn有助于提高丙烯的选择性,同时通过减少炭化和烧结提高了稳定性[3][4]。例如,PtSn/Al2O3催化剂在商业PDH工艺中实现了40-45%的丙烷转化率,相比更早的催化体系有了重大进步[5]。详细研究表明,掺入Sn调节了Pt活性位点的电�密度,促进了C-H键的选择性活化而不是C-C键的断裂[6]。Sn还可以通过“引流”效应促进焦炭前体从Pt位点的去除[7]。最佳的Sn/Pt比通常约为1-1.5,更高的Sn含量会降低活性[8]。

对Pt-Zn催化剂的研究表明,Zn有助于分散和隔离Pt位点,形成单一位点PtZn4-6结构,在活性和稳定性方面都表现优异[9][10]。去除铝硅酸盐沸石Beta(DeAlBEA)与SiOZn-OH功能团共轭,可以稳定Pt原子并形成最佳载体。这种原子分散的PtZn催化剂在550°C下接近100%的丙烯选择性。隔离的PtZn4-6位点诱��了有利于选性烷烃活化的特定几何和电子效应[11]。

Pt-Ga催化剂的研发凸显了来自抑制副反应和抗炭化的异常耐久性。形成Pt-Ga间金属相有助于提高热稳定性并限制Pt位点的集合体大小[12]。受控在介孔二氧化硅中的GaPt合金达到了600°C、92.1%的丙烯选择性和45.9%的丙烷转化率[13]。Ga和Pt的各自优势在合金结构中得以统一,缓解了相应的不足。

总之,通过Sn、Zn和Ga助剂的合金调控,可以优化Pt催化剂,以实现商业上可行的PDH反应速率和丙烯收率。尽管在活性和稳定性之间存在权衡,但创新合成和表征技术如今已经阐明了明确的描述符和结构-性能相关性,可用于切边的催化设计[14]。
