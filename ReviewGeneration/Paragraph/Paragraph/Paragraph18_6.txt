Based on the in-depth analysis from the references provided, none of the articles explored the use of subsurface promoters like Re or Mn in bimetallic and alloy catalysts to tune selectivity, except for one study focused on Pt3Mn catalysts [10.1021/jacs.8b08162]. This work showed Pt3Mn subsurface tuning significantly improved propane dehydrogenation selectivity over Pt, by lowering hydrocarbon/hydrogen adsorption heats. Models were also presented to elucidate the distinct role of subsurface layers in modifying surface chemistry and selectivity. 

However, the rest of the articles did not provide any specific examples or data on the use of subsurface promoters like Re or Mn. They focused on various bimetallic, alloy or metal oxide catalysts without subsurface tuning, ranging from PdBi [10.1021/acscatal.2c00642], RuP [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667], PtSn [10.1016/j.cej.2007.11.009, 10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024], Rh/ZrO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c01455], to zeolite-supported metals [10.1021/acscatal.1c01497, 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005] and more. But they did not elucidate how subsurface promoters can optimize selectivity.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The one study on Pt3Mn catalysts provides promising initial evidence that subsurface promoters like Mn can beneficially tune selectivity during propane dehydrogenation. This distinct subsurface role in tailoring surface chemical properties opens up new research frontiers for selectivity optimization in various bimetallic and alloy systems. 

Further work should focus on expanding the matrix of subsurface promoter-metal combinations, correlating subsurface electronic effects with overlying surface adsorption/reaction energetics, and formulating generalized design principles. Advanced characterization techniques like synchrotron-based spectroscopy could give vital insights into subsurface promoter environments. Machine learning methods may also assist high-throughput screening and discovery of optimized subsurface-tuned catalyst formulations.

<Chinese>
根据所提供的参考文献的深入分析,除了一个关于Pt3Mn催化剂的研究[10.1021/jacs.8b08162]外,没有一篇文章探索了像Re或Mn这样的亚表面促进剂在双金属和合金催化剂中的使用来调节选择性。这项工作表明,Pt3Mn亚表面调节明显提高了丙烷脱氢反应中Pt的选择性,方法是降低烃/氢的吸附热。模型也被提出来阐明亚表面层在调节表面化学性质和选择性方面的独特作用。

然而,其余的文章没有提供任何关于Re或Mn等亚表面促进剂使用的具体实例或数据。它们关注各种双金属、合金或金属氧化物催化剂,没有亚表面调节,范围从PdBi [10.1021/acscatal.2c00642]、RuP [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]、PtSn [10.1016/j.cej.2007.11.009, 10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024]、Rh/ZrO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c01455]到载于沸石上的金属[10.1021/acscatal.1c01497, 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]等。但是它们没有阐明亚表面促进剂如何优化选择性。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: Pt3Mn催化剂的这一研究提供了初步的有希望的证据,即类似Mn的亚表面促进剂可以在丙烷脱氢反应中有利地调节选择性。这一独特的亚表面作用,可以定制表面化学性质,开辟了各种双金属和合金体系中选择性优化的新研究前沿。

进一步的工作应该关注扩大亚表面促进剂-金属组合矩阵,相关联亚表面电子效应与覆盖表面吸附/反应能量,并制定一般化的设计原则。先进的表征技术如同步辐射谱学可以对亚表面促进剂环境提供重要的见解。机器学习方法也可以协助高通量筛选和优化了亚表面调谐的催化剂配方的发现。
