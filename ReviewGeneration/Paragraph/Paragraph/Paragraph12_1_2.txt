Based on the details provided in the file, several studies have explored supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH).

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most successful and widely used catalysts for commercial PDH processes [DOI: 10.101/S1872-2067(19)63360-7, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. An alumina-supported Pt catalyst shows good ability to activate propane C-H bonds while minimizing C-C bond cleavage, which is desirable for selective dehydrogenation [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201507119]. Adding promoters such as B or metal oxide overcoats (e.g. Al2O3, TiO2) by ALD can enhance the yield, selectivity, and stability of supported Pt catalysts [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. Under the same conditions, a 1 wt% Pt/Al2O3 catalyst gave inferior performance for PDH compared to bimetallic PtSn and NiMo catalysts in terms of conversion and propylene selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c05711, DOI: 10.1126/science.abg7894].

Investigations into the effect of Pt particle size show that although dehydrogenation barriers increase with cluster size, Pt2 nanoclusters exhibit exceptionally high intrinsic activity akin to a volcano-type trend [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]. Isolating single Pt sites in the micropores of zeolite supports via strong metal-support bonding can also give active and stable catalysts [DOI: 10.1038/s41429-023-00968-7]. 

While Pt performs well for selective propane dehydrogenation, adding Sn significantly enhances conversion and propylene selectivity due to geometric and electronic effects [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639, DOI: 10.1126/science.abg7894]. Forming PtSn intermetallic sites prevents over-reduction of Pt that causes increased C-C scission. The ratio of Pt:Sn is also an important factor modulating selectivity [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

Besides Pt and PtSn, a few studies have explored selective dehydrogenation over supported Pd, Rh, and Ir catalysts. A single-atom RhCu alloy on silica was found to outperform a traditional Pt/Al2O3 catalyst, achieving full propylene selectivity at higher conversion levels [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]. Overall however, reports on selective PDH over supported Pd, Rh, and Ir are limited compared to Pt and PtSn.

In summary, Pt and PtSn catalysts, especially on acidic supports like alumina, are the predominant supported metal catalysts applied commercially and investigated in academic studies for selective propane dehydrogenation. Promoters, metal-support interactions, and tailoring metal ensemble size/geometry can further enhance Pt performance. Supported Pd, Rh, and Ir catalysts show promise but require more research to match Pt effectiveness.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reviewed demonstrate supported metal catalysts, chiefly Pt and PtSn, achieve good performance balancing activity and selectivity for on-purpose propylene production from propane. However, catalyst deactivation caused by factors like coking, agglomeration, and over-reduction remains a key challenge needing innovative solutions. Emerging directions may involve developing novel supports with very strong metal-support bonding to stabilize isolated metal sites, applying selective overcoats that prevent coking while maintaining accessibility, or designing dynamic systems that regenerate deactivated catalysts in situ. Exciting opportunities also exist in expanding beyond traditional supports like alumina to harness unique synergies with more recently developed materials. As computational power grows, high-throughput screening coupled with microkinetic modeling can accelerate identification of optimal catalyst compositions and structures. Overall, propane dehydrogenation is gaining increasing traction as new discoveries continue expanding the horizon of possibilities.

<Chinese>
根据文件提供的详细信息,几项研究已经探索了用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)的支撑单金属 Pt、Pd 和 Ir 催化剂。

报道称,Pt基催化剂是商业 PDH 工艺中最成功和应用最广泛的催化剂之一 [DOI: 10.101/S1872-2067(19)63360-7, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。氧化铝支撑的 Pt 催化剂表现出很好的丙烷 C-H 键激活能力,同时使 C-C 键断裂最小化,这对于选择性脱氢反应是理想的 [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201507119]。通过 ALD 法加入 B 或金属氧化物超薄层等助剂可以提高支撑 Pt 催化剂的产率、选择性和稳定性 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。在相同条件下,1 wt% Pt/Al2O3 催化剂的 PDH 性能比双金属 PtSn 和 NiMo 催化剂的转化率和丙烯选择性更差 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c05711, DOI: 10.1126/science.abg7894]。

关于 Pt 颗粒大小效应的研究表明,虽然脱氢屏蔽随着簇大小的增加而增加,但 Pt2 纳米簇表现出异常高的固有活性,呈现出火山型趋势 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。通过强金属支撑作用隔离沸石支持体中的单个 Pt 位点也可以得到活性好、稳定的催化剂 [DOI: 10.1038/s41429-023-00968-7]。

尽管 Pt 的选择性丙烷脱氢性能很好,但添加 Sn 可以显著提高转换率和丙烯选择性,这是由于几何和电子效应 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639, DOI: 10.1126/science.abg7894]。形成 PtSn 金属间化合物可以防止 Pt 过度还原导致的 C-C 键断裂增加。Pt 和 Sn 的比例也是调节选择性的重要因素 [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

除了 Pt 和 PtSn,少数研究也探索了氧化硅支撑的单原子 RhCu 合金催化剂用于选择性脱氢。结果表明它的性能优于传统的 Pt/Al2O3 催化剂,在更高的转化水平下达到100%的丙烯选择性 [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]。但是,与 Pt 和 PtSn 相比,关于 Pd、Rh 和 Ir 支撑催化剂用于选择性 PDH 的报道还比较有限。

总的来说,尤其是氧化铝等酸性载体支撑的 Pt 和 PtSn 催化剂,是应用于商业和学术研究选择性丙烷脱氢的主要金属催化剂。添加助剂、调控金属与载体相互作用以及调整金属活性组合的大小和构型可以进一步提高 Pt 的性能。支撑的 Pd、Rh 和 Ir 催化剂显示出前景,但需要更多研究才能赶上 Pt 的效果。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 通过对文件内容的综述可以看出,主要是 Pt 和 PtSn 等支撑金属催化剂在平衡活性和选择性方面取得了很好的丙烷脱氢性能。然而,焦炭生成、烧结、过度还原等因素造成的催化剂失活仍然是一个关键挑战,亟待创新解决方案。新兴研究方向可能涉及开发新的载体材料制备稳定的隔离金属活性位点,设计选择性超薄层防止焦炭生成而保持反应物可及性,或者构建动态反应系统现场再生失活催化剂。与传统载体相比,最近开发的新型载体也提供了利用其独特协同效应的激动人心的机会。随着计算能力的增强,高通量筛选结合微观动力学建模可以加速确定最佳催化剂成分和结构。总体而言,丙烷脱氢反应作为不断有新的发现扩大可能性领域的课题,其重要性日益增加。
