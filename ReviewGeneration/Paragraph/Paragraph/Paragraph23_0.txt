 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an industrially important process that produces propylene, a key building block in the petrochemical industry. However, catalyst deactivation caused by sintering and coking remains a key challenge. Recent work has explored zeolite encapsulation strategies to enhance metal catalyst stability for PDH.

A highly stable Co-based PDH catalyst was achieved by encapsulating isolated Co species within the pores of BEA zeolite (Co@Si-BEA) [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. After 8 reaction-regeneration cycles, the catalyst maintained excellent catalytic performance. X-ray absorption spectroscopy confirmed the absence of aggregated Co oxides, verifying stabilized isolated Co sites. Encapsulation prevented sintering, which commonly deactivates unsupported PDH catalysts.  

Subnanometric PtZn nanoclusters were encapsulated inside purely siliceous MFI zeolites, showing extremely high stability under PDH conditions [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. After 3 reaction cycles spanning 70+ hours, the high initial activity and cluster size was preserved. The zeolite confinement effect stabilized the active structure against sintering.

Anchoring platinum species to germanium sites in UTL-type zeolites (Pt/Ge-UTL) realized exceptional 4000+ hour stability [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. In situ XAFS proved platinum was bonded to Ge-sites via Pt-O-Ge linkages, immobilizing the clusters. This catalyst surpassed state-of-the-art Pt catalyst lifetimes by over an order of magnitude due to the anchoring effect.

Overall, zeolite encapsulation can effectively enhance metal catalyst stability during propane dehydrogenation by isolating active sites and preventing deleterious sintering, coking, and leaching processes. While additional mechanistic studies are still needed, current results clearly demonstrate the promising potential of zeolite confined catalysts for commercial PDH applications.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Current zeolite encapsulation techniques already display vast improvements in propane dehydrogenation catalyst stability. However, widespread industrial implementation remains limited by insufficient propylene yields and encapsulation difficulties hindering scalability. Future research could explore combining confinement effects with composition optimization strategies. Introducing secondary metals may modulate geometric and electronic properties to further bolster stability while enhancing activity and selectivity. High-throughput computational screening approaches assessing millions of bimetallic configurations may uncover ideal candidate compositions meriting experimental validation. Additionally, emerging zeolite synthesis methods enabling rapid crystallization rates and morphology control could streamline encapsulation processes. Overall, at the confluence of cutting-edge computational predictions, zeolite science advancements and nanocatalysis design principles lies tremendous prospects for developing commercially viable encapsulated PDH catalysts.

<Chinese>

丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一种工业上非常重要的过程,它生产丙烯,这是石化工业中的一个关键基础化合物。然而,由烧结和结焦引起的催化剂失活仍然是一个关键挑战。 最近的工作已经探索了分子筛封装策略,以提高金属催化剂在PDH中的稳定性。

通过将BEA分子筛中的隔离Co物种(Co@Si-BEA)实现了高稳定的Co基PDH催化剂 [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。经过8次反应再生循环后,该催化剂仍然保持了优异的催化性能。 X射线吸收光谱确认了 Co 氧化物聚集体的不存在,验证了稳定的隔离Co位点。 封装防止了常见的不支撑的PDH催化剂烧结。

将亚纳米PtZn纳米簇封装在纯二氧化硅MFI分子筛内,显示出在PDH条件下的极高稳定性 [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。 在反应过程中3个循环后保持了高的初始活性和簇的大小不变。 分子筛封装效应稳定了活性结构抵抗烧结。

通过将铂物种锚定在UTL型分子筛的锗位点上(Pt/Ge-UTL)实现了例外的4000小时以上的稳定性[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。 原位XAFS证明铂通过Pt-O-Ge键与Ge位点键合,使簇稳定。 这个催化剂的使用寿命超过目前铂基催化剂的状态最高寿命一个数量级,这是由于锚定效应。

总的来说,分子筛封装可以通过隔离活性位点并防止有害的烧结、焦化和溶出过程有效地提高金属催化剂在丙烷脱氢过程中的稳定性。 虽然仍需要进一步的机理研究,但当前的结果已经明确表明了分子筛限域催化剂在商业PDH应用中的有前景的潜力。

※※※  

综合理解与展望: 当前的分子筛包封技术在丙烷脱氢催化剂的稳定性上已经显示出了巨大的改进。然而,由于丙烯产量不足和包封难度妨碍规模化,广泛的工业应用仍有限。未来的研究可以探索将限域效应与组分优化策略相结合。 引入第二种金属可能会调节几何和电子性质,使其在提高活性和选择性的同时进一步增强稳定性。 高通量计算筛选方法评估数百万种双金属配置可能会找出理想的候选成分值得实验验证。 此外,新兴的分子筛合成方法使得快速结晶率和形态控制成为可能,这可能简化封装工艺。 总的来说,在计算预测、分子筛科学进步和纳米催化剂设计原理的交汇点上,发展商业化可行的封装PDH催化剂具有巨大的前景。
