Based on the details provided in the file, there are a few examples that describe synergistic effects between metals in bimetallic or alloy catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, attributed to ensemble and ligand effects:

The Ru-P bimetallic catalyst demonstrates isolated Ru sites and longer Ru-Ru bonds compared to pure Ru, which is attributed to decreased ensemble size and weaker metal-adsorbate interactions (ligand effects), improving selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]. 

The PtIn2 nanoparticles show geometric and electronic changes compared to monometallic Pt, with the PtIn2 phase demonstrating the highest activity and stability. This indicates synergistic electronic and geometric effects between the metals [10.1021/acscatal.0c01554].

The ZnZrxOy catalysts exhibit synergistic effects between Zncus and Zrcus sites, with Zncus active for the first dehydrogenation step and Zrcus active for the second step, lowering the overall activation energy [10.1021/acssuschemeng.1c03074]. 

The PtSn bimetallic clusters demonstrate differences in geometric and electronic structure compared to Pt nanoparticles, attributed to the spatial distribution and interactions between Pt and Sn, leading to unique reactivity [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

The PtZn intermetallic alloy isolates Pt sites and modulates selectivity through geometric and electronic effects, with [PtZn4] ensembles identified as the active sites [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008].

In general, many of the described synergistic effects relate to isolation of active sites, geometric changes, and electronic modifications induced by the secondary metal that serve to improve selectivity, activity, or stability compared to the monometallic catalysts.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The examples cited showcase how strategic incorporation of a secondary metal can profoundly influence the performance of noble metal catalysts like Pt. This ability to precisely tune catalytic properties through understandable structure-function relations harbors immense promise. 

An auspicious avenue is extending this concept to single atom alloy catalysts with isolated metal sites exhibiting optimized ensemble and ligand environments. Advances in in situ characterization and computational modeling can further our fundamental comprehension to enable rational design of bespoke bimetallic and alloy catalysts for specialized applications.

<Chinese>

根据文件提供的详细信息,有几个例子描述了双金属或合金催化剂中金属之间的协同效应,这些效应归因于集合体和配体效应:

Ru-P 双金属催化剂表现出隔离的 Ru 位点和比纯 Ru 更长的 Ru-Ru 键,这归因于集合体大小的减小和更弱的金属-吸附剂相互作用(配体效应),改进了选择性[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。

PtIn2 纳米粒子与单金属 Pt 相比显示出几何和电子结构的变化,其中 PtIn2 相示出最高的活性和稳定性。这表明金属之间存在协同的电子和几何效应[10.1021/acscatal.0c01554]。

ZnZrxOy 催化剂表现出 Zncus 和 Zrcus 位点之间的协同效应,Zncus 活跃于第一步脱氢,Zrcus 活跃于第二步,降低了整体的活化能[10.1021/acssuschemeng.1c03074]。

PtSn 双金属簇表现出与 Pt 纳米粒子相比具有不同的几何和电子结构,这归因于 Pt 和 Sn 的空间分布和相互作用,导致了独特的反应活性[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

PtZn 金属间化合物合金隔离了 Pt 位点,通过几何和电子效应调控选择性,其中 [PtZn4] 集合被确定为活性位点[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。

总的来说,许多所描述的协同效应与次要金属诱导的活性位点的隔离、几何变化和电子修饰有关,这些服务于改进单金属催化剂的选择性、活性或稳定性。

综合理解与未来展望:所引用的例子展示了战略掺入次要金属如何深刻影响 Pt 等贵金属催化剂的性能。通过可理解的结构-功能关系精确调控催化性能的这种能力蕴含着巨大的前景。

一个有前景的途径是将这一概念扩展到具有优化集合体和配体环境的隔离金属位点的单原子合金催化剂。 原位表征和计算建模的进步可以进一步增进我们的基本理解,以实现双金属和合金催化剂的理性设计,用于特定的应用。
