Based on the details provided in the file, several articles highlight remaining challenges and knowledge gaps in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysis research. 

One major challenge emphasized is the need to develop alternative catalysts to replace expensive platinum-based industrial catalysts, which is noted as a long-term goal for improving PDH processes [10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]. Related to this is the need to explore non-precious metal catalysts and improve catalyst stability and energy efficiency [10.1021/cr5002436].

Another key gap identified is definitively characterizing the identity and structure of active sites in promising catalysts like gallium-exchanged zeolites, where computational predictions remain controversial and experimental validation at real catalyst loadings is lacking [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724].

Several articles also note that side reactions, equilibrium limitations, and catalyst deactivation from coking remain persistent issues needing further study to optimize commercial PDH processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005, 10.1021/cr5002436].

Overall, developing lower cost and higher efficiency PDH catalysts through coordinated computational screening and experimental validation of novel architectures remains an open challenge requiring systematic long-term efforts.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The articles reviewed reveal promising directions while underscoring gaps needing research for propane dehydrogenation catalysis. Stepping back, an opportunity exists to adopt an interdisciplinary approach harnessing computational workflows and operando spectroscopies to elucidate structure-activity relationships for emerging single atom and confined cluster catalysts. Concurrently improving mechanistic understanding and thermal/coking stability via tailored catalyst-support interactions provides a pathway towards superior non-precious metal formulations. Translation to flow reactor prototypes can accelerate iterative screening. Overall a systematic framework spanning computational catalyst discovery, mechanistic validation, and chemical engineering modules for performance optimization may unlock step-change advancements.

一大挑战是需要开发替代催化剂来代替昂贵的铂基工业催化剂,这被认为是改进PDH工艺的长期目标 [10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]。与此相关的是,需要探索非贵金属催化剂,改进催化剂稳定性和能源效率 [10.1021/cr5002436]。  

另一个关键空白是确定诸如镓交换沸石等有前景催化剂中的活性中心的身份和结构,其中计算机预测仍有争议,实验验证在真实催化剂负荷下也欠缺 [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]。  

几篇文章还指出,副反应、平衡限制和焦炭导致的催化剂失活仍然存在问题,需要进一步研究以优化商业PDH工艺[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005, 10.1021/cr5002436]。  

总的来说,通过协调计算机筛选和对新型结构的实验验证来开发低成本和高效率PDH催化剂,仍然是一个需要系统长期努力的挑战。
