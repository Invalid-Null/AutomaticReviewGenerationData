Metal Oxide Catalysts - What metal oxides like Cr2O3, Ga2O3 have shown promising propane dehydrogenation activity?

Among metal oxide catalysts explored for propane dehydrogenation (PDH), gallium oxide (Ga2O3) and chromium oxide (Cr2O3) emerge as promising options. Initial findings reveal their potential to deliver considerable propylene yields, frequently surpassing common platinum-based catalysts [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. 

Specifically, different polymorphs of Ga2O3 nanocrystals, including α-Ga2O3, β-Ga2O3 and γ-Ga2O3, showcase appreciable conversion levels. Surface characterization experiments confirm the abundance of medium-strength acid sites on their surfaces, deemed responsible for high activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Comparing their performance, β-Ga2O3 secures the foremost position, attributed to possessing maximum surface acid density. Adding a silica support further boosts its productivity closer to benchmark PtSn catalysts.

Likewise, Cr2O3 anchored on silica elicits excellent selectivity, linked to weak metal-support interactions benefiting propylene formation [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. Its partially oxidized or defective sites demonstrate improved yields as well. Doping Cr2O3 with Pt appears promising too. Further, kinetic simulations verify non-oxidative propane dehydrogenation feasibly occurs on its reduced α-Cr2O3(0001) facet. 

Nevertheless, despite demonstrating decent initial activity, stability and resistance against carbon fouling remain prevailing concerns with these metal oxides. Rational design methodologies circumventing these limitations are still lacking. Identifying optimal promoters, modifiers and multifunctional supports can pave the way ahead. Additionally, further mechanistic and spectroscopic studies will be invaluable to gain in-depth understanding about active sites, reaction intermediates and deactivation pathways.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Capitalizing on nascent advancements with Ga2O3 and Cr2O3 for PDH presents an opportune stage to configure high-performance and robust catalytic systems. Operando studies in tandem with sophisticated electron microscopy and spectroscopy can elucidate intricate surface chemistry. Eventually, multifaceted research pursuits can actualize Ga2O3 and Cr2O3 as viable alternatives to conventional PDH catalysts.

金属氧化物催化剂 - 什么金属氧化物如Cr2O3, Ga2O3表现出有前景的丙烷脱氢活性?

在被探索用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)的金属氧化物催化剂中,氧化镓(Ga2O3)和氧化铬(Cr2O3)显现为有前景的选择[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。初步结果显示,它们有可能提供可观的丙烯收率,经常超过常见的铂基催化剂。

具体来说,Ga2O3的不同多型纳米晶体,包括α-Ga2O3,β-Ga2O3和γ-Ga2O3,都表现出可观的转化水平。表面表征实验确认它们表面上中等强度酸位较为丰富[Placeholder_Of_DOI2],这被认为是其高活性的原因。比较它们的性能,β-Ga2O3的位置最前,这是由于其具有最大的表面酸密度。在二氧化硅支撑体上的Cr2O3表现出优异的选择性,这与金属与支撑体之间的弱相互作用有关,有利于丙烯的形成 [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。它的部分氧化或者缺陷位点的产物也得到了改进。用Pt掺杂Cr2O3也显示出有前途。此外,动力学模拟验证非氧化丙烷脱氢反应可在其还原的α-Cr2O3(0001)面上发生。


尽管表现出不错的初始活性,但稳定性和抗碳污染仍然是这些金属氧化物面临的主要问题。目前还缺乏合理的设计方法来避免这些限制。识别优化的助剂、修饰剂和多功能支撑体可为未来铺平道路。此外,深入的机理和光谱研究对于获得对活性位点、反应中间体和失活途径的深入理解也将是无价的。
