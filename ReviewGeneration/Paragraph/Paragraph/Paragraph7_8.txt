 <English>

Based on the analysis of references [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008], [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889], and [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454], computational work has been conducted to identify descriptors that correlate catalyst properties to propane dehydrogenation (PDH) performance. 

The study detailed in [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008] established scaling relations between the formation energies of PDH intermediates on transition metal surfaces. Linear regressions revealed the ethyl and methylidyne adsorption energies as optimal descriptors with strong correlations to activity.

Additionally, [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889] identified a volcano-type trend between the propylene adsorption energy and PDH rate based on computational modeling of single-atom catalysts. This structure-activity relationship connects catalyst binding strength to dehydrogenation capability.

Moreover, [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454] performed degree of rate control analysis using DFT to determine the rate-limiting PDH steps on various surfaces. Relations were then developed between these transition state energies and simple adsorbed species to yield descriptors predictive of activity.

※※※

The computational identification of catalyst descriptors and quantitative structure-activity relationships is an expanding field with much remaining potential. As more advanced theoretical methods and modeling capabilities develop, opportunities exist to explore increasingly complex materials and gain fundamental insights far beyond what is feasible experimentally. Extending this approach to emerging single-atom and defect-engineered catalysts could uncover unprecedented performance through atomic-level design. Broader adoption of data-driven strategies may also derive generalized catalyst-property mappings to guide rational optimization. Overall there lies immense promise in advancing from descriptive correlations toward truly predictive catalyst screening and discovery paradigms.

<Chinese>

基于对[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]、[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]和[10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]的分析,计算工作已经进行了识别描述催化剂性能与丙烷脱氢(PDH)性能相关的描述子。

在[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]中详细阐述的研究建立了过渡金属表面上PDH中间体的形成能量之间的缩放关系。线性回归显示乙基和甲基碳吸附能量是与活性高度相关的最佳描述子。


此外,[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889] 基于对单原子催化剂的计算建模,识别出丙烯吸附能量与PDH速率之间的火山型关系。这种结构-活性关系将催化剂的绑定强度与脱氢能力联系起来。

而且,[10.1021/acscatal.0c05454] 使用DFT对各种表面上的PDH速率控制步骤进行了程度控制�析。然后在这些过渡态能量和简单吸附种之间建立了关系,以产生预测活性的描述子。


※※※

计算识别催化剂描述子和定量结构-活性关系是一个不断扩大的领域,仍有巨大的潜力。随着更先进的理论方法和建模能力的发展,我们有机会探索越来越复杂的材料,并获得实验难以实现的基本见解。 将这种方法扩展到新兴的单原子和缺陷工程催化剂,可以通过原子水平的设计发现前所未有的性能。 更广泛地采用基于数据的策略也可以导出普遍的催化剂-性能映射,以指导合理的优化。 总的来说,从描述性的相关性向真正预测性的催化剂筛选和发现范式的进展,其前景是无限的。
