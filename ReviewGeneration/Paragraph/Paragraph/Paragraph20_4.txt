	<English>
		Based on the detailed analysis of the file, some studies evaluate zeolite supports like MFI, BEA, and CHA structures as promising options for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. As referenced in [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548], the use of BEA zeolite support instead of amorphous silica can effectively decrease the size of PtSn particles down to 0.9 nm and improve catalytic performance. Confinement of subnanometric PtSn clusters inside the micropores of MFI zeolite is also demonstrated to optimize PDH activity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548].
		
		The potential of MFI and BEA zeolites as catalyst supports for PDH reactions is further corroborated in [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. Their facile synthesis procedure, tunable acidity and moderate pore sizes make MFI and BEA suitable for stabilizing isolated metal atoms or ultra-small clusters. Pt-Ga alloys anchored on hierarchical MFI maintain 8% propane conversion after 30 days, while Pt-Zn species on BEA approach 90% propylene selectivity at near-equilibrium conversion [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816].

		CHA zeolite is evaluated in [10.1021/jacs.2c03941] as a promising support that enables Ga-CHA catalysts to efficiently convert propane with 96% propylene selectivity. Isolated framework Ga+ sites and extra-framework Ga+ species are identified as the active PDH sites. Titanosilicate TS-1 with weaker acidity than conventional zeolites is also assessed as an improved support, increasing propane conversion, propylene selectivity and catalyst stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024].
		
		The potential benefits of zeolites are attributed to their tunable chemistry, high thermal stability, and geometric confinement effects for stabilizing isolated atoms and ultrasmall clusters. However, diffusion limitations and strong acid sites can negatively impact performance. Mitigation strategies may focus on creating mesoporosity and optimizing composition.

		※※※
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings showcase the promising potential of zeolite-supported metal and bimetallic PDH catalysts. While MFI, BEA and CHA topologies have shown particular effectiveness, ample opportunities remain to explore other framework types like FER, MOR, FAU etc. and systematic variations in composition and mesoporosity generation. Incorporating cutting-edge operando characterization techniques can offer deeper insights on structure-performance relationships. There is also scope for multi-scale modeling studies to guide optimized zeolite design. Broader commercial adoption may necessitate demonstrate scaled-up feasibility and technoeconomic viability.

	<Chinese>
		根据文件详细分析,一些研究评估MFI、BEA和CHA结构等分子筛,作为丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂的有前途支持体。正如[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]参考文献中提到的,与非晶质二氧化硅相比,使用BEA分子筛支持体可以有效降低PtSn颗粒的大小至0.9 nm,并改善催化性能。将亚纳米PtSn簇捕获在MFI分子筛的微孔内,也被证明可以优化PDH活性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]。
		
		MFI和BEA分子筛用作PDH反应催化剂支持体的潜力,在[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]文献中得到进一步证实。它们的简易合成程序、可调酸性和适中的孔径,使MFI和BEA分子筛适合稳定隔离金属原子或超小簇。锚定在分层MFI上的Pt-Ga合金,在30天内的反应中保持8%的丙烷转化率,而BEA上的Pt-Zn物种接近90%的丙烯选择性,达到近平衡转化率[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。
		
		[10.1021/jacs.2c03941]评价CHA分子筛作为一种有前景的支持体,使Ga-CHA催化剂可以高效转化丙烷,并获得96%的丙烯选择性。隔离的骨架内Ga+位点和骨架外Ga+物种被确定为PDH活性位点。对比常规分子筛,酸强度较弱的硅钛分子筛TS-1也被评估为改进的支持体,提高了丙烷转化率、丙烯选择性和催化剂稳定性[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024]。
		
		分子筛的潜在益处归因于其可调节化学性质、高热稳定性以及几何限制效应来稳定隔离原子和超小簇。然而,扩散限制和强酸位点会对性能产生不利影响。缓解策略可能侧重于制造介孔和优化组成。
		
		※※※
		综合理解与未来展望:研究结果展示了分子筛负载的金属和双金属PDH催化剂具有非常有前景的潜力。虽然 MFI、BEA 和 CHA 拓扑结构已显示出特定的有效性,但探索其他骨架类型如FER、MOR、FAU等以及组成和介孔生成的体系变化仍有足够的机会。采用尖端的原位表征技术可以深入研究结构-性能关系。还可以通过多尺度建模研究来指导分子筛的优化设计。更广泛的商业采用可能需要证明扩大规模的可行性和技术经济可行性。
