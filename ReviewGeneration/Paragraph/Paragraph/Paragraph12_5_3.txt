Based on the information extracted from the references provided, several studies have evaluated supported mono-metallic platinum (Pt), palladium (Pd), and iridium (Ir) catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most widely used and commercially successful PDH catalysts. For example, a Pt/Al2O3 catalyst demonstrated decent activity but lower selectivity of 28.5%, compared to 63.1% for a NiMo/Al2O3 catalyst [10.1021/acscatal.0c05711]. Another study showed Pt/γ-Al2O3 with 90% selectivity, increased to 98% with 1 wt% boron promoter which also reduced coking [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]. Encapsulating Pt nanoclusters in zeolites via Pt-O-Ge anchoring linkages gave exceptionally high stability [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. Further optimization of metal-support interactions seems vital.

Some studies also evaluated Pd, Rh, and Ir, but found inherent issues of poor selectivity for Pd, and high expense for Rh and Ir [10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]. A RhCu single atom alloy (SAA) catalyst did demonstrate markedly higher activity and 100% propene selectivity versus 80% on Pt, showing promise [science.abg8389].

In summary, further improvements in stability and selectivity are still needed. Elucidating metal-support effects and stabilization of isolated active sites appear to be fruitful areas for advancement.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Propane dehydrogenation via supported mono-metallic catalysts, especially Pt-based, shows considerable untapped potential. While reasonable activity has been achieved industrially, persisting selectivity and stability limitations signify ample room for optimization. Two prime areas of further investigation center on (1) systematic modulation of metal-support interplays to strongly anchor isolated metal sites, resisting sintering; and (2) stabilization of specific active site ensembles, whether single atoms or sub-nanometric clusters, that balance activity against over-hydrogenolysis. Mastering the selective breakage of strong C-H bonds while circumventing excessive C-C cleavage remains an imposing challenge. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques and computational modeling will likely play important roles in unraveling suitable active site architectures. Broader adoption of high-risk, high-reward discovery efforts instead of incremental tweaks could unveil breakthrough catalyst formulations. Instilling heightened collaboration between academia and industry via public-private partnerships may facilitate transfer of cutting-edge innovations to commercial realization. The field still retains much prospect for transformative progress.

根据所提供参考文献的信息,几项研究已经评价了包括铂(Pt)、钯(Pd)和铱(Ir)的载体单金属催化剂用于丙烷脱氢。

Pt基催化剂被报道是应用最广泛并商业化成功的PDH催化剂。例如,Pt/Al2O3催化剂表现出不错的活性但较低的选择性28.5%,而NiMo/Al2O3催化剂的选择性可达63.1% [10.1021/acscatal.0c05711]。另一项研究表明,1 wt%硼促进剂不仅将Pt/γ-Al2O3的选择性从90%提高到98%,还减少结焦 [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]。通过Pt-O-Ge锚定连接将Pt纳米簇封装于分子筛中,获得了极高的稳定性 [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。进一步优化金属与载体的相互作用显得至关重要。

一些研究也评价了Pd、Rh和Ir, 但发现Pd固有的选择性差,Rh和Ir昂贵的问题难以克服 [10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]。一种RhCu单原子合金(SAA)催化剂的活性明显更高,丙烯选择性达100%对比Pt的80%,显示了一定的潜力 [science.abg8389]。

总的来说,仍需进一步提高稳定性和选择性。阐明金属-载体效应和稳定隔离的活性位点似乎是有前景的领域。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:利用载体单金属催化剂,特别是Pt基的丙烷脱氢具有相当大的未被开发的潜力。已在工业上取得一定的活性,但持续存在的选择性和稳定性的限制意味着巨大的改进空间。进一步研究的两个主要方面集中于:(1)有系统地调控金属与载体间的相互作用,对隔离的金属位点进行紧固锚定,避免团聚;和(2)稳定某些活性位点的组成,不管是单原子还是亚纳米簇,使活性和过度氢化裂解之间取得平衡。有选择性的活化强C-H键而又避免过多碳碳键裂解,仍然是一个巨大的挑战。先进的原位表征技术和计算模拟将可能发挥重要作用来解析适宜的活性位点结构。更为广泛的采用高风险高回报的发现研究,而不是增量式调整,能揭示突破性的催化剂方案。加强学术界和产业界的协同,比如通过公私合作伙伴关系,可以促进尖端创新向商业实现的转移。该领域仍具备巨大的进步潜力。
