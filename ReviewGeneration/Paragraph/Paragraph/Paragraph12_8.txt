 <English>

Supported mono-metallic platinum (Pt) catalysts have been widely explored for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to Pt's superior ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing undesirable C-C bond cleavage. Several studies have evaluated Pt supported on various metal oxides like alumina and silica. 

One article showed Pt supported on alumina maintains high initial activity but deactivates over multiple PDH cycles due to Pt agglomeration accelerated by sulfur [10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]. Adding sulfur to Pt/alumina during preparation or in the gas phase was found to greatly enhance selectivity to propylene, with predosing at 873 K having the largest effect [10.1006/jcat.1994.1333].

Atomic layer deposition (ALD) of metal oxide overcoats on supported Pt catalysts is an emerging strategy to increase PDH yield and selectivity. Al2O3 and TiO2 ALD overcoats on extruded Pt/Al2O3 gave higher propane conversion compared to unmodified Pt/Al2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. Promoting Pt/Al2O3 with 1 wt% boron markedly reduced coking and boosted propylene selectivity from 90% to 98% over 12 hours [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548].

Besides Pt/metal oxide supports, recent work has explored single Pt sites isolated in zeolites like Ge-UTL. Confining sub-nanometric Pt clusters in Ge-UTL channels via Pt-O-Ge bonds gave a catalyst with excellent stability and 96% propylene selectivity [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7].

While less studied than Pt, some supported Pd, Rh and Ir catalysts have shown promise for PDH but face economic challenges [10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]. An supported RhCu single atom alloy (SAA) catalyst demonstrated far higher activity and 100% propylene selectivity compared to Pt/Al2O3 under identical conditions [10.1126/science.abg8389].

Overall, Pt-based catalysts have been the most widely commercialized for PDH, with strategic modifications like promoters and overcoats emerging to further enhance selectivity and stability. Beyond Pt, single atom alloy catalysts present opportunities to dramatically boost productivity if economic hurdles can be overcome.

※※※ 
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Supported mono- and bi-metallic catalysts have demonstrated tremendous advancements for propane dehydrogenation. However, opportunities remain to explore novel supports like metal-organic frameworks and optimize beyond conventional metals. Leveraging in situ characterization techniques can provide invaluable insights into working catalysts. Operando spectroscopy and microscopy methods to probe dynamic structural changes could elucidate deactivation pathways and guide design of enhanced catalysts. Machine learning additionally shows promise to accelerate identification of optimized PDH catalyst compositions and architectures.

<Chinese>
由于Pt优异的C-H键激活能力和较低的C-C键断裂活性,已广泛研究了各种载体支持的单金属铂催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。几项研究评价了支撑在各种金属氧化物(如氧化铝和二氧化硅)上的Pt。 

一篇文章表明,氧化铝支持的Pt起初表现出高活性,但在多次PDH循环中逐渐失活,因为硫加速了Pt的团聚[10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]。向Pt/氧化铝催化剂的制备中或气相中加入硫可以大大提高丙烯选择性,在873K下预处理具有最大效果[10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]。

原子层沉积(ALD)金属氧化物涂层是提高PDH产率和选择性的新策略。 AL2O3和TiO2 ALD涂层提高了挤出Pt/Al2O3的丙烷转化率,相比无涂层的Pt/Al2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。用1 wt%的硼促进Pt/Al2O3可以显著减少结焦,并在12小时内将丙烯选择性从90%提高到98% [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]。

除了金属氧化物支持的Pt,最近的工作探索了单个Pt位点隔离在分子筛如Ge-UTL中。 将亚纳米Pt簇隔离在Ge-UTL通道中通过Pt-O-Ge键,得到了一个具有优异稳定性和96%丙烯选择性的催化剂[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。

虽然研究不如Pt多,但一些受支持的Pd,Rh和Ir催化剂在PDH方面也表现出了前景,但面临经济挑战[10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]。一种支持的RhCu单原子合金(SAA)催化剂相比Pt/Al2O3在相同条件下表现出更高的活性和100%的丙烯选择性[10.1126/science.abg8389]。

总体而言,Pt基催化剂已被广泛商业化用于PDH,促进剂和涂层等战略改性不断出现以进一步提高选择性和稳定性。 除Pt之外,如果能克服经济障碍,单原子合金催化剂提供了大大提高生产力的机会。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 支持的单金属和双金属催化剂在丙烷脱氢方面已经取得了巨大进步。 但是,仍有机会探索诸如金属有机框架等新型载体,并优化除常规金属之外的材料。 利用原位表征技术可以提供关于工作催化剂的宝贵信息。 正在进行的光谱学和显微技术方法来探测动态结构变化,可以阐明失活途径并指导设计增强催化剂。 机器学习还有望加速优化PDH催化剂成分和体系结构的确定。
