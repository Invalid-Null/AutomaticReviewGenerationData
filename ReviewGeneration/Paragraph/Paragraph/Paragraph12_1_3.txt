Based on the details provided in the file, several studies have explored supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts appear to be the most widely studied and utilized. Multiple articles evaluate alumina-supported Pt catalysts, with one showing that 1 wt% boron promotion improved stability and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]. Another demonstrated excellent performance for Pt confined inside Ge-UTL zeolite due to strong metal-support interactions [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. Data also indicates common deactivation mechanisms of Pt catalysts are coking and metal agglomeration [0021-9517(86)90312-X].

Beyond Pt, a few studies explored other Group VIII metals. One article evaluated supported RhCu single atom alloy (SAA) catalysts, finding superior activity, 100% selectivity, and coking resistance compared to a traditional Pt/Al2O3 catalyst [10.1126/science.abg8389]. Another study showed poor performance for supported Ru nanoparticles - only 4.5% propylene selectivity at 25% conversion versus 46% for Pt [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]. No evaluations of supported Pd or Ir were found.

Overall, Pt-based catalysts, especially when optimized via promoters and metal-support interactions, demonstrate the best combination of activity and stability for PDH amongst the supported mono-metallic Group VIII catalysts explored thus far. Further work is still needed to mitigate deactivation issues and benchmark other metals like Pd and Ir.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Supported mono-metallic Pt catalysts currently represent the most advanced system for selective dehydrogenation of light alkanes. However, stability limitations motivate exploration of novel compositions and optimization strategies. Beyond existing methods of promoters and metal-support effects, future directions could examine single atom alloy configurations, as demonstrated for RhCu, to stabilize active sites. Pd and Ir also warrant thorough benchmarking given promising intrinsic chemistry. Furthermore, operando spectroscopic techniques combined with kinetic and DFT studies will offer valuable mechanistic clarity to guide design. Broader adoption of high-throughput computational and experimental tools can rapidly advance next-generation catalysts to meet demands for on-purpose propylene.

<Chinese>

根据文件提供的详细信息,几项研究已经探索了负载型单金属Pt、Pd和Ir催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。

Pt基催化剂似乎是研究和利用最广泛的。多篇文章评估了氧化铝支持的Pt催化剂,其中一篇文章表明1 wt%的硼促进剂改善了稳定性和选择性[10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]。另一篇文章证明Pt固定在Ge-UTL沸石内因强大的金属-支撑相互作用而显示出色的性能[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。数据还表明Pt催化剂的常见失活机制是结焦和金属团聚[0021-9517(86)90312-X]。

除Pt之外,还有几项研究探索了其他八族金属。一篇文章评估了负载RhCu单原子合金(SAA)催化剂,发现其与传统Pt/Al2O3催化剂相比,具有更高的活性、100%的选择性和抗结焦性[10.1126/science.abg8389]。另一项研究显示负载Ru纳米粒子的性能很差——25%转化率时丙烯选择性仅为4.5%,而Pt为46% [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。没有发现对负载Pd或Ir的评估。

总的来说,Pt基催化剂(特别是通过促进剂和金属-支撑相互作用进行优化)在迄今为止探索的负载单金属八族催化剂中示PDH反应最佳的活性和稳定性组合。为缓解失活问题并基准其他金属(如Pd和Ir),还需要进一步的工作。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:负载型单金属Pt催化剂目前代表了选择性脱氢轻烷烃最先进的体系。然而,稳定性的局限激发了对新颖组成和优化策略的探索。 除现有的促进剂和金属-支撑效应方法外,未来的发展方向可能会考查单原子合金配置,如文献中对RhCu的演示,以稳定活性位点。 Pd和Ir由于具有很好的本征化学活性,也值得彻底基准测试。 此外,运算体谱技术与动力学和DFT研究的结合将提供有价值的机理明晰度来指导设计。 更广泛地采用高通量计算和实验工具可以快速推进下一代催化剂,满足目的丙烯的需求。
