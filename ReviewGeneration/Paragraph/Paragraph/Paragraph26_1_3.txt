Based on the details extracted from the file, some articles employ advanced microscopy techniques like aberration-corrected STEM, HAADF-STEM, HRTEM, and integrated differential phase contrast (iDPC) imaging to characterize metal-support interfaces in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. 

Specific examples include using HAADF-STEM and EDX mapping to analyze the location, dispersion, and distribution of metal species like Pt, Sn, Co, and Zn on zeolite and metal oxide supports [10.1021/jacs.2c02636, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/jechem.2021.04.034]. The images provide insights into the coordination environment and isolated nature of active sites. Another study utilizes HRTEM and iDPC-STEM to precisely identify subnanometric Pt clusters confined within the channels of MFI zeolites [10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4]. 

Other articles track changes at the metal-support interface after pretreatment and reaction using AC-HAADF-STEM. One example shows the evolution of Pt and Sn species from single atoms to nanoclusters on alumina during calcination, reduction, and propane dehydrogenation steps [10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X]. Another article employs microscopy to relate performance changes to variations in metal particle size, morphology, and support interactions after regeneration [10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035].

Overall, advanced microscopy enables direct visualization of metal-support interfaces in propane dehydrogenation catalysts, from the micropore confinement of isolated atoms to nanoparticle encapsulation and structural dynamics. Continued development of novel imaging modes and data analytics promises ever more detailed insights.※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advanced microscopy methods covered in the references, there exists potential to explore new techniques like machine learning-powered image analysis for automated quantification of critical descriptors like coordination numbers, facet orientations, and ensemble configurations [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. 

Another promising direction is operando microscopy under working conditions to uncover proceeding mechanisms in catalyst decay and opportunities for tailored regeneration procedures [10.1021/acscatal.2c05042]. More broadly, correlative multi-modal imaging combining spectroscopy, diffraction, and simulations can provide unprecedented details into dynamic processes at critical length and time scales during propane dehydrogenation.

根据文件中提取的详细信息,一些文章采用了像球差修正STEM、HAADF-STEM、HRTEM和集成差分相差显微技术(iDPC)成像等先进的显微技术来表征丙烷脱氢催化剂的金属支撑体界面。

具体例子包括使用HAADF-STEM和EDX映射来分析Pt、Sn、Co和Zn等金属物种在分子筛和金属氧化物支撑体上的位置、分散性和分布[10.1021/jacs.2c02636,10.1021/acscatal.3c00816,10.1021/jechem.2021.04.034]。这些图像提供了有关活性位的配位环境和隔离性质的见解。另一项研究利用HRTEM和iDPC-STEM准确识别保留在MFI分子筛通道内的亚纳米Pt簇[10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4]。


其他文章使用AC-HAADF-STEM跟踪预处理和反应后金属支撑体界面的变化。一个例子显示了钙化、还原和丙烷脱氢步骤中氧化铝上Pt和Sn物种从单原子到纳米簇的演变[10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X]。另一篇文章采用显微技术将性能变化与再生后金属颗粒大小、形态和支撑体作用的变化联系起来[10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035]。


总的来说,先进的显微技术使人们能够直接观察丙烷脱氢催化剂中的金属支撑体界面,从被隔离原子的微孔内包裹,到纳米颗粒的嵌入和结构动力学。新显微成像模式和数据分析手段的不断发展有望带来更详细的见解。※※※


综合理解与未来展望:考虑到参考文献中涵盖的先进显微技术,开发新技术如基于机器学习的图像分析进行关键描述子(如配位数、面向和集合构型)的自动定量具有很大潜力[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。


另一个有前途的方向是在工作条件下进行原位显微技术研究,揭示催化剂衰减的发生机制并为定制再生程序提供机会[10.1021/acscatal.2c05042]。更广泛地说,多模态成像的关联技术整合光谱学、衍射技术和计算模拟,可以在丙烷脱氢过程的关键尺度上提供前所未有的动态过程细节。
