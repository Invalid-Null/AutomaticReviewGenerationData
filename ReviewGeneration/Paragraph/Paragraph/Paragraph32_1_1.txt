Based on the details extracted from the file, several articles summarize remaining challenges and knowledge gaps in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysis research. 

One major challenge highlighted is the need to develop alternative catalysts to replace expensive platinum-based industrial catalysts, which is noted as a "long-term pursuit" [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]. Relatedly, lowering noble metal loading and increasing catalyst stability are called out as areas needing improvement [DOI: 10.1021/cr5002436].

Additionally, multiple articles state that the intrinsic mechanism of how active sites like oxygen vacancies [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c02621] and paired framework aluminum centers [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724] enhance PDH activity is not fully understood. The identity and definitive characterization of the most active gallium species also remains an open question [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724].

Other gaps include better understanding coke formation kinetics [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331], mitigating rapid catalyst deactivation from coking [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005], overcoming limited equilibrium conversion, and reducing undesired side reactions [DOI: 10.1021/cr5002436].

To address these challenges, future research directions proposed include exploring new single atom and confined cluster catalyst architectures [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331] and using computational modeling techniques like ab initio metadynamics simulations [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816].
