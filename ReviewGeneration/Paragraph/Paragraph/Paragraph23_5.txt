 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an endothermic process that converts propane to propylene, an important building block in the petrochemical industry. Catalyst stability remains a key challenge, as harsh operating conditions of 500-700°C coupled with multiple regeneration cycles frequently lead to deactivation via sintering and coking. Recent strategies have focused on encapsulating metal nanoparticles within the microporous structure of zeolites to enhance stability. 

As analyzed in the reference information [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019], unsupported metal catalysts often suffer from severe sintering and poor stability. However, encapsulation within zeolites improves stability through a confinement effect that prevents mobility and coalescence of metal clusters. An example is provided of encapsulating bimetallic clusters within zeolite Y via a ship-in-a-bottle synthesis, which significantly enhanced stability compared to conventional impregnation. The strong metal-zeolite interaction helps stabilize the encapsulated clusters.

Further evidence is furnished in the reference data [10.1038/s41467-021-23426-z], highlighting the use of zeolites versus mesoporous supports. Encapsulating sub-nanometric PtZn clusters inside siliceous MFI zeolite channels led to the highest stability of Pt-based PDH catalysts. Diffusion limitations were overcome by using bulk MFI crystals, enabling full utilization of active sites. This delivered exceptional stability up to 220 hours time-on-stream.

Additionally, information outlined in [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034] demonstrates multi-cycle stability via zeolite encapsulation. Zn species anchored within the pores of S-1 zeolite showed good regeneration capability over several PDH cycles by re-oxidizing reduced Znδ+ back to the active Zn2+ state after calcination. Easy regeneration was enabled by the encapsulation method.


※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The analyzed findings showcase metal encapsulation in zeolites as a promising strategy to overcome stability issues in PDH catalysis stemming from metal sintering and coking. Key zeolite properties like microporosity, hydrophobicty, and strong metal-support anchoring interactions help stabilize metal clusters compared to conventional supports. 

However, several aspects warrant further enquiry. Kinetic studies should correlate site confinement effects with catalyst lifetimes. Elucidating degradation mechanisms and regenerability could guide designs enhancing multi-cycle stability. Additionally, exploring new zeolite compositions and topologies may offer improved metal anchoring while balancing activity and selectivity. Rational integration of mesoporosity could also facilitate access to encapsulated sites. Such developments may spearhead the next generation of robust PDH catalysts.

<Chinese>

丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一种将丙烷转化为丙烯的放热过程,丙烯是石化工业中的重要基础化学药剂。在500-700°C的严苛反应条件和多次再生循环下,组分烧结和结焦常常导致催化剂活性的损失,因此催化剂稳定性仍然是关键难题。近期的策略集中在将金属纳米粒子封装于分子筛的微孔结构中以提高稳定性。

正如参考文献[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019]分析的那样,非支持的金属催化剂通常遭受严重的烧结和较差的稳定性。然而,分子筛内的包封可通过抑制金属簇的运动和合并来改善稳定性。通过“瓶中船”合成法将双金属簇封装于分子筛Y中可显著增强稳定性,相比于常规浸渍法。强大的金属-分子筛相互作用有助于稳定封装的簇。


参考数据[10.1038/s41467-021-23426-z]中进一步提供的证据强调了分子筛与介孔材料的作用。将亚纳米PtZn簇封装在硅质MFI型分子筛通道中导致Pt基丙烷脱氢催化剂具有最高的稳定性。通过使用大晶粒MFI克服了扩散限制,从而实现了活性位点的全部利用。在220小时反应期内表现出卓越的稳定性。


此外,参考文献[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034]概述的信息表明,通过分子筛包封可以实现多循环稳定性。锚定在S-1分子筛孔内的Zn物种在几个PDH循环中表现出良好的再生能力,通过在焙烧后将还原的Znδ+再氧化回活性Zn2+态。分子筛包封使催化剂具有良好的再生性。



※※※

综合理解与未来展望:分析结果表明,金属在分子筛中的包封是克服PDH催化中因金属烧结和结焦导致的稳定性问题的有前途的策略。分子筛的微孔性、疏水性和强大的金属-载体锚定相互作用可帮助稳定金属簇,相比于常规载体。


但是,一些其他方面需要进一步研究。应该对位限制效应与催化剂使用寿命之间的关系进行动力学研究。阐明降解机制和再生能力可以指导提高多循环稳定性的设计。此外,探索新的分子筛成分和拓扑结构可以提供改善的金属锚定效应,同时平衡活性和选择性。合理引入介孔也可以促进进入封装位点。这些发展可能引领下一代稳定PDH催化剂的诞生。
