    Based on the detailed analysis from the references furnished [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008, 10.1021/acscatal.1c04092, 10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1006/jcat.1996.0001], bimetallic and alloy Pt catalysts with secondary metals like Sn, Ga, Zn exhibit modulated performance for propane dehydrogenation compared to monometallic Pt. Adding these secondary metals helps decrease the size of Pt ensembles and increase dispersion, altering the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites. This leads to enhanced selectivity to propylene along with improved stability by mitigating coking and sintering phenomena [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084].

Specifically, Pt-Sn catalysts supported on various carriers have been widely implemented for commercial propane dehydrogenation processes and extensively studied in literature [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1006/jcat.1996.0001]. The addition of Sn suppresses hydrogenolysis and isomerization side reactions over Pt, attributed to an electronic effect that increases the Pt d-band vacancy and strengthens Pt-C bonds while weakening Pt-H bonds [10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084]. This electronic tuning by Sn also lowers the propane dissociation barrier, facilitating selective C-H activation [10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.107]. Furthermore, Sn helps remove coke precursors from Pt sites, improving stability [10.1002/anie.201507119]. The optimal Sn/Pt ratio is around 1-1.5, beyond which excess Sn leads to loss of activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]. 

Incorporating Ga and Zn metals in Pt also modulate selectivity and stability. Pt-Ga intermetallic nanoparticles help suppress hydrogenolysis and isomerization side reactions [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]. PtZn alloys derived from isolated, atomically ordered Zn-anchored Pt single sites demonstrate remarkable stability for prolonged propane dehydrogenation operation [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. The isolated Pt sites in the PtZn4 motif activate propane with optimal ensemble size effects [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631].

Another key strategy is the incorporation of PtM alloys as sub-nanometric clusters confined within microporous zeolites and mesoporous materials. The pores help stabilize the nanoclusters and prevent agglomeration during high temperature reactions [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1021/acscatal.1c04092]. Additionally, matching the zeolite channel dimensions with the cluster size improves reactant accessibility while retaining selectivity from shape constraints. This pore confinement approach realizes great potential in further advancing PtM nanocluster catalysts for commercial propane dehydrogenation applications.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements in bimetallic and alloyed Pt catalysts furnished, tremendous prospects exist in strategically designing tri- and multi-metallic combinations that offer synergistic geometric and electronic effects at lower noble metal loadings. Heteroatom dopants like Si and B might help anchor ultra-small alloyed clusters with additional modulation [10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]. High-throughput computational screening can accelerate identification of optimal multi-metallic motifs and compositions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. Furthermore, recent progress in single-atom alloy catalysts might potentiate breakthroughs once translated to propane dehydrogenation systems [10.1126/science.abg7894]. Overall, gaining atomic-level insights into ensemble, ligand, and metal-support interaction effects will chart pathways toward next-generation ultra-stable and selective catalysts.

<Chinese>
    根据所提供参考文献的详细分析[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008, 10.1021/acscatal.1c04092, 10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1006/jcat.1996.0001],与单金属Pt相比,添加Sn、Ga、Zn等辅助金属的双金属和合金Pt催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中表现出调节性能。这些辅助金属有助于减小Pt聚簇的大小,增加分散度,改变Pt活性位点的几何和电子结构。这导致乙烯选择性的提高以及通过抑制焦化和烧结现象改善稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084]。

具体来说,各种载体支撑的Pt-Sn催化剂已经被广泛应用于商业丙烷脱氢过程,并在文献中得到了广泛研究[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1006/jcat.1996.0001]。Sn的添加抑制了Pt上的氢化裂解和异构化副反应,这是由Sn增加Pt d带空位和增强Pt-C键同时削弱Pt-H键的电子效应导致的 [10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084]。Sn的这种电子调节作用也降低了丙烷解离障碍,促进了选择性C-H激活[10.101/j.cej.2014.02.107]。此外,Sn有助于去除Pt位点上的焦化前体,从而改善稳定性[10.1002/anie.201507119]。 最佳的Sn/Pt比约为1-1.5,超过此比例,过量的Sn会导致活性的损失[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。

掺入Ga和Zn金属也可以调控选择性和稳定性。 Pt-Ga间金属纳米粒子有助于抑制氢化和异构化副反应[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]。源自被Zn锚定的隔离有序Pt单体位点的PtZn合金表现出显着的丙烷脱氢稳定性[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。 PtZn4基团中的隔离Pt位点以最佳的聚集体效应激活丙烷[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]。

另一个关键策略是将PtM合金作为亚纳米聚簇困于微孔沸石和介孔材料中。孔道有助于稳定纳米簇合物,防止在高温反应中聚集[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1021/acscatal.1c04092]。 此外,匹配沸石通道尺寸与簇合物大小可以在保持选择性的同时改善反应物的可及性。这种孔道限制方法在进一步推动PtM纳米簇状催化剂用于商业丙烷脱氢反应方面具有巨大的潜力。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 考虑到所提供的双金属和合金Pt催化剂的进步, 在更低贵金属负载量下,战略设计三金属和多金属组合,提供协同的几何和电子效应,存在巨大的前景。 Si和B等杂原子掺杂剂可能有助于锚定超小合金簇 [10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]。高通量计算筛选可以加速识别最佳多金属基团和组成 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。 此外,单原子合金催化剂的最新进展,一旦转化为丙烷脱氢体系,可能会取得突破 [10.1126/science.abg7894]。总的来说, 在原子水平上洞察集合体效应、配体效应和金属-支持体交互效应,将为下一代超稳定和高选择性催化剂指明发展方向。
