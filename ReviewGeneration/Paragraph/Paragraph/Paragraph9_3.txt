A comprehensive analysis of the literature reveals insights into how tuning oxidation states, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties of metal oxides impacts propane dehydrogenation performance. 

Studies on vanadium oxide catalysts demonstrate that partially reduced V3+/V4+ species act as active sites under propane dehydrogenation conditions, while highly reactive V5+ promotes ready propane activation in oxidative dehydrogenation via a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [10.1021/jacs.9b09235, 10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]. The chemical states of bridge oxygen also play critical roles, with weaker VOx-support interactions lowering bridge oxygen electron density and enhancing C-H bond rupture capability [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]. For chromium oxides, generation of Cr3+/Cr2+ species helps achieve high propylene yield, although completing the Cr6+/Cr3+ redox cycle is essential for stability [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999, 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183]. Aluminum and gallium oxides rely on tetrahedral cation sites to provide medium-strong Lewis acidity that correlates with activity [10.1021/acscatal.0c05009, 10.1016/j.jcat.2006.02.017, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]. Adding secondary metal oxides like ZnO [10.1021/acscatal.0c01580] or MoOx [10.1021/jacs.9b09235] alters properties like oxidation state, oxygen mobility, and acid-base character to tune selectivity. Overall, isolating highly dispersed metal oxide sites, whether by controlling loading, incorporation into supports, or interactions between mixed metal oxides, helps minimize overoxidation and coking issues.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Propane dehydrogenation over metal oxides relies on synergies between oxidation state, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties to balance activity, selectivity and stability. As research elucidates these structure-function relationships, opportunities emerge to extend these concepts to mixed metal-oxide systems with tunable multifunctionality. Complex solid solutions and heterostructures with nanoscale interphases could enable exquisite control over oxidation states and oxygen speciation. Strain engineering and defect chemistry approaches may also allow dynamic tuning of oxygen mobility. By integrating such strategies with operando spectroscopy and predictive modeling, next generation catalysts could unlock enhanced performance and sustainability.

对文献的全面分析揭示了调节金属氧化物的氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性如何影响丙烷脱氢性能的见解。

钒基催化剂的研究表明,在丙烷脱氢条件下,部分被还原的V3+/V4+物种作为活性位点起作用,而高活性的V5+通过Mars-van Krevelen机理促进氧化脱氢中丙烷的激活[10.1021/jacs.9b09235, 10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。桥氧的化学态也起关键作用,VOx与支撑体的相互作用越弱,桥氧的电子密度越低,C-H键断裂能力越强[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。 对于铬氧化物,Cr3+/Cr2+物种的生成有助于实现高丙烯产率,尽管完成Cr6+/Cr3+氧化还原周期对稳定性至关重要[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999, 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183]。氧化铝和氧化镓依赖四面体阳离子位点提供中等强度的路易斯酸性,与活性呈相关性[10.1021/acscatal.0c05009, 10.1016/j.jcat.2006.02.017, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]。添加二氧化锌[10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]或MoOx[10.1021/jacs.9b09235]等次要金属氧化物改变氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性质以调节选择性。总的来说,无论是通过控制负载量、掺入支撑体或不同金属氧化物之间的相互作用,隔离高度分散的金属氧化物位点都有助于最小化过氧化和结焦问题。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:丙烷脱氢对金属氧化物的依赖性在于氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性质之间的协同作用,以平衡活性、选择性和稳定性。随着研究阐明这些结构-功能关系,出现了将这些概念扩展到具有可调整的多功能性混合金属氧化物体系的机会。复杂的固溶体和异质结具有调控氧化态和氧键合态的功能。应变工程和点缺陷化学方法也可能允许动态调节氧的迁移能力。通过将这些策略与操作表征和预测建模相结合,新一代催化剂可能会释放增强的性能和可持续性。
