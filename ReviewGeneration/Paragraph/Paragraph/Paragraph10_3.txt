 <English>
Based on the details extracted from the literature, two neighboring coordinatively unsaturated Zr cations (Zrcus) located at an oxygen vacancy have been proposed as the active sites responsible for propane dehydrogenation over ZrO2 catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]. The reaction occurs via homolytic C-H bond breaking to form an isopropyl intermediate, followed by further dehydrogenation steps [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]. For CrZrOx mixed oxides, isolated Cr3+ species coordinate with Zr cations and help reduce the energy barrier for certain dehydrogenation steps based on DFT calculations [10.1021/acscatal.9b05063]. Over supported vanadium oxides like VOx/ZrO2, a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism has been suggested, where lattice oxygen activates the propane and reduced V centers get re-oxidized by O2 [10.1006/jcat.1999.2510]. Sub-monolayer and monolayer VOx structures on TiO2 can help suppress overoxidation side reactions [10.1002/anie.202005968]. For gallium oxides, a heterolytic dissociation pathway via Ga hydride and alkoxide intermediates is proposed [10.1016/j.jcat.2006.02.017]. Overall, the nature of active sites and mechanistic scenarios is found to be dependent on the specific metal oxide composition and structure.

※※※ 
The mechanistic understanding of propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts has seen significant advances, with proposals of homolytic, heterolytic and redox pathways over isolated cationic sites, oxygen vacancies and metal-oxide interfaces. Key challenges still remain in further clarifying the identity and geometric parameters of active sites using advanced characterization, and extending computational studies to larger surface models. By gaining fundamental insights into factors influencing activity and selectivity, rational design principles can be formulated to discover improved metal oxide compositions and nanostructures. An emerging area is coupling CO2 with propane dehydrogenation to tune selectivity and mitigate coking issues. New opportunities also lie in exploring interactions between metal oxide catalysts and unconventional plasmonic photocatalysts under light irradiation. Such endeavors can pave the way for better optimized and more active metal oxide catalysts for on-purpose propylene production.

<Chinese>
根据文献细节,位于氧空位的两个坐标未饱和的锆阳离子 (Zrcus) 已被提出为ZrO2 催化剂上丙烷脱氢的活性中心 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]。该反应发生在通过同解C-H 键断裂形成异丙基中间体,然后经过进一步的脱氢步骤的同解途径 [10.1016/j.jcat.2019.02.012]。 对于CrZrOx混合氧化物, 锆阳离子被隔离的Cr3+ 种协调, 根据DFT计算,有助于降低某些脱氢步骤的能垒 [10.1021/acscatal.9b05063]。 在像VOx/ZrO2 等载体钒氧化物上,已被证明了  Mars-van Krevelen机制,其中晶格氧活化丙烷,还原的V中心被O2 氧化 [10.1006/jcat.1999.2510]。 在TiO2 上次一层和一层VOx结构可以抑制副反应的氧化过度反应[10.1002/anie.202005968]。 对于镓氧化物,已被证明了 通过镓氢化物和醇盐中间体的等离子体解离途径 [10.1016/j.jcat.2006.02.017]。 总的来说, 活性中心和机理情景取决于特定的金属氧化物成分和结构。


※※※
对不同金属氧化物催化剂的丙烷脱氢反应机理理解有了重大进展, 提出了经由隔离的阳离子位点,氧空位和金属氧化物界面上的线性途径,等价途径和氧化还原途径的建议。 加深对活性位点身份和几何参数的进一步澄清仍然是一个关键的挑战, 这需要使用先进的表征, 并将计算研究扩展到更大的表面模型。 通过对影响活性和选择性因素的基本见解,可以制定合理的设计准则,以发现改进的金属氧化物组成和纳米结构。 一个新兴的领域是将CO2与丙烷脱氢偶合使用以调节选择性并减轻结焦问题。 当在光照下,金属氧化物催化剂与非常规等离子体光催化剂的相互作用也提供了新的机遇。 这些努力可以为最佳化的和更活跃的金属氧化物催化剂铺平道路,用于专用丙烯生产。
