Based on the details provided in the file, several articles evaluate supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, or Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and provide relevant data and insights. 

Specifically, articles with DOIs 10.1006/jcat.1994.1333, 10.1016/S0021-9517(86)90312-X, 10.1016/0021-9517(94)90121-X, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, 10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391, and 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548 evaluate supported Pt catalysts. Key findings show:

- Adding S to Pt/Al2O3 improves selectivity by allowing propene formation from the first pulse of propane [10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]  

- Pt/MgAl2O4 maintains high activity over multiple cycles before deactivating due to Pt agglomeration accelerated by sulfur [10.1016/S0021-9517(86)90312-X]

- Pt exists as isolated atoms and potentially small clusters on Zn-zeolite, demonstrating high PDH activity and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]  

- ALD metal oxide overcoats on Pt/Al2O3 increase PDH yield and selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]

- Adding B promoter to Pt/Al2O3 reduces coking and improves selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]

Article 10.1016/0021-9517(94)90121-X evaluates supported Ir nanoparticles, showing very low selectivity compared to Pt and optimized Ru phosphide nanoparticles.

Overall, supported Pt catalysts have been the most extensively studied for commercial propane dehydrogenation processes. Key optimizations like promoters, overcoats, and supports aim to improve Pt stability, suppress coking, and maintain selectivity.

Considering the strong promise yet need for enhancement of supported Pt catalysts, future research may focus on elucidating degradation mechanisms and applying novel design strategies to inhibit sintering while retaining selectivity. Advanced in situ characterization techniques could offer mechanistic clarity. Exploring unconventional supports with metal-support interactions or single atom catalysts present opportunities to overcome limitations. Broader horizon efforts can aim to discover new selective metals or alloys guided by computational screening and high-throughput experimentation. Ultimately, progress requires intersecting materials discovery with catalytic engineering and mechanistic comprehension.
