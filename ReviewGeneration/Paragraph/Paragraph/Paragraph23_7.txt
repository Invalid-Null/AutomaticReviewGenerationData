 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an important reaction for producing propylene, a key building block in the petrochemical industry. However, conventional supported metal catalysts often suffer from rapid deactivation via sintering, coking, and leaching during the harsh regeneration steps. Recently, zeolite materials have shown promise as catalyst supports by providing confined spaces to encapsulate metal particles and clusters. This helps stabilize the active sites and prevent aggregation and coke deposition. 

A Co@Si-BEA catalyst was synthesized with cobalt species embedded in the micropores of a siliceous BEA zeolite [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. The catalyst demonstrated excellent stability for 8 reaction-regeneration cycles in PDH testing, highlighting both the strong structural stability and the reversible coking resistance of the encapsulated cobalt sites. In situ XAFS confirmed the absence of cobalt oxide aggregates, consistent with isolated sites anchoring to the zeolite framework.

Encapsulated PtZn nanoclusters within the 10R channels of pure silica MFI zeolite also exhibited high catalytic stability.[10.1021/acscatal.1c04092] Attributing to the regioselective confinement effect, the special core-shell structure shut down carbon deposition while retaining dehydrogenation activity. Moreover, diffusion limitations were overcome for the encapsulated clusters in MFI crystals compared to bulk zeolites.

An extremely stable Pt/Ge-UTL catalyst was achieved recently by localizing sub-nanometer Pt clusters on germanium-enriched defects in UTL-type zeolite.[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] The catalyst displayed exceptional stability during 4000+ hours of propane dehydrogenation testing. In situ XAFS verified Pt was strongly bonded to Ge-d4r units, preventing agglomeration even after high-temperature regeneration. This again validates zeolites as promising supports for metal encapsulation in PDH catalysts.  

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The key findings consolidate zeolites, especially germanosilicates and pure silica materials, as encouraging supports to stabilize metal active sites via confinement for enhanced propane dehydrogenation stability. While recent demonstrations focused on traditional metals like Pt, Co and Zn, upcoming research could explore encapsulating more cost-effective metals. And apart from traditional zeolite synthesis methods, innovative techniques like ligand-assisted assembly may enable precise control over the coordination environment. Furthermore, operando spectroscopy coupled with theory and simulations will offer critical atomic-level insights to continue optimizing encapsulated metal zeolites for dehydrogenation reactions.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢(PDH)是生产丙烯的重要反应,丙烯是石油化工行业的关键基础化合物。然而,常规的支持金属催化剂通常在严苛的再生步骤期间会发生快速的失活,比如烧结,结焦和金属溶出。近来,分子筛作为催化剂支撑体提供了局限空间来包覆金属颗粒和簇合物,从而有望稳定活性中心,防止团聚和结焦。

一种Co@Si-BEA催化剂被合成,钴物种嵌入了无硅硅铋A分子筛的微孔中 [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。该催化剂在PDH测试中展现出8次反应-再生循环的优异稳定性,这凸显出钴活性中心在分子筛结构框架上锚定的稳定性和可逆结焦添加性。 原位XAFS确认钴氧化物团聚体不存在,这与钴活性中心孤立同源。

嵌入在纯硅MFI分子筛10R通道中的PtZn纳米团簇也展现出高催化稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.1c04092]。归因于其定位选择性空间约束效应,其特殊的核壳结构抑制了碳沉积同时保留了其脱氢活性。此外,与块状分子筛相比,嵌入的纳米团簇克服了PtZn在MFI晶体中的扩散限制。  

最近通过将亚纳米Plt团簇定位在UTL型分子筛中的富锗缺陷位点上合成出一种极其稳定的Pt/Ge-UTL催化剂 [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。该催化剂在4000多小时的丙烷脱氢反应测试中展现出异常的稳定性。原位XAFS证实Pt通过Pt-O-Ge键与Ge-d4r位点强烈结合,防止烧结和聚集,即使在高温再生条件下也是如此。这再次验证了分子筛作为金属包覆PDH催化剂支撑体的有望前景。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 关键发现巩固了硅锗分子筛和纯硅材料作为鼓励的支撑体以稳定通过空间约束的金属活性中心从而实现丙烷脱氢稳定性的改进。最近的研究重点放在传统金属如Pt, Co和Zn的包覆上,未来的研究可以探索更加经济高效金属的包覆。此外,传统的分子筛合成方法之外,像配体辅助组装这样的创新技术可以精确控制配位环境。 操作原位光谱技术配合理论和模拟将提供关键的原子水平洞察继续优化金属分子筛的脱氢反应。
