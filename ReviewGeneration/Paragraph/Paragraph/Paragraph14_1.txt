Most of the studies are focused on characterization and evaluation of catalytic performance over various supported metal catalysts, without delving into mechanistic proposals or pathways. 

A few exceptions that do discuss mechanisms include reference [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391], which proposes different pathways over Pt(111) and Pt(211) surfaces - a reverse Horiuti-Polanyi route on Pt(111) and an alternative non-reverse pathway on Pt(211) involving multiple dehydrogenation and hydrogenation steps. Reference [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] also puts forth a mechanism over Pt-Ge dual sites on zeolite UTL, with the first C-H cleavage as the rate limiting step. 

In summary, most literature lacks in-depth mechanistic analysis and future work should focus more strongly on gaining molecular-level insights into the catalytic cycles and active site requirements for supported metal catalysts in propane dehydrogenation. Computational studies combined with advanced spectroscopic analysis can help reveal mechanistic details and aid the rational design of improved supported metal catalysts for this important reaction.
