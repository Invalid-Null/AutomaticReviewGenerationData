Based on the details extracted from the references, mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over certain metal oxide catalysts, while several studies did not investigate reaction mechanisms. 

Over Cr-based catalysts, active sites have been identified as Cr3+ species [DOI1], mononuclear Cr(III) sites [DOI2], or in situ generated Cr(III) sites from Cr(VI) precursors [DOI3]. Reaction pathways involve redox cycles between Cr3+ ↔ Cr2+ or Cr6+ ↔ Cr3+ ↔ Cr2+ [DOI4]. Over Ga-based catalysts, heterolytic dissociation routes are described, with C3H8 dissociation as the rate-limiting step and Ga3+ sites facilitating hydride abstraction [DOI4, DOI5]. VOx catalysts are proposed to follow Mars-van Krevelen type mechanisms, using lattice oxygen for initial alkane activation and gas phase O2 for reoxidation [DOI6, DOI7]. Isolated V3+ sites catalyze subsequent non-oxidative dehydrogenation as lattice oxygen depletes [DOI8]. ZrO2 catalysts are described to have Zr cations neighboring oxygen vacancies as active sites that break C-H bonds homolytically or heterolytically depending on catalyst structure [DOI9, DOI10].

Several studies focused more on catalyst characterization and testing without proposing detailed mechanistic steps [DOI11-DOI13]. Others examined non-metal oxide catalysts like supported metals or zeolites [DOI14, DOI15]. Overoxidation to COx and coking have been noted as key side reactions and deactivation routes [DOI4, DOI16, DOI17].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The mechanistic scenarios described reveal valuable molecular-level insights into propane dehydrogenation over metal oxide catalysts. However, the reaction networks have not been fully elucidated, with questions remaining about specific intermediates, multifunctional sites, and deactivation pathways. As research continues to uncover these details through advanced in situ/operando techniques and multi-scale modeling, opportunities exist to design optimized catalysts compositionally and structurally. Knowledge gained could guide exploration of novel supports, tailored active site environments, and stabilized oxygen mobility. By coupling emerging mechanistic comprehension with high-throughput and machine learning methods, next-generation metal oxide catalysts may reach improved selectivities, lifetimes and energetics. Broader implementation of fundamental principles could facilitate transferring laboratory advancements to practical industrial processes.

在Cr基催化剂上,活性位点被确定为Cr3+物种 [DOI1]、单核Cr(III)位点 [DOI2]或原位生成的从Cr(VI)前体生成的Cr(III)位点 [DOI3]。 反应途径涉及Cr3+↔ Cr2+或Cr6+↔ Cr3+↔ Cr2+之间的氧化还原循环[DOI4]。 在Ga基催化剂上,已经描述了杂解消除途径,C3H8裂解是速度限制步骤,Ga3+位点促进氢化物的提取得到 [DOI4,DOI5]。 已经提出VOx催化剂遵循Mars-van Krevelen类型机制,使用晶格氧进行初始烷烃活化和气相O2氧化再生 [DOI6,DOI7]。 当晶格氧耗尽时,隔离的V3+位点催化后续的非氧化脱氢 [DOI8]。 文献报道,与氧空位相邻的Zr阳离子作为活性位点,根据催化剂的结构以同裂或杂裂方式断裂C-H键 [DOI9,DOI10]。



一些研究更多地关注催化剂的表征和测试,而没有提出详细的机制步骤 [DOI11-DOI13]。 其他一些研究检查了非金属氧化物催化剂,如支持的金属或分子筛 [DOI14,DOI15]。 报道过量氧化生成COx和焦炭沉积是关键的副反应和失活途径 [DOI4,DOI16,DOI17]。


※※※

综合理解与前景展望:所描述的机制方案揭示了有关金属氧化物催化剂上丙烷脱氢的具有价值的分子水平的见解。 但是,反应网络尚未被完全阐明,仍有关于特定中间体、多功能位点和失活途径的疑问。 随着研究继续利用先进的原位/原位技术和多尺度建模来揭示这些细节, 存在机会来设计优化的催化剂成分和结构。 获得的知识可以指导对新型载体、定制活性位点环境和稳定氧迁移性的探索。 通过将新出现的机制理解与高通量和机器学习方法相结合,新一代金属氧化物催化剂可能会达到改进的选择性、使用寿命和能量效率。 更广泛地应用基本原理可以促进将实验室的进步转移到实际的工业过程中。
