 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an industrially important reaction that produces propylene, a key building block for polymers and chemicals. A major challenge is developing catalysts that exhibit both high activity and prolonged lifetime stability against deactivation pathways like sintering and coking. Recent strategies have explored encapsulating metal nanoparticles and clusters within zeolite frameworks to enhance stability.

The study by Wu et al. [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726] demonstrates encapsulating isolated cobalt species within a siliceous BEA framework (Co@Si-BEA) leads to a PDH catalyst with excellent catalytic performance and stability. After eight reaction-regeneration cycles, conversion was maintained, showcasing remarkable regeneration capability and avoidance of irreversible deactivation. In situ XAFS verified the preserved structure was due to stabilized isolated Co0 sites resistant against sintering or aggregation into larger CoOx clusters during cycling. 

Additionally, PtZn nanoclusters have been synthesized within the micropores of MFI zeolites using direct hydrothermal methods [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. Encapsulating the PtZn clusters prevented sintering, leading to exceptional stability with only ~3% conversion loss after 220 hours on stream during PDH. This significant enhancement versus conventional impregnated PtZn/MFI highlights the importance of regioselective single-site encapsulation over multi-site surface deposition.

Looking forward, further tailoring organic structure-directing agents and zeolite composites to meticulously coordinate anchoring pocket geometries with custom active site dimensions constitutes an intriguing approach for next-generation catalysts [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. Prudently designing encapsulation architectures to balance confinement stabilization and mass transfer limitations is imperative.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The assortment of techniques like ultrafast crystallization and smart organic ligands provide exciting headways to induce defect-rich zeolites and enable isolated, stabilized metal clusters. Several advancements discussed hitherto have already actualized remarkable stability spanning thousands of hours. Nonetheless, industrial demands stipulate catalyst cycles exceeding years. Constructing mesoporosity in microporous matrices could alleviate diffusion constraints. More in-depth comprehension into metal-support synergies driving exceptional stabilization against varying reaction ambiences is essential. Promisingly, emerging operando spectroscopies and microscopic methods might unveil clustering dynamics. Explorations at this intersection of materials and interfacial chemistry likely constitute the research epicenter for transformative propane dehydrogenation systems in the imminent future.

<Chinese>

丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一项工业上非常重要的反应,它生产丙烯,丙烯是聚合物和化学品的关键基础原料。主要挑战是开发同时表现出高活性和延长使用寿命稳定性的催化剂,以抵抗烧结和结焦等失活途径。 最近的策略探索了在分子筛框架内封装金属纳米粒子和簇,以提高稳定性。

 Wu等人的研究 [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726] 证明,在硅质BEA框架内封装隔离的钴种(Co@Si-BEA)导致PDH催化剂具有出色的催化性能和稳定性。 在八次反应-再生循环后,转换率得以维持,展示了显著的再生能力和避免不可逆失活。 原位XAFS验证了保留结构的稳定性是由于稳定的隔离Co0位点抵抗烧结或聚集成较大的CoOx簇在循环期间。

 此外,通过直接水热方法在MFI分子筛的微孔内合成了PtZn纳米簇[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。 封装PtZn簇可防止烧结,在PDH过程中导致异常稳定,220小时内流量仅损失约3%。 与常规浸渍PtZn/MFI相比,这种显著增强突出了单点封装的重要性,而不是多点表面沉积。

展望未来,进一步调整有机结构导向剂和分子筛复合材料,以精确配合锚固袋几何与定制活性位的尺寸,是下一代催化剂的有趣方法[[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]]。 谨慎设计封装体系以平衡限制稳定性和传质限制至关重要。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 从超快速结晶到智能有机配体等技术的应用为诱导缺陷丰富的分子筛并实现稳定的隔离金属簇提供了激动人心的进展。 到目前为止,所讨论的几项进步已经实现了惊人的数千小时的稳定性。 尽管如此,工业需求规定的催化剂使用周期要超过数年。 在微孔矩阵中构建介孔可能会减轻扩散限制。 对驱动异常稳定性的金属-支撑协同作用的更深入理解对各种反应环境至关重要。令人鼓舞地,新兴的原位光谱法和显微技术可能会揭示聚集动力学。这一材料和界面化学交汇领域的探索可能构成推动变革性丙烷脱氢体系的近期研究重心。
