 <English>
Based on the detailed analysis from the literature, tuning the oxidation states, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties of metal oxides has been shown to significantly affect the performance of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. 

For chromium oxide catalysts, the oxidation state of chromium is critical, with Cr(VI) species being the most active [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183, DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]. The dispersion and evolution of partially reduced Cr(III) sites also play a key role in determining activity and stability [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. Reoxidation helps regenerate the active Cr(VI) sites.

For vanadium oxides, the creation of oxygen vacancies and isolated monomeric VOx sites leads to very high propane activation capability [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.8b04701]. Partially reduced V(IV)/V(III) species show better selectivity than fully oxidized V(V), but can cause coking [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.9b09235]. Modifying supports or doping with metals like Mo helps tune oxygen mobility and prevent overoxidation. 

The abundance of coordinatively unsaturated metal sites and oxygen vacancies also boosts the activity of other metal oxides like TiOx [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c00818] and ZrOx [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201508731]. Optimizing acid-base properties is also essential, with catalysts like FeNi/CeO2 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999] and Ga2O3 [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001] showing the highest selectivity when medium-strong acid sites dominate. Additionally, strong metal-support interactions help stabilize active phases and prevent sintering.

For supported noble metals like Pt, controlling particle size, shape, oxidation state and interactions with the support is critical. Smaller dispersion, optimized metal-metal distances, and electron donation from the support boost selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639, DOI: 10.1016/S0920-5861(96)00063-0].

※※※  
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The reviewed studies have systematically dissected how intrinsic attributes of metal oxides and supported metals govern catalytic performance for PDH. However, most works have focused on conventional oxide catalysts like Cr, V, Ga and some noble metals. An immense opportunity exists to explore more exotic multifunctional catalysts with tailored active sites and tunable oxidation states using advanced synthetic techniques. Operando spectroscopy can help track oxidation state changes and build quantitative structure-activity relationships. Machine learning models based on such data may accelerate screening and discovery of optimal catalyst compositions and structures. The role of promoters and poisons in modulating oxygen mobility and acid-base properties merits deeper study. Elucidating degradation pathways and applying this knowledge to design sinter-resistant single atom catalysts could enable stable, selective and energy-efficient PDH processes.

<Chinese>
根据文献的详细分析,调控金属氧化物的氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性质能显著影响丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂的性能。

对于铬氧化物催化剂,铬的氧化态至关重要,其中Cr(VI)物种最为活泼 [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183, DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。部分还原的Cr(III)位点的分散和演变也在确定活性和稳定性方面起关键作用 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]。氧化再生有助于再生活性Cr(VI)位点。

 对于钒氧化物,氧空位和隔离单体VOx位点的产生导致极高的丙烷活化能力 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.8b04701]。部分还原的V(IV)/ V(III)物种比完全氧化的V(V)显示出更好的选择性,但可能导致结焦 [DOI: 10.1021/jacs.9b09235]。 修饰载体或掺杂金属如Mo有助于调节氧迁移能力并防止过度氧化。

无定形金属位点和氧空位的丰富也可以提高TiOx [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c00818]和ZrOx [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201508731]等其他金属氧化物的活性。 优化酸碱性质也是必不可少的,FeNi/CeO2 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]和Ga2O3 [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]等催化剂在中等强酸位占主导地位时显示出最高的选择性。 此外,强大的金属支持物间相互作用有助于稳定活性相,防止烧结。

 对于受支持的贵金属如Pt,控制颗粒大小、形状、氧化态和与支撑体的相互作用至关重要。 更小的分散度、优化的金属间距离和来自支撑体的电子供体增强选择性 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639, DOI: 10.1016/S0920-5861(96)00063-0]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望:综述研究系统地分析了金属氧化物和受支持金属的固有属性如何支配PDH催化性能。 然而,大多数工作都集中在常规氧化物催化剂如Cr、V、Ga和一些贵金属上。 利用先进的合成技术探索更多具有定制活性位点和可调氧化态的多功能催化剂仍有巨大的机会。 原位光谱可以帮助跟踪氧化态变化并建立定量的结构-活性关系。 基于这样的数据的机器学习模型可以加速筛选和发现最佳催化剂成分和结构。 探讨促进剂和抑制剂调控氧迁移能力和酸碱性质的作用值得更深入的研究。 阐明降解途径并应用这些知识设计抗烧结的单原子催化剂可以实现稳定、选择性和节能的 PDH 工艺。
