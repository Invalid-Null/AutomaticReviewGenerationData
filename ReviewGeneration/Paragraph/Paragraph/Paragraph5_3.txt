 <English>

Several studies have utilized density functional theory (DFT) calculations to elucidate propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and pathways over various catalysts. Gao et al. [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] demonstrated how DFT revealed key insights, including lower activation barriers on Zn-O-Zn sites compared to Zn2+ sites which supports experimental observations, and correlations between Pt's d-band center and propylene selectivity in PtM@Pt models. DFT analysis by Zuo et al. [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008] related microscopic dehydrogenation mechanisms to macroscopic catalytic behavior through microkinetic modeling, and identified descriptor variables based on scaling relations between intermediate adsorption energies. Specific computational methodologies are also described, such as the plane-wave DFT approach applying the BEEF-vdW functional implemented in VASP to screen dehydrogenation catalysts.

Further applications of DFT are delineated across additional reports. Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889] performed spin-polarized DFT with a plane-wave basis in VASP to study propane dehydrogenation on single-atom catalysts, revealing insights into stability and barriers. The work by Zhang et al. [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.018] is centered on DFT exploration of the reaction pathway over Zn-exchanged mordenite, with computations unveiling key energetic and entropic effects. Collectively, these DFT-driven explorations advance fundamental comprehension of propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and kinetics.

※※※

The insightful application of density functional theory has significantly enhanced comprehension of propane dehydrogenation pathways across diverse catalysts. Building on these advancements, emerging opportunities exist to integrate high-throughput DFT frameworks to swiftly screen novel substrates, potentially discovering enhanced catalytic motifs. Additionally, amalgamating experiments with multi-scale simulations can facilitate pragmatic analysis under industrially-relevant environments. Such synergistic pursuits can expand the boundaries of current knowledge, driving the field forward.

<Chinese>

几项研究已经应用了密度泛函理论(DFT)计算来阐明各种催化剂上的丙烷脱氢机理和途径。高等人[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]展示了DFT如何揭示关键见解,包括Zn-O-Zn位点上的激活能垒低于Zn2+位点,这支持了实验观察结果,并且在PtM@Pt模型中存在Pt的d带中心与丙烯选择性之间的相关性。左等人[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008] 的DFT分析通过微观动力学建模将微观脱氢机理与宏观催化行为联系起来,并基于中间体吸附能量之间的缩放关系确定描述子变量。还描述了特定的计算方法,例如应用在VASP 中实现的BEEF-vdW泛函的平面波DFT方法来筛选脱氢催化剂。

DFT的进一步应用在其他报告中也有详细阐述。王等人[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889] 在VASP中采用自旋极化的DFT和平面波基组对单原子催化剂上的丙烷脱氢反应进行研究,揭示了视角到稳定性和屏障。张等人[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.018] 的工作集中在Zn交换沥青麦上的DFT反应途径的探索,计算揭示了关键的能量和熵效应。总的来说,这些DFT驱动的探索推进了对丙烷脱氢机理和动力学的基本理解。

※※※

密度泛函理论的深入应用极大地增强了对各种催化剂上丙烷脱氢途径的理解。在这些进步的基础上,利用高通量DFT框架快速筛选新底物的新兴机会存在,可能会发现增强的催化剂构型。 此外,将实验与多尺度模拟相结合可以促进在工业相关环境下的实用分析。这种协同追求可以拓展当前知识的界限,推动该领域的发展。
