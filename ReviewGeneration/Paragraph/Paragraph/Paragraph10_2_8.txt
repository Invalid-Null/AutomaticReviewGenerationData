Based on the details extracted from the references, mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over some of the studied metal oxide catalysts, while several other works did not delve into mechanistic investigations. 

On chromium-based oxide catalysts, the active sites and mechanistic pathways appear to depend on the catalyst structure and preparation method. Isolated Cr3+ sites [DOI1], Cr3+ species with coordination vacancies [DOI2], and oligomeric chromia domains [DOI3] have been proposed as active sites. Reaction mechanisms range from Mars-van Krevelen type redox cycles [DOI4, DOI5] to heterolytic C-H bond dissociation [DOI6]. 

Investigations on vanadium oxide catalysts also point to different active sites and pathways depending on catalyst structure. These include isolated V5+ sites [DOI7], redox cycles between V5+/V3+ [DOI8], and synergies between VOx domains and support sites like Al-O-V [DOI9]. Lattice oxygen evolution influences the transition between oxidative and non-oxidative routes [DOI10].

On zirconia-based catalysts, coordinatively unsaturated Zr4+ sites neighboring oxygen vacancies emerge as common active centers [DOI11, DOI12]. They activate propane concertedly or in C-H bond dissociation steps with lattice oxygen species [DOI13]. Gallium oxide catalysts are also proposed to catalyze propane dehydrogenation predominantly through a heterolytic dissociation mechanism over Ga3+ sites [DOI14].

Several works emphasize the significant role of metal-support interfaces and synergies between metal and support sites, such as Zn-Zr [DOI15], Mn-Ce [DOI16], and Cr-Ce [DOI17] pairs. Overall, a diversity of active sites and mechanistic pathways have been implicated depending on catalyst composition and structure. 

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the references, mechanistic scenarios for propane dehydrogenation remain highly structure and composition dependent. Nonetheless, some unifying themes and future opportunities emerge:

1) Defects and coordinative unsaturation enable high activity, suggesting site isolation and tuning metal-oxygen bond strengths as targets.

2) Metal/oxide synergies Should be leveraged through strategic catalyst architectures. 

3) Operando methods to track lattice oxygen, site speciation, coking, and redox transitions can offer tremendous insights.

3) Selective bond activation modes present opportunities to boost intrinsic activity and precisely guide product selectiviy.

By integrating these themes and expanding our operando characterization toolkit, rational pathways for next-generation propane dehydrogenation catalysts come clearly into focus.

根据从参考文献中提取的细节,对一些研究的金属氧化物催化剂提出了丙烷脱氢的机理场景,而其他一些工作并没有深入地研究机理。  

在铬基氧化物催化剂上,活性位点和机理途径似乎取决于催化剂结构和制备方法。单体Cr3+ 址 [DOI1],具有配位空位的 Cr3+ 表 [DOI2]以及寡聚体铬域 [DOI3] 已被提出作为活性位点。 反应机制范围从Mars-van Krevelen类型的氧化还原周期 [DOI4,DOI5] 到杂解 C-H 键断裂[DOI6]。  

对钒氧化物催化剂的研究也指出,根据催化剂结构,不同的活性位点和途径。这些包括分离的 V5+ 址 [DOI7]、V5+/ V3+ 氧化还原循环 [DOI8]以及 氧化钒域与载体址之间的协同作用,如 Al-O-V [DOI9]。 晶格氧的演变影响氧化还原和非氧化路之间的转变[DOI10]。  

在基于ZrO2的催化剂上,配位不饱和的 Zr4+ 址邻近氧空位出现为常见的活性中心[DOI11,DOI12]。 它们与晶格氧物种协同或采用C-H 键解离步骤激活丙烷[DOI13]。 
氧化镓催化剂也被提出主要通过 Ga3+位上的一种杂解解离机理催化丙烷脱氢 [DOI14]。  

几项工作强调了金属支持体界面以及金属与载体高位之间的协同作用,例如 Zn-Zr [DOI15], Mn-Ce [DOI16] 和 Cr-Ce [DOI17] 对。 总的来说,活性位点和机理途径的多样性已被证明取决于催化剂组成和结构。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 考虑到参考文献中讨论的进步和发现,丙烷脱氢的机理场景仍然高度依赖结构和组成。尽管如此,一些统一的主题和未来的机会出现了:

1)缺陷和配位不饱和性使高活性成为可能,这表明隔离位点和调节金属氧键强度是目标。  

2) 应通过战略催化剂体系利用金属/氧化物协同效应。

3) 操作方法来跟踪晶格氧、位点化合物、结焦和氧化还原过渡可以提供巨大的洞察力。  

3) 选择性键断裂模式为提高固有活性和精确指导产物选择性提供了机会。  

通过整合这些主题并扩大我们的操作性表征工具包,明确了下一代丙烷脱氢催化剂的合理途径。
