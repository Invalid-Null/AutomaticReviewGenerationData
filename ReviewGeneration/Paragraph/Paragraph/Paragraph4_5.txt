	<English>
		Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an endothermic process (ΔH298K = 124.3 kJ/mol) that is thermodynamically limited, requiring high temperatures of 500-650°C to achieve reasonable equilibrium conversion [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]. The reaction is also equilibrium-limited, with conversion enhanced by lower propane partial pressures [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]. These constraints result from the challenge of breaking strong C-H bonds in propane. Alternative oxidative PDH routes using O2 or CO2 co-feeds can improve thermodynamic conversion by providing exothermic oxidation reactions [10.1002/advs.202207756].
		
		Kinetically, PDH proceeds through a complex network of pathways with varying activation barriers. Steps like α-hydrogen abstraction have slightly lower barriers around 0.71 eV [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889], while other elementary steps can be rate-limiting. Side reactions like cracking and coking also occur, especially at the high temperatures needed, exhibiting their own kinetic dependencies [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01374]. But the precise mechanistic details and rate-determining steps remain unclear [10.1021/acscatal.5b01423].
		
		Computational studies have provided additional insights, with DFT often used to map out potential energy surfaces and transition states for key steps like C-H activation and dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. Such calculations elucidate kinetic and even thermodynamic considerations by estimating critical energies. However, further work is needed to fully capture the complexity of industrial PDH catalysts and reaction networks.
		
		※※※
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings discussed, there are still opportunities to better understand PDH mechanisms, intermediates, and site requirements through advanced operando studies and multi-scale modeling. An improved comprehension of thermodynamic and kinetic constraints could inform the design of next-generation catalysts and processes to enhance productivity and selectivity. Looking ahead, control over critical elementary steps may overcome current limitations.

	<Chinese>
		丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一个内吸热过程(ΔH298K = 124.3 kJ/mol),在热力学上受到限制,需要500-650°C的高温来实现合理的平衡转化[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]。该反应也受平衡限制,低丙烷偏压可以提高转化[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。这些限制源于打破丙烷中的强C-H键的挑战。使用O2或CO2共送氧化剂的替代氧化PDH路线可以通过提供放热氧化反应来改善热力学转化[10.1002/advs.202207756]。

		在动力学上,PDH通过一系列活化能垒不同的复杂途径进行。像α-氢原子转移这样的步骤活化能垒略低于0.71 eV [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889],而其他基元步也可能是速率控制步骤。裂化和结焦等副反应也会发生,尤其是在所需的高温下,表现出它们自己的动力学依赖性[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01374]。但精确的机理细节和速率控制步骤仍不清楚[10.1021/acscatal.5b01423]。

		计算研究提供了额外的见解,DFT通常用于映射关键步骤(如C-H活化和脱氢)的潜在能量面和过渡态[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。这种计算揭示了关键能量的动力学甚至热力学考虑。然而,仍需进一步的工作,以全面捕捉工业PDH催化剂和反应网络的复杂性。

		※※※
		综合理解与未来展望: 考虑到所讨论的进展和发现,仍有机会通过先进的本征研究和多尺度建模更好地理解PDH机理、中间体和位点要求。对热力学和动力学约束的改善理解可以指导新一代催化剂和过程的设计,以提高生产力和选择性。展望未来,对关键基元步骤的控制可能会克服当前的局限性。
