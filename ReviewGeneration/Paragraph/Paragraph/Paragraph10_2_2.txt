Based on the details extracted from the references, mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over various metal oxide catalysts, with some key aspects summarized below:

On Cr-based oxides, isolated Cr3+ sites [DOI1], Cr3+/Cr2+ redox cycles [DOI2], and mononuclear Cr(III) species with coordinative vacancies [DOI3] have been proposed as active sites. Steps like CH bond activation by CrO4 tetrahedra that get reduced to CrO6, generation of active Cr3+ sites in situ, and differences in oligomeric Cr species activity between supports are described.  

VOx centers follow Mars-van Krevelen type pathways, using lattice oxygen for initial alkane activation and gas phase O2 for reoxidation [DOI4, DOI5]. Isolated V5+ sites, V3+ centers formed after VO reduction, and sub-monolayer vanadia species help avoid overoxidation. Loading-dependent activity trends relate to the chemical states and mobility of oxygen atoms.

On ZrO2, dual Zrcus sites formed at oxygen vacancies activate propane through homolytic CH cleavage. Neighboring lattice oxygen, Zrcus‐C3H7 intermediates, and issues with catalyst reoxidation are covered [DOI6]. Ga-doping is proposed to enable a more concerted path involving simultaneous dissociation of two CH bonds [DOI7].

Gallium and indium oxides are predicted to activate propane heterolytically, with cationic Ga+ and In+ sites exchanging hydride and redox steps being avoided. Specific pathways show oxidative addition on metal centers, β-H elimination, and key differences in transition state stabilization [DOI8].

Several common mechanistic considerations emerge, like the prevalence of Mars-van Krevelen cycles, challenges with overoxidation, and the frequent involvement of coordinatively unsaturated metal sites adjacent to oxygen vacancies. However, some aspects like the distinction between oxidative and non-oxidative routes mediated by lattice oxygen evolution require further elucidation.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Significant advances have been made in unraveling mechanistic scenarios and active site requirements for propane dehydrogenation over metal oxides. However, several promising areas remain less explored. For instance, few studies have systematically evaluated the impact of synergies between metal and oxide components to potentially engender alternative pathways. Heterostructured interfaces may offer new opportunities to understand and control selectivity. Operando spectroscopic techniques coupled with isotopic labeling can offer additional experimental validation. Computationally, high-throughput DFT screenings assessing a wider set of descriptors on reaction energetics may reveal generalized trends. Moreover, microkinetic models incorporating these mechanistic details can better predict performance limits and optimization strategies, guiding the design of next-generation catalysts.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,已经为各种金属氧化物催化剂上的丙烷脱氢提出了机理场景,其中一些关键方面总结如下:

在Cr基氧化物上,已提出隔离的Cr3+位点[DOI1]、Cr3+/ Cr2+氧化还原循环[DOI2]和配位空位的单核Cr(III)种类[DOI3]作为活性位点。 描述了诸如CrO4四面体的CH键激活(这些四面体被还原为CrO6)、原位生成活性Cr3+位点以及不同支撑体上的寡聚Cr种类之间的活性差异等步骤。

VOx中心遵循Mars-van Krevelen类型的途径,使用晶格氧进行初始烷基活化和气相O2进行再氧化[DOI4,DOI5]。 隔离的V5+位点、VO还原后形成的V3+中心和亚单层钒酸盐物种有助于避免过氧化。 加载依赖性活性趋势与氧原子的化学态和运动能力有关。

在ZrO2上,形成在氧空位上的双重Zrcus位点通过均裂CH键激活丙烷。 讨论了相邻的晶格氧、Zrcus‐C3H7中间体和催化剂再氧化的问题[DOI6]。 已经提出Ga掺杂能够实现更协同的机理,其中涉及两个CH键的同时分离[DOI7]。

根据预测,镓和铟氧化物会使丙烷发生杂解活化,镓和铟阳离子位点与氢化物进行交换,并避免还原步骤。 具体的途径显示了金属中心上的氧化加成、β-H消除以及关键的过渡态稳定差异[DOI8]。

出现了几个常见的机理考虑,例如Mars-van Krevelen循环的普遍性、过氧化的挑战以及经常涉及氧空位相邻的配位不饱和金属位点。 然而,一些方面如受晶格氧进化调节的氧化和非氧化途径的区别还需要进一步阐明。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 在阐明金属氧化物上丙烷脱氢的机理场景和活性位点要求方面已经取得了重大进展。 然而,一些有前景的领域仍然少有人开发。 例如,很少有研究系统地评估金属和氧化物组分之间的协同作用对潜在催生替代途径的影响。 异质结界面可能提供发现和控制选择性的新机会。 同位素标记的原位光谱技术可以提供额外的实验验证。 在计算方面,高通量DFT筛选评估更广泛的描述子对反应能量的影响,可能会发现一般化的趋势。 此外,结合这些机理细节的微观动力学模型可以更好地预测性能极限和优化策略,指导下一代催化剂的设计。
