 <English>

The article with DOI 10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008 presents microkinetic modeling efforts that have provided insights into propane dehydrogenation kinetics. Specifically, it used descriptors based on formation energies of key surface species to screen and rank intermetallic compound catalysts for predicted propane dehydrogenation activity and propylene selectivity [1]. Rate equations were also utilized in developing this microkinetic model [2].

Additional microkinetic modeling insights are provided in the article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00939. This work used DFT-calculated parameters to develop mean-field microkinetic models for understanding propane dehydrogenation over PtSn catalysts [1]. Details on the rate equations, surface coverages, turnover frequencies, selectivities, and identification of rate-controlling steps are provided [3]. 

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889 also presents microkinetic modeling of propane dehydrogenation over single-atom catalysts. Specifically, the CATKINAS software was used along with DFT data to evaluate the kinetics and practical viability of the catalysts under industrial conditions [2,3]. This provided insights into the activity trends and controlling factors.

Lastly, the work in 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916 discusses prior microkinetic modeling efforts that examined Pt alloy catalysts. Barriers for key elementary steps like propane dehydrogenation were calculated and used as descriptors to identify optimal materials balancing activity and selectivity [1,2].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The microkinetic modeling efforts summarized here have utilized advanced computational approaches to gain fundamental insights into propane dehydrogenation kinetics over different catalyst classes. Key descriptors derived from DFT calculations, rate equations mapped to elementary steps, and microkinetic modeling software have shed light on activity-determining factors, selectivity control, and practical potential. Building on these works, future opportunities exist to explore increasingly complex models, larger sets of materials, and integration with experimental validation. Broader issues around process intensification, catalyst stability, and technoeconomic analysis could also be assessed computationally. Overall there is substantial promise in applying these modeling strategies to guide innovation across the propane dehydrogenation landscape.

<Chinese>

DOI 10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008的文章提出了微动力学建模工作,这些工作提供了丙烷脱氢动力学方面的见解。 具体来说,它使用基于关键表面物种的形成能的描写剂来屏幕和排名金属间化合物催化剂,用于预测丙烷脱氢活性和丙烯选择性[1]。 反应速率方程也被用来开发这个微动力学模型[2]。

DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00939的文章中提供了额外的微动力学建模见解。 这项工作使用DFT计算的参数来开发PtSn催化剂上丙烷脱氢的平均场微动力学模型[1]。 提供了关于反应速率方程、表面覆盖率、周转频率、选择性和识别速率控制步骤的详细信息[3]。

DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889的文章也提出了单原子催化剂上丙烷脱氢的微动力学建模。 具体来说,CATKINAS软件与DFT数据一起被用来评估催化剂在工业条件下的动力学和实际可行性[2,3]。 这为活性趋势和控制因素提供了见解。

最后,10.1021/acscatal.1c01916中的工作讨论了先前对Pt合金催化剂的微动力学建模工作。 计算了关键基本步骤(如丙烷脱氢)的屏障,并将其用作描述符来识别在活性和选择性之间实现平衡的最佳材料[1,2]。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 这里总结的微动力学建模工作利用了先进的计算方法来深入理解不同催化剂类上的丙烷脱氢动力学。 来自DFT计算的关键描写符,映射到基本步骤的反应速率方程,以及微动力学建模软件已经阐明了决定活性的因素、选择性控制和实际潜力。 在这些工作的基础上,未来存在探索越来越复杂模型、更大规模材料集和与实验验证相结合的机会。 更广泛的问题,如过程强化、催化剂稳定性和技术经济分析,也可以通过计算进行评估。 总的来说,将这些建模策略应用于引导整个丙烷脱氢领域的创新具有巨大的前景。
