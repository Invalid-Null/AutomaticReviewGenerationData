Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-based catalysts have been widely studied for propane dehydrogenation, however monometallic Pt catalysts suffer from limitations in activity and selectivity. Alloying Pt with secondary metals has emerged as an effective strategy to tune the catalytic performance. Common promoters explored are post-transition metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.008]. 

Pt-Sn Alloys
Several studies have demonstrated that incorporating Sn as a promoter increases propylene selectivity and stability compared to Pt catalysts. This is attributed to the Sn altering the ensemble and electronic effects of Pt active sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]. The addition of Sn helps minimize side reactions like hydrogenolysis and coke formation [10.1016/S0920-5861(96)00096-8, 10.1002/anie.201507119]. Computational studies reveal Pt-Sn intermetallic bonding increases dehydrogenation selectivity but also raises the propane dissociation barrier, causing lower initial activity [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]. Experimental data on supported Pt-Sn catalysts confirms this trade-off between activity and stability/selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]. An optimal Sn/Pt ratio around 1-1.5 is reported before excess Sn causes blocked sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]. Highly selective and stable Pt-Sn catalysts have been achieved by confinement of subnanometric clusters in zeolites [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00548].

Pt-Ga Alloys  
Pt-Ga bimetallic catalysts also demonstrate improved catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation. The electronic and geometric changes induced by Ga are suggested to minimize side reactions like aromatization and coking [10.1002/anie.202107210]. Data shows supported Pt-Ga nanoparticles can achieve equilibrium conversion levels at mild temperatures like 450°C, with exceptional stability [10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731]. Zeolite encapsulated single-atom Pt-Ga catalysts displayed >95% propylene selectivity across a broad 520-620°C range [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. The uniform dispersion and synergy between Pt and Ga enables optimization of selectivity and resistance to deactivation.

Pt-Zn Alloys
Studies on Pt-Zn catalysts reveal isolated Pt stabilized by grafted SiOZn-OH sites in dealuminated zeolite beta attained extremely high propane conversion, selectivity and longevity [10.1021/jacs.1c10261]. The Zn helps minimize Pt ensemble size and anchors isolated Pt sites. Intermetallic PtZn nanoparticles in mesoporous MFI frameworks also achieve remarkable stability from the ordered alloy arrangement [10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]. The strong metal-support interactions prevent agglomeration. Appropriate Zn incorporation thus acts through electronic and geometric alterations of Pt to enhance catalytic performance. 

In summary, alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn has proven highly effective for optimizing propane dehydrogenation catalysis. While various mechanisms are implicated, the key phenomenon is the secondary metal tuning the ensemble and electronic properties of Pt to minimize side reactions and improve selectivity. Confinement in zeolites allow unprecedented control over geometry and interactions. Future work can further explore highly stable ordered intermetallic nanoalloys.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on:

1) Combining computational studies with in-situ characterization to offer fundamental structure-activity insights into Pt alloy catalysis, potentially discovering new bimetallic combinations.

2) Leveraging recent advances in single-atom and single-site catalyst stabilization within zeolite frameworks to engineer ultraselective Pt alloy active centers.

3) Designing ordered intermetallic Pt alloy nanocluster arrays to minimize ensemble size and maximize stability, pushing towards commercial viability. 

4) Incorporating promoters like Sn, Ga, Zn in tandem on Pt to induce synergistic electronic and geometric alterations through multi-metallic doping.
 
5) Expanding the portfolio of supports from conventional metal oxides to emerging materials like MXenes, metal-organic frameworks etc. with tunable features to further fine-tune Pt alloy interactions.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

Pt基催化剂已经被广泛研究用于丙烷脱氢反应,但是单金属Pt催化剂在活性和选择性方面存在局限性。合金化Pt与次要金属已经成为优化催化性能的有效策略。常见的添加剂有类似Sn、Ga和Zn等后过渡金属 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.008]。

Pt-Sn合金
几项研究表明,与Pt催化剂相比,掺入Sn作为助剂可增加丙烯的选择性和稳定性。这是Sn改变了Pt活性位点的配位和电子效应的结果[10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]。 Sn的添加有助于最小化如氢化裂解和焦炭生成等副反应[10.1016/S0920-5861(96)00096-8, 10.1002/anie.201507119]。计算研究揭示Pt-Sn间金属键的形成增加了脱氢选择性,但也提高了丙烷解离屏障,导致初始活性降低[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。载体支持Pt-Sn催化剂的实验数据证实了这种活性和稳定性/选择性之间的权衡关系 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]。文献报道Sn/Pt比率为1-1.5时为最佳,过量Sn会堵塞活性位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。通过在分子筛中的钳位可实现高选择性和稳定的Pt-Sn催化剂[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]。

Pt-Ga合金
Pt-Ga双金属催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中也显示出更好的催化性能。 Ga引起的电子和构型变化有助于最小化芳构化和焦炭生成等副反应[10.1002/anie.202107210]。实验数据显示,负载的Pt-Ga纳米颗粒催化剂可以在温和条件如450°C下达到平衡转换,同时具有卓越的稳定性[10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731]。分子筛钳位的单原子Pt-Ga催化剂在520-620°C范围内展现了>95%的丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。 Pt和Ga之间的均匀分散和协同效应实现了选择性和抗脱活性的优化。

Pt-Zn合金  
Pt-Zn催化剂的研究表明,硅锌羟基位点接枝于脱铝β沸石的隔离Pt活性中心实现了极高的丙烷转换率,选择性和稳定性[10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。Zn有助于最小化Pt的配位体大小和锚定隔离的Pt位点。 MFI骨架的间金属PtZn纳米粒子也由于有序合金排列实现了卓越的稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]。 强大的金属-载体相互作用防止了聚集。Zn的适当掺入因此通过Pt的电子和构型改变来增强催化性能。

总的来说,与Sn、Ga 和Zn等助剂形成Pt合金对于优化丙烷脱氢催化显得非常有效。尽管涉及多种机制,关键的现象是次要金属调控Pt的配位和电子性质,以减少副反应和改善选择性。分子筛中的钳位催化剂允许前所未有的几何和相互作用的控制。未来工作可以深入探索高稳定的有序间金属纳米合金。
