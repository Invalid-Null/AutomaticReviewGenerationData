	<English>
		Industrial propane dehydrogenation (PDH) processes utilize supported Pt and CrOx catalysts to activate C-H bonds in propane and facilitate dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]. These catalysts provide alternative reaction pathways with lower activation barriers compared to the highly endothermic thermal PDH process [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. The key mechanism enabling catalyst activity is the presence of coordinatively unsaturated metal sites that can activate propane's strong C-H bonds (409 kJ/mol) [10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63196-1]. For example, isolated Pt atoms preferentially break C-H over C-C bonds [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019], while Sn atoms incorporated into zeolites introduce Lewis acid sites for C-H activation [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]. Balancing sufficient reactivity to break C-H bonds while avoiding deep dehydrogenation and coking remains an ongoing challenge elucidated through density functional theory modeling [10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63196-1, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916].
		※※※
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The reviewed studies establish that introducing specific coordinatively unsaturated metal sites enables catalysts to facilitate propane dehydrogenation through C-H activation mechanisms with lower kinetic barriers. While supported Pt and CrOx catalysts drive existing processes, continued research into novel compositions and zeolite structures promises enhanced activity, selectivity and stability. Future directions may increasingly integrate high-throughput DFT screening with rapid synthesis and testing of designed catalysts. Machine learning models trained on accumulated literature data could also accelerate identification of active site descriptors and optimize catalyst regeneration strategies.
