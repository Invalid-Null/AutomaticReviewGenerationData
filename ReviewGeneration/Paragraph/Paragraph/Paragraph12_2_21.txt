Based on the details extracted from the file, several studies have explored supported mono-metallic platinum (Pt), palladium (Pd), and iridium (Ir) catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. 

Specifically, Pt/alumina catalysts have been widely investigated, with one study showing the addition of sulfur during preparation or in the feed improved selectivity [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]. Other works demonstrated atomic layer deposition (ALD) metal oxide overcoats on Pt/alumina extrudates increased yield and selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391], while 1 wt% boron promotion reduced coking 3-fold and boosted selectivity to 98% [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]. At the atomic scale, isolating single Pt sites on silica [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-020-16693-9] or incorporating sub-nanometric Pt clusters into zeolite pores via Pt-O bonds [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] yielded extremely stable and selective catalysts. Beyond Pt, a RhCu single atom alloy on silica exhibited much higher activity and 100% propene selectivity compared to Pt/alumina [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389].

Some studies also compared mono-metallic Pt to bimetallic PtSn catalysts. While PtSn generally showed improved performance attributed to geometric and electronic effects [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639], decreasing cluster size and increasing Sn content was found to lower Pt reactivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]. Still, Pt/alumina itself displayed relatively poor 15% conversion versus PtSn analogs [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg7894]. 

Additionally, the file notes Pt-based catalysts are the most successful commercially for propane dehydrogenation, employed in UOP's Oleflex process [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]. Studies also analyzed coking behavior on supported Pt catalysts using temperature-programmed oxidation [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

Overall, the literature provides substantial insights into supported mono- and bi-metallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, with extensive focus on rationally designing stable, selective Pt catalysts. Continued research to optimize these systems and elucidate degradation mechanisms will further advance commercial applications.

根据文件提取的详细信息,已经有几项研究探索了负载的单金属铂(Pt)、钯(Pd)和铱(Ir)催化剂用于丙烷脱氢。

具体来说,Pt/氧化铝催化剂已经被广泛研究,一项研究表明,在制备或反应饲料中添加硫可提高选择性 [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]。其他工作证明,原子层沉积(ALD)金属氧化物包覆Pt/氧化铝挤出催化剂可提高收率和选择性 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391],而1 wt%硼促进剂可将焦炭形成量降低3倍,并将选择性提高到98% [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]。在原子水平上,通过Pt-O键将单一Pt活性位隔离在二氧化硅上 [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-020-16693- 9]或将亚纳米Pt簇螯合进分子筛孔内 [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7],产生了极其稳定和选择性高的催化剂。 此外,相比Pt/氧化铝,负载在二氧化硅上的RhCu单原子合金表现出更高的活性和100%丙烯选择性 [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]。

一些研究还将单金属Pt与双金属PtSn催化剂进行了比较。虽然PtSn通常由于其几何和电子效应而表现出更好的性能 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639],但研究发现,Pt簇大小的减小和Sn含量的增加会降低Pt的反应活性[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。尽管如此,Pt/氧化铝本身相比PtSn类似物显示出相对较差的15%转化率 [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg7894]。

此外,该文件指出,基于Pt的催化剂是丙烷脱氢反应商业上最成功的,在UOP公司的Oleflex工艺中被采用 [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]。 一些研究还使用温度程序氧化分析了负载Pt催化剂上的焦化行为[DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

总的来说,文献提供了丰富的负载单金属和双金属催化剂用于丙烷脱氢反应的信息,并对设计稳定选择性高的Pt催化剂进行了广泛深入的关注。继续研究以优化这些系统和阐明降解机制将进一步推动商业应用。
