Based on the details extracted from the references, the articles do not propose specific mechanistic pathways for propane dehydrogenation over supported metal catalysts in most cases. 

A few exceptions describe possible reaction mechanisms over specific supported metal catalysts like Pt, PtSn and VOx/ZrO2. One study proposes a reverse Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism on Pt(111) surfaces [DOI1], while an alternative non-reverse pathway is suggested on Pt(211) involving multiple dehydrogenation and hydrogenation steps [DOI1]. Other works calculate reaction energetics and propose pathways involving dehydrogenation, cracking and hydrogenation intermediates over supported Pt, PtSn and Pt-Ge nanoclusters [DOI2, DOI3]. A Mars-van Krevelen redox mechanism is also described for VOx/ZrO2 catalysts [DOI4].

Overall however, most references do not delve into mechanistic details over supported metals. The predominant focus is on evaluating catalytic performance and effects of varying composition, structure and supports. Detailed microkinetic studies establishing clear reaction networks, intermediates and active sites appear lacking, representing an area for future research. Advances in operando spectroscopy and simulations can shed light on transient intermediates and rate-determining steps guiding catalyst optimization.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the limited mechanistic understanding of propane dehydrogenation over heterogeneous supported metal catalysts, opportunities exist to apply state-of-the-art operando tools and multi-scale modeling to unravel mechanistic intricacies. By elucidating structure-activity relationships linking catalyst structure, adsorption modes, active sites and selectivity patterns, rational optimization strategies can be devised to tune catalytic properties. Further research can focus on designing optimal metal-support interfaces to curb deactivation pathways. Uncovering mechanisms can spur innovation of multi-functional single atom alloy catalysts with programmable selectivity.

根据参考文献中提取的详细信息,在大多数情况下,这些文章没有提出在受支持的金属催化剂上的丙烷脱氢的具体机理途径。

少数几个例外描述了可能的反应机理,例如在Pt(111) [DOI1]表面上的反向Horiuti-Polanyi机理,而在Pt(211)上的替代非反向途径则涉及多个脱氢和加氢步骤[DOI1]。其他研究计算了反应能,并在受支持的Pt、PtSn和Pt-Ge纳米簇上提出了可能的途径,其中包括脱氢、裂化和加氢中间体[DOI2,DOI3]。Mars-van Krevelen氧化还原机理也用于VOx/ZrO2催化剂[DOI4]。

然而,总的来说,大多数参考文献没有深入研究受支持金属上的机理细节。主要关注评估催化性能以及组成、结构和支持的影响。建立清晰的反应网络、中间体和活性位点的详细微动力学研究似乎仍然缺乏,这是一个未来研究的领域。先进的原位光谱和模拟可以揭示瞬态中间体和速率决定步骤,以指导催化剂的优化。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望:鉴于对异相受支持金属催化剂上丙烷脱氢反应机理理解的局限性,运用前沿的原位分析工具和多尺度建模来阐明机理复杂性的机会正在出现。通过阐明催化剂结构、吸附模式、活性位点和选择性模式之间的结构-活性关系,可以制定出合理的优化策略,以调节催化性能。进一步的研究可以关注设计优化的金属-支持体界面,以遏制失活途径。揭示反应机理可以推动创新多功能单原子合金催化剂,实现可编程的选择性。
