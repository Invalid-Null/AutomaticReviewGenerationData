Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Several studies have investigated the effect of alloying platinum with secondary metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), and zinc (Zn) for improving the performance of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. 

Pt-Sn alloy catalysts have been widely explored for PDH, with the commercial Oleflex process utilizing a Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalyst [DOI: 10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Adding Sn helps increase propene selectivity and stability compared to monometallic Pt by altering the electronic properties of Pt, decreasing the number of multiple binding sites, and removing coke precursors [DOI: 10.1007/s10562-019-02788-z, DOI: 10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.015]. A Pt:Sn ratio around 2:1 to 3:1 balances activity and selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]. Higher Sn content generally decreases activity but increases stability [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X, DOI: 10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.015]. 

For Ga and Zn promoters, alloying with Pt helps maintain small, highly dispersed Pt clusters that are stable against sintering [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, DOI: 10.1021/jacs.1c10261]. Pt-Ga intermetallic nanoparticles can achieve equilibrium propane conversion with >90% propylene selectivity [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]. Isolated PtZn4-6 sites stabilized by SiOZn-OH groups show remarkable stability for months without deactivation [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631].

In summary, strategic alloying of Pt with secondary metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn has been widely utilized to enhance catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation in different aspects:

- Sn increases selectivity and stability but can decrease initial activity at high loadings
- Ga and Zn maintain highly dispersed Pt clusters for improved activity and resistance to sintering
- Balanced ratios around Pt:M = 2:1 to 3:1 (M = Sn, Zn, Ga, etc.) optimize stability and activity  
- Electronic and geometric effects alter binding energies and ensemble sizes for enhanced performance

Ongoing research aims to elucidate detailed structure-performance relationships to further advance Pt alloy catalysts for propane dehydrogenation.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

几项研究调查了与锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn)等第二金属合金化的铂在改进丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂性能方面的效果。

Pt-Sn合金催化剂已被广泛研究用于PDH,商业Oleflex工艺使用Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂[DOI: 10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。添加Sn有助于通过改变Pt的电子性质、减少多重结合位点的数量和去除焦炭前体来增加丙烯的选择性和稳定性,与单金属Pt相比[DOI: 10.1007/s10562-019-02788-z, DOI: 10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.015]。Pt:Sn比率约为2:1至3:1,在活性和选择性之间达到平衡[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。较高的Sn含量通常会降低活性,但会增加稳定性[DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X, DOI: 10.1016/j.apcata.2010.11.015]。

对于Ga和Zn助剂,与Pt合金有助于维持小的,高度分散的Pt簇,使其免受烧结的影响[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, DOI: 10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。 Pt-Ga中间合金纳米粒子可以达到平衡丙烷转化率,丙烯选择性>90%[DOI: 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]。被SiOZn-OH基稳定的孤立PtZn4-6位点在数月内没有失活,显示出显着的稳定性[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]。

总之,战略性地与Sn、Ga和Zn等第二金属合金化Pt已被广泛利用以提高丙烷脱氢的催化性能:

- Sn增加选择性和稳定性,但在高负载下可能降低初始活性
- Ga和Zn维持高度分散的Pt簇,改善活性和抗烧结性
- 约Pt:M = 2:1至3:1(M = Sn、Zn、Ga等)的平衡比率优化了稳定性和活性
- 电子和几何效应改变了结合能和配体集效应,从而提高了性能

当前的研究旨在阐明详细的结构-性能关系,以进一步推进Pt合金催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的应用。

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The studies reviewed provide significant insights into how strategic alloying of Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn can optimize propane dehydrogenation performance through electronic, geometric and ensemble effects. However, atomic-level understanding of the active sites and deactivation mechanisms remains rather limited. Advanced characterization techniques like in-situ spectroscopy combined with computational modeling may further elucidate the catalyst structure under working conditions. Exploring unconventional supports and synthetic techniques can also help stabilize isolated single sites and tune selectivity. More emphasis on evaluating catalyst performance in industrially relevant conditions over longer periods would accelerate commercial adoption of emerging catalyst formulations. Overall there exist rich opportunities to design high-performance and robust Pt alloy catalysts by optimizing composition-structure-function relationships.
