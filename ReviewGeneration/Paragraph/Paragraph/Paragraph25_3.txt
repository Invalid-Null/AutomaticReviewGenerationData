Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724] reveals insights into the nature of active gallium species in propane dehydrogenation catalysts using quantitative in situ FTIR spectroscopy and pulse titration reactions. The data shows that isolated extraframework Ga+ sites displace Bronsted acid sites upon H2 reduction. Additional insights stemming from references [10.1021/acscatal.3c00818] and [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3] demonstrate the utility of operando DRIFTS, Raman, and XPS in probing the electronic structure and coordination environments of surface active sites like titanium and zinc oxides.
