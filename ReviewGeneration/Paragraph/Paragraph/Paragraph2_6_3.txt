Based on the details extracted from the references, catalysts facilitate propane dehydrogenation by providing alternative reaction pathways and active sites that lower the activation energy barriers [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]. Industrial PDH processes utilize supported metal catalysts like Pt/Al2O3 and CrOx/Al2O3, where the metal sites activate C-H bonds in propane with lower barriers than the uncatalyzed thermal conversion [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]. More recently, alternative metal oxide catalysts have been explored, including ZrO2, VOx, Ga2O3 and zeolites, which provide Lewis acid sites or oxygen vacancies that facilitate dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454, 10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63196-1]. 

DFT calculations and kinetic models have elucidated specific mechanisms like the Horiuti-Polanyi mechanism, where propane undergoes dissociative adsorption and stepwise dehydrogenation on catalyst active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. The high C-H bond activation ability coupled with relatively low C-C bond cleavage minimizes secondary cracking reactions [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]. Balancing these factors by tuning catalyst composition and structure has been a major focus [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]. Overall, catalyst development has aimed to improve selectivity towards propylene while maintaining activity and stability [10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63196-1].
