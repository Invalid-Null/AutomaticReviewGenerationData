Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an industrially important and actively researched reaction for production of propylene. Platinum-based catalysts exhibit high initial activity but often suffer from rapid deactivation through coke deposition, sintering, and over-reduction [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. Alloying platinum with post-transition metals has emerged as an effective strategy to overcome these limitations and achieve optimal catalytic performance. 

Pt-Sn alloys have been widely explored for PDH, with multiple studies demonstrating significantly enhanced selectivity, stability and resistance to coke/deactivation compared to monometallic platinum [Placeholder_Of_DOI2], [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. DFT calculations reveal Sn incorporation lowers propane dehydrogenation barriers on Pt while raising propane hydrogenolysis barriers, directing selectivity toward propylene [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. Experiments confirm initial activity decreases but stability improves with Sn content up to an optimal Sn/Pt ratio around 0.5-1, attributed to an ensemble effect diluting the number of contiguous Pt sites [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. Above this ratio, excess Sn is found to block sites and cause drastic activity loss. Structural characterization by XRD, XPS, EXAFS verifies Pt-Sn alloy formation with varying composition from Pt3Sn to PtSn achieves the optimal balance [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].  

Analogous performance improvements are attained by alloying Pt with Ga or Zn modifiers. Pt-Ga intermetallics demonstrate remarkable thermal stability and selectivity over 92%, attributed to Ga isolating unsaturated Pt coordination sites [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. Isolated, single-site PtZn4 structures prepared by stabilizing Pt atoms in zeolite frameworks achieve >95% propylene selectivity across 520-620°C with negligible deactivation [Placeholder_Of_DOI8]. This precise site isolation prevents over-reduction and sintering issues plaguing conventional Pt catalysts.

In summary, strategic incorporation of secondary metals like Sn, Ga, Zn in proper coordination with Pt has proven immensely successful in augmenting stability and steering selectivity in propane dehydrogenation systems. Future research might focus on extending this concept to other promising bimetallic combinations and site-isolated catalyst architectures to push performance boundaries.

双金属和合金催化剂——Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一种具有重要工业意义和活跃研究的反应,用于生产丙烯。铂基催化剂表现出高初始活性,但通常会因焦炭沉积、烧结和过度还原而快速失活[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]。利用铂与后过渡金属形成合金已成为克服这些限制、实现最佳催化性能的有效策略。 

Pt-Sn合金已经被广泛研究用于PDH,多项研究表明与单金属铂相比,Pt-Sn显著提高了选择性、稳定性和抗焦化性[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。DFT计算显示Sn掺入可以降低Pt上的丙烷脱氢能垒,同时提高丙烷氢解能垒,从而提高向丙烯的选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。实验确认Sn含量增加可以降低初始活性,但提高Sn/Pt比例至0.5-1时稳定性得到改善,这被归因于组分效应稀释连续Pt活性位的数量[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。高于此�例时,过量Sn被发现会堵塞活性位,导致活性大幅损失。XRD、XPS、EXAFS等结构表征验证了Pt-Sn合金的形成,Pt3Sn至PtSn不同组成比可以达到最佳平衡[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。

与Ga或Zn形成合金显示出类似的性能改善。 Pt-Ga互金属组合表现出显着的热稳定性和超过92%的选择性,这被归因于Ga隔离不饱和Pt配位位[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。将Pt原子稳定在分子筛框架中形成的[PtZn4]单一活性位结构,在520-620°C实现了>95%的丙烯选择性和可忽略的失活率[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。这种精确的位点隔离防止了常规Pt催化剂中过度还原和烧结的问题。

总之,有策略地引入Sn、Ga、Zn等次金属与Pt形成适当配位可以极大地增强稳定性和控制丙烷脱氢体系的选择性。未来研究可能会关注将这一概念扩展到其他有前途的双金属组合和位隔离催化剂体系,以突破性能极限。
