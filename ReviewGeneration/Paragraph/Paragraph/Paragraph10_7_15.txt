Based on the detailed analysis from the references, the proposed mechanistic scenarios for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts can be summarized as follows:

On Cr-based oxide catalysts, active Cr3+ sites, especially isolated sites or sites with coordinative vacancies, are responsible for activating the C-H bond in propane [DOI1, DOI2]. Neighboring lattice oxygen assists in hydrogen abstraction, and redox cycles between Cr6+/Cr3+/Cr2+ also facilitate dehydrogenation. Over ZrO2, oxygen vacancies create coordinatively unsaturated Zr cations (Zrcus) which pair up and catalyze dehydrogenation through both C-H bonds [DOI3, DOI4]. Introduction of Ga changes the mechanism from stepwise to concerted [DOI5]. 

For VOx catalysts, Mars-van Krevelen type pathways are widely reported, where lattice oxygen initiates dehydrogenation to propene, leaving behind oxygen vacancies that get replenished by gas phase O2 [DOI6, DOI7]. Isolated V5+ sites reduce to V3+ under propane flow before reoxidizing. Sub-monolayer VOx structures help minimize overoxidation. MoVO mixed oxides also show oxidative then non-oxidative routes based on lattice oxygen availability [DOI8].

On Ga/zeolite catalysts, Ga+ sites activate propane via oxidative addition, hydride elimination and reductive elimination steps to release propene [DOI9]. Ga2O3 polymorphs likely follow heterolytic dissociation paths, with propane adsorption, C-H activation and further decomposition [DOI10]. TiO2-based catalysts show promise with tetrahedral Ti4+ sites interacting with oxygen vacancies to dehydrogenate propane [DOI11].

In summary, isolated cationic sites like Cr3+, V5+, Ga+, Ti4+ interacting with lattice oxygens facilitate propane dehydrogenation through various mechanistic steps tuned by the oxide properties and structures.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reveal crucial mechanistic insights on how metal oxide catalysts can be optimized for selective propane dehydrogenation, avoiding undesired combustion. Isolated cationic sites interacting with labile lattice oxygen promote desired dehydrogenation pathways. Future research could explore coupling metal oxides with other catalysts like zeolites to leverage acid-base functionalities, expanding beyond traditional CrOx. Operando spectroscopy can elucidate working state structures under reaction environments. Reactor and process intensification also offers promise through chemical looping concepts.

根据参考文献的详细分析,在不同金属氧化物催化剂上丙烷脱氢的拟议机理情景可以总结如下:

在以Cr为基础的氧化物催化剂上,活性Cr3+位点,特别是隔离的位点或配位不饱和的位点,负责激活丙烷中的C-H键[DOI1,DOI2]。相邻的晶格氧协助氢的提取,而Cr6+/ Cr3+/ Cr2+之间的氧化还原循环也促进了脱氢。在ZrO2上,氧缺陷产生了配位不饱和的Zr阳离子(Zrcus),这些阳离子成对出现,通过两个C-H键催化脱氢[DOI3,DOI4]。引入Ga使机理从步进变为协同[DOI5]。

对于VOx催化剂,广泛报道了Mars-van Krevelen类型的途径,其中晶格氧启动脱氢生成丙烯,留下氧空位,这些空位通过气相O2补充[DOI6,DOI7]。 隔离的V5+位点在丙烷流下还原为V3+,然后再氧化。 亚单分子层VOx结构有助于最大限度地减少过氧化。 MoVO混合氧化物根据晶格氧的可用性也显示氧化然后非氧化的途径[DOI8]。

在沸石/Ga催化剂上,Ga+位点通过氧化加成,氢化消除和还原消除步骤激活丙烷并释放丙烯[DOI9]。 Ga2O3多形体可能遵循异裂解路径,包括丙烷吸附,C-H激活和进一步分解[DOI10]。 二氧化钛基催化剂的前景被四面体Ti4+位点与氧空位相互作用从而脱氢丙烷[DOI11]。

总之,如Cr3+、V5+、Ga+、Ti4+等隔离的阳离子位点与晶格氧相互作用,通过各种机理步骤促进丙烷脱氢,这些步骤由氧化物的性质和结构调节。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 研究结果揭示了金属氧化物催化剂如何通过避免不desired燃烧来优化选择性丙烷脱氢的关键机理信息。与活泼晶格氧相互作用的隔离的阳离子位点促进期望的脱氢途径。未来的研究可以探索将金属氧化物与沸石等其他催化剂组合使用,利用酸碱功能性,扩展传统CrOx催化剂。操作光谱可以阐明反应环境下的工作态结构。反应器和工艺强化的概念也通过化学循环提供了前景。
