 <English>

The article by Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878] explores metal-support interfacial effects in VOx catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). It systematically investigates the effects of different oxide supports on PDH activity over VOx catalysts, finding VOx/ZrO2 to be 4 times more active. Density functional theory calculations reveal this is due to weaker VOx-ZrO2 interactions leading to lower electron density on bridge oxygen atoms, enhancing their capability to rupture C-H bonds in propane. The principles established aim to help tune the VO active site microenvironment.

Similarly, the study by Li et al. [10.1021/acscatal.5b01679] shows that adding Na+ ions to silica supports promotes the formation of reactive SiONa+ sites, enhancing 2D vanadia dispersion to match levels seen on other supports like Al2O3 or ZrO2. This enhances VOx/SiO2 catalyst activity while maintaining selectivity.

In the Cr-Al2O3 system explored by Ma et al. [10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63202-4], varying alumina calcination temperature modulates acidity, affecting catalyst stability and coking. The synthesized alumina's rod-shaped nanostructure and rough surface are proposed to enhance Cr2O3 dispersion. Lower support acidity associates with better anti-coking ability.  

For Co-BEA, Gao et al. [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726] show dealumination generates anchoring silanol groups that stabilize isolated and selective Co2+ sites.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The studies highlighted showcase how rational design of metal-support interfaces - by tuning acid-base properties, controlling dispersion, modulating electronic structure, or stabilizing isolated active sites - can effectively optimize PDH catalyst performance. This mechanistic understanding of structure-activity relationships carries promise for targeted advancement of PDH catalysts by interfacing emerging support materials like metal-organic frameworks with known selective metal sites. Controlled nanoscale engineering of defined interfacial sites also opens up opportunities to limit undesirable consecutive combustion reactions. Further integration of operando spectroscopies with theoretical simulations will likely reveal additional descriptors to predict and design optimized multifunctional interfaces. Broader adoption of this interfacial approach beyond conventional oxides to emerging catalysts could expand the toolkit for selective alkane activation.

<Chinese>

王等人的文章[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]探讨了丙烷脱氢(PDH) VOx催化剂中的金属支撑界面效应。该文系统地研究了不同氧化物支撑材料对VOx催化剂的PDH活性的影响,发现VOx/ZrO2的活性是其他样品的4倍。密度泛函理论计算显示,这是由于VOx-ZrO2相互作用较弱导致桥氧原子上的电子密度更低,增强了其断裂丙烷中C-H键的能力。建立的原理旨在帮助调谐VO活性位的微环境。

同样,Li等人的研究[10.1021/acscatal.5b01679]表明,在二氧化硅支撑材料上添加Na+离子促进了反应性SiONa+位点的形成,增强了V氧化物在二氧化硅上的分散度,与Al2O3或ZrO2等其他支撑材料相当。这提高了VOx/SiO2催化剂的活性,同时保持选择性。

在Ma等人研究的Cr-Al2O3体系中[10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63202-4],改变氧化铝的焙烧温度调节酸度,影响催化剂的稳定性和焦炭沉积。所合成氧化铝的棒状纳米结构和粗糙表面有助于提高Cr2O3的分散度。支撑体酸度较低与更好的抗焦炭能力有关。

对于Co-BEA,高等[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]的研究表明,去铝生成的锚定硅醇基团稳定了孤立且选择性好的Co2+位点。


※※※

整体理解和前景展望:重点研究表明,通过调节酸碱性质、控制分散度、调控电子结构或稳定隔离的活性位点等方式合理设计金属-支撑体界面,可以有效优化PDH催化剂性能。这种结构-活性关系的机理理解为针对性改进PDH催化剂提出了前景,例如通过在金属有机框架材料与已知选择性金属位点之间的界面设计。纳米尺度的隔离界面工程也为限制不想要的连续燃烧反应打开了机会。若与理论模拟相结合的原位光谱技术的进一步整合也可能揭示更多用于预测和设计优化的多功能界面的描述符。这种界面方法的更广泛采用不仅限于常规氧化物,还可扩展应用于新兴催化剂,从而扩大选择性烷烃活化的方法工具包。
