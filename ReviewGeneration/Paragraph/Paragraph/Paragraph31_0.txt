The literature reviewed does not discuss safety issues like flammability and explosion hazards associated with propane dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.06.016, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05711, 10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030, 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63369-3, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01814, 10.1016/j.cej.2017.05.058, 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724, 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, 10.1021/cr5002436, 10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05042, 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005, 10.1016/j.cej.2007.11.009, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1016/j.jcat.2014.10.018, 10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014, 10.1016/j.cej.2020.126656, 10.1016/j.jcat.2017.12.004]. The articles focus on catalyst design, characterization, and performance evaluation without examining process safety considerations. As propane dehydrogenation involves flammable reactants and products, an awareness of potential fire and explosion hazards is prudent from both process safety and environmental perspectives. Careful reactor design, hazard analysis, fail-safe measures, and strict operating procedures can help mitigate risks.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The research landscape on propane dehydrogenation catalysis remains largely confined to advances in materials discovery and microkinetic characterizations. An opportunity exists to expand investigations from the predominant material-centric fathoming to encompass macroscopic process intensification and safety dimensions. As the field matures towards commercial actualizations, multifaceted optima spanning catalytic efficacy, operability limits, hazard appraisals, and sustainability benchmarks will garner increasing relevance.
