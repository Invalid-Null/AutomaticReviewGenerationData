Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Alloying platinum (Pt) with post-transition metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), and zinc (Zn) has been extensively explored to enhance the performance of Pt-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). As discussed in the analyses of references [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548], [10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.084], [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671], and [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816], the addition of these secondary metals modulates the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites, leading to optimized activity, selectivity, and stability. 

Pt-Sn alloys are the most widely studied system, implemented commercially in the Oleflex process [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Adding Sn suppresses side reactions like cracking and coke formation by blocking Pt ensemble sites [10.1002/anie.201507119], [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]. Sn also increases electron density on Pt through ligand effects, improving selectivity towards dehydrogenation [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. However, excess Sn reduces activity by weakening propane adsorption strength [10.1016/j.jcej.2017.09.035]. Thus, optimal Sn content is key, with Pt-rich Pt-Sn phases like Pt3Sn giving balanced performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016].  

For Ga and Zn promoters, similar electronic and geometric alterations optimize Pt speciation. GaPt intermetallics suppress hydrogenolysis and enhance thermal stability [10.1002/anie.202107210]. Isolated Pt-Zn sites minimize ensemble size, achieving equilibrium conversion [10.1021/jacs.1c10261]. Overall, tuning the secondary metal content tailors the Pt-M interaction strength to maximize PDH productivity.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The discussed analyses provide vital perspectives into engineering optimized Pt-based bimetallic and alloy catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and related C3 transformations. While existing systems like Pt-Sn have achieved commercial viability, opportunities remain to further advance selectivity, conversion efficiency and stability through novel synthetic strategies and advanced characterization. 

Emerging focus areas might encompass extending this approach to unexplored tri- and multi-metallic combinations with synergistic functionalities, and developing novel supports like zeolites and MOFs that interact favorably with target phases. Operando and in situ spectroscopy can offer mechanistic clarity to guide synthetic optimization. Machine learning can accelerate identification of optimal compositions and structures. Ultimately, progress in these interlinked domains can drive step-change improvements in catalytic propane valorization.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

用锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn)等后过渡金属与铂(Pt)合金化,已被广泛研究用于增强Pt基催化剂在丙烷脱氢(PDH)中的性能。 正如参考文献[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]、[10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.084]、[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671] 和 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]的分析所讨论的,这些次要金属的添加调节了Pt活性位点的几何和电子性质,导致了优化的活性、选择性和稳定性。

Pt-Sn合金是研究最广泛的体系,在Oleflex工艺中得到商业化应用 [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。添加Sn通过堵塞Pt齐聚位点抑制副反应如裂解和焦炭生成 [10.1002/anie.201507119], [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]。Sn还通过配体效应增加Pt上的电子密度,提高脱氢选择性 [10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。然而,过量Sn会通过削弱丙烷吸附力减少活性 [10.1016/j.jcej.2017.09.035]。因此,Sn含量的优化至关重要,Pt富Pt-Sn相如Pt3Sn给出平衡的性能 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。

对于Ga和Zn助剂,类似的电子和几何改变优化了Pt形态。 GaPt金属间化合物抑制氢解和增强热稳定性[10.1002/anie.202107210]。 隔离的Pt-Zn位点最小化集团体积,达到平衡转化率 [10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。总体而言,调节次要金属含量量身定制Pt-M间的相互作用强度,以最大化PDH生产率。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:讨论的分析为工程优化Pt基双金属和合金催化剂用于丙烷脱氢和相关C3转化提供了重要视角。尽管现有体系如Pt-Sn已经实现商业化,但通过新颖的合成策略和先进表征来进一步提高选择性、转化效率和稳定性的机会依然存在。

新兴重点领域可能包括将这种方法扩展到未探索的三元和多元金属组合,这些金属组合具有协同功能性;并开发如沸石和MOF等新的载体,这些载体与目标相互作用良好。实时和原位光谱可以提供机理澄清来指导合成优化。机器学习可以加速识别最佳组成和结构。最终,这些相互关联领域的进步可以推动催化丙烷价值化的步入式改进。
