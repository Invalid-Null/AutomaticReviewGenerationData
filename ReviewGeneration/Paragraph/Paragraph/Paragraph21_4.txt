Based on the details extracted from the file, most of the articles do not investigate the effects of tuning zeolite Si/Al ratio or incorporating mesoporosity on propane dehydrogenation catalyst performance. However, a few studies reveal some structure-function relationships:

One article shows tuning zeolite Si/Al ratio by dealumination creates abundant silanol nests in Beta zeolites, enhancing metal dispersion and stabilization [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. Another investigates CHA zeolites with varying Si/Al ratios from 5-25 and demonstrates that optimally tuning the Ga/Al ratio leads to stable 96% propylene selectivity, although rates decrease over time [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. One study explores the complex interplay between Si/Al and Ga/Al ratios, concluding the optimal Ga loading depends on the Si/Al ratio [10.1021/acscatal.1c01497]. Finally, one article evaluates smaller TS-1 crystals with mesoporosity, showing enhanced diffusion and avoided coking, improving conversion, selectivity and lifetime [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The few studies revealing structure-function relationships highlight opportunities to further explore tuning zeolite properties for propane dehydrogenation. Creating mesoporosity and silanol defects through dealumination, crystal size control or desilication could be systematically studied to enhance metal stabilization and diffusion. Optimizing metal loadings based on the zeolite framework Si/Al ratio also shows promise to maximize selectivity and lifetime. Nevertheless, decreases in activity over time remain a key challenge. Future work could focus on elucidating deactivation mechanisms and improving hydrothermal stability through framework substitutions or protective coatings. Operando spectroscopy and microscopy studies would provide essential insights to guide rational design. Overall, significant potential exists to enhance propane dehydrogenation performance through zeolite materials optimization.

一篇文章通过除铝处理调节Beta分子筛的Si/Al比,形成大量硅醇巢,增强了金属的分散和稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。另一篇研究了Si/Al比从5到25不同的CHA分子筛,证明了最佳调节Ga/Al比可以使丙烯选择性保持在96%稳定,尽管活性随时间下降 [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]。一项研究探索了Si/Al比和Ga/Al比之间的复杂相互作用,得出最佳Ga负载量取决于Si/Al比的结论[10.1021/acscatal.1c01497]。最后,一篇文章评估了具有介孔的较小TS-1晶体,显示增强了扩散并避免焦炭,改善了转化率,选择性和使用寿命[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 揭示结构-性能关系的少数研究凸显了进一步探索调节分子筛性质用于丙烷脱氢的机会。通过除铝、晶体大小控制或去硅化制备介孔和硅醇缺陷,可以有系统地研究以增强金属的稳定和扩散。根据分子筛框架的Si/Al比优化金属负载量也显示了最大化选择性和使用寿命的前景。尽管如此,活性随时间下降仍然是一个关键挑战。未来的工作可以集中在阐明失活机制和通过框架取代或保护涂层提高热稳定性。同位素研究和显微技术可以提供必要的见解来指导合理的设计。总的来说,通过优化分子筛材料有极大的潜力提高丙烷脱氢性能。
