Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Multiple studies have evaluated the effect of alloying platinum with tin, gallium, and zinc for propane dehydrogenation catalysis. Adding these secondary metals as promoters offers considerable benefits compared to monometallic platinum catalysts.

Tin is the most widely studied promoter for platinum in propane dehydrogenation. Supported PtSn catalysts such as PtSn/Al2O3 and PtSn/zeolites have been successfully commercialized, like in the Oleflex process by UOP [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. Adding Sn helps decrease the size of Pt ensembles [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631], increase electron density on Pt through ligand effects [10.1002/anie.201507119], and improve selectivity to propylene while inhibiting coke formation and hydrogenolysis reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006, 10.1016/j.fuproc.2009.07.019]. However, some studies note Sn can also decrease initial activity by increasing propane dissociation barriers [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]. An optimal Pt:Sn ratio around 3:1 or 2:1 maintains good activity balanced with high stability and selectivity [10.1016/j.cej.2017.09.035, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016].

Gallium is emerging as an effective co-metal for platinum in propane dehydrogenation. Pt-Ga intermetallic nanoparticles and single atoms anchored on various supports including Al2O3, zeolites, silica etc. achieve remarkable conversion, selectivity and stability [10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129, 10.1016/j.catcom.2019.04.033, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]. The synergy between Pt and Ga helps stabilize dispersed Pt sites and alter adsorption properties through electronic effects. Ga neighbors also play a key role in suppressing side reactions like hydrogenolysis and coke formation [10.1002/anie.202107210].

Platinum-zinc catalysts represent another promising combination, especially atomically isolated Pt stabilized by Zn species in the framework of zeolites like dealuminated Beta and ZSM-5. These single-site PtZn catalysts approach equilibrium conversion limits with >90% propylene selectivity [10.1021/jacs.1c10261, 10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5]. The Zn neighbors tune the valence orbitals of Pt to improve selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. However, a Zn:Pt ratio >4 is optimal for maintaining stability [10.1021/jacs.1c10261].

In summary, adding secondary metals like Sn, Ga and Zn as promoters enables modulating the size, valence electrons, and adsorption properties of Pt active sites. This results in improved activity, propylene selectivity and stability compared to monometallic Pt catalysts for propane dehydrogenation through electronic, ensemble and ligand effects. Each co-metal has unique benefits, and ongoing research aims to optimize the type, ratio and structure of Pt alloy combinations.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements discussed in exploring Pt alloy catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, there exist rich opportunities to discover further synergies by alloying platinum with other transition metals. Combining experimental studies with computational modeling can help screen many bimetallic and even ternary combinations to identify electronic and geometric motifs that optimize the trade-off between dehydrogenation activity and stability. Controlled synthesis techniques like ALD enable precise tuning of alloy compositions and structures. Apart from particle catalysts, innovative concepts like single atom alloys anchored in zeolites merit greater focus. There is also potential for alloying strategies in emerging alternative dehydrogenation chemistries like non-oxidative routes using CO2. By continuing to leverage fundamental insights from Pt alloy catalysts, researchers can aid the development of selective and robust propane dehydrogenation processes.

双金属和合金催化剂——Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

多项研究评估了Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化以用于丙烷脱氢催化的效果。与单金属Pt催化剂相比,添加这些辅助金属促进剂具有可观的好处。

Sn是在丙烷脱氢中研究最广泛的Pt助剂。 支持的PtSn催化剂(如PtSn/Al2O3和PtSn/分子筛)已成功商业化,如UOP公司的Oleflex工艺[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。 添加Sn有助于减小Pt组分的大小[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631],通过配体效应增加Pt上的电子密度[10.1002/anie.201507119],并改善丙烯的选择性同时抑制焦炭形成和氢解�应[10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006, 10.1016/j.fuproc.2009.07.019]。 然而,一些研究指出Sn也可以通过增加丙烷解离屏障来降低初始活性[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。 3:1或2:1左右的Pt:Sn比例可以在高稳定性和选择性的同时保持良好的活性平衡[10.1016/j.cej.2017.09.035,10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。

镓正日益成为丙烷脱氢中Pt的有效助剂金属。 支持在Al2O3、分子筛、二氧化硅等上的Pt-Ga中间体纳米粒子和单原子催化剂达到了显着的转换率、选择性和稳定性[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129, 10.1016/j.catcom.2019.04.033, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]。 Pt和Ga之间的协同作用有助于稳定分散的Pt位点,并通过电子效应改变吸附特性。 Ga邻居也发挥关键作用,抑制氢解和焦炭形成等副反应[10.1002/anie.202107210]。

 Pt-Zn催化剂代表了另一种有希望的组合,特别是原子隔离Pt被分子筛(如脱氢Beta和ZSM-5)的Zn位稳定。 这些单位PtZn催化剂接近平衡转换限值,丙烯选择性>90% [10.1021/jacs.1c10261, 10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5]。 Zn邻居调谐Pt的价轨道以提高选择性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。 然而,Zn:Pt比> 4是维持稳定性的最佳选择[10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。

总之,添加Sn、Ga和Zn等辅助金属作为促进剂可以调节Pt活性位点的大小、价电子和吸附特性。 通过电子、组semble和配体效应,这导致了比单金属Pt催化剂更高的活性、丙烯选择性和稳定性丙烷脱氢。 每种共金属都具有独特的好处,当前的研究旨在优化Pt合金组合的类型、比率和结构。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:考虑到探索Pt合金催化剂用于丙烷脱氢的进步,通过使Pt与其他过渡金属合金化存在发现进一步协同作用的丰富机会。 结合实验研究与计算建模可以帮助筛选许多双金属甚至三元组合,以识别优化脱氢活性与稳定性之间权衡的电子和几何基元。 ALD等受控合成技术可以精确调节合金成分和结构。 除了颗粒催化剂,分子筛中锚定的单原子合金等创新概念也值得更多关注。 在新兴的替代脱氢化学(如非氧化脱氢使用CO2)中合金化策略也具有潜力。 通过继续利用来自Pt合金催化剂的基础见解,研究人员可以帮助开发选择性强且稳定的丙烷脱氢工艺。
