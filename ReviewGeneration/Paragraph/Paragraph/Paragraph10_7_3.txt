Based on the details extracted from the references, propane dehydrogenation mechanistic scenarios have been proposed over various metal oxide catalysts. 

Over CrZrOx catalysts, two neighboring coordinatively unsaturated Zr (Zrcus) sites located at an oxygen vacancy are mainly responsible for catalyzing the dehydrogenation of propane to propene [1]. The Zrcus sites are formed upon reductive treatment of the catalyst, and their concentration depends on factors like the CrOx-ZrO2 interaction strength and ZrO2 crystallite size [1]. DFT calculations reveal that the presence of Cr helps reduce the energy barrier for breaking the methyl C-H bond in an adsorbed iso-C3H7 intermediate compared to bare ZrO2, making propene formation easier [1].

Zn/MFI catalysts are proposed to activate propane via dissociative adsorption on zinc oxide species to form propyl species, followed by propene evolution and recombinative desorption of hydrogen [2]. Propene can further oligomerize on acidic sites, while hydrogen participates in propane hydrogenolysis [2].

Alumina catalysts with oxygen vacancies create Alcu-O pairs that facilitate propane dehydrogenation through concerted/stepwise pathways, tuning energies of key steps like CH activation and H2 formation [3].

Isolated vanadium oxide sites in Mars-van Krevelen type catalysts activate propane via abstraction of hydrogen using lattice oxygen and formation of alkyl species that eliminate propene. The reduced V centers then reoxidize by O2 for catalyst regeneration [4].

Ga/ZSM-5 is suggested to activate propane on Ga+H+ pair sites or isolated Ga+ sites to form alkyl species. Proximity of Ga+ and Brønsted acid sites enables hydride abstraction to form propylene [5]. An alternative pathway is proposed over GaO+ involving a pi-complex intermediate [6].

Furthermore, heterolytic dissociation pathways have been proposed over gallium oxide [7] and supported gallium oxide [8] catalysts, as well as redox mechanisms involving Cr6+/Cr3+ sites [9]. Key challenges noted across different systems include avoiding overoxidation side reactions and stability of the active sites.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,已在各种金属氧化物催化剂上提出丙烷脱氢的机理场景。

在CrZrOx催化剂上,主要是位于氧空位的两个相邻的未饱和配位的锆(Zrcus)位点催化丙烷到丙烯的脱氢[1]。在催化剂的还原处理过程中形成Zrcus位点,其浓度取决于诸如CrOx-ZrO2相互作用强度和ZrO2晶粒尺寸等因素[1]。DFT计算表明,Cr的存在有助于降低吸附的异丙基中间体中甲基C-H键的断裂能垒,使丙烯的形成更容易[1]。

Zn/MFI催化剂被提出通过在锌氧化物种上发生解离吸附来活化丙烷形成丙基种,其后随着丙烯的形成和氢气的可逆解吸[2]。丙烯还可以在酸性位点上发生寡聚,而氢气可以参与丙烷加氢裂解[2]。

含氧空位的氧化铝催化剂通过协同/步骤机理途径,调节诸如CH激活和H2形成等关键步骤的能量[3]。

Mars-van Krevelen类型催化剂中的隔离钒氧化物位点通过使用晶格氧摘除氢原子来活化丙烷并形成烷基种,然后脱去丙烯。还原的V中心随后通过O2氧化再生催化剂[4]。

Ga/ZSM-5表面被认为丙烷在Ga+H+位点或隔离Ga+位点上活化形成烷基种。Ga+和Brønsted酸位点的接近有助于提取氢原子形成丙烯[5]。在GaO+上也提出了另一种通过π络合中间体的途径[6]。

此外,在氧化镓[7]和负载氧化镓[8]催化剂上还提出了异裂解途径,以及涉及Cr6+/Cr3+位点的氧化还原机理[9]。在不同体系中注意到的关键挑战包括避免副反应过氧化和活性位点的稳定性。
