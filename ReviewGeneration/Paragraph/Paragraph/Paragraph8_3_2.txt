Studies have shown β-Ga2O3 can outperform Cr2O3 and benchmark catalysts in the presence of CO2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. Supported Pt-Sn catalysts, forming intermetallic Pt3Sn sites, have very high reported propane conversion and propylene selectivity, and are utilized commercially [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. The geometric and electronic effects of the Pt-Sn alloy help inhibit coking and deep dehydrogenation. Pt-Sn catalysts stabilized on zeolites and other supports via strong metal-support interactions have achieved excellent performance and stability [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]. 

		In summary, while Pt and Pt-Sn dominate propane dehydrogenation catalysis research, some metal oxides like Cr2O3 and Ga2O3 have potential, especially when modified by promoters and supports.
