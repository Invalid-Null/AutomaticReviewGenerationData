<English>

Metal oxides such as Cr2O3, Pt-Sn, and Ga2O3 have shown promising activity for propane dehydrogenation based on results from several studies [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999, 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183, 10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]. Among the polymorphs, β-Ga2O3 exhibited the highest propylene yield, followed by α-Cr2O3 and then γ-Ga2O3 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]. The high activity of Ga2O3 has been attributed to its surface acidity, where the initial activity maximized on β-Ga2O3 which had the highest surface acid site density [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. For Cr2O3 catalysts, the catalytic activity can be enhanced by supporting on inert carriers like SiO2 due to the weak metal-support interactions [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. Doping Cr2O3 with metals like Pt has also improved performance [10.1021/acscatal.1c01814]. As for Pt-Sn catalysts, forming intermetallic Pt-Sn nanoparticles helps improve propylene selectivity and stability [10.1126/science.abg7894]. Overall, tailoring the composition, crystal phase, and metal-support interactions have been effective strategies for enhancing the catalytic activity, selectivity, and stability of metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The studies reviewed showcase the potential of metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, achieveing comparable or better performance than conventional metallic catalysts in some cases. However, room remains to further advance metal oxide catalysts through optimization of parameters like acid-base properties, morphology, defects, and coupling with co-catalysts. Emerging in-situ characterization techniques combined with computational modeling can offer valuable mechanistic insights to guide rational design.  There is also opportunity to explore more unconventional metal oxide compositions by high-throughput methods. Overall, continuous research efforts to unravel structure-activity relationships and reaction mechanisms hold promise for realizing highly efficient and stable metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation.

<Chinese>

根据几项研究结果,Cr2O3、Pt-Sn 和 Ga2O3等金属氧化物催化剂表现出了将丙烷转化为丙烯的催化活性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999, 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183, 10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]。在多种晶型中,β-Ga2O3 的丙烯收率最高,其次是 α-Cr2O3,然后是 γ-Ga2O3 [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]。Ga2O3 的高活性被归因于其表面酸性,其中 β-Ga2O3 由于具有最高的表面酸性中心密度而显示出最大的初始活性 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]。对于 Cr2O3 催化剂,将其负载于 SiO2 等惰性载体上可增强其催化活性,这是由于存在弱的金属支撑物相互作用 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]。使用 Pt 等金属掺杂 Cr2O3 也改善了性能 [10.1021/acscatal.1c01814]。至于 Pt-Sn 催化剂,形成 Pt-Sn 金属间化合物纳米粒子有助于提高丙烯选择性和稳定性 [10.1126/science.abg7894]。总体而言,调控组成、晶相和金属支撑物的相互作用都是提高丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂催化活性、选择性和稳定性的有效策略。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:综述的研究展示了金属氧化物催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的潜力,在某些情况下其性能可与传统金属催化剂媲美或优于之。然而,通过优化酸碱性、形态、缺陷以及与共催化剂的耦合等参数,金属氧化物催化剂还有进一步提升的空间。新兴的原位表征技术结合计算模型化可以提供有价值的机制信息以指导合理设计。高通量方法也为探索更非传统的金属氧化物组合提供了机会。总之,持续的研究工作以阐明结构-活性关系和反应机制,这对实现高效稳定的丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂具有前景。
