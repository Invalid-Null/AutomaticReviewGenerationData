For VOx catalysts, redox cycles between different V oxidation states (V3+, V4+, V5+) via Mars-Van Krevelen type mechanisms are commonly proposed, using lattice oxygen for dehydrogenation and O2 for reoxidation [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. Sub-monolayer VOx structures on supports can help minimize overoxidation during these redox cycles [Placeholder_of_DOI5].

On Ga-based oxides, heterolytic dissociation of propane on tetracoordinated Ga3+ sites to form gallium hydride and alkoxide intermediates has been suggested [Placeholder_of_DOI6]. Subsequent β-H elimination results in propene formation. Pt clusters are proposed to facilitate hydrogen recombination in Ga-Pt dual sites [Placeholder_of_DOI7].  

For ZrO2 catalysts, two neighboring coordinatively unsaturated Zr4+ sites near oxygen vacancies are identified from DFT calculations as active sites responsible for homolytic C-H bond scission [Placeholder_of_DOI8]. Synergistic effects between Zn2+ and ZrO2 sites are also proposed for dehydrogenation over Zn/ZrO2 catalysts [Placeholder_of_DOI9].

In summary, surface oxygen species and coordination unsaturation around metal cation sites allows for controlled C-H activation while minimizing complete oxidation. Replenishment of these oxygen species by lattice oxygen, gas phase O2 or CO2 enables redox catalytic cycles.

While significant advances have been made in identifying active sites and plausible mechanistic pathways, several aspects still warrant further enquiry for more precise structure-activity elucidations overlying propane dehydrogenation on metal oxides:

- Transient reaction intermediates and their binding modes remain relatively ambiguous at a molecular level. In-depth spectroscopic tracking of surface species can offer enhanced insights.

- Reaction kinetics have been predominantly appraised through overall turnover rates and activation energies. Discerning rate constants for individual mechanistic steps could reveal decisive descriptors more precisely. 

- Although isolated cationic sites and oxygen defects have been recurrently implicated for activity, optimizing their relative abundances and geometric configurations still seems nebulous from a synthetic viewpoint. Novel design endeavors correlating synthetic handles to resulting site densities and distributions merit investigation.

根据从文献中提取的详细信息,已经为不同金属氧化物催化剂上的丙烷脱氢提出了几种机制场景。

在Cr基氧化物催化剂上,Cr6+产生的体系活性Cr3+位点参与了丙烷中C-H键的断裂[占位符_DOI1]。在介孔硅载体催化剂中,具有配位不饱和的隔离Cr3+位点被具体提议为活性位点[占位符_DOI2]。在Cr2O3上,也建议了一种Mars-Van Krevelen型氧化还原途径,其中包括用晶格氧进行脱氢和用气相O2进行再氧化[占位符_DOI3]。

对于VOx催化剂,通过Mars-Van Krevelen类型机制在不同V氧化态(V3+、V4+、V5+)之间进行氧化还原循环的建议很常见,使用晶格氧进行脱氢,使用O2进行再氧化[占位符_DOI4]。支撑物上的亚单分子层VOx结构可以帮助最小化这些氧化还原循环期间的过氧化[占位符_DOI5]。

在Ga基氧化物上,建议丙烷在四配位Ga3+位点上发生杂解裂化为镓氢化物和烷氧化物中间体[占位符_DOI6]。随后的β-H消除导致丙烯的形成。 Pt簇被提出可以促进Ga-Pt双位点上的氢重组合[占位符_DOI7]。

对于ZrO2催化剂,DFT计算确定了两个相邻的无配位饱和Zr4+位点靠近氧空位是负责致使丙烷C-H键均裂断裂的活性位点[占位符_DOI8]。 在Zn/ZrO2催化剂上,Zn2+和ZrO2位点之间的协同作用也被提议用于脱氢[占位符_DOI9]。

总之,表面氧物种和金属阳离子位点周围的配位不饱和允许对C-H活化的控制,同时最大限度地减少完全氧化。 晶格氧、气相O2或CO2对这些氧物种的补充使氧化还原催化循环成为可能。

※※※

虽然在识别活性位点和可行的机制途径方面取得了重大进展,但在金属氧化物上的丙烷脱氢上仍有几个方面值得进一步调查,以获得更精确的结构-活性关系:

- 过渡态反应中间体及其结合模式在分子水平上仍然相对模糊。 深入的表面物种光谱追踪可以提供更好的洞察力。

- 反应动力学主要通过整体周转率和活化能进行评估。 分辨个别机制步骤的速率常数可以更精确地揭示决定性描述符。

- 尽管孤立的阳离子位点和氧缺陷一再被指责活性,但从合成角度来看,优化其相对丰度和几何配置似乎仍然模糊。 新的设计努力将合成处理与产生的位点密度和分布相关联值得调查。
