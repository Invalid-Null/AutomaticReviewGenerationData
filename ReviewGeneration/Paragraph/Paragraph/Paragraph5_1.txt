Detailed analysis established from the study of reference [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. The DFT calculations revealed insights into propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and kinetics over some catalysts, showing lower activation barriers on Zn-O-Zn sites compared to Zn2+ sites, and correlations between Pt's d-band center and propylene selectivity. 

Additional insights stemming from references [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008] and [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. Microkinetic analysis based on DFT elucidated structure-activity relationships and identified descriptor variables. Calculations also provided understanding of propane dehydrogenation on 3N-coordinated single-atom catalysts, showing reaction energies, barriers, and catalyst stability.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the computational advancements discussed, DFT has become an indispensable tool for elucidating mechanisms and design principles in propane dehydrogenation. As theory and hardware continue improving, calculations will tackle increasingly complex systems with heightened accuracy. An integrated framework synergistically combining modeling, experiments and data science could accelerate discoveries. Key opportunities include extending models to industrial conditions, systematically surveying expanded catalyst spaces, and integrating across scales to link descriptors from electronic structure to overall kinetics and process optimization.
