Based on the details extracted from the references, several mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts. 

On CrOx/ZrO2 catalysts, two neighboring coordinatively unsaturated Zr (Zrcus) sites located at an oxygen vacancy are suggested to be responsible for breaking C-H bonds in propane to form propene [10.1021/acscatal.9b05063]. Isolated Cr3+ species with vacant coordination sites are also proposed as active sites [10.1006/jcat.1994.1183]. 

On Zn/ZrO2 catalysts, isolated tricoordinated Zn2+ sites anchored on ZrO2 catalyze the initial dehydrogenation step, with neighboring Zrcus sites facilitating the second dehydrogenation step in a synergistic manner [10.1021/acssuschemeng.1c03074].

On VOx/metal oxide catalysts, a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism is often proposed involving lattice oxygen in reversible redox cycles between higher and lower V oxidation states like V5+/V3+ [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.01.023, 10.1021/jacs.9b09235]. 

On Ga/zeolite catalysts, monovalent Ga+ sites activate propane via a Ga-(C3H7) intermediate, followed by hydride abstraction to give propene [10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.004, 10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. A heterolytic dissociation path is preferred over redox routes.

Additionally, concerted dehydrogenation pathways [10.1021/acscatal.3c01002], alkyl mechanism via metal-alkyl intermediates [10.1021/acscatal.1c01497], and Mars-van Krevelen pathways utilizing lattice oxygen [10.1021/acscatal.2c05286] have also been proposed over other metal oxide catalysts.

In summary, isolated metal sites like Cr3+, Zn2+, Ga+, or V species in close proximity seem to be the key active sites on metal oxide catalysts for catalyzing individual steps in propane dehydrogenation reactions.
※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The current mechanistic proposals provide important insights that can guide the design of improved propane dehydrogenation metal oxide catalysts. Key features like isolated redox-active sites, reversible lattice oxygen, concerted dehydrogenation pathways, and synergistic site pairs have emerged. Building on these concepts, future research could focus on elucidating full catalytic cycles spanning activation to regeneration steps and quantitative kinetics. Operando spectroscopic techniques combined with isotopic labeling and DFT calculations can offer detailed molecular-level information to unravel complex mechanisms. Atomic-level catalyst synthesis methods also allow precise control over site geometries and proximity to test theoretical proposals. Extending the mechanistic knowledge to other light alkane systems could uncover potentially generalizable concepts. Broader adoption of systems approaches integrating synthesis, characterization, and testing can accelerate these mechanistic explorations.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,已经为不同金属氧化物催化剂上的丙烷脱氢反应提出了几种机理场景。

在CrOx/ZrO2催化剂上,位于氧空位的两个相邻的无配位的锆(Zrcus)位点被认为是负责断裂丙烷中的C-H键以生成丙烯的部位[10.1021/acscatal.9b05063]。被孤立的具有配位空位的Cr3+物种也被提议为活性位点[10.1006/jcat.1994.1183]。

在Zn/ZrO2催化剂上,锚定在ZrO2上的被孤立的三配位Zn2+位点催化初始脱氢步骤,而相邻的Zrcus位点则促进了第二步脱氢,二者之间存在协同作用[10.1021/acssuschemeng.1c03074]。

在VOx/金属氧化物催化剂上,通常提出了Mars-van Krevelen机制,其中涉及不同价态的V如V5+/V3+之间棕櫚氧的可逆氧化还原循环[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.01.023, 10.1021/jacs.9b09235]。

在Ga/分子筛催化剂上,一价Ga+位点通过Ga-(C3H7)中间体激活丙烷,随后发生氢化取代生成丙烯[10.1016/j.jcat.2006.03.004, 10.1021/jacs.2c03941]。优先选择的是异裂解路径而不是氧化还原途径。

此外,在其他金属氧化物催化剂上还提出了协同脱氢途径[10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]、通过金属烷基中间体的烷基机理[10.1021/acscatal.1c01497]和利用晶格氧的 Mars-van Krevelen 途径[10.1021/acscatal.2c05286]。

总的来说,诸如 Cr3+、Zn2+、Ga+或 V 等相互接近的被孤立的金属位似乎是金属氧化物催化剂上催化丙烷脱氢反应各个步骤的关键活性位点。

综合理解与未来展望: 当前的机理提议为指导改进的丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂的设计提供了重要启示。诸如被孤立的氧化还原活性位点、可逆的晶格氧、协同脱氢途径和协同位点对等关键特征已经出现。在这些概念的基础上,未来的研究可以侧重于阐明完整的催化循环,跨越从激活到再生的步骤和定量动力学。联用光谱技术与同位素标记和DFT计算可以提供详细的分子层次的信息来阐明复杂的机理。原子水平的催化剂合成方法也允许精确控制位点几何和邻近关系以测试理论提议。将机制知识扩展到其他低碳烷烃体系可能会发现潜在的可推广的概念。更广泛地采用综合合成、表征和测试的系统方法可以加速这些机理探索。
