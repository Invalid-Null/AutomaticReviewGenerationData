Based on the details extracted from the file, one article ([10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]) investigates electronic and geometric effects of the secondary metal phosphorus in Ru-P bimetallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. It shows that increasing the P content reduces the Ru ensemble size (geometric effect), decreasing the hydrogenolysis rate. It also demonstrates through XPS and XANES data that Ru-P bond formation shifts the spectra to higher energies, suggesting decreased Ru 4d orbital energies that weaken metal-adsorbate bonds (electronic effect). 

Another article ([10.1021/jacs.8b08162]) explores electronic and geometric effects of manganese in Pt-Mn bimetallic nanoparticles by manipulating the Mn precursor amount to control intermetallic formation. The Pt3Mn intermetallic subsurface significantly improved propane dehydrogenation selectivity over Pt, attributed to lowered hydrocarbon and hydrogen adsorption strengths from electronic effects of Mn incorporation.

Furthermore, the electronic effects of gallium are investigated in a GaPt bimetallic catalyst ([10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]), where XPS and IR data indicate electron donation from Ga leading to a higher electron density on Pt sites. This is ascribed to GaPt alloy formation and improves catalytic performance.

In summary, modulating secondary metal incorporation allows tuning of geometric and electronic properties in bimetallic systems, enabling optimization of catalyst structure and function. Further explorations may focus on expanding the range of secondary metals studied and combining in situ characterization with testing to elucidate transformation pathways.※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The investigations of secondary metal electronic and geometric effects provide valuable insights into performance modulation and structure-activity relationships in bimetallic catalysts. However, most studies have focused on a narrow range of systems, and opportunities exist to broaden the exploration to unconventional metals/structures. Operando methodology combining spectroscopy, microscopy and testing could offer dynamic information to correlate structural evolution with catalytic function. Multi-scale modeling may also help bridge the material gaps between atomic-level electronic structure and reactor-scale testing. Such endeavors can expand the catalyst library and transform bimetallic design from Edisonian screening toward knowledge-based development.

<Chinese>
根据文件提取的细节,一篇文章([10.1021/acscatal.0c01667])研究了副金属磷在Ru-P双金属催化剂中的电子和几何效应。它显示,增加P含量可以减小Ru的集聚体大小(几何效应),从而减少氢解反应速率。它还通过XPS和XANES数据表明,Ru-P键的形成使光谱向更高的能量位移,这意味着Ru 4d轨道能量的下降削弱了金属吸附键(电子效应)。

另一篇文章([10.1021/jacs.8b08162])通过操纵Mn前驱体的量来控制金属间化合物的形成,从而探究了锰在Pt-Mn双金属纳米粒子中的电子和几何效应。与Pt相比,Pt3Mn金属间化合物次表面明显提高了丙烷脱氢的选择性,这是由于锰掺入导致的碳氢化合物和氢的吸附强度降低的电子效应。

此外,一种GaPt双金属催化剂([10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129])的研究表明,XPS和红外数据表明Ga导致Pt位点上的电子密度更高。这归因于GaPt合金的形成,并提高了催化性能。

总之,调控次要金属的掺入可以调谐双金属系统的几何和电子性质,以实现催化剂结构和功能的优化。进一步的探索可能会关注扩大所研究的次要金属范围,并结合原位表征与测试来阐明转化途径。※※※  

综合理解与未来展望:对次要金属电子和几何效应的研究为双金属催化剂性能调控和结构-活性关系提供了宝贵的见解。然而,大多数研究集中在很窄的几个体系上,因此存在扩大探索范围至非常规金属/结构的机会。操作原位法结合光谱学、显微技术和测试能够提供动态信息,以关联结构演变和催化功能。多尺度建模也可以帮助弥合原子水平的电子结构与反应器水平测试之间的差距。这些努力可以扩大催化剂库,并将双金属设计从埃迪生式筛选转向以知识为基础的开发。
