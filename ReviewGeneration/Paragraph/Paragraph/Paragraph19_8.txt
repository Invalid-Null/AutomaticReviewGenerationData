Based on the details provided in the file, some of the studies describe synergistic effects between metals in bimetallic and alloy propane dehydrogenation catalysts, attributed to ensemble and ligand effects, while others do not investigate such synergistic effects. 

Specifically, the study [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667] shows that increasing P content in Ru-P catalysts reduces the Ru ensemble size and isolates Ru atoms (ensemble effect), while also lengthening the Ru-Ru bonds compared to metallic Ru, weakening metal-adsorbate interactions (ligand effect). Both effects improve selectivity.

The study [10.1002/anie.202107210] demonstrates synergistic electronic promotion from Ca and geometric blocking by Pb in the bimetallic catalyst, leading to remarkably improved stability in propane dehydrogenation. 

In [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008], [PtZn4] surface ensembles in PtZn alloys isolate the Pt sites and alter the electronic state through ligand effects. This weakens propylene adsorption and inhibits over-dehydrogenation, increasing selectivity and stability.

The study [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019] explains adding a secondary metal can block sites on the Pt surface (ensemble effect) and change the Pt electron density (ligand effect) to improve catalytic performance.

Overall, while some studies provide examples, most do not describe specific synergistic ensemble or ligand effects between metals. Further research is still needed to elucidate the precise origin of synergistic effects in bimetallic propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements discussed, opportunities exist to systematically investigate synergistic metal interactions through advanced characterization and computational methods. An improved understanding of structures enabling synergistic ensemble and ligand effects would facilitate rational design of bimetallic catalysts with optimized selectivity, activity and stability. Studies could also explore extending these design concepts to other catalytic systems.

根据文件提供的详细信息,一些研究描述了双金属和合金丙烷脱氢催化剂中金属之间的协同效应,这些效应归因于集合体和配体效应,而其他一些研究并没有调查这种协同效应。

具体而言,研究 [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667] 表明,增加 Ru-P 催化剂中的 P 含量会减小 Ru 集合体的大小,并隔离 Ru 原子(集合体效应),同时也会使 Ru-Ru 键长比金属 Ru 的键长更长,削弱金属-吸附剂相互作用(配体效应)。这两种效应都能改进选择性。

研究 [10.1002/anie.202107210] 演示了双金属催化剂中 Ca 的电子提升和 Pb 的几何封锁之间的协同效应,这显著改进了丙烷脱氢的稳定性。

在 [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008] 中,[PtZn4] PtZn合金表面集合隔离了 Pt 位点,并通过配体效应改变了电子态。这削弱了丙烯的吸附和过度脱氢的抑制,从而增加了选择性和稳定性。

研究 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019] 解释了添加副金属可以阻断 Pt 表面上的位点(集合体效应)并改变 Pt 原子的电子密度(配体效应)以改进催化性能。

总的来说,虽然一些研究提供了例子,但大多数研究没有描述金属之间具体的协同集合体或配体效应。关于双金属丙烷脱氢催化剂中协同效应的准确起源还需要进一步的研究。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:鉴于讨论的进步,通过先进的表征和计算方法系统地调查协同金属相互作用的机会存在。对启用协同集合体和配体效应的结构的更好理解将有助于理性设计优化选择性、活性和稳定性的双金属催化剂。研究还可以探索将这些设计概念扩展到其他催化系统。
