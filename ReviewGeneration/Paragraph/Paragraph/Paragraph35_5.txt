Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the references, while most work has focused on incremental improvements, there is significant potential in exploring novel single atom and confined cluster catalyst architectures. By stabilizing isolated active sites and confining them, these architectures offer opportunities to minimize undesirable side reactions like coking while maximizing selectivity. Future research could focus on synthesizing and characterizing a broader range of single atom and cluster catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, assessing their stability under reaction conditions, and optimizing the surrounding ligand environment to tune catalytic performance. Combined with computational modeling, this can help screen promising next-generation, transformational catalyst architectures.
