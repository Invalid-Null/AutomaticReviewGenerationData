Based on the details extracted from the references, mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over various metal oxide catalysts, with coordinatively unsaturated metal sites often identified as the active sites. 

For Cr-based catalysts, isolated Cr3+ sites [DOI1], Cr3+/Cr2+ redox couples [DOI2], and mononuclear Cr(III) species with coordinative vacancies [DOI3] have been proposed as active sites. Reaction mechanisms include Mars-van Krevelen type pathways involving lattice oxygen [DOI4, DOI5], or direct dehydrogenation routes [DOI1]. 

On VOx catalysts, V3+ sites produced by reduction of higher oxidation states are active for non-oxidative dehydrogenation after loss of lattice oxygen [DOI6], based on a Mars-van Krevelen mechanism [DOI7]. Isolated and oligomeric surface vanadium oxide species have also been proposed as sites for oxidative dehydrogenation [DOI8].  

For ZrO2, DFT studies indicate two Zr cations with unsaturated coordination located at an oxygen vacancy (Zrcus sites) catalyze dehydrogenation [DOI9, DOI10]. These break C-H bonds in propane and activate the methyl C-H bond in the resulting isopropyl intermediate to form propene.

Introducing dopants like Ga can alter the mechanism on ZrO2 from stepwise to concerted [DOI11], while isolated metal cations like Zn2+ and Ga3+ on supports enable heterolytic or oxidative addition pathways [DOI12, DOI13]. Overall, progress has been made in identifying active sites and mechanistic possibilities, but further work is still needed.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the references, promising opportunities exist to design tailored sites and achieve optimized mechanisms for propane dehydrogenation. As lattice oxygen availability, coordination environment, oxidation states, and proximity of disparate sites modulate mechanistic behavior, controlled synthesis of tailored multifunctional ensembles could promote selective pathways. Operando spectroscopic techniques combined with computational modeling will likely play crucial roles in providing molecular-level insights to guide these endeavors. Discerning synergistic contributions and targeted tuning of specific sites also emerges as an exciting direction. Prospects remain bright for transducing mechanistic comprehension into superior catalytic systems through deliberate design.
