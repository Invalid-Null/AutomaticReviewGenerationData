	<English>
		The study detailed in [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878] investigates the effects of different oxide supports like ZrO2, TiO2, SiO2 on the catalytic performance of supported VOx catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). It finds that VOx supported on ZrO2 shows over 4 times higher activity compared to other supports. Density functional theory calculations reveal that this enhanced activity stems from weaker VOx-ZrO2 interactions, which lower the electron density on bridge oxygen atoms and increase their capability to rupture C-H bonds in propane. The principles established aim to help further tune the microenvironment of the VO active site to achieve high activity and propylene selectivity.
		
		Additionally, [10.1021/acscatal.5b01679] explores metal-support effects in vanadium oxide catalysts supported on silica. The study shows that compared to other conventional supports, silica triggers undesired 3D nanoparticle formation due to less reactive surface silanol groups, which reduces dispersion. Adding a small amount of Na+ promotes the formation of more reactive SiONa+ anchoring sites that facilitate 2D vanadia dispersion comparable to other supports like Al2O3 or ZrO2. This enhances catalytic activity while maintaining selectivity.
		
		※※※
		The findings showcase how subtle variations in metal-support interactions, mediated by strategic alterations in the support identity or surface chemistry, can profoundly influence the dispersion, geometry, electronic properties and stability of supported metal oxide active sites. This in turn enables targeted modulation of catalytic performance parameters such as activity, selectivity and lifespan. 
		
		An expansive perspective for future explorations is to conduct high-throughput computational and experimental screening of diverse mixed metal oxide compositions across various families of supports like zeolites, mesoporous silicas and metal oxides. This combinatorial approach spanning wide swaths of catalytic space could unveil formerly unknown synergies that spur transformational advances in propane dehydrogenation. For instance, bi- or tri-metallic sites dispersed on hierarchically porous supports with tailored acid-base characteristics might unlock unprecedented multifunctional capability. Or novel supports like metal-organic frameworks or covalent organic frameworks with rational incorporation of coordinatively unsaturated sites could stabilize Single Atom Catalysts to push selectivity limits.

	<Chinese>
		在[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]的研究中,调查了不同的氧化物载体如ZrO2、TiO2、SiO2对载有VOx的丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂的催化性能的影响。结果发现,载在ZrO2上的VOx的活性是其他载体的4倍以上。密度泛函理论计算表明,这种增强的活性源自较弱的VOx-ZrO2相互作用,这降低了桥氧原子上的电子密度,增加了其断裂丙烷中C-H键的能力。建立的原理旨在进一步调节VO活性位的微环境,以获得高活性和丙烯选择性。
		
		此外,[10.1021/acscatal.5b01679] 探讨了负载在硅质载体上的钒氧化物催化剂的金属载体界面效应。研究表明,与其他常规载体相比,由于表面硅醇基团反应性较低,二氧化硅会引起不良的三维纳米粒子形成,从而降低了分散度。添加少量Na+促进了更活跃的SiONa+锚定位点的形成,这使得二维钒酸盐的分散度与其他载体如Al2O3或ZrO2相当。这增强了催化活性,同时保持选择性。
		
		※※※
		结果展示了通过战略改变支撑体的身份或表面化学性质调节金属载体之间相互作用的微妙变化,可以显著影响支撑金属氧化物活性位点的分散度、几何形状、电子特性和稳定性。这反过来可以有针对性地调控催化性能参数,如活性、选择性和使用寿命。
		
		未来研究的广阔视角是进行高通量的计算和实验筛选,涵盖多种混合金属氧化物成分,并跨越各种载体家族,如分子筛、介孔硅和金属氧化物。这种组合方法可以揭示前所未知的协同作用,推动丙烷脱氢的转型进步。例如,分散在分层多孔载体上的二元或三元位点,具有经过调控的酸碱特性,可能解锁前所未有的多功能性。或者嵌入协同缺失位点的新型载体,比如金属有机框架或共价有机框架,可以稳定单原子催化剂,推动选择性极限。
