Most of the articles focus solely on the surface properties and compositions of various mono and bimetallic catalysts such as Pt, Pd, Ru, Rh, Cr, etc. without investigating the role of subsurface promoters. 

Only one article with the DOI [10.1021/jacs.8b08162] shows the use of subsurface Mn promoter in Pt3Mn bimetallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. The Pt3Mn subsurface significantly improved selectivity over Pt alone by altering the heats of adsorption. This highlights the distinct role of subsurface promoters in modifying surface chemical properties.

Overall, there is a paucity of research on subsurface promoter effects, presenting an opportunity for future work to explore emerging methodologies in this area.
