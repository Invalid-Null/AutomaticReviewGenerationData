Zeolites and Mesoporous Supports - What zeolite topologies like MFI, FER, BEA have shown promise as supports?

Based on the detailed analysis from the provided data, zeolite supports with MFI, BEA, and CHA topologies have demonstrated promise for propane dehydrogenation catalysts. 

MFI zeolites like silicalite-1 and ZSM-5 provide strong confinement effects to stabilize isolated metal atoms and sub-nanometer clusters, preventing sintering and loss of activity over time [10.1021/acscatal.2c05042, 10.1038/s41467-022-30522-1]. Dealuminated or hierarchical MFI zeolites offer additional mesoporosity to facilitate diffusion and access to active sites [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]. FER zeolites have not been extensively studied based on the provided data. 

BEA zeolites like dealuminated beta zeolite strongly confine Pt atoms within Zn nests, leading to high propane conversion near equilibrium and 90% propylene selectivity [10.1021/jacs.1c10261]. The BEA topology provides ideal confinement for sub-nanometer PtSn clusters, boosting stability [10.1021/acscatal.9b03527].

The CHA zeolite framework stabilizes isolated Ga+ sites upon dehydrogenation, delivering 96% propylene selectivity [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. The low acidity and small pores permit only propane activation while suppressing secondary reactions.

In summary, zeolite supports enable precise control over active site spec, oxidation state, and proximity to other ions. Their rigid frameworks prevent agglomeration yet permit reversibility of elementary steps. These characteristics manifest in excellent activity, selectivity, and stability for propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Further work should detail the role of framework composition and secondary sites.

※※※ 
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed analysis has highlighted several zeolite topologies like MFI, BEA and CHA that provide confinement and isolation of selective sites for propane dehydrogenation while suppressing over-reaction. However, steric constraints and limited mesoporosity can restrict access and diffusion. An exciting avenue for future exploration is the rational integration of zeolites with hierarchical mesoporous architectures. Templating zeolite nanosheets on mesoporous scaffolds may unlock vast densities of accessible active sites. Doping the siliceous mesopores with isolated heteroatoms could furnish auxiliary active centers cooperative with the zeolitic sites. Such kindred hybrid materials reconciling microporosity and mesoporosity could supersede conventional catalysts for propane dehydrogenation.

分子筛支撑——诸如 MFI、FER 和 BEA 等分子筛拓扑结构有哪些具有作为支撑的前景?

根据提供的数据的详细分析,具有 MFI、BEA 和 CHA 拓扑结构的分子筛已展现了作为丙烷脱氢反应催化剂支撑的前景。

如硅沸石-1 和 ZSM-5 等 MFI 型分子筛提供了强的限域效应来稳定隔离的金属原子和亚纳米颗粒,防止烧结和活性损失 [10.1021/acscatal.2c05042, 10.1038/s41467-022-30522-1]。除铝或分层的 MFI 分子筛提供额外的介孔性,促进扩散和进入活性位点 [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。根据提供的数据,没有广泛研究 FER 型分子筛。

 除铝的 BEA 型沸石(如 Beta 分子筛)可强烈限制 Pt 原子进入 Zn 巢穴中,导致丙烷转化率接近平衡转化率并达到 90% 丙烯选择性 [10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。BEA 拓扑结构为亚纳米 PtSn 簇提供了理想的限域作用,增强了稳定性 [10.1021/acscatal.9b03527]。

CHA 分子筛框架稳定了脱氢过程中的隔离 Ga+ 位点,提供 96% 的丙烯选择性 [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]。其低酸度和小孔道仅允许丙烷激活而抑制副反应。

总之,�子筛支撑可以精确控制活性位点的种类、氧化态和与其他离子的距离。它们刚性的框架可以防止团聚但允许元素步骤的可逆性。这些特性体现在丙烷脱氢反应催化剂的优异活性、选择性和稳定性上。未来的工作应该详细说明框架成分和次生位点的作用。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望:详细分析强调了几种如 MFI、BEA 和 CHA 的分子筛拓扑,它们为丙烷脱氢反应提供位点的限域和隔离,同时抑制副反应。但是,限域和有限的介孔性可能限制进入和扩散。一个令人兴奋的未来探索方向是有理性地将分子筛与分层介孔结构集成。在介孔支撑上模板化分子筛纳米片层可能释放大量可访问的活性位点。将有隔离杂原子掺杂到无定形的介孔中,可能提供协同分子筛位点的辅助活性中心。这种协调微孔性和介孔性的同类杂化材料可能会取代传统的丙烷脱氢催化剂。
