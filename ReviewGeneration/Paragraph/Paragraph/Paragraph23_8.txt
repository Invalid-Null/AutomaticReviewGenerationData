 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an important reaction for producing propylene, a key building block in the petrochemical industry. However, most PDH catalysts suffer from rapid deactivation issues, with common mechanisms being sintering, coking, and agglomeration of active sites. Strategies to improve catalyst stability have therefore garnered much interest.

One promising approach is encapsulating metal species within zeolite frameworks to prevent mobility and sintering. Examples have demonstrated how zeolite confinement offers superior stability compared to conventional supported catalysts. Co@Si-BEA with encapsulated cobalt species maintained excellent catalytic performance over 8 reaction-regeneration cycles, showing the reversible nature of coke deactivation as well as stabilization of active sites [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. Pt@UTL with sub-nm Pt clusters anchored on germanosilicate sites exhibited outstanding 4000+ hour stability for PDH, enabled by the unique Pt-O-Ge bonding [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. Encapsulated PtZn clusters in MFI zeolite suffered almost no deactivation over 220 hours under PDH conditions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816].

The exceptional stability provided by zeolites arises from restrictive diffusion and spatial constraints that prevent encapsulated metal clusters from aggregating. Recent work has combined zeolite encapsulation strategies like ligand-assisted synthesis [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] and ultrafast crystallization [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] to incorporate metal nanoclusters and sub-nm Pt species. Local zeolite composition also plays an important role. Introduction of heteroatoms like Ge provides unique anchor sites for metal binding [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7],[10.1038/s41929-023-00974-9].

Overall, current advancements demonstrate remarkable progress in zeolite-based encapsulation methods for stabilizing PDH catalysts. However, most examples remain confined to lab-scale studies over limited operating durations. Future work should address scalability and extend performance validation over months or years. Achieving commercial adoption would be a major breakthrough towards establishing encapsulated zeolite catalysts as next-generation solutions for PDH processes.

<Chinese>

丙烷脱氢(PDH)是生产丙烯的重要反应,丙烯是石化工业中的关键基础化合物。然而,大多数PDH催化剂都遭受快速失活的问题,常见的机制是活性位点的烧结、结焦和聚集。因此,改进催化剂稳定性的策略引起了很多关注。  

一种有前景的方法是在分子筛框架内包封金属种,以防止活性位点的迁移和烧结。例子表明,与常规负载型催化剂相比,分子筛限域效应提供了出色的稳定性。具有包封钴物种的Co@Si-BEA在8次反应-再生循环中保持优异的催化性能,表明可逆的结焦失活以及活性位点的稳定 [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。具有亚纳米Pt簇的Pt@UTL通过Pt-O-Ge键与硅锗分子筛位点锚定,在PDH反应中展现出众的4000小时以上稳定性[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。 MFI分子筛中包封的PtZn簇,在PDH条件下220小时几乎无失活[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。  

分子筛提供的卓越稳定性源于其限制扩散和空间约束,防止包封金属簇的聚集。 最近的工作结合了配体辅助合成[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]和超快速结晶[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]等分子筛包封策略,将金属纳米簇和亚纳米Pt种嵌入其中。局部分子筛组成也发挥关键作用。引入Ge等杂原子为金属提供独特的锚定位点[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7],[10.1038/s41929-023-00974-9]。

总体而言,当前的进展证明了分子筛基础的包封方法在稳定PDH催化剂方面取得了显着进步。但是,大多数实例仍局限于实验室规模和有限的运行时间。未来的工作应解决产业化可扩展性,并扩大表现验证的时间跨度至数月或数年。实现商业化采用,将是实现分子筛包封催化剂作为PDH工艺新一代解决方案的重大突破。
