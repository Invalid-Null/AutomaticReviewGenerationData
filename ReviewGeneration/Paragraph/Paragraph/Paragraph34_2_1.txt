The article with DOI 10.1021/cr5002436 highlights needs for improved in-situ characterization tools like time-resolved UV-vis and Raman spectroscopies to monitor industrial chromia-alumina catalysts and improve overall efficiency of propane dehydrogenation processes [1,2]. However, most other articles do not discuss needs for new in-situ characterization capabilities and rely on existing ex-situ methods.

One exception is the article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, which identifies needs for advanced in-situ tools like iDPC-STEM, in-situ spectroscopy, and 2D solid-state NMR to gain better understanding of propane dehydrogenation catalysts [3]. 

Overall, the literature focuses more on reporting experimental results rather than examining characterization needs or opportunities to apply emerging techniques. This represents an area for further investigation to elucidate catalyst functionality through operando studies.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The prevailing reliance on conventional ex-situ characterization techniques underscores prospects for pioneering in-situ and operando methodologies that can capture propane dehydrogenation catalysis in action. By tracking structural transformations, electronic modifications, and chemical reactions in real-time, cutting-edge tools like MEMS-based reactors, aberration-corrected EM, and ultrafast spectroscopy can unravel mechanistic intricacies and transient phenomena previously inaccessible. 

Elucidating fundamental pathways also creates opportunities to rationally tailor catalyst design and optimize process conditions. Furthermore, high-throughput experimentation enabled by automation and lab-on-a-chip systems may accelerate discovery and knowledge growth. An integrated approach harnessing advanced multi-modal characterization and computational modeling can provide unprecedented insights into propane dehydrogenation catalysts.
