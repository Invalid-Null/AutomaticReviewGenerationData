Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Among the investigated Pt alloy catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, Pt-Sn catalysts have been most widely studied and even commercialized. Multiple studies have demonstrated that the addition of Sn to supported Pt catalysts helps decrease the Pt particle size, improve Pt dispersion, enhance the propylene selectivity, and decrease coke formation and catalyst deactivation [Placeholder_of_DOI1, Placeholder_of_DOI2, Placeholder_of_DOI3]. Computational and experimental evidence indicates that Pt-Sn alloy formation modulates the electronic properties of Pt, weakening the C-C bond rupturing ability while maintaining C-H activation for selective dehydrogenation over cracking [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. A Sn/Pt ratio around 1-1.5 is generally considered optimal for balancing high conversion and selectivity [Placeholder_of_DOI5]. Alumina and silica supported Pt-Sn catalysts have achieved up to 40-45% propane conversion with over 98% propylene selectivity [Placeholder_of_DOI6, Placeholder_of_DOI7]. Zeolite-confined subnanometric PtSn clusters also exhibit markedly improved stability and prolong dehydrogenation lifetime [Placeholder_of_DOI8].

Besides Sn, alloying Pt with Ga or Zn has shown promising results. Encapsulated Pt-Ga intermetallic nanoalloys can suppress side reactions including coke formation and maintain 8-10% conversion for over 720 hours time-on-stream [Placeholder_of_DOI9]. Isolated PtZn4 sites stabilized by zeolites restrict the ensemble size and achieve >95% propylene selectivity over a broad 520-620°C range [Placeholder_of_DOI10]. The combination of Pt with these secondary metals alters the geometric and electronic environment of Pt active sites, tuning selectivity, activity and stability. Further development of well-defined single atom alloy catalysts presents an opportunity to optimize these modulation mechanisms. Overall, these highlighted examples demonstrate Pt alloying with Sn, Ga or Zn generates favorable geometric and electronic modifications enabling enhanced propane dehydrogenation performance.
	
※※※
	
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The investigated articles provide valuable insights into how alloying platinum with secondary metals like tin, gallium and zinc generates electronic and geometric alterations that favorably tune catalytic performance for the selective dehydrogenation of propane to propylene. As the demands grow for converting abundant alkanes to value-added olefins, these fundamental compositional-performance structure-activity learnings pave the pathway for rational design of optimized bimetallic and alloy catalysts. 

An expansive perspective reveals that beyond propane, there exist opportunities to extend such modulation mechanisms to other alkane dehydrogenations central to the chemical and energy industries. An integrated approach combining advanced spectroscopic and microscopic characterization techniques with computational modeling can offer explicit atomic-level descriptions elucidating how the secondary metal integrates with platinum and manipulates catalytic properties. 

Equally importantly, while typical synthetic protocols generate catalysts with a distribution of bi- and multi-metallic sites, recent works highlight the promise of single atom alloys with uniform isolated sites to achieve optimized selectivity, conversion and stability. Pioneering surface organometallic chemical methods that precisely dictate metal-metal coordination environments present a prospect to systematically unravel the underlying compositional and configurational principles governing tuning and optimization of Pt alloy catalysts from single atoms to nanoparticles.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢反应性能?

在调查的Pt合金催化剂中, Pt-Sn催化剂已被最广泛地研究,甚至商业化。多项研究表明,向受支撑的Pt催化剂中添加Sn有助于降低Pt粒子尺寸、改善Pt分散性、增强丙烯选择性并减少焦炭形成和催化剂失活[占位符_DOI1,占位符_DOI2,占位符_DOI3]。计算机和实验结果表明,Pt-Sn合金形成调节了Pt的电子性质,削弱了C-C键断裂的能力,同时保持C-H活化以选择性脱氢优于裂解[占位符_DOI4]。Sn/Pt比率在1-1.5之间通常被认为在高转化和选择性之间达到最佳平衡[占位符_DOI5]。受支撑的Pt-Sn催化剂实现了高达40-45%的丙烷转化率和超过98%的丙烯选择性[占位符_DOI6,占位符_DOI7]。分子筛限制的亚纳米PtSn簇也明显改善了稳定性并延长了脱氢寿命[占位符_DOI8]。

除了Sn外,Pt与Ga或Zn合金化的结果也很有希望。 封装的Pt-Ga金属间化合物纳米合金可以抑制副反应包括焦炭形成,并在720小时内保持8-10%的转化[占位符_DOI9]。 硅酸盐分子筛稳定的隔离PtZn4位点限制了集团大小,并在宽广的520-620°C范围内实现了>95%的丙烯选择性[占位符_DOI10]。 Pt与这些次要金属的组合改变了Pt活性位点的几何和电子环境,调谐选择性、活性和稳定性。进一步开发定义良好的单原子合金催化剂为优化这些调制机制提供了机会。总的来说,这些突出的例子证明了Pt与Sn、Ga或Zn合金化产生了有利的几何和电子修饰,从而实现增强的丙烷脱氢性能。

※※※

整合性理解与前瞻展望:调查的文章提供了宝贵的见解,即Pt与锡、镓和锌等次要金属合金化产生的电子和几何改变有利于调谐催化剂在丙烷选择性脱氢制丙烯中的性能。随着对将丰富的烷烃转化为高附加值烯烃的需求不断增长,这些基本组成-性能结构-活性关系的学习为合理设计优化的双金属和合金催化剂铺平了道路。  

一个广阔的视角揭示了除丙烷之外,还存在将这种调制机制扩展到其他烷烃脱氢反应上的机会,这些反应对化学和能源行业至关重要。集成的方法将先进的光谱与显微技术与计算建模相结合,可以提供明确的原子水平描述,阐明辅助金属如何与铂结合并操纵催化性质。

同样重要的是,尽管典型的合成方案生成具有不同双金属和多金属位点分布的催化剂,但最近的研究突出表明,具有均匀隔离位点的单原子合金有望实现优化的选择性、转化率和稳定性。精确控制金属-金属配位环境的先锋表面有机金属化学方法为系统地揭示调谐和优化从单原子到纳米颗粒的Pt合金催化剂的基本组成和配置原理提供了前景。
