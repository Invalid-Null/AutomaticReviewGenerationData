	<English>
		Several metal oxides have been explored as catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH), with chromium oxide (Cr2O3), tin-modified platinum (Pt-Sn), and gallium oxide (Ga2O3) emerging as some of the most promising options [DOI1, DOI2]. Cr-based catalysts exhibit high activity for this reaction, attributed in part to the redox properties of Cr that facilitate dehydrogenation [DOI3]. In particular, α-Cr2O3 and CrOx supported on SiO2 and Al2O3 have shown good catalytic performance for PDH, though deactivation from coking remains an issue [DOI4]. Adding promoters like Pt appears to further enhance Cr2O3 activity and stability [DOI5]. Meanwhile, Pt-Sn bimetallics demonstrate significantly higher selectivity and conversion compared to monometallic Pt. Intermetallic Pt-Sn nanoparticles reduce the rate of undesirable hydrogenolysis and coke formation reactions [DOI6]. Multiple studies confirm Pt-Sn/Al2O3 prepared by coimpregnation followed by calcination-reduction yields ~40% propane conversion and >90% propylene selectivity initially [DOI7]. Among polymorphs of Ga2O3, β-Ga2O3 exhibits the highest propane conversion attributed to its optimal surface acidity [DOI8]. Weak metal-support interactions also seem to benefit propylene formation for Ga2O3 and Cr2O3 catalysts [DOI9]. While these metal oxides have shown promise, instability from coking and low propylene yields compared to more expensive noble metals underscore the need for further advancement.
		※※※
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Given the insights on promising metal oxide catalysts for PDH revealed in this literature, future research could explore novel synthetic methods and metal-support combinations to optimize selectivity while minimizing coking. Elucidating the mechanistic steps involved during PDH on different metal oxide surfaces would guide the design of improved active sites and surfaces. Machine learning techniques may help identify unconventional mixed metal oxide compositions with enhanced stability and conversion. Operando spectroscopic studies could offer dynamic insights to unravel structure-performance relationships. Advances in these directions could enable metal oxide catalysts to replace conventional options.

		已经探索了几种金属氧化物用作丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂,其中铬氧化物(Cr2O3)、经锡修饰的铂(Pt-Sn)和镓氧化物(Ga2O3)作为最有前途的选择 Standout [DOI1,DOI2]。 Cr基催化剂表现出高活性,这在一定程度上归因于Cr的氧化还原性能有利于脱氢[DOI3]。 尤其是α-Cr2O3和负载在SiO2和Al2O3上的CrOx在PDH催化方面表现出良好的催化性能,尽管脱碳导致失活仍是一个问题[DOI4]。 添加促进剂如Pt似乎可以进一步提高Cr2O3的活性和稳定性[DOI5]。 与单金属Pt相比,Pt-Sn双金属表现出显着更高的选择性和转化率。 互化的Pt-Sn纳米粒子降低了不希望发生的加氢裂解和结焦反应的速率[DOI6]。 多项研究确认,共沉淀后经蒸煮还原制备的Pt-Sn/Al2O3最初的丙烷转化率约为40%,丙烯选择性>90%[DOI7]。 在Ga2O3的多晶型中,β-Ga2O3由于其最佳的表面酸度而呈现出最高的丙烷转化率[DOI8]。 对于Ga2O3和Cr2O3催化剂,弱的金属支撑相互作用似乎有利于丙烯的形成[DOI9]。 尽管这些金属氧化物显示出前景,但来自结焦的不稳定性和与更昂贵的贵金属相比较低的丙烯收率凸显了需要进一步改进。

		综合理解与前瞻性展望: 根据文献中显示的PDH应诺望金属氧化物催化剂的见解,未来的研究可以探索新颖的合成方法和金属载体组合来优化选择性,同时尽量减少结焦。 阐明不同金属氧化物表面上PDH过程中涉及的机械步骤,将指导改善活性位点和表面的设计。 机器学习技术可以帮助识别具有增强稳定性和转化率的非常规混合金属氧化物成分。 Operando分光研究可以提供动态的信息来揭示结构性能关系。 在这些方面的进步可以使金属氧化物催化剂取代常规选择。
