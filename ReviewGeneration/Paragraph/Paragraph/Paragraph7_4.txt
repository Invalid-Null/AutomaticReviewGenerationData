Based on the detailed analysis from the references, the article with DOI [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008] describes computational work that performed linear regressions between formation energies to identify the ethyl and methylidyne adsorption energies as optimal descriptors correlating catalyst properties to propane dehydrogenation performance. 

Additionally, the reference [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889] reveals a structure-activity relationship between catalyst binding strength and dehydrogenation activity, with a volcano-type trend found for activity as a function of propylene adsorption energy.

The study with DOI [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454] also utilizes degree of rate control analysis on DFT-calculated transition states to identify the rate-limiting steps in propane dehydrogenation. Relations between the energies of these steps and adsorbed species are then established to identify descriptors that correlate to activity.

Furthermore, experimental and computational work in the report [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019] shows that separating Pt atoms and increasing distance between them correlates with improved performance through weaker propylene adsorption and restrained coke formation. A Pt-In intermetallic with optimized 3.25 Angstrom Pt-Pt distance demonstrated excellent stability.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The current advancements reveal promising pathways for continued exploration of structure-activity relationships linking quantifiable catalyst descriptors to propane dehydrogenation performance. As density functional theory calculations and microkinetic modeling become more sophisticated and experimentally validated, they can further bridge molecular-level insights with bulk catalytic behavior. Additionally, high-throughput computational screening of novel single-atom and intermetallic catalysts may uncover optimized materials. On the experimental side, operando spectroscopy techniques can provide dynamic structural data to better correlate catalytic function with form.

根据参考文献的详细分析,DOI [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]的文章描述了一项计算工作,该工作通过在形成能之间进行线性回归来识别乙基和甲基类吸附能作为与丙烷脱氢性能相关的优化描述符。

此外,参考文献[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]揭示了催化剂结合强度与脱氢活性之间的结构-活性关系,并发现了丙烯吸附能的函数的活性呈现火山型趋势。

DOI [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]的研究还利用基于DFT计算的过渡态的反应速率控制分析来识别丙烷脱氢中的速率控制步骤。然后建立这些步骤的能量与吸附种之间的关系,以识别与活性相关的描述符。

此外,报告[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]中的计算和实验工作表明,分离Pt原子并增加它们之间的距离与通过削弱丙烯吸附和抑制焦炭形成来改善性能相关。 Pt-In金属间化合物具有优化的3.25安氏Pt-Pt距离,表现出优异的稳定性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:当前的进展为继续探索结构-活性关系以将可以定量的催化剂描述符与丙烷脱氢性能联系起来指明了前景广阔的道路。随着密度泛函理论计算和微动力学建模变得更加复杂和经过实验验证,它们可以进一步桥接分子水平的洞察与体相催化行为。 此外,新型单原子和金属间化合物催化剂的高通量计算筛选可能会发现优化材料。 在实验方面,原位光谱技术可以提供动态结构数据,以便更好地将催化功能与形态相关联。
