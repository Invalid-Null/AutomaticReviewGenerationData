<English>
A highly dispersed Pt-based catalyst was prepared using a simple in-situ emulsion method to decorate dendritic mesoporous silica nanoparticles (DMSN) with MnOx, thereby modifying the silica support for improved Pt cluster dispersion and stability [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.06.016]. However, most of the analyzed literature relies predominantly on ex-situ characterization techniques without examining needs for advancing in-situ tools. 

One study recognizes opportunities to employ time-resolved UV-vis and Raman spectroscopies for online monitoring of industrial chromia-alumina catalysts to enhance process efficiencies [10.1021/cr5002436]. This underscores prospects for customizing existing in-situ spectroscopic setups to gain operando insights into propane dehydrogenation reactions. Furthermore, advanced microscopy methods like iDPC-STEM coupled with in-situ cells, in addition to high-resolution solid-state NMR systems, can elucidate structural evolution of active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. 

Nevertheless, there exists scope for designing novel in-situ cells to replicate industrial conditions more precisely. This can empower capturing actual catalyst degradation pathways and coking phenomena, thereby devising informed counter-strategies. Moreover, hyphenating multiple operando techniques can produce holistic insights beyond individual capabilities. For instance, concurrently tracking spectroscopic signatures and microscopy images during reactions can connect optical responses to surface morphological changes.
</English>
