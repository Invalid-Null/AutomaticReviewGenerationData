Based on the details provided in the file, most of the articles do not specifically investigate how properties like metal particle size, shape, and support interactions affect the performance of supported metal catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. 

However, a few articles do reveal some pertinent structure-function relationships:

The article with DOI [10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7] states that Pt nanoparticle size and structure greatly influence the activity, selectivity, stability, and mechanism of propane dehydrogenation over Pt catalysts. Specifically, small Pt particles with (211)-dominating crystal planes have lower dehydrogenation energy barriers and higher activity, while large Pt particles with (111) planes have higher energy barriers. 

The article [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391] shows increasing Al2O3 ALD cycles leads to more stable Pt nanoparticles and higher propylene selectivity on Pt/Al2O3 catalysts, with optimal results after 5-7 ALD cycles giving a 6-8 Å Al2O3 coating. This demonstrates improved performance from controlled metal-support interactions.

The article [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548] investigates how properties like impregnation sequence and boron loading affect coke formation and activity of the Pt/γ-Al2O3 catalyst. Adding boron before platinum reduced coke more than after. Increasing boron loading from 0.5 wt% to 1.5 wt% decreased coke formation.

The article [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671] shows NiMo/Al2O3 with 15-20 nm NiMo alloy nanoparticles exhibits better selectivity than Pt/Al2O3 with ~2 nm Pt nanoparticles, suggesting alloy formation and optimized particle size improve selectivity.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The articles reveal metal nanoparticle size, shape, composition, and interactions with the support can strongly impact propane dehydrogenation performance. Ultra-small single Pt atoms or tiny clusters exhibit high selectivity, while appropriately sized intermetallic nanoparticles resist deactivation. Control of metal-support interactions through over-coatings or dopants inhibits sintering. Further studies could explore stabilizing additional ultra-small metal ensembles through novel synthetic methods and supports. Operando imaging techniques probing transformations at the single atom level would also advance mechanistic comprehension.

然而,一些文章确实揭示了一些相关的结构-功能关系:

DOI为[10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]的文章指出,Pt纳米颗粒的大小和结构在很大程度上影响了Pt催化剂脱氢丙烷的活性、选择性、稳定性和机理。具体来说,具有(211)优势晶面 orientingcrystal planes的小Pt颗粒具有更低的脱氢能垒和更高的活性,而具有(111)晶面优势的大Pt颗粒具有更高的能垒。

文章[10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]显示,增加Al2O3 ALD周期导致Pt纳米颗粒更稳定,Pt/Al2O3催化剂的丙烯选择性更高,在5-7个ALD周期后达到最佳结果,得到6-8 Å的Al2O3涂层。这演示了受控的金属载体交互作用改进了性能。

文章[10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]研究了浸渍顺序和硼负载等性质如何影响Pt/γ-Al2O3 催化剂的焦炭生成和活性。在铂之前添加硼比之后减少焦炭更多。硼负载从0.5 wt%增加到1.5 wt%会降低焦炭的生成。
 
文章[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]显示,具有15-20 nm NiMo合金纳米颗粒的NiMo/Al2O3催化剂比具有~2 nm Pt纳米颗粒的Pt/Al2O3 催化剂具有更好的选择性,这表明合金形成和优化的颗粒大小改善了选择性。

※※※  

综合理解与未来展望:这些文章显示,金属纳米颗粒的大小、形状、组成和与载体的相互作用会极大地影响脱氢丙烷的性能。超小的单个铂原子或细小簇状物表现出高选择性,而适当大小的金属间化合物纳米颗粒可以抵抗失活。通过涂层或掺杂控制金属与载体之间的相互作用可以抑制烧结。进一步的研究可以通过新颖的合成方法和载体探索稳定更多的超小金属配合物。同位素探针技术可以在单原子水平上检测变化,这也将推进机理理解。
