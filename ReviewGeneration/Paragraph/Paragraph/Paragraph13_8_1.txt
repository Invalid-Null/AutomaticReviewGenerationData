 <English>

The article with DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008 investigates how calcination and reduction treatments affect metal-support interactions between Pt and boron nitride (BN). It reveals that calcination causes partial encapsulation of Pt nanoparticles by boron oxide (BOx) overlayers. These overlayers block low-coordinated Pt sites and constrain the Pt particle size, which suppresses side reactions and coke formation, improving selectivity. Additionally, the BOx overlayers inhibit Pt nanoparticle sintering during periodic reaction-regeneration cycles, enhancing stability [DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008].

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631 utilizes HAADF-STEM imaging to demonstrate that ultra-small Pt species down to single atoms are stabilized on a zeolite support even after pretreatment and propane dehydrogenation reactions. EXAFS data suggests changes in Pt coordination during the reaction, indicating metal-support interactions. The ultra-small Pt species exhibit high catalytic activity for propane dehydrogenation [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]. 

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reveal opportunities to tune metal-support interactions and precisely control metal nanoparticle size and composition to optimize propane dehydrogenation performance. Future research could explore novel supports and multi-metallic combinations to isolate highly selective single sites or constrain nanoparticle ensembles. Operando spectroscopy and microscopy during propane dehydrogenation may provide additional insights into structure-function relationships and deactivation pathways. Identifying durable single-site catalysts could dramatically enhance selectivity while reducing costs.  Ultimately, progress requires analyzing real-world performance across extended pilot-scale trials.

<Chinese>

DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008的文章研究了煅烧和还原处理如何影响Pt和氮化硼之间的金属支撑体相互作用。它揭示了煅烧会导致Pt纳米粒子被氧化硼覆层部分包裹。这些覆层堵住了低配位的Pt位点,并约束了Pt颗粒的大小,抑制了副反应和焦炭形成,提高了选择性。此外,氧化硼覆层抑制了Pt纳米粒子在周期性反应-再生循环期间的烧结,增强了稳定性 [DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]。

DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631的文章利用HAADF-STEM成像表明,即使在预处理和丙烷脱氢反应后,超小Pt单原子稳定存在于分子筛支撑体上。EXAFS数据显示反应过程中Pt的配位发生了变化,这表明了金属与支撑体之间的相互作用。超小的Pt物种表现出了高的丙烷脱氢活性 [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:研究结果揭示了调控金属与支撑体间相互作用并精确控制金属纳米颗粒的大小和组成以优化丙烷脱氢性能的机会。未来的研究可以探索新型支撑体和多金属组合来隔离高选择性的单一位点或约束纳米颗粒集团。脱氢过程中原位光谱和显微技术可能提供有关结构-功能关系和失活途径的额外信息。识别耐用的单位点催化剂可能大大提高选择性同时降低成本。最终,进步需要分析扩展试验中实际应用的性能。
