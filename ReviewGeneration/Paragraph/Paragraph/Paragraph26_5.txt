	<English>
		Based on integrated differential phase contrast scanning transmission electron microscopy (iDPC-STEM) imaging, one study revealed that isolated cobalt motifs exhibiting a distorted (SiO)2Co(HOSi)2 tetrahedral configuration were homogeneously embedded into the T1(7) and T3(9) sites within the MFI framework [10.1021/jacs.2c02636]. This demonstrates advanced microscopy's ability to precisely locate metal active sites and determine their coordination environment within zeolitic supports.
		
		In another investigation, aberration corrected high-angle annular dark field scanning transmission electron microscopy (AC-HAADF-STEM) paired with energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS) mapping successfully characterized the atomic structure of intermetallic PtZn nanoparticles exhibiting alternately bright Pt columns and darker Zn columns along the [110] direction on the SiO2 support [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. The images provided insights into the highly exposed (110) facets and periodic [PtZn4] ensembles in these particles.
		
		By correlating high-resolution HAADF-STEM data with integrated differential phase contrast images, researchers identified the preferential confinement of subnanometric Pt clusters within the sinusoidal channels of purely siliceous MFI zeolites [10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4]. This technique integration enables precise determination of metal localization by simultaneously visualizing zeolite micropore architectures and heavy metal species.
		
		※※※
		
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Advanced microscopic methodologies have substantially advanced the characterization of metal-support interfaces, unraveling the coordination chemistry, spatial distributions, and interfacial interactions governing isolated sites and nanoparticles in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Moving forward, these techniques may be coupled with in situ capabilities to monitor working catalysts. Novel image analysis algorithms leveraging machine learning can also quantify observations. More broadly, these approaches could illuminate emerging research directions—like single atom catalyst design principles—with implications across chemical manufacturing.

	<Chinese>
		基于成像差分相位对比扫描透射电子显微术 (iDPC-STEM),一项研究揭示了存在于 MFI 框架 T1(7) 和 T3(9) 位点的均匀分散的、由扭曲的 (SiO)2Co(HOSi)2 四面体构成的隔离钴活性位点 [10.1021/jacs.2c02636]。这证明了先进的显微技术可以精确定位金属活性位点并确定它们在沸石支持体中的配位环境。
		
		在另一项调查中,球差修正的高角环形暗场扫描透射电子显微镜 (AC-HAADF-STEM) 与能谱 X 射线映射成功表征了沿 [110] 方向上交替的亮白铂柱和深灰锌柱的 PtZn 金属间化合物纳米粒子的原子结构 [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。这些图像提供了这些粒子中高度暴露的 (110) 面及周期性的 [PtZn4] 配合物方面的信息。  
		
		通过相关高分辨率 HAADF-STEM 数据与差分相位对比图像,研究人员确定了微米级硅质 MFI 分子筛微孔结构中亚纳米 Pt 簇的优先定位 [10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4]。这种技术整合使得通过同时可视化沸石微孔体系结构和重金属种类来精确确定金属定位成为可能。
		
		※※※
		
		整体理解与前景展望:先进的显微技术大大推进了金属-载体界面表征,揭示了隔离场所和纳米粒子中的配位化学、空间分布和界面相互作用的奥秘。展望未来,这些技术或将与原位表征能力相结合以监测工作态催化剂。此外,新的图像分析算法还可利用机器学习来定量观测结果。更广泛而言,这些方法可能会启发新兴的研究方向 —— 比如单原子催化剂的设计原则 —— 并对整个化学制造业产生影响。
