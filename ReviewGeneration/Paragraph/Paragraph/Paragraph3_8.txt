The file introduces several major catalyst classes that have been explored for propane dehydrogenation, including metal oxides, supported metals, alloys, and zeolites. 

Specific metal oxide catalysts discussed include CrOx, VOx, GaOx, ZnOx, FeOx, and ZrOx. Supported metal catalysts such as Pt, Pd, and Ni supported on materials like silica and graphene are mentioned. Alloys explored include intermetallic compounds of Pt, Pd, and Ni with promoters like Sn, Zn, Fe, In, and Ga. Zeolites like H-Beta and iron-silicate zeolites are also introduced. Commercial catalyst systems utilizing Pt, CrOx, and PtSn are described, including the Catofin, Oleflex, and Ga-CHA processes [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008][10.1021/acscatal.3c00816][10.1021/acscatal.3c00889].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The major catalyst classes studied for propane dehydrogenation are diverse, spanning metal oxides, supported metals, alloys, and zeolites. As research continues to uncover optimized combinations and formulations, there lies great potential to further improve conversion, selectivity, stability and cost-effectiveness. Areas warranting more in-depth exploration include single-atom catalysts, multifunctional composite systems, and zeolites with targeted active sites. Advancements in characterization techniques and computational modeling can also elucidate intricate structure-activity relationships to guide rational design. Overall, sustained efforts to develop and advance alternative catalysts may eventually surpass conventional options on technical and economic fronts.
