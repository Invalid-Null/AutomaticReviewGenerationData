Based on the details extracted from the files, several articles highlight remaining challenges and knowledge gaps in propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysis that need to be addressed in future research [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1, 10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030, 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724, 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, 10.1021/cr5002436]. 

A major challenge emphasized is the need for alternative PDH catalysts to replace expensive platinum-based industrial catalysts, which also suffer from thermal deactivation issues [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]. Developing non-precious metal catalysts with high efficiency, stability and propylene selectivity at lower costs remains an ongoing pursuit. In particular, the intrinsic mechanisms of how promoters and defects like oxygen vacancies affect catalytic performance needs further clarification [10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724].

Overcoming other limitations like equilibrium constraints, undesired side reactions at higher temperatures, excessive coke formation, and mass transfer limitations are also highlighted [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, 10.1021/cr5002436, 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]. Additionally, the lack of definitive spectroscopic characterization of proposed active sites such as cation pairs remains a gap [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]. 

Advanced computational screening approaches and microkinetic modeling have been suggested to help accelerate identification of active sites and optimize catalyst architectures [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]. Overall, developing fundamental insights into PDH reaction mechanisms via multi-scale characterization and modeling is still needed guide rational catalyst optimization.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the progress made but also gaps remaining, future PDH catalysis research might expand in multiple directions. Exploring alternative metals and supports beyond conventional options could uncover promising catalytic motifs. High-throughput and machine learning methods can accelerate screening and optimization. Operando spectroscopies with improving resolutions can help bridge gaps between proposed active sites and actual working structures. Advanced multi-scale modeling can provide needed mechanistic visions to guide design. Ultimately, progress will require collaborative, interdisciplinary efforts leveraging leading-edge developments across chemical engineering fields.

根据文件提取的详细信息,几篇文章强调丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂中仍存在的挑战和知识空白,需要在未来的研究中解决[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1, 10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030, 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724, 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, 10.1021/cr5002436]。

一个主要的挑战是需要替代昂贵的铂基工业催化剂,这些催化剂也存在热失活的问题[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]。开发低成本、高效率、高稳定性和高丙烯选择性的非贵金属催化剂仍然是一个长期的追求。特别是推动剂和缺陷如氧空穴如何影响催化性能的本质机理仍然需要进一步澄清 [10.1021/acscatal.1c02621, 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]。

克服其他限制如平衡约束、高温下的不良副反应、过度焦炭形成和质量转移限制也被强调 [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, 10.1021/cr5002436, 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005]。此外,对提出的活性位点如阳离子对的明确光谱表征的缺乏也是一个空白 [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]。

先进的计算筛选方法和微动力学建模已被建议用于帮助加速识别活性位点和优化催化剂体系 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331]。总的来说,通过多尺度表征和建模开发PDH反应机理的基本见解仍然是需要的,以指导催化剂的合理优化。


※※※

综合理解和未来展望:考虑到取得的进展以及保留的差距,未来丙烷脱氢催化剂研究可能会向多个方向扩展。探索传统选择之外的替代金属和载体可能会发现有前景的催化活性基团。高通量和机器学习方法可以加速筛选和优化。操作条件下分辨率不断提高的光谱技术可以帮助弥合提出的活性位点和实际工作结构之间的差距。先进的多尺度建模可以提供指导设计所需的机制愿景。最终,进展将需要利用跨化学工程学科尖端进展的协作和跨学科努力。
