Based on the details extracted from the files, various major catalyst classes have been explored for propane dehydrogenation, including metal oxides, supported metals, alloys, and zeolites. 

Specifically, metal oxides such as VOx/CrOx supported on mesoporous silicas like MCM-41 and SBA-15 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816], Ga2O3 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999], and ZrO2 [10.1021/acscatal.0c01455] have been studied. Supported metals like Pt [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008], [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331] and Mo2C [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999] have also been explored. Additionally, alloys like PtSn [10.1021/acscatal.3c00939], [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916] and intermetallic compounds [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008] have been investigated. Regarding zeolites, H-Beta [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019] and Ga-exchanged zeolites [10.1021/jacs.2c03941] have shown promise.
