 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an industrially important process for producing propylene, a key building block in the petrochemical industry. However, traditional PDH catalysts often suffer from rapid deactivation and poor stability due to factors like metal sintering and coke deposition. Recent work has explored encapsulating metal nanoparticles and clusters within the micropores and channels of zeolites as a stabilization strategy. The rigid zeolite framework helps constrain the size of metal particles during high temperature reaction conditions. This literature analysis will discuss key examples and insights demonstrating how zeolite encapsulation enhances metal catalyst stability for propane dehydrogenation.

A highly stable Pt/Ge-UTL catalyst was achieved by anchoring subnanometric Pt clusters to germanium sites in UTL-type zeolite pores [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7‏]. In situ XAFS showed Pt was strongly bonded to zeolite framework germanium in Pt-O-Ge linkages, likely preventing mobility and agglomeration. Exceptionally high stability was demonstrated under propane dehydrogenation at 550°C for over 4000 hours, outperforming common supported catalysts. Stability was attributed to the rigid zeolite structure and Pt anchoring. Similarly, encapsulating Pt and PtZn metallic clusters inside MFI and BEA type zeolites showed no deactivation or loss of active sites over 70-220 hours under working conditions [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103‏]. Compare this to conventional impregnated Pt on silica, which lost nearly all activity within 25 hours due to substantial 6 nm nanoparticle growth. Enhanced thermal stability has also been reported for Zn [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034‏] and Co [10.1021/jacs.2c02636‏] exchanged into zeolite frameworks.

The zeolite cavity size strongly influences nanoparticle stabilization. Computational studies predict certain pore topologies better confine particles below critical aggregation sizes [10.1021/jz5017202‏]. For example, ZSM-5 and Silicalite-1 possessing 3-dimensional 10-member ring channels were found optimal for stabilizing sub-1 nm Pt, Pd, and Ru clusters, verified experimentally for carbon monoxide oxidation [10.1021/jz5017202‏]. However, reactant accessibility should also be considered, as overly restricted pores may limit activity.

In summary, examples now clearly demonstrate zeolite encapsulation strategies enhance stability in propane dehydrogenation catalysts by restricting nanoparticle mobility and growth. Future work may explore how zeolite properties like composition, hydrothermal stability, and pore dimensions can be tuned to balance activity and longevity.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一项产业上重要的过程,用于生产丙烯,这是石化工业中的一个关键基础化合物。但是,传统的PDH催化剂通常由于诸如金属烧结和炭黑沉积等因素而迅速失活和稳定性差。近期的工作探索了在分子筛的微孔和通道内封装金属纳米粒子和簇作为稳定化策略。刚性的分子筛框架有助于在高温反应条件下约束金属粒子的大小。本文献分析将讨论关键的例子和信息,证明分子筛封装如何增强金属催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的稳定性。

 通过将亚纳米Pt簇锚定在UTL型分子筛孔内的锗原子上,实现了高度稳定的Pt/Ge-UTL催化剂[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7‏]。 原位XAFS表明Pt通过Pt-O-Ge连接强烈地与分子筛框架锗结合,这可能防止了运动和聚集。 在550°C下丙烷脱氢反应条件下,超过4000小时展现出异常高的稳定性,优于常见的负载型催化剂。 稳定性归因于刚性的分子筛结构和Pt锚定作用。 类似地,封装Pt和PtZn金属簇在MFI和BEA型沸石内,在工作条件下70-220小时无失活或活性位点损失[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103‏]。 相比之下,常规的硅载Pt催化剂,由于显著的6 nm纳米粒子生长,在25小时内几乎完全失活。 已经报道了Zn [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034‏]和Co [10.1021/jacs.2c02636‏]掺入到分子筛框架中的增强的热稳定性。

 分子筛的洞穴大小对纳米粒子的稳定化有很大影响。 计算研究预测某些孔径拓扑结构可以更好地约束粒子大小低于临界凝聚值[10.1021/jz5017202‏]。 例如,具有三维10元环通道的ZSM-5和硅沸石-1被发现是稳定化亚1纳米Pt、Pd和Ru簇的最佳选择,这在一氧化碳氧化反应中得到了实验验证[10.1021/jz5017202‏]。 然而,也应考虑反应物的可及性,因为过于受限的孔可能会限制活性。

 总的来说,现在的例子清楚地表明,分子筛封装策略通过限制纳米粒子的运动和生长来提高丙烷脱氢催化剂的稳定性。未来的工作可以探索如何调节分子筛的成分、水热稳定性和孔径尺寸以平衡活性和抗久性。
