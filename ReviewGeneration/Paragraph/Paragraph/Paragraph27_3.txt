Propane Dehydrogenation Catalysts - Catalyst Synthesis and Characterization: How have X-ray techniques elucidated oxidation states during catalysis?

Several studies have utilized X-ray based techniques to probe the oxidation state changes of active species during propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysis. 

Co@Si-BEA: In one study [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726], in situ X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) including XANES and EXAFS revealed isolated Co2+ sites in Co@Si-BEA catalyst remained stable without oxidation state change after the PDH reaction. 

Cr/Al2O3: XAS data [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014] showed active tetrahedral Cr(VI) species in Cr-MCM-41 were reduced to less active Cr(III) octahedral sites during initial PDH reaction stages. 

Zn/ZSM-5: XAS at the Zn K-edge [10.1021/cs200441e] elucidated the influence of preparation method on Zn speciation. No oxidation state changes occurred during testing.

PtSn/Al2O3: In situ HERFD-XANES and EXAFS [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018] revealed Sn oxidation/segregation from Pt nanoparticles during regeneration, and re-alloying after re-reduction. 

VOx/MgO: XPS data [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878] quantified V5+, V4+, and V3+ species after H2 reduction, elucidating reduction extent. XANES showed local structure similar to Mg3V2O8 monomeric surface VO4 sites.

Thus, X-ray techniques have been pivotal in unraveling metal oxidation states and coordination environments during various catalyst treatment and reaction conditions. Further explorations could focus on elucidating evolution pathways of multifunctional sites using emerging in situ/operando X-ray methodologies.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights afforded through in-situ X-ray probes of working catalysts underline these techniques' indispensability in contemporary materials chemistry inquiries. Building on recent advances, an opportunity exists to push boundaries by tracking site transformations across multiple length scales in increasingly representative environments. Efforts could focus on bridging gaps between atomic-level coordination chemistry and collective dynamics over micro- and mesoporous architectures. Multi-modal X-ray instrumentation and data science tools sensitive to subtle electronic perturbations and temporary intermediates could catalyze these endeavors. Ultimately, the goal is to transition isolated observations in model configurations toward interconnected descriptions of functional landscapes in real-world systems.

丙烷脱氢催化剂 - 催化剂合成与表征:X射线技术如何阐明催化过程中的氧化态变化?

几项研究已经利用基于X射线的技术来探测丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化过程中活性物种的氧化态变化。

Co@Si-BEA: 一项研究[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]中,原位X射线吸收光谱(XAS)包括XANES和EXAFS揭示了Co@Si-BEA催化剂中的隔离Co2+位点在PDH反应后保持稳定,没有氧化态变化。

Cr/Al2O3: XAS数据[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]显示,Cr-MCM-41中的四面体活性Cr(VI)物种在最初的PDH反应阶段被还原为较不活跃的Cr(III)八面体位点。

Zn/ZSM-5: Zn K边缘的XAS[10.1021/cs200441e]阐明了制备方法对Zn取向的影响。 在测试期间没有发生氧化态变化。

PtSn/Al2O3: 原位HERFD-XANES和EXAFS [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018]透露,在再生过程中,Pt纳米粒子从Sn氧化/分离,并在再还原后重新合金。

VOx/MgO: XPS数据[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]定量了H2还原后V5+、V4+和V3+物种,阐明了还原程度。XANES显示局部结构类似于Mg3V2O8单体表面VO4位点。

因此,X射线技术在阐明各种催化剂处理和反应条件下金属的氧化态和配位环境方面起着关键的作用。进一步的探索可以关注利用新兴的原位/操作X射线方法来阐明多功能位点的演变途径。

※※※ 

综合理解与未来展望: 通过原位X射线技术探测工作状态下催化剂的见解凸显了这些技术在当代材料化学研究中的不可或缺。 建立在最近的进展上,存在着推进技术发展的契机,通过跟踪多个尺度下微观和介观结构中位点转化,跨越现有技术界限。努力的重点可能放在弥合原子级配位化学和整体动力学之间的差距上。对微弱电子扰动和瞬态中间体敏感的多模式X射线仪器和数据科学工具可以催生这些努力。 最终,我们的目标是从模型配置下的孤立观察过渡到对实际系统中功能景观的相互关联描述。
