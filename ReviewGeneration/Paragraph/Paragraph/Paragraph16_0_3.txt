Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts: How Pt Alloying with Sn, Ga, Zn Affects Propane Dehydrogenation Performance

Several studies have evaluated how alloying platinum (Pt) with tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), or zinc (Zn) affects the catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation (PDH).

Greater stability is conferred by the ability of Sn to remove coke precursors from Pt sites. Computational studies predict and experimental trends confirm that increasing Sn content generally decreases activity but increases stability [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]. An optimal Pt:Sn ratio around 2-3:1 maintains good activity while leveraging these promotional effects [Placeholder_Of_DOI7], [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. 

Pt-Ga Alloys  

Investigations on supported Pt-Ga nanoparticles also demonstrate markedly enhanced catalytic performance over Pt catalysts for PDH [Placeholder_Of_DOI8]. The formation of the Pt-Ga alloy phase suppresses undesirable reactions leading to heightened propylene selectivity and lower deactivation rates. Controlled studies directly comparing Pt-Ga with Pt catalysts corroborate the superior selectivity and superior suppression of deactivation by coke with the Pt-Ga system [Placeholder_Of_DOI9], [Placeholder_Of_DOI10].

Pt-Zn Alloys

Encapsulating Pt atoms within protective Zn nests in the framework of zeolites or grafted on supports stabilizes isolated Pt sites and prevents sintering, enabling stable and selective PDH performance [Placeholder_Of_DOI11], [Placeholder_Of_DOI12]. The anchored Zn species allow maintaining small Pt ensembles. Interactions between Pt and Zn also modulate the valence band structure of Pt, improving selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI13]. Like Sn promotion, Zn interacts with Pt sites to foster selective C-H cleavage over C-C bond scission.

In summary, strategic incorporation of secondary metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn into Pt catalysts through alloying interactions using techniques like controlled impregnation, zeolite confinement, and grafting stabilizes small, isolated Pt sites. This subsequently enhances selectivity by blocking hydrogenolysis and oligomerization side-reactions while also conferring superior stability via attenuation of catalyst coking and sintering processes. The synergistic coupling generates bimetallic Pt alloys aptly tailored for catalyzing propane dehydrogenation with high propylene yield.

双金属和合金催化剂:Pt合金化Sn、Ga、Zn如何影响丙烷脱氢性能

几项研究评估了将铂(Pt)与锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)或锌(Zn)合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢(PDH)的催化性能。

Pt-Sn合金

多项工作表明,在Pt催化剂中添加Sn作为助剂可显著改善PDH的选择性和稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。 更高的丙烯选择性源于Sn帮助抑制氢解、裂化和焦化副反应,通过配体和组分效应改变Pt的吸附性质和电子结构[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。 Sn能够从Pt位点除去焦炭前体,因此具有更好的稳定性。 计算研究预测和实验趋势确认,Sn含量的增加通常会降低活性但提高稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。 Pt:Sn比率约为2-3:1可保持良好的活性,同时利用这些助剂效应[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]、 [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。

Pt-Ga合金

对负载Pt-Ga纳米粒子的研究也证明,与Pt催化剂相比,PDH催化性能有明显提高[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。 Pt-Ga合金相的形成抑制了不良反,导致丙烯选择性更高,脱活速率更低。 Pt-Ga系统与Pt催化剂的直接对比实验证实了其卓越的选择性和抑制焦化脱活的能力[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI10]。

Pt-Zn合金

将Pt原子封装在分子筛或载体上接枝的Zn巢中可以稳定隔离的Pt位点,防止烧结,使PDH性能稳定而选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI11]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI12]。 锚定的Zn种类使小的Pt集合体得以维持。 Pt和Zn之间的相互作用还可以调节Pt的价带结构,改善选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI13]。 与Sn助剂一样,Zn与Pt位点相互作用,促进选择性C-H键裂化而不是C-C键裂化。

总结来说,通过受控浸渍、分子筛限域和接枝等技术,将Sn、Ga和Zn等辅助金属策略性地掺入Pt催化剂中,形成合金互作用,可以稳定小的、隔离的Pt位点。 这随后通过阻断氢解和低聚反应提高了选择性,并通过减少催化剂结焦和烧结过程提高了稳定性。 协同效应生成的双金属Pt合金适合催化高丙烯收率的丙烷脱氢反应。
