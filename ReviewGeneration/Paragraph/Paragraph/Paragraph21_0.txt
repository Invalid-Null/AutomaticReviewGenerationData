 <English>

The article with DOI [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] investigates tuning zeolite Si/Al ratio by dealumination to create silanol nests in Beta zeolites, enhancing metal dispersion and stabilization.

The study with DOI [10.1021/jacs.2c03941] evaluates propane dehydrogenation over CHA zeolites with varying Si/Al ratios from 5 to 25. It shows that extra-framework Ga+ sites are formed regardless of Si/Al ratio, with propylene selectivity remaining stable at 96%. This reveals that properly tuning the Ga/Al ratio can optimize performance across different Si/Al ratios.

In another article with DOI [10.1021/acscatal.1c01497], propane dehydrogenation rates over Ga/H-ZSM-5 zeolites demonstrate sensitivity to both Si/Al and Ga/Al ratios. Two types of Ga sites with distinct activities are identified, confirming the complex interplay between composition and catalytic properties. 

Additionally, the work published in [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024] explores the impacts of TS-1 crystal size, from 100 nm to 5.7 μm. Smaller crystals introduce mesoporosity, improving diffusion and selectivity while avoiding coking. This significantly enhances catalytic performance.

※※※

The investigations into zeolite dealumination, controlled introduction of hierarchy, and meticulous compositional tuning substantiate that judicious modulation of zeolite properties can tailor activity, selectivity and stability in propane dehydrogenation. However, most of the articles focus on specific systems without broader exploration across catalyst families, support variations or process conditions. 

Future efforts might undertake more extensive high-throughput screening to map out structure-function landscapes more comprehensively. Operando spectroscopy and microkinetic modeling can also elucidate detailed reaction mechanisms and descriptors. Ultimately, research could shift towards industrial deployment by demonstrating longer-term operation, exploring sustainability implications, and targeting commercial specifications.

<Chinese>

DOI [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]的文章通过脱铝来调节Beta分子筛的Si/Al比,形成硅醇巢,提高了金属的分散度和稳定性。

DOI [10.1021/jacs.2c03941] 的研究评估了Si/Al比从5到25不等的CHA分子筛上的丙烷脱氢反应。它表明,无论Si/Al比如何,额外的Ga+位点都会形成,丙烯的选择性保持在96%左右稳定。这揭示了通过正确调节Ga/Al比可以优化不同Si/Al比的性能。

在另一篇DOI [10.1021/acscatal.1c01497]的文章中,Ga/H-ZSM-5分子筛上的丙烷脱氢反应速率显示出对Si/Al比和Ga/Al比都很敏感。确认了两种具有不同活性的Ga位点,这证实了组成与催化性能之间的复杂相互作用。


此外,发表在[10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024]上的工作探索了TS-1晶体大小的影响,从100 nm到5.7 μm。较小的晶体引入了介孔,改进了扩散和选择性,同时避免了结焦。这显著提高了催化性能。

※※※

对分子筛脱铝、层次结构的控制引入以及精心的组成调节的研究证实,谨慎调控分子筛的性质可以定制丙烷脱氢反应的活性、选择性和稳定性。然而,大多数文章都集中在特定的系统上,没有更广泛的探索不同的催化剂家族、载体变化或过程条件。

未来的努力可能会进行更广泛的高通量筛选,以更全面地描绘结构-功能关系。同位素技术和微观动力学建模也可以阐明详细的反应机理和描述子。 最终,研究可能会转向工业应用,通过演示更长期的操作、探索可持续性意义以及针对商业规格来推动。
