Here is a draft review section on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, emphasizing 'Catalyst Synthesis and Characterization - How have X-ray techniques elucidated oxidation states during catalysis?':

X-ray based techniques have provided valuable insights into oxidation state changes of active species during propane dehydrogenation catalysis. For example, XANES and EXAFS were used to show that isolated Cr(VI) species in Cr-MCM-41 catalysts are reduced to less active Cr(III) species during reaction [DOI1]. This demonstrates changes in chromium oxidation state and coordination under working conditions. In another study, in-situ XPS revealed partial reduction of TiO2 to TiOx and more lattice oxygen vacancies during H2 pretreatment of TiO2-based catalysts [DOI2]. 

Additionally, XPS has elucidated changes in other metals like Ga [DOI3], V [DOI4], and Zn [DOI5] under different preparation and pretreatment conditions. This has revealed relationships between oxidation states and catalytic activity. For instance, XANES showed Zn2O species are the active sites in ZnOx catalysts, and do not change oxidation state during reaction [DOI6]. Meanwhile, XPS showed changes in Pt, Sn, and Ce oxidation caused by steam and reduction treatments influence stability in PtSn catalysts [DOI7].

Overall, tracking metal oxidation states via X-ray techniques gives molecular-level insights into active sites, reductions, phase changes etc. during catalyst synthesis and working conditions. This can guide design of selective and stable catalysts. Further development of operando cells and data analysis methods will enable more representative studies during propane dehydrogenation.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights revealed so far utilizing X-ray techniques open doors for advanced in-situ/operando studies under industrially-relevant conditions. Emerging structural biology beamlines at upgraded synchrotron facilities could enable probing disperse metal sites and dynamic changes in working catalysts. Machine learning holds promise for accelerating analysis of complex spectral data. On the computational side, increased computing power can better integrate experiment and theory to derive predictive models. These forward-looking directions leveraging state-of-the-art X-ray science integrated with advanced data analytics and simulations can accelerate progress in designing optimized propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

基于X射线的技术为丙烷脱氢催化过程中活性物种的氧化态变化提供了有价值的见解。例如,XANES和EXAFS被用来表明Cr-MCM-41催化剂中的离子Cr(VI)物种在反应过程中被还原为较不活跃的Cr(III)物种[DOI1]。这证明了铬在工作条件下的氧化态和配位的变化。在另一项研究中,原位XPS揭示了H2预处理过程中TiO2的部分还原为TiOx和更多的晶格氧空位[DOI2]。

此外,XPS阐明了Ga [DOI3]、V [DOI4]和Zn [DOI5]等其他金属在不同的制备和预处理条件下的氧化态变化。这揭示了氧化态和催化活性之间的关系。例如,XANES表明ZnOx催化剂中的Zn2O物种是活性中心,在反应过程中氧化态不变[DOI6]。与此同时,XPS显示Pt、Sn和Ce的氧化态变化会影响PtSn催化剂在蒸汽处理和还原后的稳定性[DOI7]。

总的来说,通过X射线技术跟踪金属氧化态的变化可以在分子水平上洞察活性位点、还原、相变等在催化剂合成和工作条件下的变化,这可以指导选择性和稳定的催化剂的设计。进一步发展操作条件下的电池和数据分析方法将有助于在丙烷脱氢过程中进行更具代表性的研究��
