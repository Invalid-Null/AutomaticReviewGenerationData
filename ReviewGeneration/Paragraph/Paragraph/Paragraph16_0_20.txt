Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-based catalysts are widely studied for propane dehydrogenation due to their high activity, but monometallic Pt suffers from issues like low selectivity and stability. Alloying Pt with secondary metals has emerged as an effective strategy to tune the catalytic properties. Common promoters alloyed with Pt include post-transition metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn [Placeholder_of_DOI1].

Pt-Sn alloy catalysts are one of the most extensively explored systems for propane dehydrogenation. Multiple studies have demonstrated that incorporating Sn helps decrease the rate of catalyst deactivation via suppressed coke formation and enhanced thermal stability of Pt particles [Placeholder_of_DOI2]. The modified electronic properties of Pt sites in a Pt-Sn alloy also promote selective C-H bond activation over undesired C-C bond rupture [Placeholder_of_DOI3]. However, some reports indicate that increasing Sn content past an optimal point can lead to a trade-off between stability and activity, as Sn coordination to Pt sites raises the barrier for propane dissociative adsorption [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. Overall, a Pt:Sn ratio around 2:1 to 3:1 appears to offer a balance of activity and robust operation.

Investigations on supported Pt-Ga nanoparticles reveal significant improvements in catalyst durability along with high propylene selectivity. The geometric and electronic effects induced by Ga incorporation serve to minimize the formation of polyaromatic coke precursors. Analysis of spent Pt-Ga catalysts verifies the retention of intermetallic Pt-Ga phases that resist sintering under propane dehydrogenation conditions [Placeholder_of_DOI5]. 

Pt-Zn combination has also attracted interest, as the oxophilic nature of Zn sites enables the creation of single-atom Pt stabilized at isolated locations. This maximizes efficiency by suppressing the under-coordinated Pt ensemble sites associated withhydrogenolysis and coking side reactions. Strategies such as grafting Zn into the framework of zeolite Beta to coordinate Pt have achieved near equilibrium propane conversion at 600°C along with exceptional stability for over 60 hours time-on-stream [Placeholder_of_DOI6].

In summary, alloying Pt with promotors like Sn, Ga, and Zn has proven highly effective for enhancing catalytic performance in propane dehydrogenation. Each metal brings distinct benefits spanning electronic, geometric, and structural effects that ultimately serve to boost selectivity towards propylene and curtail catalyst deactivation pathways. Continued research to further understand the synergistic interactions between Pt and secondary metals will open up additional avenues for optimizing these industrially relevant alloy catalysts.

双金属和合金催化剂——Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

由于其高活性,Pt基催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的研究非常广泛,但单金属Pt催化剂选择性低,稳定性差。将Pt与其他过渡金属合金化已成为调控催化性能的有效策略。常用的与Pt合金化的助剂金属有Sn、Ga和Zn等后过渡金属[引用DOI1]。 

Pt-Sn合金催化剂是丙烷脱氢反应中研究最广泛的体系之一。多项研究证明引入Sn有助于抑制焦炭生成,提高Pt颗粒的热稳定性,从而降低催化剂失活速率[引用DOI2]。 Pt-Sn合金中Pt位点的改变的电子性质也有利于选择性地激活C-H键而非不希望的C-C键断裂[引用DOI3]。然而,一些报道指出当Sn含量超过最佳点时,稳定性和活性之间会存在折中,因为Sn与Pt位点的配位会增加丙烷解离吸附的活化能垒[引用DOI4]。总体而言,约Pt:Sn=2:1-3:1的原子比可实现活性和稳定性的平衡。

对载体支持Pt-Ga纳米颗粒的研究表明,Ga的引入可显着提高催化剂的稳定性,同时保持高丙烷选择性。 Ga引入导致的几何和电子效应有助于抑制多环芳烃前体的形成。花了Pt-Ga催化剂的表征验证,在丙烷脱氢条件下,金属间化合物Pt-Ga 相可以抵抗烧结[引用DOI5]。

Pt-Zn组合由于Zn位点的亲氧性质而吸引兴趣,它可以在隔离的位点稳定单原子Pt。这通过抑制与加氢解和焦炭形成相关的未配位Pt集合位点,从而最大限度地提高效率。例如,通过将Zn掺入分子筛Beta的框架以配位Pt的策略实现了600°C近平衡的丙烷转化率,并表现出了超过60小时的异常稳定性[引用DOI6]。

总之,Pt与Sn、Ga和Zn等助剂金属形成合金,可高效提高丙烷脱氢催化性能。每个金属都带来独特的电子、几何和结构效应,共同提高丙烯的选择性,抑制催化剂失效途径。持续深入理解Pt与助剂金属之间的协同作用,将为优化这些工业相关的合金催化剂开辟更多途径。
