 <English>
Based on the detailed analysis of the references provided, bimetallic and alloy catalysts comprising platinum (Pt) and post-transition metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), zinc (Zn), etc. have shown great promise for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-Sn catalysts are the most widely studied system, with commercial adoption in the Oleflex process by UOP using PtSn/Al2O3 [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Adding Sn as a promoter helps increase propene selectivity and stability by modifying Pt's electronic properties, ensemble size, and geometry [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]. An optimal Sn/Pt ratio around 1.5-2 is found before excess Sn decreases activity by blocking sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]. Detailed characterization reveals Pt-Sn phases like Pt3Sn1 and PtSn clusters are the active sites [10.1038/s41467-022-30522-1].

Investigations on supported Pt-Ga catalysts also demonstrate remarkable enhancement versus Pt, with PtGa intermetallics able to suppress side reactions and enable operation near equilibrium conversion [10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731, 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]. Meanwhile, recent work highlights emerging Pt-Zn systems that approach Pt-Sn performances. Isolated PtZn4 sites in dealuminated zeolite Beta gave exceptional activity, >95% propylene selectivity across 520-620°C [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. Overall, strategic introduction of secondary metals like Sn, Ga, Zn unlocks modulation of Pt's critical attributes - particle size, ensemble requirement, ligand environment, stability and suppresses over-hydrogenation.

Key pathways forward include:

- Systematic optimization of elemental ratios and nanoparticle sizes governed by synthetic techniques [10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X]

- Elucidation of structure-performance correlations connecting isolated sites, nanoclusters, and extended surfaces to catalytic behavior [10.1038/s41467-022-30522-1]

- Targeted catalyst-support interactions through confinements in zeolites, layered materials and rational integration of promoters [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816].

<Chinese>
基于所提供参考文献的详细分析,双金属和合金催化剂,包括铂(Pt)和锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)、锌(Zn)等过渡后金属,在丙烷脱氢(PDH)反应中显示出巨大的应用前景。

Pt-Sn催化剂是研究最广泛的体系,UOP公司商业化的Oleflex工艺采用PtSn/Al2O3 [10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。添加Sn作为助剂有助于通过调节Pt的电子性质、配位体系大小和几何形状来提高丙烯选择性和稳定性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]。在Sn/Pt比例约为1.5-2时表现最佳,过量的Sn会通过堵塞活性位点降低活性 [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。详细表征揭示Pt-Sn相如Pt3Sn1和PtSn簇是活性位点 [10.1038/s41467-022-30522-1]。

载体支持Pt-Ga催化剂的研究也证明其催化性能较Pt有明显提高,PtGa金属间化合物能抑制副反应并实现接近平衡转换量的运行 [10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731, 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]。与此同时,最新研究重点关注新兴的Pt-Zn体系,其性能可与Pt-Sn催化剂媲美。受控硅钙白脱铝β分子筛中的单一PtZn4位点表现出卓越活性,在520-620°C范围内选择性超过95% [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。总的来说,战略引入锡、镓、锌等次级金属能够调控Pt的关键属性——颗粒大小、配体体系、配体环境、稳定性,并抑制过度氢化反应。

未来的关键研究方向包括:

- 通过合成技术系统优化元素比例和纳米粒子大小 [10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X] 

- 建立单点位、纳米簇和表面结构与催化性能之间的结构-性能相关性 [10.1038/s41467-022-30522-1]

- 通过分子筛和层状材料的限域作用以及助剂的合理设计来实现定向催化剂-载体相互作用 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。
