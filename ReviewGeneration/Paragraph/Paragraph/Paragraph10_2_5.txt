Based on the details extracted from the references, several mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts.

Over CrZrOx catalysts, two neighboring coordinatively unsaturated Zr (Zrcus) sites located at an oxygen vacancy are suggested to be responsible for breaking C-H bonds and catalyzing dehydrogenation [DOI1]. The presence of Cr helps reduce the energy barrier for subsequent steps like methyl C-H bond activation. 

Isolated tricoordinated Zn2+ anchored on ZrO2 sites along with neighboring ZrO2 are also proposed to be synergistically active, with Zn2+ sites catalyzing the first dehydrogenation step and ZrO2 catalyzing the second [DOI2].  

Additionally, Mars-Van Krevelen type mechanisms have been suggested over several catalysts like VOx/ZrO2 [DOI3], MoVO mixed oxides [DOI4], and Cr2O3 [DOI5], involving lattice oxygen participation. Here lattice oxygen initiates dehydrogenation by abstracting hydrogen, leaving an oxygen vacancy that is replenished by gas phase O2.

Over gallium oxide polymorphs, a heterolytic dissociation pathway is preferred, involving dissociative propane adsorption and subsequent decomposition via proton exchange steps [DOI6]. The conjugated effect of Ga and protons can help avoid slow steps like hydrogen recombination.

Other proposed scenarios include alkyl and carbenium ion mechanisms over Ga/zeolites [DOI7], redox cycles over Cr oxides [DOI8], and pathways involving specific unsaturated metal cation (e.g. Zr, V) pairs as active sites [DOI9, DOI10].

※※※
The findings reveal multiple promising avenues for tuning active sites like coordinatively unsaturated transition metal cations and lattice oxygens to promote propane dehydrogenation. Key challenges like overoxidation and coking may be addressed through smart catalyst compositions and additives. More atomic-level studies can offer further clarity on synergies between different sites. Exploring emerging concepts like single atom catalysis may spawn innovative mechanistic directions and active site designs. Computational screening also holds immense potential to rapidly assess novel compositions and dehydrogenation pathways.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,已经为不同金属氧化物催化剂上的丙烷脱氢提出了几种机理场景。

在CrZrOx催化剂上,位于氧空位的两个相邻的无配位锆(Zrcus)位点被认为是负责断裂C-H键和催化脱氢的位点 [DOI1]。铬的存在有助于降低后续步骤(如甲基C-H键激活)的能垒。

与ZrO2相邻的锆石上锚定的三配位Zn2+孤立位点也被提出与ZrO2具有协同活性,Zn2+位点催化第一步脱氢,ZrO2催化第二步脱氢 [DOI2]。

此外,像VOx/ZrO2 [DOI3]、MoVO混合氧化物[DOI4] 和Cr2O3 [DOI5]等几种催化剂都提出了Mars-Van Krevelen类型的机理,涉及晶格氧的参与。这里晶格氧通过抽取氢原子来发起脱氢,留下一个氧空位,该空位被气相O2再补充。

在锗氧化物多形体上优先选择异裂解途径,涉及解离丙烷吸附和随后的通过质子交换步骤的分解[DOI6]。镓和质子的共轭效应可以帮助避免缓慢的步骤,如氢的重组。

其他提出的场景包括Ga/沸石上的烷基和碳正离子机理 [DOI7]、Cr氧化物的氧化还原循环[DOI8]以及涉及特定的不饱和金属阳离子(如Zr、V)对的途径作为活性位点 [DOI9, DOI10]。

※※※
结果揭示了多种有前途的途径来调控活性位点,如无配位过渡金属阳离子和晶格氧以促进丙烷脱氢。通过智能催化剂成分和添加剂可以解决过氧化和结焦等关键挑战。更多的原子水平研究可以进一步澄清不同位点之间的协同作用。探索新兴概念,如单原子催化,可能会催生创新机理方向和活性位点设计。计算筛选也具有巨大的潜力来快速评估新成分和脱氢途径。
