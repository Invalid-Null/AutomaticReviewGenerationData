- Chromium oxide (Cr2O3) is identified as one of the most promising metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation in some studies. Cr-based catalysts exhibit high activity for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation of propane [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c05286]. The reduced α-Cr2O3(0001) surface is investigated using kinetic Monte Carlo simulations and shows promise for non-oxidative propane dehydrogenation [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c01814]. Doping Cr2O3 with Pt is found to further improve catalytic performance [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c01814].

- Gallium oxide (Ga2O3), especially the β-Ga2O3 polymorph, demonstrates high propane dehydrogenation activity in a few reports. Among different Ga2O3 polymorphs, β-Ga2O3 gives the highest propylene yield which correlates with its surface acidity [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. Unsupported nanocrystalline β-Ga2O3 and γ-Ga2O3 also exhibit significant propane dehydrogenation activity [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c05009].

- Supported Pt-Sn catalysts are identified as effective propane dehydrogenation catalysts in some studies. Pt-Sn/SBA-15 synthesized by coimpregnation shows 40% propane conversion and 92% propylene selectivity [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639]. Pt-Sn supported on zeolite Beta gives 50% conversion and 99% propylene selectivity, with excellent stability [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.9b03527].
