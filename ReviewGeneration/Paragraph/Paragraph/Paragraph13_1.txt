Based on the details provided in the file, most of the articles do not investigate how properties like metal particle size, shape, and support interactions affect the performance of supported metal catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. 

A few articles provide some relevant insights. Reference [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008] shows that calcination and reduction treatments of Pt/BN catalysts cause partial encapsulation of Pt particles by BOx overlayers. This constrains the Pt particle size, suppresses side reactions, and improves selectivity. The BOx overlayers also inhibit Pt sintering during reaction-regeneration cycles.

Reference [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631] utilizes HAADF-STEM imaging to demonstrate that ultra-small Pt species down to single atoms are stabilized on a zeolite support even after pretreatment and reaction. This ultra-high Pt dispersion contributes to high propane dehydrogenation activity. EXAFS data also suggests changes in Pt coordination during reaction, indicating metal-support interactions.

Additionally, reference [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671] theoretically investigates how increasing cluster size and Sn content affect the stability and activity of supported PtxSny clusters. Results show Sn addition enhances stability, but increasing size and Sn content reduce activity by increasing barriers and destabilizing intermediates.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The few articles providing insights on structure-function relationships indicate stabilizing ultra-small, highly dispersed Pt species through metal-support interactions is crucial for achieving both high activity and selectivity. Constraining sites and ensemble sizes is also critical. However, most studies still do not thoroughly characterize or optimize key catalyst properties. Future research should focus more systematically on tuning metal nanoparticle size, composition, shape and support effects to advance the fundamental understanding and performance of propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Operando spectroscopy and microscopy techniques combined with computational modeling will likely play an important role. There are also opportunities to explore more Earth-abundant alternatives to precious metal catalysts by applying these structure-function optimization principles.

根据文件提供的详细信息,大多数文章没有研究金属颗粒大小、形状和载体相互作用等性质如何影响负载金属催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的性能。

少数几篇文章提供了一些相关的见解。参考文献[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]显示,钾的烧结和还原处理会导致Pt颗粒被BOx覆盖层部分包裹。这限制了Pt颗粒的大小,抑制了副反应,提高了选择性。 BOx覆盖层也抑制了Pt在反应-再生循环过程中的烧结。

参考文献[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]利用HAADF-STEM成像技术演示,即使在预处理和反应之后,超小的Pt单原子种也稳定地分散在分子筛载体上。这种超高 Pt分散有助于提高丙烷脱氢活性。 EXAFS数据也表明,反应过程中Pt的配位发生了变化,提示了金属与载体之间的相互作用。

此外,参考文献[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]从理论上研究了群簇大小和Sn含量的增加如何影响PtxSny簇在载体上的稳定性和活性。结果表明,Sn的添加可以增强稳定性,但增加大小和Sn含量通过增加壁垒和使中间体不稳定来降低活性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:少数几篇提供结构-功能关系见解的文章表明,通过金属与载体之间的相互作用来稳定超小且高度分散的Pt物种对于实现高活性和选择性都是至关重要的。限制活性位和配体环境也很关键。然而,大多数研究仍然没有彻底表征或优化关键的催化剂性能。未来的研究应更加系统地调优金属纳米颗粒的大小、组成、形状和载体效应,以推进丙烷脱氢催化剂的基本理解和性能。操作谱学和显微技术结合计算建模可能会发挥重要作用。还有机会通过应用这些结构-功能优化原则来探索更丰富的非贵金属催化剂替代品。
