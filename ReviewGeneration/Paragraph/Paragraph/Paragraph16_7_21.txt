Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-Sn Catalysts
Studies have shown that alloying Pt with Sn in supported Pt-Sn catalysts helps improve selectivity to propylene and stability by inhibiting coke formation in propane dehydrogenation (PDH)[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. The promotional effects have been attributed to both electronic and ensemble effects, where Sn helps decrease the number and size of multiple Pt atom adsorption sites and increases electron density at Pt through Pt-Sn bond formation [\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. Adding Sn lowers propane activation barriers but increases propylene desorption barriers, improving propylene selectivity [\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. DFT studies suggest optimal Sn/Pt ratios around 1:2 to 1:3 for maximizing this stability-selectivity enhancement [\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. The industrial Oleflex process by UOP uses Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalysts.

Pt-Ga Catalysts  
Alloying Pt with Ga has also been shown to greatly enhance selectivity and stability versus Pt catalysts alone in propane dehydrogenation [\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. A key role is attributed to Ga helping anchor and disperse Pt sites while blocking unselective multiple Pt sites. Studies find supported Pt-Ga catalysts achieve very high propylene selectivities >95% across a range of temperatures, close to equilibrium conversion, along with low deactivation rates [\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. The structure-performance relationship depends significantly on the synthetic method and Pt:Ga ratio.

Pt-Zn Catalysts
Several studies have explored Pt-Zn catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, showing Zn helps confine and stabilize isolated Pt sites [\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. PtZn intermetallic clusters supported on hierarchical zeolites or mesoporous materials achieve relatively high activity and selectivity. Dealuminated zeolite Beta with isolated PtZn4-6 sites exhibited exceptional stability - 30 days on stream with <2% deactivation[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]. The Zn isolates Pt sites while also modulating valence orbital interactions. An optimal Zn/Pt ratio > 4 maintains performance.

In summary, alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn has been clearly demonstrated by numerous studies to enable significant improvements in propane dehydrogenation performance - enhancing selectivity, stability and lifetime through electronic, ensemble and anchoring effects with the Pt active sites.

※※※

Taking into account the advancements around tuning bimetallic and intermetallic Pt alloy catalyst compositions and synthetic methods to optimize propane dehydrogenation performance, there are further fruitful research directions that may be explored. One prospect could be expanding the toolbox of secondary metals beyond Sn, Ga and Zn that have been extensively studied. Other plentiful elements like In, Fe, Ni may offer unique promotion pathways. Additionally, ternary or higher order multi-metallic combinations could enable further fine-tuning of catalytic properties. 

Another opportunity is to leverage the insights around Pt-alloy sites stabilized on tailored supports like hierarchical zeolites to break through intrinsic material constraints. Creating specialized supports, potentially utilizing additive manufacturing techniques, that overcome mass transfer limitations and precisely tune site isolation could drive step-change improvements. Integrating such exceptional catalysts into optimized reactor configurations also offers possibilities for commercial advancement. There remain rich possibilities for innovations in bimetallic propane dehydrogenation catalysts and processes.

双金属和合金催化剂——Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

Pt-Sn 催化剂
研究表明,在支撑的Pt-Sn催化剂中,Pt与Sn的合金化有助于提高丙烯的选择性和稳定性,通过抑制丙烷脱氢(PDH)过程中的焦炭形成[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。推广效应被归因于电子效应和集合效应,Sn有助于减少Pt多原子吸附位点的数量和大小,并通过Pt-Sn键的形成增加Pt的电子密度[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。添加Sn降低丙烷的活化能垒,但增加丙烯脱附的能垒,提高丙烯的选择性[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。 DFT研究表明Sn/Pt比率最佳在1:2到1:3之间,以最大限度地提高这种稳定性-选择性的增强效果[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。UOP公司的Oleflex工艺使用Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂。

Pt-Ga 催化剂
将Pt与Ga合金化也被证明可以极大地提高选择性和稳定性,相比仅Pt催化剂,在丙烷脱氢中[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。Ga的一个关键作用是帮助锚定和扩散Pt活性位点,同时阻断非选择性的多个Pt位点。研究结果表明,支撑的Pt-Ga催化剂可以在广泛的温度范围内实现非常高的丙烯选择性>95%,接近平衡转换,以及低的失活率[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。结构-性能关系在很大程度上取决于合成方法和Pt:Ga比。

Pt-Zn 催化剂  
几项研究探索了丙烷脱氢的Pt-Zn催化剂,表明Zn有助于限制和稳定隔离的Pt位点[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。载有在分层沸石或介孔材料上的PtZn金属间化合物簇体可以实现相对较高的活性和选择性。除铝Beta沸石与隔离的PtZn4-6位点表现出异常的稳定性——Online 30天的失活率<2%[\u0432\u0441\u0435\u0440\u0435\u0434\u0438\u043d\u0435]。 Zn隔离Pt位点的同时也调节价轨道相互作用。 Zn/Pt比>4可以保持性能。

总之,通过无数研究已经清楚地证明,使用Sn、Ga和Zn等促进剂合金化Pt可以显著改善丙烷脱氢性能——通过电子、集合和锚定效应提高Pt活性位点的选择性、稳定性和使用寿命。

※※※

考虑到调优双金属和金属间化合物Pt合金催化剂组成和合成方法以优化丙烷脱氢性能的进展,还有一些有前景的研究方向值得探索。一个前景是拓展二次金属的工具箱,Sn、Ga和Zn等已有大量研究。其他丰富的元素如In、Fe、Ni可以提供独特的促进途径。 此外,三元或更高级的多金属组合可以实现性能的进一步微调。

另一个机会是利用形成稳定在定制支撑体(如分层沸石)上的Pt-合金位点的见解,突破固有的材料限制。 创造专门的支撑体,可能利用增材制造技术,克服传质限制并精确调节位点隔离,可以取得突破性改进。 将这样异常的催化剂集成到优化的反应器配置中也提供了商业进步的可能性。 双金属丙烷脱氢催化剂和工艺还有丰富的创新可能。
