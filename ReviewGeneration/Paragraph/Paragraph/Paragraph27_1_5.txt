 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts play a vital role in the conversion of propane to propylene. Gaining insights into the structural transformations of active sites during reaction is crucial to advancing catalysis design. In this pursuit, X-ray based techniques have elucidated pivotal oxidation state changes of metal species during key steps of PDH catalysis.

Specifically, in situ XANES and EXAFS analyses have delineated dynamic changes in the coordination environment, geometry, and oxidation state of crucial promoter atoms like Cr, Ga, Zn, and V under working conditions [[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]]. Thesemeasurements unravel the nature of site speciation from time-resolved data acquired over redox processes. For instance, XAS tracking has captured the reduction of isolated Cr(VI) sites in tetrahedral coordination to less active Cr(III) species in octahedral geometry during initial PDH reaction stages [[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]]. Analogously, quantitative pulse titration coupled with XANES has identified the pivotal divalent Ga2O2^2+ species as precursors to highly active dehydrogenation sites [[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]]. Elucidation of such transient multivalent configurations and structures grants opportunities to strategize site compositions and stabilities. 

Expanding beyond geometric attributes, XPS allows semi-quantitative assessments of relative fractions of different metal oxidation states in the catalyst [[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]], [[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]]. Peak fitting analyses provide insights into the ratios of lower or partially oxidized metal species favorable for C-H activation. Concurrently, shifts in binding energies verify critical electron transfers and alloy formations between combinations of Pt, Sn, Ga, and In during pretreatment steps[[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]]. These insights direct modulation of charge densities over target domains.

※※※

The intricate understandings of transient site structures and oxidation states under working conditions can now be leveraged to design superior next-generation catalysts. Strategic adjustments in bimetallic configurations, type of support interaction, steric effects, and stabilizing ligands/promoters might help lock desired multivalent states. Moreover, explorations at realistic industrially-mimicking environments using elevated temperatures and pressures can reveal additional facets overseen thus far. Custom reaction cells that allow introduction of various feedstocks can also track influences of process conditions. In parallel, computational studies and DFT-level simulations may validate experimental deductions besides predicting hitherto unidentified transient phenomena. Overall, the insights from X-ray techniques establish robust foundations primed for creating upgraded catalysts.

丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂在丙烷转化为丙烯中的作用至关重要。深入理解活性位点在反应过程中的结构转变,对于推进催化剂设计至关重要。在这方面,X射线技术已经阐明了金属物种在PDH催化关键步骤中的氧化态变化。   

具体来说,原位XANES和EXAFS分析已经详细描绘了Cr、Ga、Zn和V等关键促进剂原子在工作条件下的配位环境、几何形状和氧化态的动态变化[[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]]。这些测量从原位跟踪氧化还原过程获得的时间分辨数据中揭示了位点化学计量的本质。例如,XAS跟踪记录了初始PDH反应阶段四面体配位的 Cr(VI) 孤立位点向八面体几何形状的较不活跃Cr(III) 物种的还原 [[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]]。类似地,定量脉冲滴定结合 XANES 确定了高活性脱氢位点的关键二价 Ga2O2^2+ 前体[[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]]。这种瞬态多价配置和结构的阐明为策略性地控制位点组成和稳定性提供了机会。  

超越几何属性,XPS 允许催化剂中不同金属氧化态的相对含量的半定量评估[[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]], [[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]]。峰分解分析为 C-H 活化优选的较低或部分氧化态金属物种的比率提供了信息。同时,结合能的位移验证了Pt、Sn、Ga 和 In 之间在预处理步骤中的关键电子转移和合金形成[[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]]。这些信息指导目标区域上的电荷密度调控。

※※※  

对瞬态位点结构和工作条件下的氧化态的精细理解现在可以被利用,来设计优异的下一代催化剂。策略性地调节双金属配置、载体相互作用的类型、位阻效应和稳定配体/促进剂等都可能有助于锁定所需的多价态。此外,在较高的温度和压力下模拟实际工业环境的探索可能会揭示到目前为止被忽视的附加面。允许导入各种进料的定制反应池也可以跟踪过程条件的影响。与此同时,计算研究和 DFT 级模拟可以验证实验演绎以外预测未确定的瞬态现象。总的来说,X 射线技术的信息为打造升级版催化剂奠定了坚实的基础。
