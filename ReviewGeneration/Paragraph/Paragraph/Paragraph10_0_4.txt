Based on the details extracted from the references, several mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts. 

On Cr/ZrO2 catalysts, two neighboring coordinatively unsaturated Zr (Zrcus) sites located at an oxygen vacancy are concluded to be responsible for breaking C-H bonds in propane homolytically [DOI1]. Isolated Cr3+ sites with coordination vacancy are also suggested as active sites [DOI2]. 

For Zn/ZrO2 catalysts, DFT calculations reveal isolated tricoordinated Zn2+ and Zrcus sites showing synergy - Zn2+ sites catalyze the first dehydrogenation step and Zrcus sites catalyze the second step [DOI3].

On Ga/ZSM-5, monovalent Ga+ sites are proposed to activate propane via a Ga-(C3H7) intermediate. An alternative pathway is described over oxidized GaO+ species involving a pi-complex [DOI4]. 

Several articles describe Mars-van Krevelen type mechanisms, where lattice oxygen initiates dehydrogenation over sites like VOx or MoVO, creating oxygen vacancies that get replenished by gas phase O2 [DOI5][DOI6].

CO2-assisted pathways are also proposed, where CO2 helps re-oxidize reduced metal sites, providing a soft oxidant compared to O2 [DOI7].

Additionally, heterolytic dissociation routes are suggested over Ga oxides, with dissociative propane adsorption forming gallium hydride and alkoxide species [DOI8].

※※※

The findings reveal multiple viable mechanistic routes for propane dehydrogenation over metal oxides, with redox, acid-base, and bifunctional pathways possible depending on the catalyst composition and structure. Key knowledge gaps relate to definitively confirming proposed mechanisms and clarifying structure-activity relationships to guide rational catalyst design. Future research could focus on operando spectroscopy under industrially relevant conditions to capture transient intermediates, combined with isotopic labeling and DFT calculations to probe hypothesized pathways. Elucidating fundamental mechanisms will inform strategies to optimize catalysts balancing activity, selectivity and stability.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,已经为不同金属氧化物催化剂上的丙烷脱氢提出了几种机理场景。

在Cr/ZrO2催化剂上,结论是位于氧空位的两个相邻的无配位Zr (Zrcus)位负责同源裂解丙烷中的C-H键[DOI1]。也有数据表明,具有配位空位的隔离Cr3+位也是活性位点[DOI2]。

对于Zn/ZrO2催化剂,DFT计算揭示了协同效应的三配位Zn2+位和Zrcus位——Zn2+位催化第一步脱氢,而Zrcus位催化第二步[DOI3]。

在Ga/ZSM-5上,单价Ga+位通过Ga-(C3H7)中间体激活丙烷被提出。 氧化的GaO+物种通过π复合物参与另一条途径[DOI4]。

几篇文章描述了类Mars-van Krevelen机理,其中晶格氧通过诸如VOx或MoVO等位点启动脱氢,产生氧空位,这些空位随后被气相O2复原[DOI5] [DOI6]。

还提出了CO2助燃途径,其中CO2帮助再氧化还原的金属位点,提供比O2更温和的氧化剂[DOI7]。

此外,在Ga 氧化物上还提出了杂裂路线,其中解离的丙烷吸附形成镓水合物和碱化物物种[DOI8]。

※※※  

结果揭示了金属氧化物上存在多种可行的丙烷脱氢机理途径,根据催化剂的组成和结构,氧化还原、酸碱和双功能途径都是可能的。关键的知识差距与明确地证实所提出的机制和澄清结构-活性关系以指导合理的催化剂设计有关。未来的研究可以考虑在工业相关条件下的同位素标记和DFT计算,以捕捉瞬态中间体,从而证实假设的途径。阐明基本机制将为优化平衡活性、选择性和稳定性的催化剂提供策略。
