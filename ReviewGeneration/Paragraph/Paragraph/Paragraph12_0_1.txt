 <English>

Supported mono-metallic platinum (Pt) catalysts have been widely explored for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to Pt's superior ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing undesired C-C bond cleavage [1]. Several studies have provided data and insights on Pt-based catalysts supported on various carriers like alumina, silica, and zeolites [2-4].

A study by Khan et al. [5] evaluated sulfur-modified supported Pt/alumina catalysts. Adding sulfur during preparation or in the gas phase was found to improve selectivity to propene, especially through pretreatment at 873 K. Sulfur addition in the preparation stage was more effective than during reaction.

Böhme et al. [6] compared propane dehydrogenation activity of Pt/SBA-15 catalysts prepared by wet impregnation (Pt/SBA-15_WI) and deposition-precipitation (Pt/SBA-15_DP). The deposition-precipitation catalyst showed higher activity, attributing to increased metal dispersion.

Several studies have explored using overcoat layers on supported Pt catalysts to prevent agglomeration and coking. Ruddy et al. [7] applied atomic layer deposition (ALD) Al2O3 and TiO2 layers throughout extruded Pt/Al2O3 catalysts. The overcoat layers increased propane conversion yield and selectivity over 12 hours on-stream.

Beyond monometallic Pt, some studies have evaluated Pd, Rh, and Ir, but note they remain expensive options [8]. Nonetheless, Liu et al. [9] developed an active and selective supported RhCu single atom alloy catalyst, demonstrating superior performance to a traditional Pt/Al2O3 catalyst.

In summary, Pt-based catalysts, especially with promoters and overcoat layers, have shown promise for commercial PDH processes due to Pt’s intrinsic properties. But further work is needed to optimize stability while minimizing costs.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The articles provide valuable insights on supported metal catalyst design for PDH. Key factors influencing activity and stability include metal type, particle size, support interaction, and use of promoters/overcoats. However, developing optimal supported metal catalysts that balance high selectivity, resistance to deactivation, and process economics remains an open challenge. Future research could explore more cost-effective metals, tailored support materials, and novel promoters to unlock the full potential of supported metal catalysts for commercial PDH technology.

<Chinese>

基于Pt优异的C-H键活化和最小化C-C键断裂的能力[1],人们广泛探索了负载单组分铂(Pt)催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。 几项研究提供了负载在氧化铝、硅酸和分子筛等载体上的Pt基催化剂的数据和见解[2-4]。

Khan等[5]评价了含硫改性的负载Pt/氧化铝催化剂。 结果发现,无论是在制备阶段还是在气相中添加硫,都可以提高丙烯的选择性,特别是通过873 K下的预处理。制备阶段添加硫比反应期间添加更有效。

Böhme等[6] 比较了浸渍(Pt/SBA-15_WI)和沉淀淀积法(Pt/SBA-15_DP)制备的Pt/SBA-15催化剂的丙烷脱氢活性。沉淀淀积法催化剂表现出更高的活性,这归因于金属分散度的增加。

几项研究探索了对负载Pt催化剂使用覆层来防止团聚和积炭。 Ruddy等[7] 在挤压Pt/ Al2O3催化剂上应用了氧化铝和二氧化钛的原子层沉积(ALD)覆层。 结果表明,这些覆层可以在流经12小时后提高丙烷转化率和选择性。

除单组分Pt之外,一些研究还评价了Pd、Rh和Ir,但指出它们仍然是昂贵的选择[8]。 尽管如此,Liu等[9] 开发了一种活性高、选择性好的负载RhCu单原子合金催化剂,展示了明显优于传统Pt/ Al2O3催化剂的性能。

总体而言,Pt基催化剂(特别是使用助剂和覆层)由于Pt的固有属性,在商业PDH工艺中展示了前景。 但需要进一步的工作来在优化稳定性的同时最大限度地降低成本。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 这些文章为PDH负载金属催化剂的设计提供了有价值的洞察。 影响活性和稳定性的关键因素包括金属类型、颗粒大小、载体相互作用和助剂/覆层的使用。 然而,开发权衡高选择性、抗失活性和工艺经济性的优化负载金属催化剂仍然是一个悬而未决的挑战。 未来的研究可以探索更经济高效的金属、定制的载体材料和新型助剂,以释放负载金属催化剂在商业 PDH 工艺中的全部潜力。
