For cobalt catalysts [10.1038/s41467-022-35698-0], XANES and EXAFS data showed evolution of tetrahedral Co(II) sites during direct H2 reduction. On platinum catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.1c02553], XANES revealed maintenance of Pt0 state during repeated propane dehydrogenation cycles. 

X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) also offers valuable insights. As demonstrated in references [10.1021/acscatal.3c00818] and [10.1021/acscatal.1c03146], XPS can quantify changes in oxidation states like partial reduction of Ti4+ to Ti3+ under H2. For bimetallic PtSn catalysts [10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X], XPS coupled with XANES tracked alloying between Pt and Sn during pretreatment.

Overall, synchrotron X-ray based techniques enable operando characterization of working catalysts, granting a detailed perspective into metal oxidation states and coordination environments during pivotal preparation and testing steps.
