	<English>
		The study by Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548] evaluated three representative silica supports - amorphous SiO2, dealuminated zeolite Beta, and purely siliceous MFI zeolite - for anchoring PtSn species for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. Using zeolite Beta instead of amorphous silica effectively decreased the PtSn particle size from 1.6 nm to 0.9 nm, forming PtSn nanoclusters in PtSn/Beta-600H2. Moreover, subnanometer PtSn clusters of 0.5-0.6 nm were visualized inside the MFI zeolite channels.

		Furthermore, Liu et al. [10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.084] explored the advantages of Sn-Beta zeolite for PDH, attributed to the open 3D pore system of 12-membered rings, with applications in transfer hydrogenation and isomerization reactions. The group had previously applied Sn-Beta for PDH as well [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019], where different Sn-Beta variants exhibited good catalytic performance. Especially, Na-Sn-Beta with higher Lewis acid sites demonstrated superior activity and stability. 

		In another investigation by Zhang et al. [10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4], PtLa nanoparticles were anchored on dealuminated MFI zeolite (PtLa/mz-deGa), achieving ~40% propane conversion initially and exceptional 30-day stability. Recent successes of PtZn and PtSn catalysts on zeolites for efficient propane conversion were also highlighted.

		Overall, zeolites are versatile supports owing to properties like high surface areas, thermal resilience, tunable acidity, and crystalline microporosity. Variants like Sn-Beta, hierarchical and dealuminated MFI and BEA facilitate controlled metal dispersion, confinement effects, and optimized PDH performance. 

		※※※
		Zeolite-assisted propane dehydrogenation catalysts have witnessed remarkable progress, yet broader challenges persist regarding kinetic and thermodynamic limitations. Several groups have pushed reaction conditions towards equilibrium conversion using strategies like tandem reactors [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. Additionally, host-guest interactions in metal-zeolite frameworks present avenues for increased stabilization against sintering and coking. Elucidation of bimetallic motif-zeolite synergies through operando spectroscopy could inform rational improvements as well. Extending these design principles to emerging zeolitic architectures besides MFI and BEA remains an open prospect. Systematically harnessing shape selectivity and tailored active site isolation through zeolite engineering might engender breakthroughs in commercially relevant PDH catalysis.

	<Chinese>
		Wang等人的研究[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]评估了三种代表性的二氧化硅载体——无定形SiO2、脱铝Beta分子筛和纯硅氧化物MFI分子筛,用于锚定PtSn物种以用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂。使用Beta沸石而不是无定形硅可以有效地将PtSn粒子大小从1.6 nm降低到0.9 nm,在PtSn/Beta-600H2样本中形成PtSn双金属纳米簇。此外,在MFI分子筛通道内可视化了0.5-0.6 nm的亚纳米PtSn簇。
		
		此外,Liu等[10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.084]探索了Sn-Beta沸石用于PDH的优势,这是由于其开放的3D 12员环孔隙结构在转移氢化和异构化反应中的应用。该研究小组此前也曾将Sn-Beta应用于PDH [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019],其中不同的Sn-Beta变体表现出良好的催化性能。尤其是Lewis酸性位更高的Na-Sn-Beta表现出优异的活性和稳定性。
		
		在Zhang等的另一项研究[10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]中,PtLa纳米颗粒锚定在脱铝MFI沸石(PtLa/mz-deGa)上,最初的丙烷转化率约为40%,30天内稳定性异常。 PtZn和PtSn催化剂也有效地将丙烷转化成丙烯。
		
		总体而言,分子筛是多功能的载体,具有高比表面积、热稳定性、可调酸性和有序微孔结构等特性。 Sn-Beta、分级和脱铝MFI及BEA等变体有助于控制金属分散、局限效应和PDH性能的优化。
		
		※※※
		分子筛辅助的丙烷脱氢催化剂取得了显着进展,但更广泛的挑战仍在动力学和热力学限制方面存在。 几个小组利用串联反应器等策略[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]将反应条件推向平衡转化。 此外,金属-分子筛框架中的主客体相互作用为抵御烧结和积碳提供了途径。 通过在位光谱分析金属双组分-分子筛协同效应可以用于理性改进。 将这些设计原理扩展到除MFI和BEA以外的新兴分子筛结构仍然是一个开放的前景。 系统地利用分子筛工程实现形状选择性和定制的活性位点隔离,可能会在商业相关的PDH催化方面取得突破。
