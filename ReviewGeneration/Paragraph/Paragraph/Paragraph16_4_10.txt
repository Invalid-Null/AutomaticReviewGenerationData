Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Several studies have evaluated the effect of alloying platinum with secondary metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), and zinc (Zn) for improving the performance of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. 

Pt-Sn alloys
Adding Sn as a promoter to Pt PDH catalysts helps increase propylene selectivity and stability compared to monometallic Pt catalysts. One study on PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts found 40% higher propane conversion and improved propene selectivity over the catalyst lifetime [Placeholder_of_DOI1]. The promotional effects have been attributed to Sn altering the electronic properties of Pt active sites, leading to selective C-H bond activation over C-C bond rupture [Placeholder_of_DOI2]. Sn also helps remove coke precursors, minimizing deactivation [Placeholder_of_DOI3]. However, some reports indicate excessive Sn can block active sites and reduce activity [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. The optimal Sn/Pt ratio varies from 1-3 across different studies. 

Pt-Ga alloys  
 Studies on Pt-Ga intermetallic alloy catalysts like Pt3Ga/Al2O3 report remarkable propene selectivity (>95%) and stability, though lower activity than PtSn initially [Placeholder_of_DOI5]. The specific interaction between Pt and Ga atoms helps suppress side reactions and coke formation responsible for deactivation [Placeholder_of_DOI6].

Pt-Zn alloys
For Pt-Zn alloys, encapsulating PtZn clusters within protective zeolite frameworks yields exceptional stability. A PtZn@S-1-Fin catalyst maintained 17 molC3H6/molPt/s productivity over 150 hours, showing little deactivation [Placeholder_of_DOI7]. The Zn environment isolates Pt sites, while still allowing selective dehydrogenation. A Pt/Zn atomic ratio >4 gives optimal stability [Placeholder_of_DOI8].

In summary, alloying modulates the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites to tune catalytic performance. Adding Sn mainly improves stability, Ga enhances selectivity, and Zn offers site-isolation. However, excessive secondary metal can also block active sites and inhibit activity. The optimal ratio depends on the specific catalyst composition and support interaction.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢反应性能?

几项研究评估了将铂与锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn)等次要金属合金化,以改善丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂的性能。

Pt-Sn合金
将Sn作为助剂添加到Pt PDH催化剂中有助于提高丙烯的选择性和稳定性,相比于单金属Pt催化剂。一项关于PtSn/Al2O3催化剂的研究发现,与Pt/Al2O3催化剂相比,丙烷转化率提高40%,丙烯选择性在催化剂的整个使用寿命中得到改善[占位符_of_DOI1]。推广作用被归因于Sn改变了Pt活性位点的电子性质,导致选择性C-H键断裂而不是C-C键断裂 [占位符_of_DOI2]。Sn还有助于去除焦炭前体,最大限度地减少脱活[占位符_of_DOI3]。但是,一些报告指出过量的Sn会堵塞活性位点并降低活性[占位符_of_DOI4]。不同研究中的最佳Sn/Pt比例在1-3之间变化。

Pt-Ga合金 
关于Pt3Ga/Al2O3等Pt-Ga中间金属合金催化剂的研究报告了显着的丙烯选择性(>95%)和稳定性,尽管活性低于PtSn。 Pt和Ga原子之间的特定相互作用有助于抑制导致失活的副反应和焦炭形成[占位符_of_DOI5]。

Pt-Zn合金
对于Pt-Zn合金,将PtZn簇封装于保护性分子筛框架内使其稳定性异常。 PtZn@S-1-Fin 催化剂在150小时内保持17 molC3H6/molPt/s 的产品率,脱活很小[占位符_of_DOI7]。 Zn环境隔离了Pt位点,同时仍允许选择性脱氢。 Pt/Zn原子比>4时给出最佳稳定性[占位符_of_DOI8]。

总之,合金化调节了Pt活性位点的几何和电子性能以优化催化性能。添加Sn主要改善稳定性,Ga增强选择性,Zn提供位点隔离。但是,过量的次要金属也可以堵塞活性位点并抑制活性。 最佳比率取决于具体的催化剂组成和载体相互作用。
