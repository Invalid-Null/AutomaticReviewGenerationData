Several studies have explored supported mono-metallic platinum (Pt), palladium (Pd), and iridium (Ir) catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be the most widely used commercially for this reaction [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. Experiments on an alumina-supported Pt catalyst showed maintaining high activity over multiple cycles before deactivating due to Pt agglomeration accelerated by sulfur [Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. Adding promoters such as boron [Placeholder_Of_DOI3] and metal oxide overcoats [Placeholder_Of_DOI4] are shown to enhance Pt catalyst stability and selectivity. TEM imaging confirmed small Pt clusters around 1.3 nm dispersed on Al2O3 supports [Placeholder_Of_DOI5].

Besides Pt, a supported Rh-Cu single atom alloy (SAA) catalyst demonstrated superior propane dehydrogenation activity and 100% propene selectivity compared to a Pt/Al2O3 benchmark [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]. For Ru, monometallic nanoparticles had very poor 4.5% selectivity, significantly below Pt and optimized Ru phosphide nanoparticles [Placeholder_Of_DOI7].

Overall, Pt-based catalysts, especially with promoters and overcoats, appear most promising for selective and stable propane dehydrogenation among the supported metals surveyed. Further mechanistic studies are still needed to optimize these catalysts and mitigate deactivation over long-term operation.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings showcase Pt as an active metal for propane dehydrogenation, but stability remains a key challenge, motivating recent innovations with promoters and overcoats. Beyond Pt, opportunities exist to explore single atom alloy configurations to potentially surpass Pt. Broader trends point to interplay between metals and supports modulating performance, warranting systematic investigations spanning metals, supports, and promoters. Advanced operando spectroscopies paired with kinetic and computational modeling will likely play an integral role revealing degradation pathways and guiding optimization.
