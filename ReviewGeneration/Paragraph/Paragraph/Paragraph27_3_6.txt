Based on the details extracted from the file, propane dehydrogenation catalysts have been extensively studied using X-ray based techniques to probe oxidation state changes during catalysis. 

Specifically, X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) including X-ray absorption near edge structure (XANES) and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) have been widely utilized. As demonstrated in the quotes from reference [DOI: 10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726], these techniques can reveal how coordination environments and oxidation states of active metal species like Co2+ evolve from fresh catalysts to spent catalysts after propane dehydrogenation reactions. The data confirms that incorporated Co2+ cations acting as predominant sites remain structurally stable against this reaction [DOI: 10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726].

In addition to tracking bulk changes before and after catalysis, XAS enables operando and in-situ investigations under working conditions to obtain insights into oxidation state transformations during catalysis. For instance, XANES data in reference [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c00928] showed partial reduction of isolated tetrahedral Cr(VI) species to Cr(III) species under propane dehydrogenation conditions as reaction proceeds on Cr-MCM-41 catalysts. This indicated decreased activity of the reduced Cr(III) sites compared to the starting Cr(VI) sites based on catalyst testing results [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014].

Beyond metal oxidation states, XAS can also probe the formation of anchored metal-support interface sites that enhance stability. EXAFS revealed the growth of PtOGe bonds during H2 reduction of Ge-containing zeolites, fixing isolated Pt atoms to the support against agglomeration even after 100 hours on stream [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7].

X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) has also illuminated oxidation state changes during propane dehydrogenation catalysis. For example, varying amounts of V4+ and V5+ were detected in VSiBeta catalysts by deconvoluting XPS spectra, showing the impact of vanadium loading on the ratios of these oxidation states [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63444-3]. XPS also revealed partial reduction of isolated tetrahedral Sn(IV) species anchored on Pt, forming Ptδ--Snδ+ sites active for dehydrogenation [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c00878].

根据从文件中提取的详细信息,丙烷脱氢催化剂已被广泛地使用X射线技术来探测催化过程中的氧化态变化。

具体来说,X射线吸收光谱(XAS)包括X射线近边吸收结构(XANES)和延伸的X射线细微结构(EXAFS)已被广泛利用。 正如参考文献[DOI:10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]中的引文所示,这些技术可以揭示活性金属物种(如Co2+)的配位环境和氧化状态是如何从新鲜催化剂转变到丙烷脱氢反应后的花了催化剂的。数据证实,作为主要活性位点的掺入Co2+阳离子在结构上对这个反应稳定[DOI:10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。

除了在催化前后跟踪批量变化之外,XAS还可以进行操作和原位研究,在工作条件下获得氧化态转变的见解催化作用。例如,XANES数据在参考文献[DOI:10.1021/acscatal.2c00928]中显示,Cr-MCM-41催化剂上孤立的四面体Cr(VI)物种随反应的进行部分还原为Cr(III)物种。这表明还原后的Cr(III)位点比起始的Cr(VI)位点活性降低,这是根据催化剂测试结果[DOI:10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。


除了金属氧化态,XAS还可以探测增强稳定性的锚定金属-载体界面位点的形成。 EXAFS显示,在Ge存在的分子筛的H2还原过程中,生长了PtOGe键,固定分离的Pt原子抵抗团聚,即使在线上运行100小时后也是如此[DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。

X射线光电子能谱(XPS)也揭示了丙烷脱氢催化过程中的氧化态变化。 例如,通过对VSiBeta催化剂的XPS光谱进行端解,检测到不同数量的V4+和V5+物种,这显示钒负载量对这些氧化态的比率的影响[DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63444-3]。 XPS还显示锚定在Pt上的四面体Sn(IV)物种的部分还原,形成Ptδ- -Snδ+位点,活跃于脱氢反应[DOI:10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。

- 基于硬X射线的高能分辨荧光检测(HERFD) XAS,以增强对稀释单原子位点的灵敏度,其氧化态在反应过程中波动。
