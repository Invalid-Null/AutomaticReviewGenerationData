 <English>
The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816 investigates tuning zeolite Si/Al ratio by dealumination of Beta zeolites. Dealumination creates abundant silanol nests and vacant T-sites, serving as efficient anchor sites to improve metal dispersion and stabilization. 

Additionally, the study with DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c03941 explores the effects of tuning the Si/Al ratio in CHA zeolites from 5 to 25 on propane dehydrogenation performance. It shows that extra-framework Ga+ sites are formed regardless of Si/Al ratio. While reaction rates decrease over time, propylene selectivity remains stable at 96% across different Si/Al ratios when the Ga/Al ratio is optimized.

Moreover, the article with DOI 10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024 investigates reducing TS-1 crystal size from 100 nm to 5.7 μm. The smaller crystal size introduces mesoporosity, enhancing diffusion and avoiding coking. This significantly improves catalyst conversion, selectivity and lifetime.

Lastly, the investigation with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c01808 synthesizes a PtZn propane dehydrogenation catalyst supported on mesoporous MFI zeolite nanosheets. The mesoporous structure and abundant silanol groups promote intermetallic alloy nanoparticle formation.

※※※
The examined articles provide some initial glimpses into how modulating zeolite properties like Si/Al ratio, crystal size, and mesoporosity can impact catalytic performance. However, most studies are narrow in scope, evaluating only one or two zeolite types under limited conditions. More systematic research is needed to derive overarching structure-function relationships. 

Future work should explore these effects across various framework types like FER, MOR, BEA using advanced characterization techniques. Incorporating computational modeling can also elucidate the atomistic underpinnings of observed trends. There are opportunities to engineer multifunctional sites via dealumination, metal substitution, or mesoporosity introduction. Such developments can overcome limitations around stability and selectivity that currently hinder commercial viability. By broadening the knowledge base regarding zeolite tuning effects, rational design of optimized propane dehydrogenation catalysts may be realized.

<Chinese>
DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816的文章通过脱铝Beta分子筛来调节Si/Al比。脱铝生成了丰富的硅醇巢和空出的T位点,起到改善金属扩散和稳定的锚定位点作用。

此外,DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c03941 的研究探讨了CHA分子筛中Si/Al比从5到25的调节对丙烷脱氢反应性能的影响。它表明,无论Si/Al比如何,额外的Ga+位点都会形成。尽管反应速率随时间下降,但在Ga/Al比得到优化的情况下,丙烯选择性在不同Si/Al比下保持在96%左右稳定。

而且,DOI 10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024的文章研究了TS-1晶粒大小从100纳米到5.7微米的减小。更小的晶粒大小引入了介孔,增强了扩散速率,避免了结焦。这显著提高了催化剂的转化率,选择性和使用寿命。

最后,DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c01808合成了负载在介孔MFI分子筛纳米片上��PtZn丙烷脱氢催化剂。介孔结构和丰富的硅醇基团促进了金属间化合物纳米粒子的形成。

※※※
检讨的文章对调节像Si/Al比,晶粒大小和介孔性等分子筛属性对催化性能的影响提供了一些初步的认识。然而,大多数研究范围狭窄,仅在有限条件下评估一两种类型的分子筛。需要更加系统的研究来推导普适的结构-功能关系。

未来的工作应该在各种框架类型如FER,MOR,BEA上利用先进的表征技术探索这些效应。计算模型化也可以阐明观察到的趋势背后的原子级机理。通过脱铝,金属取代或引入介孔,有机会工程化多功能位点。这种发展可以克服稳定性和选择性方面的局限,这当前阻碍了商业应用的可能。通过拓宽对分子筛调节效应的知识基础,可以实现对优化的丙烷脱氢催化剂的合理设计。
