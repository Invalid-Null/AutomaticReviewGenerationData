 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the references, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) faces considerable thermodynamic constraints due to being a highly endothermic process. As stated in [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103], the standard reaction enthalpy is 124.3 kJ/mol, necessitating high temperatures of 500-600°C to achieve sufficient equilibrium conversion [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01924]. The unfavorable thermodynamics also make PDH prone to side reactions like cracking, coking, and over-dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.01.055, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]. 

To overcome these limitations, oxidative PDH (ODHP) using O2 or CO2 co-feeds can lower the temperature requirements by providing exothermic oxidation reactions [10.1002/advs.202207756]. As [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999] notes, CO2-ODHP is of interest because its exothermicity removes the thermodynamic constraints at lower temperatures. However, non-oxidative PDH still plays a major role, with zeolites and metal oxides offering reasonably stable sites [10.1021/jacs.2c03941, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454].

Regarding kinetics, apparent activation energies around 160-200 kJ/mol were estimated for PDH and side reactions on some catalysts [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. First-principles DFT calculations also provided insights on transition states and intermediates, like the 0.71 eV barrier for alpha hydrogen abstraction being rate-limiting on certain surfaces [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. Overall, while some catalyst-specific kinetic factors were given, comprehensive details were lacking in most studies.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Propane dehydrogenation is an extensively studied yet persistently challenging process, bound by intrinsic thermodynamic and kinetic barriers. While oxidative techniques and computational approaches have illuminated promising pathways, continued efforts bridging fundamental surface chemistry and realistic catalyst environments might uncover innovative solutions. An exciting possibility is directly coupling PDH exothermicity to drive other endothermic processes in cyclic or cascaded reactor systems, transforming an apparent constraint into a new opportunity. More broadly, establishing quantitative structure-activity-stability relationships across varying conditions via multi-modal characterization and modeling constitutes a frontier with immense untapped potential.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,丙烷脱氢(PDH)由于是一个高度放热的过程,因此面临相当大的热力学约束。正如[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]中所述,标准反应焓为124.3 kJ/mol,需要500-600°C的高温来达到足够的平衡转换率[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01924]。不利的热力学也使PDH容易发生副反应,如裂化、结焦和过度脱氢[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.01.055, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]。

为了克服这些限制,使用O2或CO2共饲氧化PDH(ODHP)可以通过提供放热的氧化反应来降低温度要求[10.1002/advs.202207756]。正如[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]指出的,CO2-ODHP由于其放热性在较低温度下消除了热力学限制而备受关注。然而,非氧化PDH仍然发挥着重要作用,沸石和金属氧化物提供了相当稳定的活性位[10.1021/jacs.2c03941, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]。

关于动力学方面,在一些催化剂上estimated约160-200 kJ/mol的表面活化能PDH和副反应。第一性原理DFT计算也提供了关于过渡态和中间体的见解,比如在某些表面的0.71 eV屏障是速率控制步骤[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。总的来说,虽然给出了一些催化剂特定的动力学因素,但大多数研究都缺乏全面的细节。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:丙烷脱氢是一项研究广泛且持续具有挑战性的过程,受困于内在的热力学和动力学障碍。虽然氧化技术和计算方法揭示了有前景的途径,但继续努力桥梁基础表面化学和现实的催化剂环境可能会发现创新解决方案。一个令人兴奋的可能性是直接将PDH的放热耦合到驱动其他吸热过程的循环或级联反应器系统中,将表面的约束转化为新的机遇。更广泛地说,通过多模态表征和建模确立定量的结构-活性-稳定性关系是横跨各种条件的前沿,具有巨大的未开发潜力。
