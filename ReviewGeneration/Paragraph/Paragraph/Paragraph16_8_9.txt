Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-based catalysts have been extensively utilized for propane dehydrogenation due to their high activity. However, monometallic Pt catalysts suffer from issues like low selectivity and stability. Introducing a secondary metal promoter forming Pt alloy catalysts has been an effective strategy to enhance selectivity and stability [Placeholder_of_DOI1]. 

Pt-Sn alloy catalysts are among the most widely studied systems. The addition of Sn serves to decrease the number of Pt multiple adsorption sites via an ensemble effect and alter the electron density via a ligand effect, consequently enhancing propylene selectivity and stability [Placeholder_of_DOI2, Placeholder_of_DOI3]. DFT studies suggest Sn increases dissociative adsorption barriers, lowering hydrogenolysis rates [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. The Sn4+ species also facilitate removal of coke precursors from Pt sites via a “drain-off” effect [Placeholder_of_DOI5]. XPS and IR data has revealed strong Pt-Sn electronic interactions with electron donation from Sn to Pt [Placeholder_of_DOI6]. However, excessive Sn can overly dilute Pt ensembles, detrimentally impacting activity [Placeholder_of_DOI7]. 

Investigations into Pt-Ga bimetallic catalysts have also shown Ga promotes dispersion and selectivity [Placeholder_of_DOI8]. The low Tammann temperature of Ga facilitates forming small, highly dispersed Pt-Ga nanoparticles. EXAFS data has confirmed the presence of Pt-Ga bonds with coordination numbers around 4 [Placeholder_of_DOI9]. DFT calculations indicate Ga weakens C-H and strengthens C-C bond energies in hydrocarbons over Pt, suppressing hydrogenolysis and coking reactions [Placeholder_of_DOI10]. This enables operating at lower temperatures while maintaining equilibrium conversion [Placeholder_of_DOI11].

Several reports have recently emerged on Pt-Zn catalysts. Isolated Pt stabilized on (SiO)Zn species in the framework of zeolite Beta provided remarkable stability and near equilibrium conversion approaching thermodynamic limitations [Placeholder_of_DOI12]. EXAFS measurements confirmed the presence of PtZn scattering akin to the PtZn alloy system [Placeholder_of_DOI13]. The appropriately tuned Pt:Zn ratio balances activity and selectivity [Placeholder_of_DOI14]. PtZn intermetallic clusters in confinement of hierarchical zeolites have also achieved excellent productivity and stability [Placeholder_of_DOI15].

In summary, alloying Pt with Sn, Ga or Zn helps modulate ensemble, ligand and metal-support interaction effects to enhance propylene selectivity and stability while maintaining high propane conversion activity. Each promoter imparts its unique benefits – Sn helps remove coke precursors, Ga improves dispersion and lowers operating temperatures, while Zn facilitates stabilization of single atom Pt. An optimal ratio between Pt and the promoter balancing activity and selectivity exists and depends on the specific catalyst composition and architecture.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The presented analyses provide compelling evidence on how rational design of tailored bimetallic Pt alloy catalysts allows breaking intrinsic limitations and pushing reaction equilibria. Building on these advancements, future research could focus on extending this existing knowledge base to hitherto unexplored multi-metallic compositions and reaction spaces. Novel concepts like single atom stabilization and zeolite confinement could be coupled with high throughput computational screening to uncover next generation ultrastable and selective catalyst formulations. An intriguing possibility is expanding the substrate scope to heavier alkanes like butane which possess higher propensity for undesirable reactions.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

由于高活性,Pt基催化剂已被广泛用于丙烷脱氢反应。然而,单金属Pt催化剂存在选择性低和稳定性差的问题。引入第二种金属推动剂形成Pt合金催化剂已被证明是提高选择性和稳定性的有效策略 [Placeholder_of_DOI1]。

Pt-Sn合金催化剂是研究最广泛的系统之一。添加Sn有助于通过配体效应和团簇效应减少Pt多重吸附位点的数量,从而提高丙烯选择性和稳定性[Placeholder_of_DOI2,Placeholder_of_DOI3]。 DFT研究表明,Sn�加解离吸附的障碍,降低氢解反应速率[Placeholder_of_DOI4]。 Sn4+物种还通过“排放”效应促进Pt位点上可焦炭前体的去除[Placeholder_of_DOI5]。 XPS和红外光谱数据显示Sn向Pt提供电子,两者之间存在强烈的电子相互作用[Placeholder_of_DOI6]。 然而,过量的Sn可能会过度稀释Pt配体,从而对活性产生不利影响[Placeholder_of_DOI7]。

对Pt-Ga双金属催化剂的研究也表明Ga促进分散和选择性[Placeholder_of_DOI8]。 Ga的低Tammann温度有利于形成小型高度分散的Pt-Ga纳米粒子。 EXAFS数据证实了Pt-Ga键的存在,配位数约为4 [Placeholder_of_DOI9]。 DFT计算表明,Ga弱化烃类分子上的C-H键,而C-C键变强,抑制氢解和焦炭反应 [Placeholder_of_DOI10]。 这使得在保持平衡转化率的条件下,可以在更低的温度下运行[Placeholder_of_DOI11]。

最近出现了几份关于Pt-Zn催化剂的报告。 镶嵌在沸石Beta 框架中的(SiO)Zn 物种稳定隔离态 Pt提供了显著的稳定性和接近热力学限制的转化率 [Placeholder_of_DOI12]。 EXAFS 测量确认了PtZn散射的存在,类似于PtZn合金体系 [Placeholder_of_DOI13]。 适当调节Pt:Zn比例以平衡活性和选择性 [Placeholder_of_DOI14]。 封装在分层沸石中的PtZn金属间化合物簇也实现了出色的生产力和稳定性 [Placeholder_of_DOI15]。

总之,Pt与Sn、Ga或Zn合金有助于调节配体、团簇和金属载体相互作用效应,在保持高丙烷转化活性的同时提高丙烯选择性和稳定性。 每种助推剂都具有其独特的益处——Sn有助于去除可焦炭前体,Ga改善分散度和降低操作温度,而Zn有利于稳定单原子Pt。 Pt与助推剂之间存在一个最佳比例以平衡活性和选择性,这取决于具体的催化剂组成和体系结构。
