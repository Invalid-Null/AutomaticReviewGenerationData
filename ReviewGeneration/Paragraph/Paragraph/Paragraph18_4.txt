The articles explored provide no information on the use of subsurface promoters like Re or Mn in bimetallic and alloy catalysts to tune selectivity for propane dehydrogenation. Most state clearly that they do not investigate subsurface promoters, with the exception of one article [10.1021/jacs.8b08162] that shows subsurface Mn in Pt3Mn catalysts significantly improves selectivity for propane dehydrogenation over Pt alone. This distinct role of subsurface layers in modifying surface chemical properties and subsequent catalytic performance presents unique opportunities for selectivity optimization.

※※※
Overall, the current literature lacks substantive details and insights into harnessing subsurface promoters like Re and Mn to tune selectivity of bimetallic and alloy catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. However, the one example demonstrating enhanced selectivity with subsurface Mn highlights the potential of this approach. Further research could systematically investigate various subsurface promoter configurations to elucidate the relationships between electronic structure, binding energies, and selectivity. Advanced characterization techniques and computational modeling may also lend detailed perspectives into the role of subsurface species. Broader horizons yet untapped likely exist in extensions to other catalytic systems and reaction chemistries.
