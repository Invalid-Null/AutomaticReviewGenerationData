Based on the details extracted from the references, catalysts facilitate propane dehydrogenation by providing alternative reaction pathways and active sites that lower the activation energy barriers [DOI]. 

Industrially used catalysts like Pt, CrOx, and GaOx supported on materials such as Al2O3 create active metal sites capable of adsorbing propane and selectively cleaving C-H bonds over C-C bonds via mechanisms like the Horiuti-Polanyi process [DOI]. The molecular structure of the support and electronic properties of the metal sites modulate selectivity and reactivity [DOI].

DFT calculations and microkinetic modeling have elucidated fundamental steps and descriptors for achieving balanced activity to break propane's strong C-H bonds while avoiding overcracking to deep dehydrogenation products [DOI]. Structural characteristics like metal cluster size, coordination number, and single atom configurations further tune the catalyst behavior [DOI]. 

Alternative catalyst architectures have been proposed using porous supports like MCM-41 or metal-exchanged zeolites, introducing preferential adsorption orientations or confinement effects that direct selectivity [DOI]. Hierarchically structured catalysts create complementary Lewis and Brønsted acid sites for a multifunctional pathway [DOI].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Supported metal catalysts have demonstrated capability for propane dehydrogenation at reasonable rates and selectivity. However, stability issues still pose limitations industrially. Further investigations might focus on emerging single atom and confined active site configurations with resistance to deactivation. Operando spectroscopy and machine learning approaches could offer additional descriptors for rational design. Beyond metals, research into solid acid catalysts presents an alternate approach less prone to coking. Overall there are opportunities to develop innovative PDH catalyst architectures with enhanced efficiency, sustainability, and economics.

根据参考文献的详细信息,催化剂通过提供替代反应途径和活性位点来降低丙烷脱氢的活化能垒[DOI]。

工业上使用的Pt、CrOx和GaOx等催化剂,负载在Al2O3等材料上的活性金属位点,能够通过Horiuti-Polanyi等机理选择性地吸附丙烷和断裂C-H键而不是C-C键[DOI]。支撑体的分子结构和金属位点的电子性质调节选择性和反应活性[DOI]。

DFT计算和微动力学建模已经阐明了实现平衡活性以断裂丙烷强健的C-H键而避免过度裂解为深度脱氢产物的基本步骤和描述子[DOI]。金属簇的大小、配位数和单原子配置等结构特征进一步调谐催化剂的性能[DOI]。

另外,孔状支撑体(如MCM-41)或金属交换沸石等替代催化剂体系被提出,引入优先吸附方向或约束效应以指导选择性[DOI]。分级结构催化剂创造了相辅相成的Lewis酸和Brønsted酸位点,实现多功能途径[DOI]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 支持的金属催化剂表现出合理反应速率和选择性实现丙烷脱氢的能力。然而,稳定性问题在工业上仍然是限制。进一步的研究可能会关注新出现的单原子和限域活性位点配置,具有抗脱活性能。Operando光谱和机器学习方法可以提供额外的描述子进行合理设计。除金属催化剂外,研究固体酸催化剂提供了另一种不易产生焦炭的方法。总体上,开发具有增强效率、可持续性和经济性的创新丙烷脱氢催化剂体系存在良好契机。
