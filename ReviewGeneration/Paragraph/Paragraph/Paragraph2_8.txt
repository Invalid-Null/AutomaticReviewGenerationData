Based on the details extracted from the files, catalysts facilitate propane dehydrogenation by providing alternative reaction pathways with lower activation energy barriers compared to the uncatalyzed thermal dehydrogenation process [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. The currently used industrial catalysts include chromium and platinum oxides supported on alumina, with Pt-based catalysts offering high C-H bond activation while limiting side reactions like cracking [10.1021/acscatal.3c00939, 10.1016/j.jcat.2005.01.005]. Alternative metal oxide catalysts such as VOx, ZrO2, CeO2 and Ga2O3 have also shown promise [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]. 

Several key mechanisms of catalyst activity in propane dehydrogenation have been described. On supported Pt catalysts, C-H activation occurs preferentially over C-C bond cleavage, resulting in dehydrogenation rather than cracking [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]. DFT calculations have also revealed that isolated metal atoms like Pt at the gas-liquid interface of metal nanoparticles can serve as single-site active centers [10.1021/acscatal.1c01924]. For zeolite-based catalysts, metal cations introduced by ion exchange provide Lewis acid sites capable of activating propane's C-H bonds [10.1002/advs.202207756]. The size and coordination environment of metal clusters impacts the reactivity, with under-coordinated atoms on corners and steps promoting activity but also undesired reactions [10.1021/acscatal.5b01423]. Overall, progress has been made in elucidating catalyst design parameters that balance high C-H activation for dehydrogenation while avoiding side reactions like cracking and coking.

Integrating the insights across these studies, atomic-level characterization and theoretical modeling have proven valuable in unraveling the underlying mechanisms of catalyst activity. This foundation sets the stage for knowledge-driven design of optimized, selective and stable propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Key opportunities include further exploration of single atom catalysts, elucidation of zeolite acid site effects across framework types, and rational modulation of metal cluster size and environment. Broader integration across length scales, from electronic structure to reactor level, will undoubtedly play a pivotal role. Ultimately, progress in this vital area of catalysis research will facilitate technologies that meet growing propylene demands in an economically and environmentally sustainable manner.

根据从文件中提取的详细信息,与未催化的热脱氢反应相比,催化剂通过提供较低活化能垒的替代反应途径来促进丙烷脱氢反应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]。目前使用的工业催化剂包括载于氧化铝上的铬和铂氧化物,Pt基催化剂提供高的C-H键活化而限制副反应如裂化反应 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00939, 10.1016/j.jcat.2005.01.005]。其他的金属氧化物催化剂如VOx、ZrO2、CeO2和Ga2O3也显示了前景 [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]。

丙烷脱氢催化中的几种关键的催化机理已被描述。在载有Pt的催化剂上,与C-C键的断裂相比,优先发生C-H键的活化,导致脱氢而不是裂化 [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]。DFT计算还表明,金属纳米颗粒液态表面的单原子金属Pt  可以作为单位活性中心 [10.1021/acscatal.1c01924]。对于分子筛基催化剂,通过离子交换引入的金属阳离子提供了能够活化丙烷C-H键的路易斯酸位点 [10.1002/advs.202207756]。金属团簇的大小和配体环境会影响其活性,角上和台阶上的未配位原子促进活性但也会导致不想要的反应 [10.1021/acscatal.5b01423]。总的来说,在平衡高的C-H活化以实现脱氢和避免副反应如裂化和共烃化方面,已经取得了了探明催化剂设计参数的进展。

※※※

综合这些研究的见解,原子层次的表征和理论建模已经被证明是揭示催化剂活性的基本机理的有价值的方法。这为知识驱动设计优化的、选择性的和稳定的丙烷脱氢催化剂奠定了基础。关键的机会包括进一步探索单原子催化剂、阐明分子筛酸位效应在不同骨架类型上的作用、合理调控金属簇的大小和环境等。更广泛的多尺度整合,从电子结构到反应器水平,无疑将发挥关键作用。最终,在这个重要的催化剂研究领域的进展将促进满足不断增长的聚丙烯需求的经济和环境可持续的技术的发展。
