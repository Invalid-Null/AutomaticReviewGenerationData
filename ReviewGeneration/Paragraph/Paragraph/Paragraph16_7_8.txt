Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-Sn Catalysts
Several studies have evaluated Pt-Sn bimetallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and provided insights into how Sn incorporation affects catalytic performance [Placeholder_Of_DOI1], [Placeholder_Of_DOI2], [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. Adding Sn to Pt has been found to increase propene selectivity and stability while decreasing initial activity. For example, PtSn/Al2O3 achieved >98% propylene selectivity and 40% propane conversion at 570°C [Placeholder_Of_DOI21]. The decreased activity but improved selectivity has been attributed to electronic and geometric effects - Sn helps decrease the number of Pt ensemble sites for bond rupture reactions while increasing electron density on remaining Pt sites for selective bond activation [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. DFT studies suggest Pt3Sn1 is an optimal active phase [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. Characterization data indicates Sn interacts electronically and geometrically with Pt, as evidenced by XPS showing increased electron density on Pt [Placeholder_Of_DOI5], and EXAFS indicating Pt-Sn coordination numbers around 3.5 [Placeholder_Of_DOI9]. Additionally, binding Sn in the support helps stabilize PtSn sites and minimize deactivation [Placeholder_Of_DOI8].

Pt-Ga Catalysts  
A few recent studies have explored Pt-Ga bimetallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation [Placeholder_Of_DOI10], [Placeholder_Of_DOI9], finding improved performance versus monometallic Pt. For example, 2.5%Si@PtGa/Al2O3 achieved high activity close to equilibrium conversion at 450°C along with 21.8% propane conversion and 90.7% propylene selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI22]. Characterization indicates Ga helps disperse and stabilize Pt active sites. The Pt-Ga structure is also found to suppress side reactions and coke formation compared to Pt [Placeholder_Of_DOI23].

Pt-Zn Catalysts
Several articles have reported enhanced performance for propane dehydrogenation using Pt-Zn alloy catalysts [Placeholder_Of_DOI6], [Placeholder_Of_DOI11], [Placeholder_Of_DOI9]. Key effects of adding Zn include decreasing Pt cluster size and tuning Pt valence orbitals to increase selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI12]. For instance, a 0.5Pt/2.6Zn-Beta catalyst achieved 30% propane conversion and 90% propylene selectivity close to equilibrium levels [Placeholder_Of_DOI13]. EXAFS data confirmed isolated PtZn4-6 sites lead to high catalytic performance [Placeholder_Of_DOI9].

In summary, alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn has been shown effective for improving propene selectivity, activity and stability compared to monometallic Pt catalysts. The modulating performance effects arise from electronic, ensemble and geometric alterations enabled by the additional metals. Future research could explore extending these design principles to other novel Pt alloy combinations and reaction systems.

		Pt-Sn 催化剂
        几项研究评估了Pt-Sn双金属催化剂用于丙烷脱氢,并提供了Sn掺杂如何影响催化性能的见解[Placeholder_Of_DOI1]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI2]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。 添加Sn到Pt中发现可提高丙烯选择性和稳定性,同时降低初始活性。 例如,PtSn/Al2O3 实现了>98% 丙烯选择性和 40% 丙烷转化率,在 570°C[Placeholder_Of_DOI21]。活性下降但选择性提高被归因于电子和几何效应 —— Sn有助于减少Pt团簇位点用于断裂反应,同时增加余留Pt位点的电子密度用于选择性的活化[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。 DFT研究表明Pt3Sn1是一种理想的活性相[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。 表征数据表明,Sn与Pt发生了电子和几何上的相互作用,这可以从XPS表明Pt电子密度增加[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]以及EXAFS表明Pt-Sn配位数约为3.5[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。 此外,将Sn结合在载体上有助于稳定PtSn位点和最小化失活[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。

		Pt-Ga 催化剂
        最近有几项研究探索了Pt-Ga双金属催化剂用于丙烷脱氢[Placeholder_Of_DOI10]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI9],发现其性能优于单金属Pt。 例如,2.5%Si@PtGa/Al2O3实现了在450°C接近平衡转化的高活性,以及21.8%丙烷转化率和90.7%丙烯选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI22]。表征表明Ga有助于分散和稳定Pt活性位点。 与Pt相比,Pt-Ga结构也被发现可抑制副反应和焦炭形成[Placeholder_Of_DOI23]。

		Pt-Zn 催化剂
        几篇文章报道了使用Pt-Zn合金催化剂改进丙烷脱氢性能 [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI11]、[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。 添加Zn的关键效应包括降低Pt簇大小和调谐Pt价轨道以提高选择性[Placeholder_Of_DOI12]。 例如,一种0.5Pt/2.6Zn-Beta催化剂实现了30%丙烷转化率和90%丙烯选择性接近平衡水平[Placeholder_Of_DOI13]。 EXAFS数据确认了孤立的PtZn4-6位点导致高催化性能[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。

		总的来说,与单金属Pt催化剂相比,将Pt与Sn、Ga和Zn等助配体合金化被证明可有效提高丙烯的选择性、活性和稳定性。 性能调控效应源自额外金属引入的电子、配位和几何改变。 未来的研究可以探索将这些设计原理扩展到其他新颖的Pt合金组合和反应体系。
