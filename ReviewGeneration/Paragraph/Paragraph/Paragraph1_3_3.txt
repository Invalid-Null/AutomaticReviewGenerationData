 <English>
Propylene is a crucial chemical feedstock utilized for manufacturing a wide range of products, including polypropylene, acrylonitrile, propylene oxide, acetone, etc. [1] Owing to its versatile applicability, the global demand for propylene has been rising steadily at 2-3% annually and is predicted to persist increasing. [2] However, conventional production routes via steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking struggle to meet the heightening requirements. [3] In this context, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has become an appealing alternative for on-purpose generation of propylene, leveraging the economical propane extracted from shale gas. [4] Multiple studies have demonstrated that PDH can be an efficient, competitive and eco-friendly pathway. [5]

[2] 10.1021/acscatal.3c00103
[3] 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454
[4] 10.1002/advs.202207756 
[5] 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the increasing demand for propylene in the chemical industry and limitations of conventional production methods, PDH technology exploiting abundant propane resources presents a promising opportunity. Further research could focus on developing novel catalysts and processes to improve conversion, yield and sustainability. Techno-economic assessments compiling industrial data are also vital to demonstrate economic viability. Overall, PDH has the potential to make a greater contribution toward meeting global propylene demand through continued innovation.
