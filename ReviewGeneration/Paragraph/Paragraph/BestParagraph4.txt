Based on the detailed analysis from the references, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) faces significant thermodynamic constraints and kinetic factors that limit conversion and selectivity.

PDH is a highly endothermic reaction [[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]; [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]], with ΔH ranging from 124-200 kJ/mol [[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]; [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]]. To drive the equilibrium, high temperatures of 500-650°C are required [[10.1021/acscatal.0c01455]; [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]]], often leading to coke formation and other side reactions [[10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]; [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]]. Low partial pressures also shift equilibrium towards products [[10.1002/advs.202207756]]. Adding O2 or CO2 oxidants can lower temperature needs by providing exothermic oxidation reactions [[10.1002/advs.202207756]], but has tradeoffs.  

Kinetically, PDH has high barriers around 0.7-1.8 eV for key C-H activation steps [[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]; [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]]], limiting turnover. Support and promoter interactions help stabilize active sites against sintering [[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]] at high temperatures. Structure sensitive side reactions like coking and hydrogenolysis occur on specific sites [[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]], while main PDH pathway may be structure insensitive [[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The thermodynamic and kinetic constraints limiting propane dehydrogenation are now well characterized through advanced spectroscopic, theoretical and reactor studies. This understanding points towards next generation catalysts and processes that can enhance stability and selectivity at lower temperatures. For example, novel support materials and promoters that stabilize active sites could enable operating at 400-500°C while minimizing side reactions. Photocatalysis or hybrid thermochemical approaches could also help overcome unfavorable thermal dynamics. New operando characterization techniques will give further insights into transient intermediates and complex pathways during PDH turnover. Further developing this mechanistic foundation through experiments paired with multi-scale modeling will open innovative directions for commercial PDH processes.
