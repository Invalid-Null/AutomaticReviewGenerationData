Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts facilitate the reaction by providing alternative lower energy pathways compared to the uncatalyzed thermal process. Industrial PDH processes utilize supported metal catalysts like Pt/Al2O3 and CrOx/Al2O3 that introduce active sites capable of adsorbing and activating propane's C-H bonds [Placeholder_Of_DOI]. The metal sites preferentially break C-H bonds over C-C bonds, resulting in selective dehydrogenation to propylene instead of cracking to other products [Placeholder_Of_DOI]. 

Detailed density functional theory (DFT) calculations have elucidated the structure sensitivity and reaction mechanisms of metals like Pt [Placeholder_Of_DOI]. The calculations show that under-coordinated atoms on steps, corners, and small clusters demonstrate high activity but also increased cracking. DFT and infrared spectroscopy experiments confirm that single Pt atoms at the gas-liquid interface of Ga-Pt liquid alloys provide active sites for low-barrier dehydrogenation [Placeholder_Of_DOI].

Alternative catalytic materials like Sn-Beta zeolite [Placeholder_Of_DOI] and ZrO2 [Placeholder_Of_DOI] introduce Lewis acid sites capable of activating the first C-H bond with lower barriers around 1.76 eV [Placeholder_Of_DOI]. The zeolite confinement stabilizes intermediates and hinders overcracking. Hierarchical zeolites and mesoporous silicas offer additional tunability [Placeholder_Of_DOI].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The reviewed advances demonstrate promising paths for optimizing propane dehydrogenation catalysts through atomic-level design of active sites, mesoscale engineering of support interactions, and guidance from computational modeling. Key opportunities include stabilizing single atom Pt groups on supports, tailoring zeolites and mesoporous materials to balance activity and selectivity, and elucidating reaction networks with dynamic operando spectroscopy. Broader horizons encompass integrating PDH into processes for sustainable propane production from renewables.
