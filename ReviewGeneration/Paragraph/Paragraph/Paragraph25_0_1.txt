Based on the details furnished, the article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00728 utilizes in-situ NMR and mass spectrometry characterization techniques to study working propane dehydrogenation catalysts. The NMR data reveals insights into the nature of oxygen-functionalized boron edge sites that are critical for activity and selectivity in propane dehydrogenation over hydroxylated boron nitride catalysts. Additionally, the online synchrotron vacuum ultraviolet photoionization mass spectrometry provides direct evidence for the existence of gas-phase methyl radicals during the reaction.

The article with DOI 10.1002/advs.202207756 employs several in-situ techniques including X-ray absorption spectroscopy, infrared spectroscopy, temperature programmed reduction, and pulse titration experiments. These probe changes in metal speciation and oxidation states under propane dehydrogenation conditions, showing the formation of isolated Ga+/In+ sites upon H2 reduction that displace Bronsted acid sites. 

Combining the insights from these two studies, future research could further explore single-site heterogeneous catalysts with customized active centers to optimize propane dehydrogenation performance. Advanced in-situ spectroscopic, microscopic, and computational methodologies would be invaluable in revealing mechanistic pathways and designing superior catalysts. The synergistic integration of materials discovery, characterization tools, and reaction engineering may unlock new horizons in selective alkane activation.
