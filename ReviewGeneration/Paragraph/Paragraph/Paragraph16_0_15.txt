Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Several studies have evaluated the effect of alloying Pt with secondary metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn for improving the performance of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. Alloying helps modulate the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites.

Pt-Sn catalysts have been widely studied for PDH, with the commercial Oleflex process utilizing a Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalyst [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. Investigations on supported Pt-Ga catalysts also demonstrate increased conversion and propylene selectivity over Pt. The electronic and structural changes induced by Ga addition inhibit side reactions like hydrogenolysis and coking [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. Stabilizing ultra-small Pt-Ga nanoparticles or clusters within porous supports produces active and stable PDH catalysts [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].

In summary, alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga, and Zn allows simultaneously attaining high activity, selectivity and stability for propane dehydrogenation over Pt alone. This stems from the modulated ensemble requirements and ligand effects governing the reactivity of Pt active sites.
