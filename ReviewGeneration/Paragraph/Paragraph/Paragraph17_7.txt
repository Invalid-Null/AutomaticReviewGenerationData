 <English>

Based on the detailed analysis from the study with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, larger Pt nanoparticles can accommodate more Sn species to form PtSn alloy nanoparticles, while smaller Pt clusters tend to be Pt-rich with low Sn incorporation. The choice of secondary metal and its interaction with Pt sites could be further optimized to tune the catalyst structure and performance, as suggested in references [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548] and [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, emerging opportunities can be envisioned to design novel bimetallic and alloy catalysts by carefully selecting and incorporating secondary metals that can modulate the geometric and electronic properties of the active sites. For instance, future research could explore new combinations of secondary metals that optimize steric and electronic effects to inhibit coke formation while retaining high activity. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques could also elucidate reaction mechanisms and dynamic structural changes under working conditions. Overall, by gaining fundamental insights into geometric and electronic tuning knobs, increasingly selective and stable catalysts could be rationally designed.
