Based on the quotes and details extracted from the file, one article investigates electronic and geometric effects of the secondary metal tin (Sn) in PtSn bimetallic catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]. It shows that larger Pt nanoparticles can accommodate more Sn to form PtSn alloy nanoparticles, while smaller Pt clusters tend to be Pt-rich with low Sn incorporation. This demonstrates a geometric effect where the Pt nanoparticle size impacts Sn accommodation. The article also notes that tuning the choice and interaction of the secondary metal with Pt sites could optimize catalyst structure and performance.

Another article uses DFT calculations to identify electronic regulation and geometric modulation of the Pt site when alloyed with a secondary metal M [10.1038/s41565-023-01344-z]. A descriptor called "degree-of-isolation" depicts the extent of Pt-C repulsion, corresponding to an isolation effect. PtZn with moderate isolation is found to be optimal, while PtMn with high isolation markedly improved propylene selectivity to 93% at 49% propane conversion. This exemplifies both electronic and geometric effects tuning catalyst selectivity.

Other examples showcase secondary metals like In, Zn, Ga, and Sn transferring electrons to Pt sites upon alloying, increasing the electron density and weakening intermediates' adsorption [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667, 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]. Breaking down Pt ensembles via alloying is also identified as critical in limiting side reactions like coke formation and hydrogenolysis [10.1021/jacs.8b08162, 10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the electronic and geometric modulation of Pt sites enabled by secondary metals highlighted in this analysis, future research could further explore the impact of ternary and higher-order multi-metallic combinations. The possibility of synergistically integrating promoting electronic effects while geometrically isolating sites could unlock enhanced selectivity and stability. 

Another promising direction is applying core-shell nanoparticle synthesis techniques to concentrate electronic and strain effects within ultrathin shell regions encapsulating secondary metal cores. Such precision nanoscale engineering of defined sites could stretch the boundaries of tuning geometric and electronic properties. Advanced operando spectroscopic probes capable of tracking dynamic structural and electronic fluctuations of specific sites during reactions will also shed invaluable insights.

根据从文件中提取的引文和细节,一篇文章研究了PtSn双金属催化剂中次要金属锡(Sn)的电子和几何效应[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]。它显示,更大的Pt纳米颗粒可以容纳更多的Sn形成PtSn合金纳米颗粒,而较小的Pt簇趋于 Pt-rich,Sn掺入量低。这展示了一个几何效应,Pt纳米颗粒的大小影响Sn的容纳。该文还指出,调整次要金属的选择和与Pt活跃位点的相互作用可以优化催化剂的结构和性能。 

另一篇文章利用DFT计算识别了与次要金属M合金时Pt位点的电子调控和几何调控[10.1038/s41565-023-01344-z]。一个称为“隔离度”的描述符描绘了Pt-C排斥的程度,对应于一种隔离效应。具有适度隔离的PtZn被发现是最佳的,而具有高度隔离的PtMn将丙烷转化率提高到49%时,丙烯选择性显著提高到93%。这例证了电子和几何效应都调节了催化剂的选择性。

其他例子展示了In、Zn、Ga和Sn等次要金属与Pt合金时向Pt位点转移电子,增加了Pt的电子密度,并削弱了中间体的吸附[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667, 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]。破坏Pt配合物通过合金化的作用也被确定为限制深度脱氢和焦炭生成等副反应的关键[10.1021/jacs.8b08162, 10.1016/j.jcat.2018.09.006]。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望:考虑到本分析中强调的次要金属导致的Pt位点的电子和几何调控,未来的研究可以进一步探索三元和更高阶多金属组合的影响。同时整合促进电子效应而几何隔离位点的可能性可以释放增强的选择性和稳定性。

另一个有前景的方向是应用核壳纳米颗粒合成技术,将电子和应变效应集中在包裹次要金属核的超薄壳层区域。这样的纳米级工程中精确定义位点可以推进调控几何和电子性质的边界。先进的原位光谱技术能够跟踪特定位点在反应过程中的动态结构和电子波动也会提供宝贵的信息。
