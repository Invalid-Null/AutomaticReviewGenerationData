		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The studies discussed demonstrate that metal-support interfacial effects can significantly impact the performance of metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. Weaker interactions between the metal oxide phase and the support in some cases leads to enhanced activity and selectivity by modulating the metal oxide electronic structure or stabilizing isolated active sites. There are still many oxide support and promoter combinations left unexplored that may offer further performance improvements. Future research could focus on high-throughput computational screening of metal-support pairs to determine optimal interactions, combined with targeted synthesis and testing of promising candidates. Operando spectroscopy techniques probing interfacial sites during catalysis would also provide key insights. Overall there is significant room for advancing our fundamental understanding of metal-support interplays and using this knowledge to design superior next generation propane dehydrogenation catalysts.
