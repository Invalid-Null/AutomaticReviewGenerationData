Based on the details provided in the file, catalyst preparation methods aimed at optimizing active phase dispersion for propane dehydrogenation include:

Using controlled impregnation methods like incipient wetness impregnation to disperse metal precursors onto supports and achieve isolated metal sites or ultra small nanoparticles. Examples include dispersing Pt, Zn, VOx, CrOx, MoOx onto zeolites, silica, alumina, and other structured supports [DOI1, DOI2, DOI3].

Employing direct hydrothermal synthesis and confined crystallization strategies to encapsulate finely dispersed metal clusters or single atoms within zeolite or metal-organic frameworks. This stabilizes the active sites against sintering [DOI4, DOI5].  

Grafting metal precursors using surface organometallic chemistry to form isolated metal centers with strong metal-support bonding, preventing aggregation [DOI6, DOI7].

Adding promoters like Sn, Zn, Ge, La, Ga to facilitate superior dispersion through electronic or spatial isolation of active sites like Pt [DOI8, DOI9].

Calcining under controlled atmospheres, varying metal precursor chemistry, and staging impregnation steps to modulate final dispersion [DOI10, DOI11]. 

Advanced electron microscopy techniques confirm achievements of site isolation and uniform nanoparticle diameters below 2 nm for many optimized systems [DOI12, DOI13].

The methods elucidated in the references furnished demonstrate promising pathways for designing catalysts with remarkable activity, selectivity, and stability profiles via dispersion optimization. Nonetheless, most preparations remains ex-situ and lack operando monitoring. An emerging opportunity lies in extending these advancements to in-situ synthesis under reaction environments. Such embodiments could enable self-organization of catalytically optimal active site motifs. Explorations at this intersection of materials chemistry and reaction engineering might pave the way for next-generation catalysts with unprecedented performance metrics.

基于文件提供的详细信息,用于优化丙烷脱氢反应的活性相分散的催化剂制备方法包括:

使用受控浸渍方法如浸渍湿法将金属前体分散到载体上并获得分散的金属位点或超小纳米粒子。例子包括将Pt, Zn, VOx, CrOx, MoOx分散到分子筛,二氧化硅,氧化铝和其他结构化载体上 [DOI1, DOI2, DOI3]。

采用直接水热合成和受限结晶策略将细分散的金属簇或单原子封装在分子筛或金属有机框架内。这稳定了活性位点,防止其烧结[DOI4, DOI5]。

使用表面有机金属化学锚定金属前体以形成孤立的金属中心并与载体形成强大的金属-载体键合,防止聚集[DOI6, DOI7]。

添加助剂如Sn, Zn, Ge, La, Ga有助于通过Pt等活性位点的电子或空间隔离来实现优越的分散[DOI8, DOI9]。

在受控气氛下煅烧,改变金属前体化学成分以及分级浸渍步骤以调节最终分散[DOI10, DOI11]。

先进的电子显微技术确认了位点隔离和均匀纳米粒子直径低于2 nm的成就,适用于许多优化系统 [DOI12, DOI13]。

※※※
参考文献中阐明的方法显示了通过优化分散设计催化剂以获得显著的活性,选择性和稳定性轮廓的有希望的途径。尽管如此,大多数制备仍然是原位的,并缺乏操作监测。新兴的机会在于将这些进步扩展到原位合成反应环境下。这种实施可能使催化最佳活性位点基元的自组织。在材料化学和反应工程学的交叉点对这一领域的探索可能为新一代催化剂铺平道路,使其具有前所未有的性能指标。
