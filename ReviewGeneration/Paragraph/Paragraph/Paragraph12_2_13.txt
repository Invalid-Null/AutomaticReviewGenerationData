Based on the information extracted from the file, several studies have evaluated supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most widely used and commercially successful PDH catalysts. For example, Pt/Al2O3 is a key component of the commercial Oleflex process due to Pt's ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing C-C cleavage [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]. One study showed adding 1 wt% boron promoter to Pt/γ-Al2O3 catalysts increased propylene selectivity from 90% to 98% over 12 hours, by reducing coke formation [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]. Atomic layer deposition (ALD) of metal oxide overcoats on Pt/Al2O3 extrudates was also found to improve PDH yield and selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391].

Investigations on supported Ir or Pd catalysts for PDH were more limited based on the extracted information. One study compared a RhCu single atom alloy on SiO2 to a standard Pt/Al2O3 catalyst. Under identical conditions, the RhCu catalyst demonstrated higher intrinsic activity, 100% propene selectivity, and coking resistance compared to 80% selectivity for the Pt catalyst [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389].

Several studies provided insights into deactivation mechanisms of supported metal PDH catalysts. Temperature-programmed oxidation experiments identified three types of coke formed on PtSn/Al2O3 during PDH, associated with the metal, support, and graphitic coke. Adding hydrogen suppressed coke formation [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]. For Pt/MgAl2O4 catalysts, loss of activity over multiple PDH cycles was linked to Pt agglomeration accelerated by sulfur [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X].

In summary, while Pt-based catalysts have seen the most widespread use and commercialization, emerging catalysts based on other metals have shown promising performance. Elucidating deactivation pathways also enables targeted catalyst improvements to enhance stability. Further research should continue assessing alternative metals and supports, as well as tailored operating conditions that maximize selectivity and prolong lifetime.

Future horizons may encompass exploiting single atom alloy catalysts to maximize metal efficiency; crafting multifunctional catalyst architectures with promoters or coatings that tackle coking or agglomeration; and machine learning guided catalyst discovery. Broader implications include intensifying propane-to-propylene processes to increase profitability in an evolving chemical landscape.

<Chinese>
根据文件中提取的信息,已有几项研究评价了 supported 单金属 Pt、Pd 和 Ir 催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。

报道称,Pt 基催化剂是最广泛使用和商业上最成功的 PDH 催化剂之一。例如,由于 Pt 能激活 C-H 键而最小化 C-C 键的断裂,Pt/Al2O3 是商业 Oleflex 工艺的关键组成部分 [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]。一项研究表明,向 Pt/γ-Al2O3 催化剂添加 1 wt% 的硼促进剂,可以在12小时内将丙烯选择性从90%提高到98%,通过减少焦炭的形成来实现 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。原子层沉积(ALD)金属氧化物涂层在 Pt/Al2O3 挤出催化剂上也被发现可以提高 PDH 产率和选择性 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]。

根据提取的信息,supported Ir 或 Pd 催化剂用于 PDH 的研究相对有限。一项研究将 SiO2 支持的 RhCu 单原子合金与标准 Pt/Al2O3 催化剂进行了比较。在相同条件下,RhCu 催化剂表现出更高的固有活性、100% 丙烯选择性和抗焦炭性,而 Pt 催化剂的选择性为 80% [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]。

几项研究提供了 supported 金属 PDH 催化剂失活机理的见解。程序升温氧化实验确定了 PDH 过程中 PtSn/Al2O3 上形成的三种焦炭类型,与金属、载体和石墨焦炭有关。添加氢气抑制了焦炭的形成 [DOI: 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。 对于 Pt/MgAl2O4 催化剂,多次 PDH 周期活性的丢失与硫促进的 Pt 凝集有关 [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]。

总之,虽然 Pt 基催化剂应用最广泛并商业化,但其他金属的新型催化剂表现出了很有前景的性能。阐明失活途径也可以针对性改进催化剂以增强稳定性。未来的研究应继续评估其他金属和载体,以及可以最大化选择性和延长使用寿命的调控操作条件。

考虑到文件概述的进展,通过利用先进的表征技术、计算建模和材料合成方法,存在机会开发尖端 PDH 催化剂技术。未来可能包括:利用单原子合金催化剂最大化金属利用效率;设计具有促进剂或涂层的多功能催化剂体系以解决焦炭或凝集问题;以及利用机器学习指导催化剂发现。更广泛的意义在于加强丙烷制丙烯工艺的强化,以增加在不断变化的化学景观中的盈利能力。
