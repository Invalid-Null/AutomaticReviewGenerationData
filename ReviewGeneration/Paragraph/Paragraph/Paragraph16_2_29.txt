 <English>
    Based on the detailed analysis extracted from the references, bimetallic and alloy catalysts comprising platinum (Pt) and post-transition metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), zinc (Zn), etc. have demonstrated enhanced catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation compared to monometallic platinum catalysts. Multiple studies have reported improved activity and propylene selectivity along with mitigated deactivation via coke formation upon alloying Pt with these secondary metals.
    
    The prototypical industrial propane dehydrogenation catalyst Pt-Sn has been extensively investigated across various supports like alumina, silica, zeolites, demonstrating a volcanotype curve where activity initially increases with Sn content before declining at higher loadings [1, 2, 3, 4]. Adding 1-2 Sn per Pt atom helps strike an optimal balance between preserving dehydrogenation capability while suppressing hydrogenolysis and coking side-reactions [5, 6]. This has been attributed to electronic and ensemble effects where Sn increases electron density on Pt while separating adjacent sites. The commercial Oleflex process by UOP leverages a Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalyst [7].
    
    Research has also focused on stabilizing ultra-small Pt-Sn clusters like PtSn4-6 within the micropores of zeolites and mesoporous materials, which limits aggregation while maximizing exposed Pt active sites [8, 9]. Such single-atom alloy catalysts (SAACs) restrict the ensemble size capable of catalyzing propane cracking and coke formation. Pt-Ga intermetallic compounds have also demonstrated exceptional stability owing to their propensity for η2-C3H6 adsorption over η3-C3H6 binding that initiates dehydrogenation [10]. This characterization data corroborates with high sustained propane conversion.
    
    Several reports have underscored the promotional impacts of incorporating Zn into Pt clusters that manifest as increased propylene selectivity beyond equilibrium constraints via modified electronic structure and dilution of Pt multiple sites [11, 12]. The formation of atomically ordered intermetallic Pt-Zn alloys with defined stoichiometry like PtZn4 has been found to be indispensable.
    
    Therefore, strategic deployment of promoters like Sn, Ga, Zn, etc. with Pt permits attaining an optimal balance of activity, selectivity and stability for practical propane dehydrogenation processes through interlinked electronic, ensemble and structural effects. Future endeavors might extend this conceptual blueprint to ternary Pt alloys and metastable extended surfaces with abundant low-coordination active sites. Development of high-throughput computational screening methodologies can also assist in efficiently navigating vast uncharted compositional domains.

<Chinese>
    根据参考文献中提取的详细分析,与单金属铂催化剂相比,由铂(Pt)和锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)、锌(Zn)等过渡后金属构成的双金属和合金催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中表现出了增强的催化性能。 多项研究报道了在Pt与这些次级金属形成合金后,活性和丙烯选择性的提高以及通过焦炭形成减轻的失活。
    
    在各种支撑体如氧化铝、二氧化硅、分子筛上的原型工业丙烷脱氢催化剂Pt-Sn已经被广泛研究,显示出随着Sn含量的增加,活性首先增大然后在较高负载量下降的火山型曲线[1,2,3,4]。 每Pt原子添加1-2个Sn原子有助于在保持脱氢能力的同时抑制氢解和焦化副反应[5,6]达到最佳平衡。 这已被归因于电子和表面效应,其中Sn增加Pt上的电子密度,同时分离相邻位点。 UOP公司的商业Oleflex工艺利用Pt-Sn/ Al2O3催化剂[7]。
    
    研究还集中在分子筛和介孔材料的微孔内稳定超小Pt-Sn簇如PtSn4-6,这限制了聚集同时最大化暴露的Pt活性位点[8,9]。 这样的单原子合金催化剂(SAAC)限制了能够催化丙烷裂解和焦炭形成的表面结构大小。 Pt-Ga金属间化合物也表现出了卓越的稳定性,这是由于它们倾向于η2-C3H6吸附而不是η3-C3H6结合,这会引发脱氢反应[10]。 这些表征数据与高丙烷转化率的持续性是一致的。
    
    几份报告强调了将Zn引入Pt簇中的促进作用,这表现在通过改变电子结构和稀释Pt多重位点超过平衡限制提高丙烯选择性[11,12]。 形成原子有序的金属间化合物Pt-Zn合金如PtZn4被发现是必不可少的。
    
    因此,有目的地使用Sn、Ga、Zn等促进剂与Pt相结合,通过相互关联的电子效应、表面和结构效应,可以为实际的丙烷脱氢工艺过程中实现活性、选择性和稳定性之间的最佳平衡。 未来的工作可能会将这一概念扩展到三元Pt合金和具有丰富低配位活性位点的超稳定扩展表面。 高通量计算筛选方法的开发也可以帮助有效地在广阔的未知成分域中进行导航。
