	<English>
		Supported mono-metallic platinum catalysts have been widely explored for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) due to Pt's superior ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing undesirable C-C bond cleavage [[10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]], [[10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]]. Data shows sulfur addition, either during catalyst preparation or gas phase pre-treatment, can enhance Pt/alumina selectivity, with predosing at 873 K improving initial propene formation [[10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]]. Atomic layer deposition (ALD) metal oxide overcoats on Pt/alumina extrudates increased yield and selectivity, attributed to tailored metal-support sites [[10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]]. 

        Confinement of sub-nanometric Pt clusters within Ge-UTL zeolites via Pt-O-Ge linkages yielded the Pt4-Ge2-d4r@UTL catalyst exhibiting excellent stability for over 60 hours [[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]]. Pt nanoclusters supported on spinel MgAl2O4 maintained high initial activity but were prone to deactivation from Pt agglomeration accelerated by sulfur [[10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]].

        Pt cluster nuclearity effects were studied computationally, finding a volcano-type dependence maximized at Pt2; reactivity decreased with increasing size correlating to higher C-H activation barriers [[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]]. Comparison between supported Pt catalysts and other metals is limited, but data shows Ru nanoparticles had very low 4.5% propylene selectivity compared to 46% for Pt [[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]].
※※※
		The explored studies provide valuable insights into supported mono-metallic Pt catalyst design for propane dehydrogenation. Key factors influencing stability and selectivity have been identified, including metal-support interactions, sulfur effects, confinement within microporous structures, cluster size effects and promoters. However further work should systematically benchmark Pt against other supported metals like Pd across different supports and conditions. More in-depth spectroscopic characterization of metal-support interfaces could elucidate stabilization mechanisms. Machine learning could assist high-throughput catalyst screening and optimization. Overall expanding the knowledge base around Pt and alternative metals can spur development of more robust PDH catalysts.

	<Chinese>
		由于铂的卓越的C-H键激活能力和最小化不需要的C-C键断裂的特性[[10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]],[[10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]],已广泛开展了支撑的单金属铂催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。 数据表明,硫的添加,无论是在催化剂制备期间还是气相预处理期间,都可以提高Pt/氧化铝的选择性,873 K预处理可以改善初期丙烯的形成[[10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]]。 Pt/氧化铝挤压体上原子层沉积(ALD)金属氧化物涂层增加了收率和选择性,这归因于调节后的金属支撑位点[[10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]]。

        通过Pt-O-Ge连接将亚纳米Pt簇囚禁在Ge-UTL分子筛内,获得了Pt4-Ge2-d4r@UTL催化剂,其在60小时以上表现出优异的稳定性 [[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]]。 镁铝酸盐MgAl2O4支撑的Pt纳米团簇保持了高的初始活性,但由于硫促进的Pt聚集而易于失活[[10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]]。

         计算研究了Pt团簇的核数效应,发现了以Pt2为最大的火山型依赖关系;与增加的C-H激活垒相关的反应活性随尺寸增加而降低[[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]]。 支持Pt催化剂和其他金属的比较有限,但数据显示与Pt的46%相比,Ru纳米颗粒的丙烯选择性很低,仅为4.5% [[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]]。
※※※
		所探讨的研究为丙烷脱氢提供了有价值的关于支撑单金属Pt催化剂设计的见解。 已确定影响稳定性和选择性的关键因素,包括金属支撑物相互作用、硫的影响、微孔结构内的限制、团簇尺寸效应和促进剂。 但是,进一步的工作应该在不同的支持和条件下,系统地比较Pt和其他支持金属(如Pd)。 更深入的光谱表征金属支撑界面可以阐明稳定机制。 机器学习可以帮助高通量催化剂筛选和优化。 总的来说,扩大围绕Pt和其他金属的知识基础可以刺激更稳健PDH催化剂的开发。
