Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Supported PtSn catalysts have been widely studied and utilized commercially for propane dehydrogenation, with the PtSn/Al2O3 catalyst successfully commercialized in the Oleflex process by UOP [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]. Adding Sn as a promoter to Pt catalysts leads to smaller Pt particle size and improves catalytic performance in terms of activity, selectivity and stability compared to monometallic Pt catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018, 10.1002/anie.201507119]. The Sn modifier is found to selectively tune the electronic properties of Pt active sites, directing them towards selective C-H bond activation over undesired C-C bond rupture [10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035]. This increases propylene selectivity while also minimizing coke formation and subsequent deactivation [10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]. 

Computational and experimental isotope studies reveal that Sn atoms inhibit dehydrogenation events from occurring in contiguous Pt atoms, necessitating appropriately spaced Pt ensembles [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]. Hence, an optimal Pt:Sn ratio balancing activity and selectivity has been identified around 3:1 [10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]. At lower Pt:Sn ratios, the electronic modification is prominent but excess Sn poisoning prevails, whereas insufficient Sn leads to uninhibited Pt sites that drive cracking and coking reactions. 

Apart from Sn, alloying Pt with promoters like Ga and Zn has also shown promise for propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. Similar to Sn, Ga and Zn modifiers steer Pt sites towards selective dehydrogenation while suppressing side reactions. The geometric and electronic perturbations engendered by the secondary metals hinder polyatomic hydrocarbon activation, minimizing overcracking and coke deposition [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]. This manifests as enhanced stability and propylene selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/jacs.1c10261].

Furthermore, alloying induces a contraction in the Pt ensemble size by isolating individual atoms [10.1021/jacs.1c10261, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]. This reduction in contiguous Pt sites curbs the incidence of dehydrogenation, restricting consecutive C-H activations and thus improving selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.2c05042]. XAS and STEM-HAADF characterization confirm the presence of isolated Pt atoms coordinated by the secondary metals like Zn and stabilized as PtZn4-6 sites upon alloy formation [10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, 10.1021/jacs.1c10261].

In summary, alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn in supported metal nanoparticles induces electronic and geometric alterations to Pt active sites that direct them towards selective propane dehydrogenation while minimizing cracking and coking reactions. This increases propylene selectivity and enhances catalyst stability. An optimal ratio balancing activity and selectivity exists around Pt:M = 3:1. The alloy structure also isolates Pt atoms, reducing ensemble size which further improves selectivity.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements in elucidating the synergistic promotional effects induced by alloying Pt with secondary metals, future research could utilize this knowledge to design optimal combinations and spatial distributions of isolated Pt sites exhibiting targeted selectivity. For instance, recent progress in site-specific immobilization of single atoms and clusters shows promise in rationally organizing isolated Pt sites [10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8, 10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]. Furthermore, the paradigm of strain and ligand engineering could be extended to modulate and enhance Pt-M intermetallic bonding in surface anchored particles [10.1021/acscatal.2c04889], going beyond bulk thermodynamic configurations. Perturbing and fine-tuning the Pt d-band center utilizing proximal adatoms may allow activating specific reaction channels. Exploring these aspects by converging computational and in-situ spectroscopy would uncover structure-activity insights guiding next-generation propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

支撑的PtSn催化剂已经被广泛研究和商业化利用, PtSn/Al2O3催化剂成功商业化为UOP公司的Oleflex工艺[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.jechem.2020.08.058]。向Pt催化剂中添加Sn促进剂导致Pt颗粒大小变小,并在活性、选择性和稳定性方面相比单金属Pt催化剂改进了催化性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018, 10.1002/anie.201507119]。 Sn修饰剂被发现可以选择性地调谐Pt活性位点的电子性质,使其朝着选择性C-H键活化而不是不desired C-C键断裂[10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035]。 这提高了丙烯选择性,同时最小化焦炭形成和随后deactivate [10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]。

计算和实验同位素研究揭示Sn原子抑制脱氢事件发生在相邻的Pt原子上,需要适当间隔的Pt配合物[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]。 因此,确定了一种Pt:Sn比率约为3:1,平衡活性和选择性[10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。 在较低的Pt:Sn比率时,电子修饰是突出的,但过量的Sn中毒占了上风,而Sn不足导致未抑制的Pt位点驱动裂解和焦炭反应。

除Sn外,Pt与Ga和Zn等促进剂的合金化在丙烷脱氢反应中也显示了前景[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]。 类似于Sn,Ga和Zn修饰剂使Pt位点朝向选择性脱氢,同时抑制副反应。 第二金属引起的几何和电子扰动抑制多原子烃激活,最大限度地减少过度开裂和焦炭沉积[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]。 这表现为增强的稳定性和丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。

此外,合金诱导Pt配合物大小的收缩,隔离单个原子[10.1021/jacs.1c10261, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]。 这种相邻Pt位点的减少抑制了脱氢的发生,限制了连续的C-H激活,从而改善了选择性[10.1021/acscatal.2c05042]。 XAS和STEM-HAADF表征确认了二次金属(如Zn)与Pt形成合金时存在被Zn和稳定为PtZn4-6位点的隔离Pt原子[10.1021/acscatal.2c01631, 10.1021/jacs.1c10261]。

总之,在支撑的金属纳米颗粒中,Pt与Sn、Ga和Zn等促进剂合金化会导致Pt活性位点的电子和几何改变,使其朝着选择性丙烷脱氢反应,同时将裂解和焦化反应最小化。 这提高了丙烯选择性,增强了催化剂稳定性。 在Pt:M = 3:1左右存在一种平衡活性和选择性的最佳比例。 合金结构还隔离Pt原子,减小集合体的大小,这进一步提高了选择性。

※※※

整合性理解与前景展望:鉴于合金化Pt与第二金属所产生的协同促进效应的进展,未来研究可以利用这些知识设计最佳的组合方案和空间分布场所用于丙烷脱氢催化剂。例如,近期在单原子和簇的定点固定化方面取得的进展充满希望[10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8, 10.1038/s41586-020-2671-4]。 此外,应变和配体工程范式可以扩展到调制和增强Pt-M金属间化合物的键合强度[10.1021/acscatal.2c04889], 这超出了泛在的热力学配置。 利用近邻原子扰动和优化Pt d-带中心可能允许激活某些反应通道。 通过汇聚计算和原位光谱研究可以揭示结构-性能关系,为引导下一代丙烷脱氢催化剂铺平道路。
