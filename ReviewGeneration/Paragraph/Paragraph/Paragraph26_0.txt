Based on the details extracted from the references furnished, advanced microscopy techniques have offered pivotal insights into metal-support interfaces in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. 

The work by Wang et al. [10.1021/jacs.2c02636] employs integrated differential phase contrast scanning transmission electron microscopy (iDPC-STEM) to fully identify the location, uniformity, and structure of isolated cobalt active sites embedded at T1(7) and T3(9) sites within the MFI framework. This reveals the {(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} configuration and its role in selective dehydrogenation. Further efforts by Wu et al. [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] integrate aberration-corrected HAADF-STEM with XAS and vibrational spectroscopy to confirm the confinement of subnanometric platinum clusters within germanium-rich regions of ultra-stable zeolite frameworks, alluding to emerging active phase-support synergies. 

In another notable study, Chen et al. [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008] use AC-HAADF-STEM to expose periodic [PtZn4] ensembles on the (110) facets of intermetallic nanoparticles. The images identify isolated single Pt sites periodically surrounded by Zn atoms, conferring optimized selectivity. Additionally, Wang et al. [10.1038/s41467-018-06967-8] employ AC-HAADF-STEM to directly visualize individual Pt atoms embedded within Cu nanoparticles post-reduction. The isolated single sites showcase remarkable stability over successive redox treatments, confirming strong metal-support anchoring.

Such emerging approaches enabling atomic-scale interfacial characterization promise to stimulate further advancements through rational design of proximity effects between active phases and zeolite cavities.
