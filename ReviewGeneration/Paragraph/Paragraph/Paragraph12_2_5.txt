Based on the details provided in the file, several studies have evaluated supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most successful and widely used catalysts for commercial PDH processes [1]. For example, an alumina-supported Pt catalyst showed good ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing C-C bond cleavage [2]. Adding promoters such as 1 wt% boron to Pt/γ-Al2O3 was found to decrease coke formation 3-fold and increase propylene selectivity from 90% to 98% over 12 hours [3]. Pt catalysts supported on other materials like BN [4] and Ge-UTL zeolite [5] also demonstrated excellent PDH performance.

Besides Pt, a few studies have explored supported Pd and Ir catalysts. One article mentions Pd and Ir have high intrinsic activity for C-H bond activation but does not provide further details [6]. An advanced RhCu single atom alloy (SAA) catalyst supported on silica was shown to outperform a standard Pt/Al2O3 catalyst, with higher activity, 100% propene selectivity, and coking resistance [7].

Overall, Pt-based catalysts, especially Pt supported on acidic supports like alumina and zeolites, prevail as the foremost choice for commercial PDH processes. Strategies like adding promoters and stabilizing isolated metal sites continue to enhance Pt's activity and stability. More work is still needed to optimize supported Pd and Ir catalysts for viable industrial implementation.
