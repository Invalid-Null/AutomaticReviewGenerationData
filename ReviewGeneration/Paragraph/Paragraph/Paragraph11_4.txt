The article by Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878] explores metal-support interfacial effects in VOx catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). It systematically investigates how different oxide supports affect the activity of supported VOx catalysts, finding VOx/ZrO2 to have over 4 times higher activity than VOx supported on other oxides. Density functional theory calculations reveal this is attributed to weaker VOx-ZrO2 interactions, leading to lower electron density on bridge oxygen atoms and thus higher capability to rupture C-H bonds in propane. The principles established provide guidance to tune the microenvironment of VO active sites for improved PDH performance.

Additionally, Liu et al. [10.1021/acscatal.5b01679] show that for vanadium oxide on silica supports, the weak interaction with surface silanol groups causes undesired 3D nanoparticle formation. Adding sodium ions creates more reactive SiONa+ sites that anchor 2D vanadia, enhancing dispersion to match other supports. This improves activity while maintaining selectivity.

Furthermore, Zhang et al. [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726] demonstrate that dealumination of zeolite BEA support generates silanol groups that anchor isolated Co2+ cations. These stable cobalt sites activate C-H over C-C bonds in propane, delivering high propylene selectivity. 

These examples highlight how strategic manipulation of metal-support interfaces in oxide catalysts provides targeted pathways to optimize PDH performance.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The studies appraised underscore metal-support interplay as a pivotal handle to direct structural configurations and resultant reactivity. Strategies like ion exchange to modulate anchoring site reactivity, solid-state reaction to alter interface morphology, and post-synthetic dealumination to generate isolated active sites offer tested blueprints. 

Expanding these design tenets to emerging heterojunctions with tunable interfaces (metal/metal oxide, metal/nitride, metal/carbide etc.) can spur innovation.
