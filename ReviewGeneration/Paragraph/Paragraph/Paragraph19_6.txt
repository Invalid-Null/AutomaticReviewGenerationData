Based on the detailed analysis from the references, there are a few examples that demonstrate synergistic effects between metals in bimetallic and alloy propane dehydrogenation catalysts, attributed to ensemble and ligand effects [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01554, 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019, 10.1016/j.chempr.2023.07.002, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]. 

Specifically, the Ru-P catalysts showed smaller Ru ensemble size and isolated Ru atoms due to the increasing P content, which decreased the hydrogenolysis rate [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]. The PtIn2 nanoparticles displayed the largest perturbation in Pt XANES energy and geometric changes, resulting in the highest activity and stability [10.1021/acscatal.0c01554]. The subnanometric Pt-Sn clusters in zeolites had different geometric and electronic structures compared to Pt-Sn nanoparticles, leading to unique reactivity [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. 

In general, adding a secondary metal can physically break down the large ensembles on Pt responsible for side reactions and alter the electronic environment to facilitate desired product desorption [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019, 10.1016/j.chempr.2023.07.002]. For example, alloying Pt with Zn isolates Pt sites and modifies selectivity [10.1016/j.chempr.2023.07.002], while Sn addition changes the electron density of Pt atoms [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The examples discussed demonstrate encouraging progress in elucidating and exploiting synergistic ensemble and ligand effects to advance bimetallic propane dehydrogenation catalysts. However, most studies have focused on traditional Pt-based catalysts. An opportunity exists to explore novel bimetallic combinations by harnessing high-throughput computational screening and state-of-the-art characterization techniques. 

Attention could also turn to affordable transition metals and systematic investigations of ternary combinations. Overall, continued efforts to unravel the intricate interplay between geometric and electronic factors governing selectivity and stability will be instrumental to break new ground.

根据参考文献的详细分析,有几个例子表明双金属和合金丙烷脱氢催化剂中金属之间存在协同集合体和配体效应[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01554, 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019, 10.1016/j.chempr.2023.07.002]。

具体而言,随着P含量的增加,Ru-P催化剂表现出更小的Ru集合体大小和隔离的Ru原子,这降低了氢裂解速率[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。PtIn2纳米粒子显示出最大的Pt XANES能量扰动和几何变化,导致最高的活性和稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.0c01554]。泡沫碱中的亚纳米Pt-Sn簇与Pt-Sn纳米粒子相比,其几何和电子结构不同,导致独特的反应活性[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

通常,添加副金属可以物理破坏Pt上的大集合体,这些集合体负责副反应,并改变电子环境以促进所需产物的解吸[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019, 10.1016/j.chempr.2023.07.002]。 例如,合金Pt与Zn隔离Pt位点并改变选择性[10.1016/j.chempr.2023.07.002],而Sn的添加改变Pt原子的电子密度[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 讨论的示例表明,阐明和利用集合体和配体效应的协同作用,以推进双金属丙烷脱氢催化剂方面取得了令人鼓舞的进展。 然而,大多数研究都集中在传统的Pt基催化剂上。 利用高通量计算筛选和最先进的表征技术来探索新型双金属组合的机会存在。

关注也可能转向可负担得起的过渡金属和系统研究三元组合。 总的来说,继续努力揭示支配选择性和稳定性的几何和电子因素之间错综复杂的相互作用,将是开辟新天地的关键。
