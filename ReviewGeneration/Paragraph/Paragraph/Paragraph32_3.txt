<English>
Several key challenges remain in developing improved propane dehydrogenation catalysts, as summarized across the literature analyzed [10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, 10.1021/cr5002436]. Thermally-induced deactivation continues to be a major issue even for commercially used catalysts, driving needs to further understand deactivation mechanisms and explore more stable formulations [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. Replacing expensive platinum-based systems with lower cost and more selective alternative catalysts is another persisting goal [10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]. Gaining fundamental insights into how factors like oxygen vacancies influence activity/selectivity also remains an open area needing clarification [10.1021/acscatal.1c02621].
※※※ 
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements and findings put forth, opportunities exist to adopt cross-disciplinary techniques from outside traditional chemical engineering domains. For instance, computational modeling and screening methodologies could help systematically survey potential single atom sites, reaction pathways, and cluster configurations [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. And operando spectroscopic characterization may lend fresh perspectives on elucidating ambiguous active phase chemistry noted in some catalyst systems [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]. Pursuing such pioneering angles grounded in interdisciplinary fusion could unlock new horizons and inform next generation propane dehydrogenation systems.
</English>
