The file details several major catalyst classes studied for propane dehydrogenation, including supported metals, metal oxides, alloys, and zeolites. Specifically, supported platinum and chromium oxide catalysts are noted as conventional options used commercially in the Catofin and Oleflex processes \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331\]. Alternative lower-cost catalysts like supported vanadium and iron oxides, gallium oxides, as well as zeolites like H-Beta and cobalt-incorporated BEA have also shown promise \[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\]. While supported metals and metal oxides dominate, intermetallic alloys and single atom catalysts have recently emerged as active and selective options \[10.1021/acscatal.1c01916, 10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63196-1\].
		※※※ 
		With the range of catalyst classes and promising options introduced, continued research into optimizing existing formulations and exploring novel architectures appears worthwhile. Emerging preparation methods may better control active site isolation and metal-support interactions to boost stability and selectivity. Incorporating promoters or dopants could potentially tune catalytic behavior as well. Broader adoption of operando studies can offer insights into working state speciation and deactivation to guide optimization.
