Pt supported on other materials like magnesium aluminum spinel and zeolites has also demonstrated excellent PDH performance before deactivating due to agglomeration or coking.   

Besides Pt, supported Pd, Rh, and Ir are intrinsically active for C-H bond activation but expensive compared to Pt [PlaceHolder_of_DOI4]. Limited data exists on their use as PDH catalysts. One study evaluated a supported RhCu single atom alloy (SAA) catalyst, showing it has higher activity, 100% propene selectivity, and coking resistance compared to a standard Pt/Al2O3 catalyst operating at 80% selectivity [PlaceHolder_of_DOI5]. This highlights the potential of specialty catalysts like SAAs for improving PDH performance.

In summary, Pt-based catalysts, especially Pt supported on metal oxides like alumina and magnesia-alumina spinel, are the most commercially successful catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. Support modifiers and promoters have shown promise for further enhancing mono-metallic Pt catalysts. Other precious metals like Pd, Rh, and Ir are also intrinsically active but remain expensive alternatives to Pt.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The evaluated studies provide significant insights into supported metal catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, with Pt dominating commercial applications.
