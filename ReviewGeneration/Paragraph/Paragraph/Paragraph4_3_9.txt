 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the references, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) faces significant thermodynamic constraints due to being a highly endothermic reaction. Multiple studies note that ΔH298K of 124.3 kJ/mol requires elevated temperatures of 500-650°C to achieve reasonable equilibrium conversion thermodynamically [DOI1, DOI2, DOI3, DOI4, DOI5]. The endothermicity also makes PDH prone to side reactions like cracking and coking at high temperatures [DOI4, DOI6]. 

Some works mention the kinetic factors that control PDH rate and selectivity. These include apparent activation energies of 180-200 kJ/mol [DOI7], barriers for key steps like α-H abstraction around 0.7 eV [DOI8], and turnover frequencies or reaction orders [DOI9]. Choosing sites that balance dehydrogenation activity vs. cracking is also important [DOI10].

Oxidative PDH approaches with O2 or CO2 co-feeds can partially alleviate thermodynamic constraints by providing exothermic oxidation reactions, but they bring additional kinetic complexities [DOI11]. DFT has elucidated some elementary steps, but further kinetic details need more exploration [DOI6, DOI12]. 

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the insights uncovered, we recognize thermodynamic barriers impose intrinsic constraints on PDH conversion, while precise kinetic control of active sites and elementary steps offers opportunities to optimize selectivity. This suggests potential pathways forward may include:

- High-throughput DFT screening of dopants or supports to discover kinetic modifications that deter side reactions 
- Operando spectroscopy under industrially relevant conditions to reveal dominant reaction mechanisms
- Machine learning based on accumulated catalytic data to predict optimal sites balancing activity and selectivity
- Advanced reactor designs that provide thermodynamic driving force through in situ oxidation while preventing kinetic losses

By complementing our strengthening theoretical grasp with additional kinetic details from advanced experiments and simulations, we can uncover the most favorable sites and conditions to push PDH toward its intrinsic thermodynamic limits.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,丙烷脱氢(PDH)由于是高度吸热反应而面临重大的热力学限制。 多个研究指出,298K下的ΔH为124.3 kJ/mol需要升高温度到500-650°C才能在热力学上达到合理的平衡转换率 [DOI1, DOI2, DOI3, DOI4, DOI5]。 在高温下,吸热性还使PDH容易发生裂解和焦炭生成等副反应 [DOI4, DOI6]。

一些文献提到控制PDH速率和选择性的动力学因素。 这些包括表观活化能180-200 kJ/mol [DOI7],关键步骤(如α-H抽象)的屏障约为0.7 eV [DOI8],以及周转数或反应级数 [DOI9]。 平衡脱氢活性和裂解的位点也很重要[DOI10]。

使用O2或CO2作为共饲料的氧化PDH可以通过提供放热的氧化反应部分缓解热力学限制,但它们带来了额外的动力学复杂性[DOI11]。 DFT已经阐明了一些基元步骤,但需要进一步探索动力学细节[DOI6, DOI12]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 考虑到发现的见解,我们认识到热力学屏障对PDH转化施加内在约束,而对活性位点和基元步骤的精确动力学控制为优化选择性提供了机会。这表明潜在的前进道路可能包括:

- 高通量DFT筛选掺杂剂或载体以发现抑制副反应的动力学修改

- 在工业相关条件下的原位光谱来揭示主导反应机制

- 基于积累的催化数据的机器学习来预测平衡活性和选择性的最佳位点

- 先进的反应器设计,通过原位氧化提供动力学驱动力而防止动力学损失

通过用先进的实验和模拟补充我们日益加强的理论把握,我们可以发现最有利的位点和条件,将PDH推向其内在的热力学极限。
