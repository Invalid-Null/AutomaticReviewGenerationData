Based on the details extracted from the file, there is evidence that tuning oxidation states, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties of metal oxides can affect propane dehydrogenation performance. 

For example, one study showed that modifying VOx/Al2O3 catalysts with phosphine treatment tunes surface acidity, separates polymerized vanadium species to form more isolated species, and weakens vanadium-support interactions [10.1016/j.apcatb.2020.119089]. These changes inhibit coking and result in enhanced catalytic stability, at the expense of some loss in initial activity. 

Another study revealed that partly reduced V3+/V4+ species on vanadium oxides are the active sites for propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]. It also showed that the chemical states of bridge oxygen in the VO bond play critical roles in activating the C-H bond of propane. Weaker VOx-support interactions lead to lower electron density on bridge oxygen, enhancing its capability to rupture the C-H bond.

For gallium oxide polymorphs, the catalytic activity correlates with surface acid site density, with β-Ga2O3 showing the highest density and also highest propane dehydrogenation activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]. No clear effects of tuning oxidation states or oxygen mobility were revealed.

Adding ZnO to different ZrO2-based supports greatly enhances propane dehydrogenation activity, likely by modifying the oxidation state and oxygen mobility of the supports [10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]. ZnO helps generate isolated, tricoordinated Zn2+ sites upon high temperature calcination, which then insert into the ZrO2 lattice, disrupting its oxygen mobility.  

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The studies analyzed reveal promising opportunities for tuning metal oxide catalysts by controlling oxidation states, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties. Key active sites like isolated VOx, Zn2+, and Ga3+ species have been identified, along with critical roles of surface oxygen and support interactions. 

However, many questions remain regarding optimizing these catalyst parameters and translating the knowledge into viable industrial processes. More research is needed on stabilized catalysts that balance activity, selectivity and longevity. Operando spectroscopy techniques combined with kinetic and DFT modeling will likely play an important role. The advent of single atom catalysts also opens up new horizons for highly efficient and selective propane dehydrogenation.

根据文件提取的详细信息,有证据表明调整金属氧化物的氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性能影响丙烷脱氢性能。

例如,一项研究显示,用磷化氢处理改性的VOx/Al2O3催化剂调节了表面酸性,将聚合的钒种分离形成更多的隔离种,并削弱了钒与支撑体的相互作用[10.1016/j.apcatb.2020.119089]。这些变化抑制了结焦,以某种初期活性的损失为代价,提高了催化稳定性。

另一项研究显示,V3+/V4+的部分还原态是钒氧化物上丙烷脱氢的活性位点[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。它还显示,VO键中桥氧的化学态在激活丙烷的C-H键中起关键作用。 更弱的VOx与支撑体的相互作用导致桥氧上的电子密度更低,增强了其断裂C-H键的能力。

对于氧化镓多晶体,催化活性与表面酸性位点的密度相关,β-Ga2O3表现出最高的密度和最高的丙烷脱氢活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]。没有明确的调节氧化态或氧迁移的效果。

在不同的ZrO2基体上添加ZnO大大提高了丙烷脱氢活性,这可能是通过改变支撑体的氧化态和氧迁移来实现的[10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]。 ZnO有助于在高温煅烧下生成孤立的、三配位的Zn2+位点,这些位点然后插入ZrO2晶格,破坏其氧迁移能力。

※※※

综合理解与前景展望:分析的研究结果揭示了通过控制氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性调节金属氧化物催化剂的前景广阔。 像VOx、Zn2+和 Ga3+等关键活性位点已被确定,以及表面氧和支撑体相互作用的关键作用。

然而,关于优化这些催化剂参数和将知识转化为可行的工业过程,还有许多问题有待研究。 需要对平衡活性、选择性和长期稳定性的稳定催化剂进行更多研究。 联合光谱技术与动力学和DFT建模可能发挥重要作用。 单原子催化剂的出现也为高效选择性的丙烷脱氢反应开辟了新地平线。
