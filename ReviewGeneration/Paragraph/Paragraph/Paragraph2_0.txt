Based on the details extracted from the references, catalysts facilitate propane dehydrogenation by providing alternative reaction pathways and active sites that lower the activation energy barriers [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.025]. Industrial PDH processes utilize supported metal catalysts like Pt/Al2O3 and CrOx/Al2O3 that activate C-H bonds in propane for dehydrogenation [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]. DFT calculations have elucidated the active site structure on these catalysts, showing how neighboring under-coordinated metal cations provide adsorption sites for propane that facilitate dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]. Metal-exchanged zeolites also introduce Lewis acid sites that selectively break C-H bonds over C-C bonds [10.1002/advs.202207756]. The specific reaction mechanisms often involve initial dissociative chemisorption of propane with elimination of H, followed by abstraction of a β-H to form propylene [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. By tuning the active site geometry and electronic properties, catalysts can achieve the appropriate balance of reactivity to break C-H bonds while avoiding over-reaction [10.1016/S1872-2067(18)63196-1].

The findings reviewed demonstrate how rational catalyst design based on mechanistic understanding can overcome the intrinsic kinetic and thermodynamic challenges in propane dehydrogenation. As shale gas resources continue to expand the availability of light alkane feedstocks, propane dehydrogenation presents a major opportunity to meet rising propylene demands through sustainable catalytic processes. Future research should focus on utilizing advanced characterization and computational methods to further optimize catalysts by identifying descriptors for C-H activation selectivity over C-C cleavage and coking resistance. In addition, operando spectroscopic techniques could elucidate dynamic structural changes during propane dehydrogenation to guide the development of stable, selective catalysts. Broader adoption of these structure-guided design principles could accelerate the deployment of direct propane dehydrogenation technologies.
