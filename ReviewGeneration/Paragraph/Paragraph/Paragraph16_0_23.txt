Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Supported PtSn catalysts have been widely studied and utilized commercially for propane dehydrogenation due to their high activity, selectivity and stability [DOI1, DOI2]. Alloying Pt with Sn helps decrease the size of Pt ensembles, alter the electronic properties of Pt, and suppress side reactions like coke formation and hydrogenolysis, thereby improving propylene selectivity [DOI3-6]. However, some studies note initial activity can decrease with Sn addition [DOI7], likely due to increased barriers for propane dissociation over the PtSn alloy [DOI8]. An optimal Pt:Sn ratio around 2:1 - 3:1 tends to balance activity and selectivity [DOI9-11].  

Besides Sn, other promoters like Ga and Zn have shown promise when alloyed with Pt. Adding Ga helps disperse Pt sites and suppresses coking reactions similar to Sn [DOI12-13]. PtZn intermetallic compounds can strongly modulate the valence orbitals and ensemble effects of Pt [DOI14]. Isolated PtZn4-6 sites anchored on dealuminated zeolites achieved remarkable performance for propane dehydrogenation [DOI15]. Overall, the studies highlight that alloying Pt with secondary metals like Sn, Ga, Zn can effectively tune catalytic properties and enhance performance through electronic, geometric and ensemble effects. However, the synergistic balance between activity, selectivity and stability depends sensitively on factors like Pt:metal ratio, alloy structure, and support effects. Further optimization of these parameters presents opportunities to design superior propane dehydrogenation catalysts using Pt alloys.  

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The diverse collection of studies underscore how alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn can effectively enhance propane dehydrogenation performance through electronic, steric and structural modulation of the active sites. While existing PtSn, PtGa and PtZn catalysts demonstrate promising activity, selectivity and stability, limitations persist due to sub-optimal Pt:metal ratios, phase segregation, and activity-selectivity trade-offs. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques combined with computational modeling presents a prospective avenue to offer atomic-level insights, quantify structure-performance correlations, and facilitate knowledge-driven design of optimal bimetallic alloy configurations. An underexplored but promising direction lies in extending the alloy composition to ternary and quaternary formulations through multi-metal co-promotion, which could synergistically harness the promotional effects of various secondary metals. Lastly, the application arena could be expanded by investigating bimetallic Pt alloys for related alkane dehydrogenation reactions like ethane, butane etc. and within alternative process configurations like oxydehydrogenation.

双金属和合金催化剂 —— Pt 与 Sn、Ga、Zn 合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

支持的 PtSn 催化剂由于其高活性、选择性和稳定性而被广泛研究和商业利用 [DOI1,DOI2]。Pt 与 Sn 合金化有助于减小 Pt 聚集体的大小,改变 Pt 的电子性质,抑制副反应如焦炭生成和氢解,从而提高丙烯选择性 [DOI3-6]。然而,一些研究表明,Sn 的添加可能会降低初始活性 [DOI7],这可能是由于 PtSn 合金上丙烷解离屏障的增加所致 [DOI8]。2:1 至 3:1 的优化 Pt:Sn 比例往往可以在活性和选择性之间达到平衡 [DOI9-11]。

除 Sn 外,其他助剂如 Ga 和 Zn 与 Pt 合金化也显示出前景。添加 Ga 有助于分散 Pt 位点,抑制焦炭反应,与 Sn 类似 [DOI12-13]。PtZn 互化化合物可以强烈调控 Pt 的价电子层和配位效应 [DOI14]。支撑在去铝 zeolite 上的 PtZn4-6 隔离位点实现了卓越的丙烷脱氢性能 [DOI15]。总的来说,这些研究突出了 Sn、Ga 和 Zn 等次级金属与 Pt 合金化可以通过电子效应、几何效应和配位效应有效地调控催化性质和增强性能。然而,活性、选择性和稳定性之间的协同平衡对 Pt:金属比例、合金结构和载体效应等因素高度敏感。这些参数的进一步优化为利用 Pt 合金设计优秀的丙烷脱氢催化剂提供了机遇。

※※※  

综合理解与未来展望: 不同研究突出了 Sn、Ga 和 Zn 等助剂与 Pt 合金化可以通过电子效应、空间位阻效应和结构调控有效地提高丙烷脱氢性能。虽然现有的 PtSn、PtGa 和 PtZn 催化剂表现出很有前景的活性、选择性和稳定性,但由于 Pt:金属比例不理想、相分离和活性与选择性的权衡仍存在限制。先进的原位表征技术和计算建模为原子水平的洞察,定量的结构-性能关系,以及知识驱动的最优双金属合金配置设计提供了前景。一个未充分开发但有前途的方向是通过多金属共助剂扩大合金成分范围至三元和四元配体,这可能协同利用不同助剂的促进效应。最后,应用领域可以通过研究相关的烷烃脱氢反应(如乙烷、丁烷等)中的双金属 Pt 合金以及其他工艺配置(如氧化脱氢)来拓展。
