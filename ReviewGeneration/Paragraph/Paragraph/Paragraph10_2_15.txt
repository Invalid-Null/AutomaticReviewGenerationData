<English>
Based on the details extracted from the literature, mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over several metal oxide catalysts, with some key aspects summarized as follows:

On Cr-based oxides, active sites are proposed to be Cr3+ species [DOI1, DOI2], potentially with coordinative vacancies [DOI3], formed via redox cycles from Cr6+ or Cr2+ [DOI4]. Reaction steps may involve oxidative hydrogen removal to form propylene [DOI5].

For V-based oxides, Mars-van Krevelen type pathways are described, where lattice oxygen activates propane, leaving reduced V centers that reoxidize with O2 [DOI6, DOI7]. Isolated V5+ sites reduce to lower oxidation states upon propane activation [DOI8].   

Ga-containing oxides are proposed to catalyze propane dehydrogenation through a heterolytic dissociation mechanism, with Ga3+ sites dissociating C-H bonds [DOI9, DOI10]. Subsequent H removal and recombinative desorption regenerates the Ga3+ sites [DOI11].

On ZrO2, coordinatively unsaturated Zr cations (ZrCus) formed at oxygen vacancies are suggested as the active sites that break C-H bonds together with lattice oxygen [DOI12, DOI13]. Neighboring ZrCus sites may cooperate in activating different C-H bonds [DOI14].

Additionally, synergies between Zn, Zr, and Ti sites are proposed, with isolated Zn2+ and ZrCus working in tandem [DOI15]. Reaction pathways have also been described over mixed metal oxides like MoVO [DOI16].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Several mechanistic aspects have come to light regarding propane dehydrogenation over metal oxides, including identification of plausible active sites, intermediates, and individual reaction steps. However, a unified mechanistic picture encompassing route bifurcations, thermodynamic and kinetic controls, multiscale simulations, and descriptor development is still lacking. Future undertakings may focus on bridging this gap through integrated theoretical and experimental efforts targeting commercially relevant catalyst formulations. Explorations of new catalytically active nanostructures and elucidation of explicit reaction networks therein also offer research and development opportunities.

根据从文献中提取的详细信息,己经为几种金属氧化物催化的丙烷脱氢提出了反应机理, 主要概括如下:  

在以Cr为基的氧化物上, 活性位点被提出为Cr3+ 种类 [DOI1, DOI2], 可能具有配体空位 [DOI3], 通过Cr6+ 或Cr2+的红氧循环形成 [DOI4]。反应步骤可能涉及氧化氢的去除以形成丙烯 [DOI5]。

对于以V为基的氧化物, 描述了Mars-van Krevelen类型的途径, 其中晶格氧活化丙烷, 留下还原的V中心, 这些中心与O2再氧化 [DOI6, DOI7]。 隔离的V5+位点在丙烷活化时减少至较低的氧化态 [DOI8]。

Ga含量氧化物被认为通过杂解离解机理催化丙烷脱氢, Ga3+位点解离C-H键 [DOI9, DOI10]。 随后通过去除氢和重组脱附再生Ga3+位点 [DOI11]。

在ZrO2上, 氧空位形成的配位不饱和Zr阳离子 (ZrCus) 被认为是活性位点, 与晶格氧一起断裂C-H键 [DOI12, DOI13]。 相邻的ZrCus位点可能协同激活不同的C-H键[DOI14]。  

此外, Zn、Zr和Ti位点之间的协同作用也已被提出, 其中孤立的Zn2+和Zrcus位点协同工作 [DOI15]。 MoVO等混合金属氧化物也已描述了反应途径 [DOI16]。

※※※  

综合理解与未来展望: 已经揭示出几个有关金属氧化物催化丙烷脱氢的机理方面, 包括识别出可能的活性位点、中间体和单个反应步骤。 但是, 一个统一的机理图片, 囊括路径分歧、热力学和动力学控制、多尺度模拟和描述符的开发, 仍然缺乏。未来的努力可能会通过针对商业上相关的催化剂配方的综合理论和实验工作来弥合这一差距。 对新型催化活性纳米结构的探索以及其中的明确反应网络的阐明也为研发提供了机会。
