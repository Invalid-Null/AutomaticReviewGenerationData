Based on the details extracted from the references, catalysts facilitate propane dehydrogenation by providing alternative reaction pathways and active sites that lower the activation energy barriers [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.025]. Industrial PDH processes utilize supported metal catalysts like Pt/Al2O3 and CrOx/Al2O3 that activate C-H bonds in propane for selective dehydrogenation over cracking reactions [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]. The molecular sieve and acid-base properties of supports like zeolites and mesoporous silicas contribute to stabilizing these active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. DFT calculations have elucidated reaction mechanisms showing proton transfer steps to form propylene [10.1021/acscatal.0c01580, 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019]. Alternative catalyst formulations like bimetallics and metal oxides aim to improve selectivity and stability [10.1021/acscatal.3c00939, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]. By providing selective active sites, catalysts make direct PDH an economically viable process for on-purpose propylene production [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The reviewed studies establish how supported metal catalysts enable commercial PDH processes by promoting C-H bond activation while minimizing secondary cracking/hydrogenolysis reactions. Opportunities exist to further tune selectivity through novel synthetic strategies producing optimized active site isolation and metal-support interfaces. Operando spectroscopic characterization paired with microkinetic modeling can provide molecular-level insights to guide rational design. Broader adoption of high-throughput DFT screening offers potential to rapidly assess new compositions. Future work should also consider process intensification via structured reactors to improve heat/mass transfer limitations. There is ample room for innovation at the intersection of materials chemistry, reaction engineering, and computational modeling that can unlock the next generation of selective and stable PDH catalysts.
