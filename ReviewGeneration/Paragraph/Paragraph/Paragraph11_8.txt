The article by Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878] explores metal-support interfacial effects in VOx catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). It systematically investigates the effects of different oxide supports including ZrO2, TiO2, SiO2, and Al2O3 on the PDH activity of supported VOx catalysts. It is found that VOx/ZrO2 displays over 4 times higher activity compared to VOx supported on the other oxides. Density functional theory calculations reveal that this enhanced activity is attributed to weaker VOx-ZrO2 interactions, which lead to lower electron density on the bridge oxygen atoms in the VOx active phase. The weakened metal-support interaction causes the bridge oxygen atoms to have increased capability to rupture the C-H bonds in propane during the initial dehydrogenation step. 

Similarly, another study by Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.5b01679] explores the role of metal-support interactions by investigating silica-supported vanadium oxide catalysts. They find that the surface silanol groups on silica lead to undesired 3D nanoparticle growth compared to other common supports, negatively impacting catalyst performance. Modification of the support by adding sodium ions helps generate more reactive SiONa+ sites that anchor isolated vanadia species, restoring 2D dispersion and enhancing activity while maintaining selectivity.

Overall, these examples demonstrate how weaker metal-support interactions can modulate the electron density and geometric structure of active sites in supported metal oxide catalysts to promote their capability for selective dehydrogenation during PDH. Further studies to tune the interfacial properties could contribute to the development of stable and selective catalysts.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reveal metal-support interfacial engineering as a promising approach to enhance supported metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation by optimizing active site isolation, stability and selectivity. Building on these fundamental insights, future research might focus on high-throughput computational screening of novel support materials and metal/support combinations to identify optimal interfacial interactions. There is also scope for multi-scale characterization spanning bulk to atomic-level structure-performance correlations. Broader adoption of operando techniques can shed light on working state interfacial phenomena during PDH. Ultimately, knowledge accrued from interfacial tuning efforts could inform the design of tailored single atom catalysts with isolated metal sites precisely anchored on optimized support surfaces.

Wang等人的文章[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]探讨了VOx催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的金属-载体界面效应。该文系统地研究了不同氧化物载体ZrO2、TiO2、SiO2 和Al2O3对支撑VOx催化剂丙烷脱氢活性的影响。结果发现,VOx/ZrO2的活性比其它样品高出4倍以上。基于密度泛函理论计算揭示,这种增强的活性源自较弱的VOx-ZrO2相互作用,该相互作用导致桥氧原子上电子密度降低。金属-载体相互作用的削弱,增强了桥氧原子断裂丙烷分子中C-H键的能力。

类似地,Wang等人的另一项研究[10.1021/acscatal.5b01679] 通过调查硅氧化物支撑的钒氧化物催化剂,探讨了金属-载体界面效应的作用。他们发现,与其他常见载体相比,二氧化硅表面的硅醇基团会导致不期望的三维纳米粒子生长,对催化剂性能产生负面影响。通过添加钠离子改性载体材料,有助于生成更具反应活性的SiONa+位点,锚定隔离的钒氧活性物种,从而恢复二维分散度,在保持选择性的同时提高活性。 

总的来说,这些例子证明,更弱的金属-载体相互作用可以调节支撑金属氧化物催化剂中活性位点的电子密度和几何结构,以促进其在丙烷脱氢过程中选择性脱氢的能力。进一步研究界面的调控,将有助于开发稳定和选择性的催化剂��
