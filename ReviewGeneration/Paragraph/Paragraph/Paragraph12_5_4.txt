Here is a comprehensive review section on propane dehydrogenation catalysts, with a focus on supported metal catalysts and specifically Pt, Pd, and Ir:

Platinum-based catalysts are widely considered among the most effective for propane dehydrogenation (PDH), and have been commercially used in industry processes like Oleflex due to Pt's superior capacity to activate C-H bonds while minimizing C-C cleavage [[DOI 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7], [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]]. Data shows alumina-supported Pt catalysts can achieve 15-25% propane conversion with 80% propylene selectivity [[DOI 10.1126/science.abg7894]], although stability over long reaction periods remains a key challenge due to coke deposition and metal agglomeration [[DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]].

Strategies like sulfur addition [[DOI 10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]], atomic layer deposition (ALD) metal oxide overcoats [[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]], and boron promotion [[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]] have all proven effective at enhancing Pt catalyst stability and lifetime for PDH. There are also indications that strong metal-support interactions in materials like Pt/BN can improve stability by limiting agglomeration [[DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]].

Beyond Pt, far less data exists examining supported Pd, Ir, or other precious metals for propane dehydrogenation. One study noted the high intrinsic PDH activity of these metals but high costs that limit viability [[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]]. Overall, Pt-based catalysts remain the predominant choice for selective dehydrogenation, with decades of commercial use and continuous innovation focused on this system.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The comprehensive analysis has spotlighted platinum's unique properties that drive its prominence as the definitive PDH catalyst today. Yet as research unearths new materials like single atom alloys with alternatively tuned selectivity [[DOI 10.1126/science.abg8389]], metal-free catalysts circumventing coking issues [[DOI 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63369-3]], and stabilization techniques augmenting stability, we may witness a gradual diversification from Pt as the default option. Future efforts can continue building foundational knowledge on emergent catalyst classes while refining multifaceted strategies to boost existing commercial catalysts. Broader adoption of high-throughput methods and data science can also accelerate progress by rapidly screening materials and deriving structure-performance descriptors.

这是一个关于丙烷脱氢催化剂的综述部分,重点关注支持金属催化剂,特别是铂、钯和铑:

基于铂的催化剂被广泛认为是丙烷脱氢反应最有效的催化剂之一,由于铂优越的C-H键活化能力及较少的C-C键断裂, 已经商业上应用于Oleflex等工艺中[[DOI 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7], [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]]。数据表明,负载在氧化铝上的铂催化剂可以实现15-25%的丙烷转化率,丙烯选择性达80% [[DOI 10.1126/science.abg7894]], 虽然由于炭沉积和金属团聚,铂催化剂的稳定性和使用寿命仍然是一个关键挑战[[DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]]。

添加硫 [[DOI 10.1006/jcat.1994.1333]]、原子层沉积(ALD)金属氧化物覆层 [[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]]和硼掺杂[[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]] 等策略已经被证明可以有效提高铂催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的稳定性和使用寿命。 铂/BN等材料中强的金属支撑相互作用也可能通过抑制团聚从而提高稳定性[[DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]]。

除了铂之外,关于负载钯、铑等贵金属催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应的研究数据还很少。 一项研究指出这些金属具有较高的固有脱氢活性,但高成本限制了其应用[[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]]。总的来说,铂催化剂仍然是选择性脱氢的优选催化剂, 已经商业化使用几十年并且持续的创新都是基于这一体系。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望: 这篇综述凸显了铂独特的性质,从而驱动其成为当今丙烷脱氢不二之选。但是,随着像单原子合金 [[DOI 10.1126/science.abg8389]]、金属游离催化剂 [[DOI 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63369-3]] 和稳定性增强技术的发现,我们可能会看到催化剂的多样化,而不仅仅局限于铂。未来的工作可以继续为新兴催化剂类型奠定基础知识的同时,也要提高现有商业催化剂的性能。采用高通量方法和数据科学也可以通过快速筛选材料和寻找结构-性能关系来加速进度。
