 <English>
Propylene is a crucial chemical feedstock utilized for producing a wide variety of products, including polypropylene, acrylonitrile, propylene oxide, acetone, etc. \cite{10.1021/acscatal.3c00816}. There exists a substantial and expanding propylene supply-demand disparity that customary production techniques cannot satisfy owing to crude oil depletion \cite{10.1021/acscatal.3c00816}. Propane dehydrogenation has surfaced as an alluring process to generate propylene by harnessing abundant shale gas resources \cite{10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008}.  

Propane dehydrogenation forms the basis for large-scale propylene generation from economical propane obtained via shale/natural gas \cite{10.1021/acscatal.0c01455}. Propylene is among the most sought-after monomers in the petrochemical business \cite{10.1021/acscatal.0c01455}. This crucial organic feedstock faces a considerable shortfall resulting from the quick expansion in market demand for derivatives \cite{10.1021/acscatal.0c03999}.

※※※

By scrutinizing recent advancements in propane dehydrogenation catalysts, this assessment offers an expansive perspective. There exist opportunities to explore emerging realms like computational catalyst screening and machine learning-empowered optimizations. Future pursuits might concentrate on elevating propylene selectivity and equilibrium conversion, potentially via tuning bimetallic interactions. The utilization of shale gas could be enhanced by melding propane dehydrogenation with other chemical production pathways in a bio- or petro-refinery setting.

从廉价的丙烷原料中大规模生产丙烯的基础是丙烷脱氢\cite{10.1021/acscatal.0c01455}。丙烯是石化行业中最受欢迎的单体之一\cite{10.1021/acscatal.0c01455}。这种关键有机反应物面临着来自产品衍生物市场需求快速增长的严重短缺\cite{10.1021/acscatal.0c03999}。
