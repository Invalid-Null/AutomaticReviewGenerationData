Propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and pathways have been explored using density functional theory (DFT) calculations to provide valuable insights at the molecular level. These computational studies have elucidated reaction kinetics, identified key intermediates and transition states, and revealed structure-activity relationships across different catalysts.

Several works have applied DFT methods to investigate propane dehydrogenation specifically over zinc-based catalysts. Calculations revealed that Zn-O-Zn sites possess lower apparent activation barriers of 1.81 eV compared to single Zn2+ sites at 2.14 eV, correlating well with higher experimentally observed activity ([Placeholder_Of_DOI2]). DFT also enabled analysis of dehydrogenation mechanisms in closed and open Sn-Beta zeolite sites, suggesting the direct pathway is kinetically more favorable over the indirect route ([Placeholder_Of_DOI6]). Entropy effects and the stabilizing role of extra-framework Zn species were also analyzed using a combination of periodic models and molecular cluster approaches with corrections for dispersion interactions ([Placeholder_Of_DOI19]).

Platinum-based catalysts have been another major focus of computational propane dehydrogenation studies. DFT has helped identify trends in selectivity over Pt alloys by comparing propylene binding energies versus dehydrogenation barriers ([Placeholder_Of_DOI17]). A propylene desorption versus dehydrogenation descriptor was proposed to evaluate relative rates controlling selectivity ([Placeholder_Of_DOI17]). Microkinetic modeling based on DFT energetics has revealed the initial C-H activation step of propane, further dehydrogenation of propylene, and C-C bond breaking of propyne to be among the most kinetically relevant ([Placeholder_Of_DOI17]). DFT also provided evidence that step sites on Pt are responsible for coke formation whereas close-packed PtSn surfaces suppress coking tendencies ([Placeholder_Of_DOI22]).

Beyond metals, DFT has also explored single atom catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, including isolated Pt species anchored on boron and nitrogen-doped graphene ([Placeholder_Of_DOI20]). Tunable effects of different dopants and defects allow adjusting catalytic performance. Reaction mechanisms and the preferred initial dissociation versus hydrogen abstraction pathways were investigated over these catalysts.

Overall DFT calculations have delivered fundamental insights ranging from electronic structure effects to microkinetic descriptions of propane dehydrogenation over major catalyst classes, paving the way for knowledge-driven optimization.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: DFT methods have successfully elucidated propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and guided catalyst design so far. However, most studies employ model surfaces and clusters under vacuum conditions. Capturing relevant spectroscopic, solvation and entropy effects during reactions remains an ongoing challenge. Emerging embedded quantum mechanical/molecular mechanical (QM/MM) approaches that retain an accurate DFT description of the active site embedded within a classical representation of the surroundings could provide a more realistic catalytic environment. Applying such multi-scale techniques to propane dehydrogenation could uncover further insights. 

Kinetically relevant intermediates and pathways identified from static DFT energetics also require validation from dynamic trajectories. Ab initio molecular dynamics and kinetic Monte Carlo simulations with on-the-fly DFT evaluations could strengthen our mechanistic interpretations and kinetic models. Ultimately, machine learning integration leveraging accumulated DFT and experimental datasets may expedite exploration of unprecedented propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

使用密度泛函理论(DFT)计算研究丙烷脱氢机制和路径,可以在分子水平上提供有价值的见解。这些计算研究阐明了反应动力学、识别了关键中间体和转化态,并揭示了不同催化剂的结构-活性关系。

几项工作应用DFT方法专门研究锌基催化剂上的丙烷脱氢。计算结果显示Zn-O-Zn位点的表观活化屏障为1.81eV,低于Zn^2+单位点的2.14eV,与实验观察到的更高活性相关([Placeholder_Of_DOI2])。 DFT还可以分析封闭和开放Sn-Beta沸石位点中的脱氢机制,表明直接途径在动力学上优于间接途径([Placeholder_Of_DOI6])。使用周期模型和分子簇方法的组合,并校正色散作用,分析了熵效应和额外结构Zn种的稳定作用。

铂基催化剂是计算丙烷脱氢研究的另一个重点。 DFT有助于通过比较丙烯的吸附能与脱氢屏障来识别Pt合金上选择性的趋势([Placeholder_Of_DOI17])。提出了丙烯解吸与脱氢的描述符,用于评估控制选择性的相对速率([Placeholder_Of_DOI17])。 基于DFT能量的微动力学建模揭示了丙烷的初始C-H激活步骤、丙烯的进一步脱氢以及丙炔的C-C键断裂是最具动力学相关性的步骤之一([Placeholder_Of_DOI17])。 DFT还提供了证据表明Pt基体位点负责焦炭形成,而PtSn紧密包层表面抑制焦炭倾向([Placeholder_Of_DOI22])。

除金属外,DFT还探讨了丙烷脱氢的单原子催化剂,包括锚定在掺硼和氮化石墨烯上的隔离Pt种([Placeholder_Of_DOI20])。 不同掺杂剂和缺陷的可调效应允许调节催化性能。 在这些催化剂上研究了反应机理和首选的初始解离与氢提取得径。

总的来说,DFT计算为从电子结构效应到主要催化剂类别的丙烷脱氢的微动力学描述提供了基本的见解,为知识驱动的优化铺平了道路。

综合理解与未来展望:到目前为止,DFT方法已经成功阐明了丙烷脱氢机制并指导催化剂设计。 然而,大多数研究采用模型表面和团簇在真空条件下。 在反应过程中捕捉相关的光谱、溶剂和熵效应仍然是一个持续的挑战。新出现的嵌入量子力学/分子力学(QM/MM)方法在保留活性位点的精确DFT描述的同时,使用经典方法表示环境,这可能提供一个更加现实的催化环境。 将这种多尺度技术应用于丙烷脱氢可能会发现进一步的见解。

从静态DFT能量得出的动力学相关中间体和通道也需要从动力学轨迹进行验证。 在the-fly DFT评价的条件下,从头分子动力学和动力学Monte Carlo仿真可以加强我们对机理解释和动力学模型的解释。 最终,机器学习整合利用积累的DFT和实验数据集可能会加速探索前所未有的丙烷脱氢催化剂。
