Based on the details extracted from the file, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts have been widely studied using X-ray based techniques to probe oxidation state changes during catalysis. Specifically, X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) methods like X-ray absorption near edge structure (XANES) and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) have been powerful tools to track changes in metal oxidation states and coordination environments under reaction conditions. 

For example, XANES data on Cr-based catalysts revealed reduction of Cr(VI) species to Cr(III) during initial PDH reaction stages [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]. In situ EXAFS on PtSn nanoparticles showed dealloying and Sn oxidation during regeneration, followed by PtSn re-alloying after subsequent H2 reduction [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018]. These examples demonstrate how XAS can elucidate reaction-induced transformations in active site structures.

X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) has also been widely utilized. By probing binding energy shifts in photoelectron spectra, researchers have tracked changes in oxidation states of metals like V, Ga, and Zn under various catalytic treatments. An instance is the XPS detection of partial Zn2+ reduction to active Znδ+ species during propane dehydrogenation [DOI: 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034].

※※※

The insights revealed by X-ray techniques highlight the value of in-situ/operando studies for elucidating catalyst structural evolutions under working conditions. However, most reports have focused on tracking oxidation state changes of specific active materials during short-term lab testing. To enable industrial advancement, future undertakings should investigate oxidation state transformations in commercial catalysts under extended reaction-regeneration cycling. Modern facilities with combined XAS, XPS, IR, and microscopic capabilities could lead prolonged operando observations. Knowledge gained can guide innovations in tailored catalyst synthesis and process intensification.

根据文件中提取的详细信息,丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂已经被广泛地研究,使用了X射线技术来探测催化过程中的氧化态变化。 具体来说,X射线吸收光谱(XAS)方法,如X射线近边吸收结构(XANES)和扩展X射线吸收细结构(EXAFS)一直是跟踪金属氧化态和配位环境变化的有力工具。   

例如,Cr基催化剂的XANES数据显示,在最初的PDH反应阶段,Cr(VI)物种被还原为Cr(III)[DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。 PtSn纳米粒子的原位EXAFS显示,在再生过程中发生脱配位和Sn氧化,随后在H2还原后再形成PtSn合金[DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018]。 这些例子表明,XAS可以阐明反应诱导的活性位点结构转化。  

X射线光电子能谱(XPS)也被广泛利用。 通过检测光电子能谱中的结合能迁移,研究人员已经跟踪了V、Ga和Zn等金属在各种催化处理下的氧化态变化。 一个实例是,XPS检测到Zn2+在丙烷脱氢过程中发生部分还原形成活性Znδ+物种[DOI: 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034]。  

※※※  

X射线技术揭示的见解强调了原位/在位研究的价值,以阐明工作条件下催化剂结构的演变。 然而,大多数报道都集中在短期实验室测试条件下特定活性材料氧化态变化的跟踪。 为实现工业进步,未来的工作应该考察商业催化剂在长时间反应-再生循环过程中的氧化态变化。 现代设备与XAS、XPS、IR和微观能力的结合可以进行长时间的原位观测。 获得的知识可以指导催化剂合成和过程强化方面的创新。
