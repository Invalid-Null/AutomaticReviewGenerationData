Based on the details extracted from the file, most of the articles do not describe any synergistic effects between metals in bimetallic or alloy propane dehydrogenation catalysts, attributed to ensemble and/or ligand effects. However, a few articles provide relevant examples:

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667 shows that increasing P content in Ru-P catalysts reduces the Ru ensemble size and isolates Ru atoms (ensemble effect), which decreases hydrogenolysis. It also indicates that longer Ru-Ru bonds in Ru-P versus Ru weaken metal-adsorbate interactions (ligand effect), improving selectivity.

The article with DOI 10.1002/anie.202107210 demonstrates combining electronic promotion from Ca and geometric blocking by Pb in a bimetallic catalyst leads to synergistic improvements in propane dehydrogenation stability. 

In the PtZn catalysts studied in 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008, the [PtZn4] surface ensembles were found to isolate the Pt active sites and alter the electronic state through ligand effects. This weakens propylene adsorption and inhibits over-dehydrogenation, improving selectivity.

The article with DOI 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019 generally describes how adding a secondary metal to Pt can block sites on the Pt surface (ensemble effect) and alter the electron density of the Pt atoms (ligand effect), leading to improved catalytic performance.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The few examples found demonstrate the potential for synergistic ensemble and ligand effects in modifying the selectivity and activity of propane dehydrogenation catalysts. However, most of the articles surveyed do not investigate these effects in depth. Future research could systematically explore how secondary metals influence the isolation and electronic properties of active sites in various bimetallic systems. Advanced characterization techniques probing geometry and electronic structure at the atomic level would shed light on the origins of synergistic effects. Combining this with kinetics and computational studies may uncover new alloy compositions and nanostructures that optimize the trade-offs between activity, selectivity and stability. More broadly, these concepts could be extended to other catalytic reactions beyond propane dehydrogenation.

根据文件中提取的详细信息,大多数文章没有描述双金属或合金丙烷脱氢催化剂中的金属之间的任何协同效应,这些效应归因于集合体和/或配体效应。 但是,一些文章提供了相关示例:

DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667的文章表明,增加Ru-P催化剂中的P含量会减小Ru集合体的大小并隔离Ru原子(集合体效应),这会降低加氢裂解。它还表明,与Ru相比,Ru-P中的较长Ru-Ru键会削弱金属吸附剂相互作用(配体效应),从而提高选择性。

DOI 10.1002/anie.202107210的文章证明,在双金属催化剂中,Ca的电子促进和Pb的几何阻隔相结合会导致丙烷脱氢稳定性的协同改进。

在10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008中研究的PtZn催化剂中,发现[PtZn4]表面集合体会隔离Pt活性位点并通过配体效应改变电子态。 这削弱了丙烯的吸附并抑制了过度脱氢,从而提高了选择性。

DOI 10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019的文章概述了向Pt添加第二种金属如何阻断Pt表面上的位点(集合体效应)并改变Pt原子的电子密度(配体效应),从而提高催化性能。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 所找到的少数例子证明了集合体和配体效应在改变丙烷脱氢催化剂的选择性和活性方面的潜力。 然而,调查的大多数文章没有深入研究这些效应。 未来的研究可以系统地探索次要金属如何影响各种双金属系统中活性位点的隔离和电子特性。 在原子水平上探测几何形状和电子结构的先进表征技术会阐明协同效应的起源。 将这与动力学和计算研究相结合,可以发现优化活性,选择性和稳定性权衡的新合金组成和纳米结构。 更广泛地,这些概念可以扩展到丙烷脱氢以外的其他催化反应。
