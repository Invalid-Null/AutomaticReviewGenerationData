 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the reference [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726], X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) experiments, including X-ray absorption near edge structure (XANES) and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS), were performed to probe oxidation state changes during propane dehydrogenation catalysis. These experiments were conducted at a synchrotron source on fresh and spent Co@Si-BEA catalysts. The EXAFS data showed that the incorporated Co2+ cations, acting as the predominant cobalt active sites, were structurally stable against the reaction with no change in oxidation state. 

This demonstrates the use of XAS techniques to directly monitor the chemical state of active sites during propane dehydrogenation, revealing their stability under working conditions in this catalyst system. Further research could explore more detailed correlations by coupling XAS with other in-situ methods to elucidate oxidation state changes of different cobalt sites and relate them to deactivation phenomena. As synchrotron facilities become more accessible, greater implementation of XAS for operando studies can uncover novel insights into catalyst function.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings showcase the tremendous potential of X-ray based operando techniques in unraveling oxidation state transformations governing activity and selectivity over transition metal catalysts. Looking ahead, by amalgamating XAS with complementary spectroscopy, diffraction and imaging methods in situ, multifaceted information can be accrued spanning from electronic structure to crystallographic rearrangements during propane dehydrogenation. This could aid the design of optimized sites and tailored support interactions. Additionally, machine learning algorithms leveraging big datasets promises automated acquisition of structure-property relationships to rapidly advance next-generation catalysts.

<Chinese>

根据从参考文献[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]中提取的详细信息,使用了X射线吸收光谱(XAS)实验,包括X射线近边吸收结构(XANES)和扩展X射线细结构(EXAFS),来探测丙烷脱氢催化过程中的氧化态变化。这些实验是在同步辐射光源上对新鲜和使用后的Co@Si-BEA催化剂进行的。EXAFS数据表明,作为主要钴活性位点的掺入Co2+阳离子在反应过程中结构稳定,氧化态没有发生变化。

这证明了XAS技术可以直接监测活性位点在丙烷脱氢反应过程中的化学状态,揭示了它们在这个催化剂体系中的稳定性。后续的研究可以通过将XAS与其他原位方法结合来获得更详细的相关性,阐明不同钴位点的氧化态变化并将其与失活现象联系起来。随着同步辐射设施的普及,XAS在原位研究中的大规模实施可以揭示催化剂功能的新见解。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 这些发现展示了基于X射线的原位技术在揭示过渡金属催化剂活性和选择性的氧化态转变方面的巨大潜力。展望未来,通过将XAS与互补的光谱学、散射和成像方法结合进行原位研究,可以获取从电子结构到结晶构型重排的多方面信息。这可能有助于优化活性位点的设计和载体相互作用的调控。此外,利用大数据训练的机器学习算法有望实现结构-性能关系的自动获取,以快速推进新一代催化剂的研发。
