The literature reviewed does not provide a comprehensive analysis of the techno-economic factors affecting the viability of propane dehydrogenation processes. The focus of the studies is primarily on catalyst design, characterization, and performance evaluation rather than process economics. 

Specific references emphasize catalyst optimization using different supports ([10.1016/j.jcat.2020.06.016], [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]), tuning preparation conditions ([10.1016/j.cej.2017.05.058]), and assessing performance under varying reaction parameters ([10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030], [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]). However, quantification of commercial viability and considerations of scale-up feasibility are not undertaken.

While the literature yields valuable insights on enhancing catalyst productivity via tailored synthesis and characterization, assessing real-world economic drivers is imperative for translation from lab-scale science to industrial application. Bridging this gap by concurrently evaluating process ROI, sensitivity to market fluctuations, and environmental regulations can accelerate the deployment of enhanced PDH catalysts. Exploring modular plant construction and dynamization of operations in response to demand shifts may further bolster viability.
