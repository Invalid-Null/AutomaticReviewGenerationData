 <English>

The article with DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008 investigates how calcination and reduction treatments affect metal-support interactions between Pt and boron nitride (BN). It reveals that calcination causes partial encapsulation of Pt nanoparticles by boron oxide (BOx) overlayers. These overlayers block low-coordinated Pt sites and constrain the Pt particle size, which suppresses side reactions and coke formation while improving propylene selectivity. Additionally, the BOx overlayers inhibit Pt nanoparticle sintering during periodic regeneration cycles. 

The article with DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7 shows that anchoring sub-nanometric Pt clusters inside the 14-membered ring (14-MR) channels of a germanium-containing zeolite via Pt-O-Ge bonds leads to high activity and stability for propane dehydrogenation. The germanium-enriched double four-ring (Ge-d4r) units in the support and the confining effects of the extra-large pores enable the distinct localization and dispersion of the Pt species. The resulting Pt4-Ge2-d4r@UTL structure demonstrates exceptional performance.

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631 utilizes HAADF-STEM imaging to demonstrate that ultra-small Pt species down to isolated atoms are stabilized on a zeolite support even after pretreatment and reaction. EXAFS data also indicate changes in Pt coordination during reaction, suggesting metal-support interactions modulate performance. The ultra-small Pt species exhibit high propane dehydrogenation activity.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The examined articles provide valuable structure-function insights, elucidating how strategic manipulation of active site isolation, metal-support interplay, nanoparticle encapsulation, and confinement in support pores can optimize selectivity, stability, and regenerability. Building on these fundamental revelations, future explorations might examine extending such precision-tailoring approaches to additional catalytic systems and applying operando spectroscopic techniques to achieve a dynamic understanding of working catalysts. There are also opportunities to integrate computational modeling with experimental studies to predictively guide rational design. Cross-coupling these knowledge spheres could unveil new horizons.

<Chinese>

DOI 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008的文章研究了烧结和还原处理如何影响Pt和氮化硼(BN)之间的金属支撑相互作用。它揭示了烧结会导致Pt纳米粒子被硼氧化物(BOx)覆盖层部分包裹。这些覆盖层阻止了低配位的Pt位点,并限制了Pt颗粒的大小,抑制了副反应和焦炭形成,同时提高了丙烯选择性。另外,BOx覆盖层可以抑制Pt纳米粒子在周期性再生循环期间的烧结。

DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7的文章表明,通过Pt-O-Ge键将亚纳米Pt簇锚定在含锗沸石的14员环(14-MR)通道内,可以实现高活性和高稳定性的丙烷脱氢。支持体中的锗富集的双四环(Ge-d4r)单元和超大孔的局限效应可以实现Pt种的明确定位和分散。结果形成的Pt4-Ge2-d4r@UTL结构展示了异常的性能。

DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631利用HAADF-STEM成像展示了超小的Pt种,甚至到单个原子,可以稳定地锚定在沸石支持体上,即使在预处理和反应后。EXAFS数据也表明Pt的配位在反应过程中发生了变化,提示金属支持体相互作用调节性能。这些超小的Pt种体现出了高的丙烷脱氢活性。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 检查的文章提供了有价值的结构-功能见解,阐明了如何战略性地操作活性位点的隔离,金属支撑体的相互作用,纳米粒子的包覆以及支撑体孔隙中的限域效应来优化选择性、稳定性和再生能力。 在这些基础发现的基础上,未来的探索可能会考查将这种精密定制方法扩展到其他催化系统,并应用操作态光谱技术来动态理解工作催化剂。 还有机会将计算建模与实验研究相结合,以有预测性地指导理性设计。 这些知识领域的交叉耦合可能会揭开新的视野。
