 <English>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts require optimized active phase dispersion to maximize selectivity towards propylene. The reviewed literature investigates various preparation methods to achieve this goal. 

Several studies utilize impregnation techniques to disperse metal species. Co@Si-BEA catalysts with 0.1-0.5 wt% cobalt prepared by vacuum impregnation show excellent PDH intrinsic activity due to isolated Co2+ sites [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. Bimetallic PtSn catalysts on alumina prepared via sequential incipient impregnation also display increased platinum dispersion due to tin stabilization effects [10.1016/j.cej.2014.09.107]. Other work explores one-pot hydrothermal synthesis for incorporating isolated metal sites like Zn2+ into the micropores of S-1 zeolite, verified by STEM-EDS mapping [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034].

Surface organometallic chemistry (SOMC) using molecular precursors enables precise grafting of isolated metals like Ga3+ onto dehydroxylated oxide surfaces [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. Cothermolysis extends this for dispersed Cr/Si/Al/O catalysts [10.1016/S0021-9517(03)00141-6]. Ordered mesoporous silicas and nanocarbon supports are also utilized to optimize site isolation and stabilization [10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7].

Overall, tailoring preparation steps like impregnation order, surface modifiers, thermal treatments, and SOMC grafting allows excellent control over active phase dispersion, enabling the next generation of optimized PDH catalysts.

※※※ 
The reviewed studies highlight the vital role preparation techniques play in optimizing active site dispersion, a key requirement for selectivity in PDH catalysts. Building on these fundamental advances, future horizons may involve extending precision preparation methods towards cheaper transition metals like iron instead of traditional platinum group metals. Additionally, novel supports with tunable acid-base properties could help anchor isolated sites and prevent mobility/aggregation over extended operating lifetimes. Overall, the foundation established on tailored synthesis methods sets the stage for cost-effective and durable PDH catalysts through rational dispersion control.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂需要优化活性相分散以最大限度地提高丙烯的选择性。文献复习了各种制备方法以实现这一目标。

几项研究利用浸渍技术来分散金属种类。通过真空浸渍制备的Co含量为0.1-0.5 wt%的Co@Si-BEA催化剂由于其隔离Co2+位点显示出色的PDH本征活性[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。通过顺序渗透浸渍法制备的氧化铝载体PtSn双金属催化剂由于锡的稳定作用也显示出更高的铂分散度[10.1016/j.cej.2014.09.107]。其他工作探索了一锅法水热合成将如Zn2+之类的隔离金属位点掺入S-1分子筛的微孔中,这通过STEM-EDS元素映射进行验证[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034]。

使用分子前驱体的表面有机金属化学(SOMC)能够精确地将如Ga3+之类的隔离金属接枝到去羟基氧化物表面[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。共热解延伸了这种方法用于分散Cr/Si/Al/O催化剂的制备[10.1016/S0021-9517(03)00141-6]。有序介孔硅和纳米碳载体也被利用来优化位点的隔离和稳定[10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]。

总的来说,通过调节如浸渍顺序、表面修饰剂、热处理和SOMC接枝等制备步骤可以很好地控制活性相的分散,这为下一代优化的PDH催化剂奠定了基础。

※※※
文献综述强调了制备技术在优化活性中心分散方面的重要作用,这是PDH催化剂选择性的关键要求。在这些基础进展的基础上,未来的发展方向可能涉及将精确的制备方法扩展到较廉价的过渡金属如铁,而不是传统的铂族金属。此外,具有可调酸碱性质的新型载体材料可以帮助锚定隔离位点,并防止长周期运行过程中活性中心的迁移和聚集。总的来说,关于定制合成方法的奠基性工作奠定了制备出具成本效益和高稳定性PDH催化剂的基础,这可以通过合理的分散控制来实现。
