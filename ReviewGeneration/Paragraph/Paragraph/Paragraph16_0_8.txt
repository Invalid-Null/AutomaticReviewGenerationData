**Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?**

Several studies have evaluated the effect of alloying platinum (Pt) with metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), and zinc (Zn) for propane dehydrogenation catalysis. Key insights are summarized below:

**Pt-Sn**

Multiple works demonstrate that adding Sn as a promoter improves the performance of Pt catalysts for propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.1016/j.jechem.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]. Specifically, Pt-Sn catalysts show higher initial activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018, 10.1021/acscatal.2c03732], propene selectivity [10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731], and stability compared to monometallic Pt [10.1021/acscatal.9b03527, 10.1021/science.abg7894]. 

This enhancement is attributed to the combination of ensemble effects from Sn diluting Pt ensembles [10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X] and electronic effects where Sn increases the electron density on Pt sites [10.1002/anie.201507119]. An optimal Sn/Pt ratio around 1-1.5 is reported, beyond which excess Sn decreases activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]. Characterization confirms the presence of intermetallic Pt-Sn phases like Pt3Sn and PtSn [10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361].

**Pt-Ga and Pt-Zn**

Alloying Pt with Ga [10.1021/jacs.8b05378, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264] or Zn [10.1021/jacs.1c10261, 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008] can also improve propane dehydrogenation performance. Key effects include increased stability [10.1021/acscatal.7b02264, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816], selectivity [10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129] and resistance to coking [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. 

Isolated, single-site PtZn4-6 sites stabilized by the zeolite framework give remarkable durability [10.1021/jacs.1c10261]. Ordered intermetallic PtZn nanoalloys minimize ensemble size and improve thermal stability [10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]. For Pt-Ga, the formation of atomically ordered structures enhances selectivity by suppressing side reactions [10.1002/anie.202107210].

In summary, strategic incorporation of secondary metals like Sn, Ga and Zn allows modulation of the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites. This optimization delicately balances stability, activity and selectivity - the pillars defining an exceptional propane dehydrogenation catalyst.

※※※

**Integrative Understanding and Prospective Outlook**

The extensive analyses presented comprehensively capture current advancements in engineering bimetallic and alloy Pt catalysts for optimized propane dehydrogenation. While Sn, Ga and Zn incorporation strategies continue to dominate, emerging explorations signal intriguing possibilities. 

Future pursuits could examine multi-metallic combinations beyond binary Pt alloys. For instance, exploring synergies between Sn, Ga and Zn codopants might offer new opportunities to push selectivity limitations. Additionally, expanding the palette of secondary metals beyond established candidates could unveil unforeseen electronic or structural catalyst-support couplings that favorably tune catalytic performance. 

Overall, the encouraging progress spearheaded thus far serves to motivate additional innovations centered on bimetallic and alloy catalyst engineering - with the goal of designing active, selective and ultrastable propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

**双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?**

几项研究评估了将铂(Pt)与如锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn)合金化用于丙烷脱氢催化的效果。主要见解总结如下:

**Pt-Sn**

多项工作表明,添加Sn作为助剂可以提高Pt催化剂在丙烷脱氢中的性能[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.1016/j.jechem.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。 具体来说,与单金属Pt相比,Pt-Sn催化剂表现出更高的初始活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018, 10.1021/acscatal.2c03732]、丙烯选择性[10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1016/j.apcatb.2021.120731]和稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.9b03527, 10.1021/science.abg7894]。

这种提高归因于Sn稀释Pt基团的集合效应[10.1016/S1872-2067(23)64402-X]和Sn增加Pt活性位电子密度的电子效应[10.1002/anie.201507119]。 报道的最佳Sn/Pt比约为1-1.5,超过这一比例会降低活性[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.09.016]。 表征证实了Pt3Sn和PtSn等铂锡间化合物相的存在[10.1016/j.jechem.2017.09.035, 10.1006/jcat.1996.0361]。

**Pt-Ga和Pt-Zn**

Pt与Ga[10.1021/jacs.8b05378, 10.1021/acscatal.7b02264]或Zn[10.1021/jacs.1c10261, 10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]合金化也可以改善丙烷脱氢性能。 主要效应包括增强的稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.7b02264, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]、选择性[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]和抗焦化性[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。

由分子筛框架稳定的隔离的PtZn4-6单位点[10.1021/jacs.1c10261]显示出卓越的持久性和稳定性。 有序的PtZn金属间化合物纳米合金最小化集合尺寸并提高热稳定性[10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]。 对于Pt-Ga, 有序结构的形成通过抑制副反应来提高选择性[10.1002/anie.202107210]。

总的来说,通过战略引入Sn、Ga和Zn等辅助金属,允许调控Pt活性中心的几何和电子性质。这种优化精确地平衡了稳定性、活性和选择性 - 定义一个卓越的丙烷脱氢催化剂的支柱。

※※※

**综合理解与未来展望**

上述广泛的分析全面捕捉了目前工程设计Pt双金属和合金催化剂用于优化丙烷脱氢反应的进展。虽然Sn、Ga和Zn掺杂策略继续占主导地位,新兴的探索讯号显示了有趣的可能性。

未来的努力可以检验超出二元Pt合金的多金属组合。例如,探究Sn、Ga和Zn共掺杂协同效应可能提供新的机会推动选择性限制。 此外,拓展碳氢化合物辅助金属的种类也可能发现意想不到的电子或结构催化剂-载体耦合,从而有利地调节催化性能。

总体上,到目前为止取得的令人鼓舞的进展激励其他创新,以针对双金属和合金催化剂设计 - 目标是设计出具有活性、选择性和超稳定性的丙烷脱氢催化剂。
