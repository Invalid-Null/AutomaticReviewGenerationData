	<English>
		X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) techniques like X-ray absorption near edge structure (XANES) and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) have been pivotal in elucidating oxidation state changes and coordination transformations of active species during propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysis [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. In situ XANES experiments on Co@Si-BEA catalysts exhibited no changes in the oxidation state of incorporated Co2+ sites after PDH reaction, confirming their structural resilience as predominant active centers [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. XPS measurements on PtSn/CeO2/C catalysts revealed alloy formation and charge transfer between Pt and Sn upon H2 activation [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019]. In situ XPS further demonstrated partial reduction of tetrahedral Cr6+ species in Cr-MCM-41 to Cr3+ under PDH conditions, correlating with heightened activity [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]. These insights showcase the competence of X-ray techniques in capturing oxidation state and coordination transformations fundamental to PDH catalysis.
		※※※
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Capitalizing on recent advancements, emerging in situ/operando X-ray methodologies can potentially unravel oxidation state changes with superior spatiotemporal resolution. For instance, high-energy X-ray diffraction amenable to reaction environments may quantify phase evolutions and crystallinity changes during PDH cycles [DOI]. X-ray ptychography can map 3D morphochemical states in catalyst bodies at nanoscale [DOI], granting perspectives on degradation phenomena. X-ray scattering deployed in tandem with spectroscopy and diffraction can foster multivariate structure-function interconnections [DOI], catalyzing knowledge discovery. These frontiers highlight the sustained, pivotal role X-ray modalities will play in unraveling intricate oxidation state phenomena integral to advancing PDH catalysis.
