         Based on the detailed analysis from the quotes and summaries provided in the file, zeolites like MFI, BEA, and CHA have shown promise as supports for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. 

         MFI zeolites such as silicalite-1 and ZSM-5 provide excellent supports to stabilize isolated Pt atoms or sub-nanometric Pt alloy clusters like PtZn and PtSn [DOI1, DOI2]. The rigid microporous structure and weak metal-support interactions facilitate uniform dispersion and prevent agglomeration of the active metal species. Compared to amorphous silica, MFI zeolites decrease PtSn nanoparticle sizes from 1.6 nm down to 0.5-0.6 nm, enhancing catalytic performance [DOI3]. Doping the framework with heteroatoms like Fe can further stabilize Pt species through additional anchoring sites [DOI4].

          Dealuminated Beta (BEA) zeolite also anchors isolated Pt atoms within Zn nests inside its pores, creating durable and highly active single-atom catalysts for PDH [DOI5, DOI6]. The BEA topology provides similar confinement and isolation effects as MFI.

           Additionally, chabazite (CHA) zeolites exchanged with Ga cations demonstrate 96% propylene selectivity in PDH. Spectroscopic analysis confirms the active site is an extra-framework Ga+ species, which is stabilized by the CHA framework [DOI7].

          In summary, MFI, BEA and CHA zeolites represent promising supports to maximize metal dispersion, tailor active site geometry, and provide confinement effects that enhance stability and selectivity for PDH catalysts. Future directions may explore the impacts of framework composition, heteroatom doping, and pore dimensions on stabilizing various single-atom and sub-nanometric multi-metallic species.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The detailed examples and data furnished in the file reveal zeolites to be uniquely suited as catalyst supports, owing to properties like high surface area, durability, controlled acidity and tunable pores that stabilize isolated active sites. An intriguing research direction is exploring more exotic zeolite structures beyond conventional materials, and rationally matching specific topologies to targeted active species and catalytic chemistries through computations and high-throughput screening. This could uncover as-yet unknown structure-performance relationships. Progress in these areas may facilitate designing optimized zeolite-supported single-atom and cluster catalysts for PDH and other critical processes.

         根据文件中提供的引文和概述的详细分析,MFI、BEA和CHA等分子筛已显示出作为丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂载体的前景。  

         诸如硅沸石和ZSM-5之类的MFI分子筛提供了优异的载体,可以稳定隔离的Pt原子或PtZn和PtSn等子纳米Pt合金簇[DOI1, DOI2]。其刚性的微孔结构和弱的金属载体相互作用有利于活性金属物种的均匀分散,并防止其团聚[DOI3]。与无定形二氧化硅相比,MFI分子筛将PtSn纳米粒子的大小从1.6 nm减小到0.5-0.6 nm,提高了催化性能。用杂原子如Fe掺杂的框架可以通过额外的锚定位点进一步稳定Pt物种[DOI4]。  

          除铝Beta分子筛(BEA)也可以在其孔内的Zn巢中锚定隔离的Pt原子,形成持久高活性的单原子PDH催化剂[DOI5, DOI6]。BEA拓扑结构提供了与MFI相似的限域和隔离效应。

           此外,在加仑交换的方沸石(CHA)分子筛表现出96%丙烯选择性的PDH反应。光谱分析证实了活性中心是一个额框架Ga+物种,该物种由CHA框架稳定[DOI7]。  

          总的来说,MFI、BEA和CHA分子筛代表了前景光明的载体,可以最大化金属分散度,定制活性位点几何形状,并提供限域效应,增强稳定性和选择性PDH催化剂。未来的研究方向可探索框架成分、杂原子掺杂和孔径尺寸对稳定各种单原子和子纳米多金属物种的影响。
