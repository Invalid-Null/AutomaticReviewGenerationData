 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and pathways have been extensively studied using density functional theory (DFT) calculations, providing valuable insights into the reaction kinetics and selectivity over different catalysts. 

DFT reveals lower energy barriers for propane dehydrogenation over Zn-O-Zn sites compared to single Zn2+ sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816], explaining the superior catalytic performance of the former. Calculations also demonstrate correlation between Pt's downshifted d-band center and improved propylene selectivity on electron-enriched Pt surfaces [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. Microkinetic modeling bridging DFT energetics and experimental data offers comprehensive structure-activity relationships, where scaling relations connect adsorption energies to macroscopic catalytic behavior [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008].

DFT coupled with computational screening has been successfully utilized to discover improved propane dehydrogenation catalysts like 3N-coordinated single atom catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. Studies employ spin-polarized DFT with pseudopotentials to model reaction pathways, revealing insights into coke-resistance and stability. Kinetically relevant steps like propane C-H activation, propylene C-H activation and propyne C-C cleavage were identified through microkinetic analyses on Pt [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916].

Computations also provided mechanistic perspectives into promoting effects of metal additives. DFT calculations on Rh-doped ZrO2 suggested facile lattice oxygen migration from zirconia in presence of Rh explaining enhanced redox capability [10.1021/acscatal.0c01455]. Analogous studies have screened myriad potential catalysts like PtSn surfaces using scaling relations and electronic structure analyses while continually expanding the computational methodologies.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Propane dehydrogenation mechanisms have been effectively elucidated using DFT calculations, relating electronic structure to reactivity via scaling relationships and microkinetic models. However, most studies are limited to model surfaces under idealized conditions. Advanced multi-scale techniques can integrate more realistic surface structures, adsorbate configurations and experimental parameters into simulations. Coupled with high-throughput screening and machine learning, this can exponentially enhance the discovery of optimal catalyst compositions and architectures for propane dehydrogenation. Exploring reaction networks through graph theoretical methods and customized reactivity descriptors also offers new horizons.

<Chinese>

丙烷脱氢机理和途径已经通过密度泛函理论(DFT)计算进行了广泛研究,为不同催化剂上的反应动力学和选择性提供了有价值的见解。

DFT揭示了Zn-O-Zn位点上的丙烷脱氢活化能垒低于单个Zn2+位点[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816],解释了前者优异的催化性能。计算也展示了Pt的下移d带中心与富电子Pt表面上丙烯选择性的改善之间的相关性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。微观动力学建模连接DFT能量和实验数据,提供全面的结构-活性关系,其中缩放关系将吸附能与宏观催化行为联系起来[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]。

DFT与计算筛选相结合已经成功地用于发现改进的丙烷脱氢催化剂,例如3N配位的单原子催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。研究采用自旋极化DFT与伪势能来模拟反应途径,揭示了抗焦炭和稳定性的见解。通过对Pt的微观动力学分析[10.1021/acscatal.1c01916],确定出丙烷C-H活化、丙烯C-H活化和丙炔C-C裂解等动力学相关步骤。

计算还为金属添加剂的促进效应提供了机制视角。 ZrO2上的Rh掺杂DFT计算表明,在Rh存在下,氧化锆的晶格氧易于迁移,解释了增强的氧化-还原能力[10.1021/acscatal.0c01455]。类似的研究已经通过缩放关系和电子结构分析对PtSn表面等潜在催化剂进行了筛选,同时不断拓展计算方法论。

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 通过DFT计算有效阐明了丙烷脱氢机理,通过缩放关系和微观动力学模型将电子结构与反应性联系起来。但是,大多数研究仅限于理想条件下的模型表面。先进的多尺度技术可以将更现实的表面结构、吸附物配置和实验参数集成到模拟中。结合高通量筛选和机器学习,这可以指数级地增强对丙烷脱氢优化催化剂组成和体系结构的发现。通过图论方法和定制反应性描述符探索反应网络,也开辟了新领域。
