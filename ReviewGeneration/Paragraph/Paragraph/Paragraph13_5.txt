Based on the details provided in the file, there is limited investigation into how properties like metal particle size, shape, and support interactions affect the performance of supported metal catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH) across the various studies. 

However, some key insights are revealed in select articles:

The article with DOI 10.1016/j.apcatb.2012.11.005 [10.1016/j.apcatb.2012.11.005] shows that a Pt/SBA-15 catalyst prepared by deposition-precipitation has much smaller Pt particles than one prepared by wet impregnation. This impacts activity and selectivity, with the smaller Pt particles giving better performance.

Another study with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391] demonstrates that applying ultrathin Al2O3 overcoatings to Pt nanoparticles helps stabilize them against sintering during PDH reactions and regeneration. Increasing the number of ALD cycles gives more stable Pt particles and improves propylene selectivity.

Work in DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7 [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] revealed that confining sub-nanometric Pt clusters within the micropores of a Ge-UTL zeolite support provides exceptional PDH activity and stability. The location and Pt-support interactions are critical in this case.

Beyond particle size effects, research with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667 [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667] showed that modulating ensemble size by alloying Ru with P helped isolate individual Ru sites and dramatically increased propylene selectivity to 91% compared to pure Ru.

While these works provide some structure-function insights, most studies did not thoroughly investigate effects of metal particle properties or support interactions. Further research is still needed to better understand these structure-activity relationships. Key opportunities include exploring novel synthesis methods to control size and isolation of active sites, applying state-of-the-art imaging techniques, and using advanced theory and simulations to model critical descriptors.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The scattered insights disclosed within the various articles highlight the potential impact of modulating metal particle size, composition, shape and support effects on propane dehydrogenation performance. However, most works did not conduct systematic investigations of structure-function relationships. This presents an opportunity for future research to more rigorously characterize supported metal catalysts using multi-modal analyses, while also leveraging computational modeling and multivariate optimization strategies. Such efforts can accelerate identification of active site descriptors and targeted development of optimized PDH catalysts boasting maximized propylene production.

然而,一些关键文献有如下见解:

DOI 10.1016/j.apcatb.2012.11.005 [10.1016/j.apcatb.2012.11.005] 的研究显示,沉淀共沉淀法制备的Pt/SBA-15催化剂的Pt颗粒远小于湿法浸渍法制备的。这影响了活性和选择性,小Pt颗粒的催化剂性能更好。

另一项研究 DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391 [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391] 表明,在Pt纳米粒子上沉积超薄Al2O3涂层有助于抑制烧结,提高丙烯选择性。ALD周期次数的增加使Pt颗粒更稳定。

DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7 [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] 的工作显示,将亚纳米Pt簇固定在Ge-UTL分子筛的微孔内,催化性能很高。 Pt的位置和与载体的相互作用至关重要。


除了颗粒大小效应,DOI 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667 [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667] 的研究表明,Ru与P形成合金可以隔离单个Ru活性位点,从而大大提高丙烯选择性达到91%,比纯Ru催化剂高得多。

尽管这些工作提供了一些结构-功能关系的洞见,但大多数研究没有全面考查金属颗粒性质或载体效应的影响。我们还需要更多研究以深入理解这些结构-活性关系。关键的机会包括探索新合成方法来控制活性位点的大小和隔离,采用先进的成像技术和高级理论,以及模拟结构-性能的关键描述符等。
