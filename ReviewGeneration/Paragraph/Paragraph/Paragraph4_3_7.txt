 <English>
Based on the details extracted from the references, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) faces considerable thermodynamic constraints due to being a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH298K = 124.3 kJ/mol). To overcome these limitations and push the equilibrium towards desired products, PDH is typically operated at high temperatures of 550-650°C [DOI1, DOI2]. However, such harsh conditions also promote various side reactions like cracking, coking, and hydrogenolysis leading to catalyst deactivation [DOI3]. 

Some studies have provided quantitative kinetic details related to PDH. Apparent activation energies on the order of 180-200 kJ/mol for propane dehydrogenation and 159-174 kJ/mol for cracking reactions were estimated over CHA catalysts [DOI4]. DFT calculations determined a 0.71 eV barrier for the rate limiting α-hydrogen abstraction step in PDH [DOI5]. Turnover frequencies, selectivities, and reaction orders have also been reported as key kinetic parameters [DOI6].

Overall, further research is still needed to fully characterize the PDH mechanism and kinetically stabilize catalysts against deactivation. Alternative processes like oxidative PDH have been proposed to partially overcome thermodynamic constraints, but require precise tuning of co-fed oxidants like O2 or CO2 [DOI7]. Achieving commercially viable propane conversion remains an ongoing challenge limited by thermodynamics and kinetics.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements reviewed, there exists opportunities to explore emerging computational, characterization, and reactor design techniques. Machine learning could be applied to discover optimized catalyst compositions over vast chemical spaces. Operando spectroscopy and microscopy might provide dynamic insights into working catalysts. Novel reactor concepts could help overcome intrinsic kinetic and thermodynamic barriers. An interdisciplinary outlook leveraging cutting-edge tools could unlock the next innovations in propane valorization.

<Chinese>
根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,丙烷脱氢(PDH)由于是一种高度放热反应(ΔH298K = 124.3 kJ/mol),因此面临着相当大的热力学限制。为了克服这些限制并推动平衡向所需产品方向发展,PDH通常在550-650°C的高温下运行[DOI1,DOI2]。然而,这样苛刻的条件也促进了裂解、焦炭和氢解等各种副反应,导致催化剂失活[DOI3]。

一些研究提供了与PDH相关的定量动力学详细信息。在CHA催化剂上,丙烷脱氢反应和裂解反应的表观活化能估计分别为180-200 kJ/mol和159-174 kJ/mol [DOI4]。DFT计算确定了PDH中限速α-氢抽取步骤的0.71 eV屏障[DOI5]。周转频率、选择性和反应顺序也被报告为关键的动力学参数[DOI6]。

总的来说,人们仍需要进一步的研究来全面描述PDH机理,并在动力学上稳定催化剂抵抗失活。已经提出了如氧化物PDH等替代工艺,以部分克服热力学限制,但需要精确调节共喂氧化剂如O2或CO2的用量[DOI7]。实现商业化可行的丙烷转化仍然面临由热力学和动力学所限制的持续挑战。

综合理解与未来展望:考虑到所审阅的进展,探索新兴的计算、表征和反应器设计技术存在机会。机器学习可以应用于在广阔的化学空间中发现优化的催化剂成分。原位光谱和显微技术可能为工作催化剂提供动态见解。新颖的反应器概念可以帮助克服固有的动力学和热力学障碍。利用尖端工具的跨学科展望可能会为丙烷附加值化解锁下一代创新。
