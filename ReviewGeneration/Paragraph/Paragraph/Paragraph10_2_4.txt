Based on the details extracted from the references, various mechanistic scenarios have been proposed for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts. 

On Cr-based catalysts, isolated Cr3+ sites [DOI1], Cr3+/Cr2+ redox couples [DOI2], and mononuclear CrIII species with coordinative vacancies [DOI3] have been suggested as active sites. The reaction steps may involve oxidative dehydrogenation via Mars-van Krevelen type cycles [DOI4, DOI5]. For ZrO2, coordinatively unsaturated Zr cations (Zrcus) paired with a neighboring lattice oxygen [DOI6, DOI7] or located at an oxygen vacancy [DOI8] can activate the C-H bond in propane. Ga-doped ZrOx alters the mechanism from stepwise to concerted, lowering the barrier [DOI9]. 

Isolated tetracoordinated Ti4c sites adjacent to oxygen vacancies were also proposed to facilitate propane dehydrogenation on TiOx nanosheets via heterolytic H2 dissociation to form hydridic H species [DOI10]. On supported vanadium oxides, redox cycles between various V oxidation states (V5+, V4+, V3+) enable propane activation, with subsequent lattice oxygen depletion shifting the mechanism from oxidative to non-oxidative pathways [DOI11, DOI12].

Additionally, mechanistic scenarios have been put forth involving gallium oxide sites, with evidence for heterolytic dissociation pathways preferential over redox routes [DOI13, DOI14]. Specific proposals include gallium hydride/alkoxide intermediates from dissociative propane adsorption [DOI15] and rate-limiting dissociation transition states on tetrahedral Ga3+ sites [DOI2].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The current mechanistic picture of propane dehydrogenation over metal oxides remains incomplete, with many open questions regarding the specific nature of active sites and key reaction intermediates. While thought-provoking scenarios have emerged from advanced spectroscopic and computational investigations, continued research is imperative to reconcile experimental data with proposed mechanisms. 

As we expand our comprehension of intrinsic activity descriptors, future undertakings might concentrate on strategically tailoring multifunctional sites to promote targeted reaction channels. Elucidating structure-activity correlations through multi-technique assessments can also guide rational design of optimized catalyst architectures. Furthermore, operando methodologies under industrially relevant conditions could unveil novel dynamical phenomena influencing selectivity and stability.

根据从参考文献中提取的详细信息,在不同金属氧化物催化剂上已经为丙烷脱氢提出了各种机理方案。

在以Cr为基的催化剂上,已经建议隔离的Cr3+位点[DOI1]、Cr3+/ Cr2+氧化还原偶合物[DOI2]和配位空位数的单核CrIII [DOI3]作为活性位点。反应步骤可能涉及通过Mars-van Krevelen类型的循环氧化脱氢[DOI4,DOI5]。 对于ZrO2,与相邻的晶格氧配对的非饱和配位Zr阳离子(Zrcus)[DOI6,DOI7]或位于氧空位处的非饱和配位Zr阳离子[DOI8]可以激活丙烷中的C-H键。 Ga掺杂的ZrOx将机理从步进更改为协奏,降低了屏障[DOI9]。

还建议相邻氧空位的四配位Ti4c位点通过杂解H2解离形成肼基H种 Facilitate丙烷脱氢在TiOx纳米片上[DOI10]。 在支持的钒氧化物上,各种V氧化状态(V5+、V4+、V3+)之间的氧化还原循环使丙烷激活成为可能,随后的晶格氧耗竭将机理从氧化转变为非氧化途径[ DOI11,DOI12]。

此外,已经提出了涉及氧化镓位点的机理方案,来自解离吸附丙烷的镓化物中间体[DOI13,DOI14]。 具体建议包括解离性丙烷吸附形成的镓化氢/醇化物中间体[DOI15]和四面体Ga3+位点上脱氢过渡态的限速步骤[DOI2]。

※※※

综合理解与前景展望:目前对金属氧化物上丙烷脱氢的机理理解仍不完整,对活性位点和关键反应中间体的具体性质还有许多未解决的问题。 虽然先进的光谱和计算调查提出了思路独到的方案,但需要继续研究来协调实验数据与拟议机理。

随着我们对内在活性描述子的理解不断拓宽,未来的工作可能会集中在战略上定制多功能位点以促进目标反应通道。 通过多技术评估阐明结构-活性相关性也可以指导优化催化剂体系结构的理性设计。 此外,在工业相关条件下的反应条件研究可以揭示影响选择性和稳定性的新动力学现象。
