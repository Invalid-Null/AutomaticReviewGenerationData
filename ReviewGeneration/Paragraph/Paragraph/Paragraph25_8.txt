Based on the details extracted from the file, several articles utilize in-situ characterization techniques to study working propane dehydrogenation catalysts and gain insights into the nature of active sites and changes occurring during reaction.

One study uses in-situ DRIFTS and temperature programmed surface reaction (TPSR) to monitor propylene adsorption/desorption and product formation over cobalt-based catalysts [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. Another combines quantitative FTIR spectroscopy, pulse titration reactions, and online MS to elucidate the identity and composition of active gallium species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]. In-situ Raman spectroscopy reveals the in-situ transformation of ZnOH to active ZnH sites over ZnOx catalysts during the induction period [10.1021/acscatal.2c00928]. 

Synchrotron-based in-situ XAS and EXAFS are utilized to detect changes in metal oxidation states, coordination numbers, and phase transitions under propane dehydrogenation conditions for catalysts containing Cr [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014], Fe [10.1021/acscatal.6b01286], Au [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030], Ni [10.1021/acscatal.2c03240], and Zn [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3] active sites. In-situ DRIFTS reveals the transformation of chromate species to reduced Cr(III) sites [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.01.005], changes in Zn speciation [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3], and oxidative dehydrogenation of propane to propylene over VOx sites [10.1002/anie.202005968].

These examples demonstrate how in-situ techniques can effectively probe catalyst structure-function relationships, intermediate species, active site transformations, and more to advance fundamental understandings that may guide rational design of improved propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The in-situ characterization studies reveal valuable insights into working propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Yet most focus on specific systems under simplified conditions rather than industrial reactors. An opportunity exists to implement operando spectroscopy, diffuse reflectance techniques, online GC/MS, and synchrotron-based probes to investigate commercial catalysts within technical reactors. The mechanistic clarity this could provide may accelerate enhancements via tailored preparation strategies or targeted modifications. More widespread adoption of in-situ analyses in an industrially relevant context could fast-track the ongoing quest to optimize propane dehydrogenation catalysts and processes.

根据文件提取的详细信息,几篇文章利用了原位表征技术来研究正在工作的丙烷脱氢催化剂,并对活性位点的性质以及反应过程中的变化获得了深入见解。

一项研究使用了原位 DRIFTS 和温程表面反应(TPSR)来监测铜基催化剂上的丙烯吸附/解吸和产物形成[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。另一篇文章结合定量 FTIR 光谱、脉冲滴定反应和在线 MS,阐明了 Ga/H-ZSM-5 催化剂中活性镓种的身份和组成[10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]。原位拉曼光谱显示了 ZnOx 催化剂中 ZnOH 向活性 ZnH 位点的原位转化,发生在诱导期[10.1021/acscatal.2c00928]。


同步辐射的原位 XAS 和 EXAFS 被用来检测包括 Cr [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]、Fe [10.1021/acscatal.6b01286]、Au [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030]、Ni [10.1021/acscatal.2c03240] 和 Zn [10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3] 在内的活性位点在丙烷脱氢条件下的金属氧化态、配位数和相变化。原位 DRIFTS 揭示了铬酸盐种转化为还原的 Cr(III)位点[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.01.005],Zn 化合价的变化[10.1038/s41586-021-03923-3],以及 VOx 位点上的丙烷氧化脱氢生成丙烯[10.1002/anie.202005968]。


这些例子展示了原位技术可以有效地探索催化剂的结构-功能关系、中间体、活性位点的转变等,以推进基本理解,这可能指导丙烷脱氢催化剂的理性设计。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望:这些原位表征研究为工作中的丙烷脱氢催化剂揭示了宝贵的见解。然而,大多数都是针对特定的简化条件,而非工业反应器。因此,一个机遇就是在技术反应器中,对商业催化剂实现 operando 光谱学、漫反射技术、在线 GC/MS 以及同步辐射探针的应用。这可能为通过定制制备策略或目标修饰来优化丙烷脱氢催化剂和工艺提供机制清晰度。在工业相关背景下,更广泛地采用原位分析可能会加速这一持续的追求。
