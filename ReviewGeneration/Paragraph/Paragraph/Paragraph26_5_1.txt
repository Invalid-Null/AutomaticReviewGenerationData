 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the file, the article with DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636 employs integrated differential phase contrast scanning transmission electron microscopy (iDPC-STEM) to characterize the Co-support interface in the propane dehydrogenation catalyst. The images reveal that isolated Co atoms with a distorted tetrahedral coordination of {(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} are homogeneously embedded into the T1(7) and T3(9) sites of the MFI zeolite framework [DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636].

Additional insights into advanced microscopy techniques applied to metal-support interfaces are provided by the article with DOI 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7. It utilizes high-resolution HAADF-STEM paired with integrated differential phase contrast (iDPC) imaging to precisely localize isolated Pt and Sn atoms as well as subnanometric PtSn clusters preferentially formed within the sinusoidal channels of the MFI zeolite after H2 reduction. Automated image analysis quantifies that over 40% of species exist as PtSn clusters after extended reduction times [DOI 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7].

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00818 also contributes characterization of metal-support interfaces, in this case for propane dehydrogenation catalysts comprised of TiOx nanosheets supported on mesoporous silica. HR-TEM reveals these TiOx sheets have thicknesses of 1.5-3.5 nm, corresponding to only a few atomic layers, providing insights into the ultrathin nature of the active phase [DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00818]. 

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advanced microscopy techniques discussed demonstrate remarkable progress in directly visualizing isolated atoms, clusters, and interfacial regions in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Approaches like iDPC-STEM and AC-HAADF-STEMpaired with spectroscopic and computational methods enable researchers to correlate atomic-scale characteristics like coordination environment, location, and dispersion with observed catalytic performance. 

These microscopic insights may guide design of next-generation catalysts with optimized single-atom active sites, tailored intermetallic interfaces, and strong metal-support anchoring tailored for dehydrogenation reactions. Emerging operando microscopy capabilities could also unveil working state morphology changes and dynamic processes occurring during propane dehydrogenation. Integration of such in situ observations with other structural, kinetic, and mechanistic probes will lead to more predictive structure-performance comprehension. Broader adoption of correlative multi-modal characterization encompassing both integral and spatially-resolved information will likely escalate researchers’ capabilities to deliberately engineer superior propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

根据文件中提取的详细信息,DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636的文章采用了集成差分相比扫描透射电镜(iDPC-STEM)来表征丙烷脱氢催化剂中的Co-支持物界面。图像显示,取向失真的{(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} 硅氧烷配位的隔离Co原子均匀嵌入 MFI 分子筛的 T1(7)和 T3(9)位点[DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636]。

DOI 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7的文章提供了有关先进显微技术应用于金属-支持物界面的额外见解。它利用高分辨度HAADF-STEM与集成差分相比较成像技术来精确定位MFI分子筛孔道内在H2还原后优先形成的隔离Pt和Sn原子以及亚纳米PtSn簇。自动图像分析量化了扩展还原时间后超过40%的物种以PtSn簇的形式存在[DOI 10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。


DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00818的文章也有助于表征丙烷脱氢催化剂的金属-支持物界面,在这种情况下,其由 mesoporous二氧化硅支持的 TiOx 纳米片组成。 HR-TEM 显示这些 TiOx 片层的厚度为 1.5-3.5 nm,对应于仅几个原子层,这为活性相的超薄特性提供了见解[DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00818]。

整体理解与前景展望:所讨论的先进显微技术显示了直接观察丙烷脱氢催化剂中隔离原子、簇以及界面区域的显著进步。像iDPC-STEM 和 AC-HAADF-STEM 等技术搭配光谱与计算方法,使研究人员能够将配位环境、位置以及分散状态等原子尺度特征与观察到的催化性能相关联。

这些显微视角提供的信息可能会指导新一代催化剂的设计,这些催化剂将具有经优化的单原子活性位点、定制的金属间化合物界面以及强大的金属支持物固定作用,以满足脱氢反应需求。新兴的 operando 显微镜功能也可能揭示丙烷脱氢过程中形态变化和动态过程。将这种原位观察与其他结构、动力学和机理探针相结合将导致更具预测性的结构-性能理解。更广泛地采用相关的多模式特征分析,整合积分与空间分辨信息,可能会加速研究人员有目的地设计出性能更优异的丙烷脱氢催化剂。
