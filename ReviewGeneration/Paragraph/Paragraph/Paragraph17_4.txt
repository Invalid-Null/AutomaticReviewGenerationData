Based on the detailed analysis from the study [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548], larger Pt nanoparticles can accommodate more Sn species compared to smaller Pt clusters, leading to the formation of PtSn alloy nanoparticles in the former case and Pt-rich clusters with low Sn content in the latter case. These results reveal that the secondary metal Sn interacts differently with Pt sites depending on the Pt nanoparticle size. As stated in quote 1, “Combining the results from theoretical and experimental studies, we can conclude that the Pt nanoparticles (>1 nm) have a higher capacity for accommodating Sn species than subnanometer Pt clusters, resulting in the formation of PtSn alloy nanoparticles, while the Pt clusters with small particle sizes tend to form Pt-rich clusters with low Sn contributions.”

Furthermore, as noted in quote 2, the choice of secondary metal influences the optimal size of Pt sites. Replacing Sn with metals like Cu, In, Zn, or Ga may change the ideal Pt dimension for maximizing catalyst performance through electronic or geometric effects. Overall, this study demonstrates that the nanoparticle size and secondary metal selection allows modulation of the bimetallic structure, which impacts critical aspects like selectivity, activity, and stability.

The article [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667] investigates both electronic and geometric effects induced by the secondary metal phosphorus in Ru-P bimetallic catalysts applied for propane dehydrogenation. As elaborated in quote 1, increasing the P content helps reduce the Ru ensemble size, serving as a structural promoter. This decreases the hydrogenolysis rate, demonstrating a geometric effect of P isolation. Quote 2 discusses how formation of Ru-P bonds shifts the Ru XPS and XANES spectra to higher energies, indicating decreased Ru 4d orbital energies and thus weakened metal-adsorbate interaction strengths. This exemplifies the electronic effect of P incorporation into the Ru lattice.

Furthermore, the study [10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5] explores how employing MXene supports facilitates the formation of ordered intermetallic compound nanoparticles between Pt and secondary metals like Ti or Nb, which is unattainable with conventional oxide or carbide carriers (quote 2). The different MXene compositions allow altering the chemical properties of the resulting bimetallic catalysts (quote 3). This demonstrates that secondary metals like Ti and Nb can induce unique electronic and geometric effects in Pt alloy nanoparticles supported on designer 2D carbide platforms.

Overall, strategic selection of secondary metals, customization of metal nanostructures, and utilization of unconventional supports provides effective means to harness favorable electronic and geometric effects in bimetallic propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Further research might focus on expanding the library of secondary metals evaluated and engineering ternary alloy compositions on compositionally modulated 2D layered supports to elicit synergistic electronic and geometric transformations for enabling enhanced catalytic performance.

根据对[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]研究的详细分析,较大的Pt纳米粒子可以吸纳更多的Sn种类,与较小的Pt簇相比,这导致在前一种情况下形成PtSn合金纳米粒子,而在后一种情况下形成低Sn含量的Pt富集簇。这些结果揭示了Sn次要金属与Pt活性位点的相互作用因Pt纳米粒子的大小而异。 正如在引用1中所述,“通过结合理论和实验研究的结果,我们可以得出结论:与亚纳米Pt簇相比,Pt纳米粒子(>1 nm)具有更高的Sn种类容纳量,这导致了PtSn合金纳米 粒子的形成,而小颗粒尺寸的Pt簇往往形成低Sn贡献的Pt富集簇。”

此外,如引用2中所述,次要金属的选择会影响Pt位点的最佳大小。 用Cu、In、Zn或Ga替代Sn可能会通过电子或几何效应改变实现最大催化剂性能的理想Pt尺寸。总的来说,这项研究表明纳米粒子大小和次要金属的选择允许调制双金属结构,这会影响选择性、活性和稳定性等关键方面。

[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]的文章调查了磷作为Ru-P双金属催化剂中次要金属诱导的电子和几何效应,应用于丙烷脱氢反应。 正如引文1中详细阐述的,提高P含量有助于减少Ru组装大小,作为一种结构促进剂。这减少了氢化裂解反应的速率,体现出P的隔离的几何效应。引文2讨论了Ru-P键的形成如何使Ru的XPS和XANES光谱向更高的能量位移,这表明Ru 4d轨道能量的下降以及金属与吸附剂相互作用强度的减弱。这证明了P并入Ru晶格的电子效应。

此外,研究[10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5] 探讨了使用MXene支撑的有序金属间化合物Pt和次要金属纳米粒子的形成,如Ti或Nb,这在传统氧化物或碳化物载体上是不可实现的(引文2)。不同的MXene成分允许改变所得到的双金属催化剂的化学性质(引文3)。这表明次要金属如Ti和Nb可以在以设计者2D碳化物平台为载体的Pt合金纳米粒子中诱导独特的电子和几何效应。

总的来说,战略选择次要金属、定制金属纳米结构以及利用非常规载体为丙烷脱氢催化剂提供了有效手段来利用有利的电子和几何效应。进一步的研究可能会集中在扩大所评估的次要金属库,以及在组成调制的2D层状支持上工程化三元合金组合,以引发协同的电子和几何转变,实现加强的催化性能。
