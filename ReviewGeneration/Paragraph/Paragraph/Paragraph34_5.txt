The article with DOI 10.1021/cr5002436 highlights needs for improved in-situ characterization tools to better understand propane dehydrogenation catalysts [1]. It identifies opportunities to use time-resolved UV-vis and Raman spectroscopies to monitor industrial chromia-alumina catalysts and improve overall process efficiency [2]. 

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816 also highlights needs for advanced in-situ characterization techniques like iDPC-STEM, in-situ spectroscopy, and 2D solid-state NMR to gain better insights into propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

The remaining articles cited do not specifically highlight needs for improved in-situ characterization tools. Several focus on reporting experimental results using established ex-situ methods rather than examining needs for new capabilities [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.02.005, 10.1016/j.cej.2007.11.009].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The scant literature underscoring requirements for advanced in-situ diagnostics intimates ample scope for exploration. Burgeoning techniques like operando spectroscopy, aberration-corrected electron microscopy, soft X-ray tomography etc. possess immense potential. Future undertakings might concentrate on honing such novel methods to glean unparalleled perception into working catalysts. Insights thus garnered can engender superior dehydrogenation processes via rational design.
