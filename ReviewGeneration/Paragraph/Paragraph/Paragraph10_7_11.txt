Based on the details extracted from the literature, mechanistic scenarios proposed for propane dehydrogenation over different metal oxide catalysts can be summarized as follows:

On Cr-based catalysts, active sites are proposed to be coordinatively unsaturated Cr3+ sites [DOI1, DOI2], Cr3+/Cr2+ redox couples [DOI3], or Cr6+/Cr3+/Cr2+ redox cycles [DOI4]. Key steps involve CH bond activation, hydride elimination, and re-oxidation of reduced Cr species. 

On VOx catalysts, Mars-Van Krevelen type mechanisms are described involving lattice oxygen for oxidative dehydrogenation, with subsequent non-oxidative steps over reduced V3+ sites [DOI5, DOI6]. Chemical looping pathways are also proposed to modulate surface oxygen species [DOI7].

For GaOx catalysts, heterolytic dissociation is preferred over a redox path, with key steps like dissociative propane adsorption, beta-H elimination from alkyl gallium intermediates, and H2 recombination [DOI8, DOI9]. Isolated Ga3+ sites or Ga+/H+ pairs are noted as active [DOI10].

On ZrO2, coordinatively unsaturated Zr4+ sites neighboring oxygen vacancies catalyze propane dehydrogenation via a Zr-propyl intermediate, beta-H elimination, and active site regeneration [DOI11, DOI12]. Modification with dopants like Ga can alter the mechanism and energetics [DOI13].

Additionally, synergies between different sites like Zn-Zr [DOI14] and interfacial sites in mixed oxides [DOI15] are proposed to facilitate key reaction steps. Pathways to limit overoxidation side reactions are also described [DOI7, DOI16].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The proposed mechanistic scenarios reveal insights into how active sites composed of coordinatively unsaturated metal cations and lattice oxygen facilitate key dehydrogenation steps, often via Mars-Van Krevelen or redox cycles. Strategies like chemical looping, heteroatom doping, and nanostructuring emerge to tune the active site speciation, oxygen availability, and resulting selectivity. Future research could focus on operando spectroscopic techniques to capture dynamic surface changes during propane conversion over promising metal oxide compositions. Advanced multi-scale simulations may also help clarify kinetically preferred pathways and opportunities to break scaling relations limiting conversion.
