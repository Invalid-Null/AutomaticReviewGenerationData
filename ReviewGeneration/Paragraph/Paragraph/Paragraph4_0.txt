<English>
Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an endothermic process (ΔH298K = 124.3 kJ/mol) that is thermodynamically limited, requiring high temperatures of 550-650°C to achieve sufficient equilibrium conversion [10.1002/advs.202207756, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. The reaction also suffers from rapid catalyst deactivation via coke formation and over-reduction [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]. To overcome these challenges, recent efforts have focused on developing novel catalysts and processes. 

Several studies have examined the kinetics of propane dehydrogenation over various catalysts. Apparent activation energies range from 180-200 kJ/mol on H-CHA zeolites [10.1021/jacs.2c03941], while Pt-based catalysts have shown lower barriers around 0.71 eV (68 kJ/mol) for the rate limiting α-H abstraction step [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. First-principles calculations have provided further insights, revealing a correlation between propylene adsorption energies and key transition states along the reaction coordinate [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889].

Introducing oxidants like O2 or CO2 can improve thermodynamic driving forces via exothermic oxidation reactions, though kinetic considerations like re-oxidation must be balanced [10.1002/advs.202207756, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. Isolated metal sites help kinetically stabilize against undesirable dehydrogenation and coking side reactions [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]. Overall, advancing PDH requires holistic catalyst design considering both thermodynamic and kinetic factors.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, there lies an opportunity to explore emerging fields and innovative methodologies. Future research endeavors might focus on developing novel catalysts like MOFs or 2D heterojunctions that can tune redox potentials and stabilize isolated active sites to balance thermodynamic and kinetic constraints. Operando spectroscopy and machine learning can also help construct quantitative structure-activity relationships to guide optimization. Broader process intensification efforts might couple PDH with other chemical production pathways in sustainable biorefinery contexts.

<Chinese>
丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一个端othermic过程(ΔH298K = 124.3 kJ/mol),在热力学上是有限的,需要550-650°C的高温才能达到足够的平衡转化[10.1002/advs.202207756,10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。该反应还因焦炭形成和过度还原而快速失活催化剂[10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]。为了克服这些挑战,最近的努力集中在开发新型催化剂和工艺。

几项研究已经检查了丙烷脱氢反应在各种催化剂上的动力学。表观活化能在H-CHA分子筛上为180-200 kJ/mol [10.1021/jacs.2c03941],而Pt基催化剂对限速步骤α-H提取的屏障约为0.71 eV(68 kJ/mol)[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。第一性原理计算提供了进一步的见解,揭示了丙烯吸附能量与反应坐标沿线的关键转变状态之间的相关性[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。

 引入氧化剂如O2或CO2可以通过放热氧化反应改善热力学驱动力,尽管必须平衡诸如再氧化之类的动力学考虑[10.1002/advs.202207756,10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]。离子金属位点有助于氢化和焦炭化等不良副反应的动力学稳定[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]。总体而言,推进PDH需要整体催化剂设计以考虑热力学和动力学因素。
