Based on the details provided in the file, there is limited evaluation of promising metal oxide catalysts like Cr2O3, Pt-Sn, Ga2O3 for propane dehydrogenation across most of the literature surveyed. However, a few studies provide some insights:

One article evaluates Cr-based catalysts as promising metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, stating they are among the most active catalysts for CO2-ODH reaction, and CrOx is a key component in the industrial CATOFIN process [10.1021/acscatal.2c05286]. Data shows Cr-based catalysts supported on MCM-41 exhibit the highest activity compared to other metal oxides like Ga, Ni, V, Fe, Mn, and Co. However, stability remains an issue.

Another study evaluates different polymorphs of Ga2O3 including α-Ga2O3, β-Ga2O3, and γ-Ga2O3 as unsupported nanocrystalline catalysts as well as silica-supported β-Ga2O3/SiO2 and γ-Ga2O3/SiO2 in propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/acscatal.0c05009]. Among them, β-Ga2O3 showed the highest catalytic activity.

One article provides data showing a Pt-Sn bimetallic catalyst supported on SBA-15 achieved 40% initial propane conversion and 92% propene selectivity, attributing the improved performance to geometric and electronic effects of the Pt-Sn alloy [10.1021/acscatal.1c00639].

Additionally, one study evaluates mixtures of Ga2O3-ZrO2 as catalysts, finding increased Ga content monotonically increased Lewis acid sites. Cr2O3/SiO2 also achieved relatively high conversion and yield compared to other metal oxides [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999].

Overall however, the vast majority of articles surveyed do not evaluate Cr2O3, Pt-Sn, Ga2O3 or other promising metal oxide catalysts. Most focus solely on supported Pt, Pt-Sn and other metallic catalysts instead.

Integrative Understanding and Prospective Outlook:

While only a handful of studies evaluate promising metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, those that do provide valuable proof-of-concept regarding the potential of chromium, gallium and mixed metal oxide catalysts. 

Building on these seminal works lies opportunity for more extensive exploration into optimal compositions, tailored metal-support interactions, in-depth mechanistic characterizations, and scale-up methodologies. 

Advanced characterization techniques like in situ spectroscopy and electron microscopy could elucidate reaction intermediates and pathways over promising oxides like Cr2O3 and Ga2O3 polymorphs under realistic conditions. Controlled nanoscale engineering of active sites may overcome intrinsic stability limitations.
