Based on the details extracted from the file, advanced microscopy techniques have revealed critical insights into metal-support interfaces in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Specifically, aberration-corrected scanning transmission electron microscopy (AC-STEM) techniques such as high-angle annular dark field (HAADF) imaging and integrated differential phase contrast (iDPC) have enabled direct visualization of isolated metal atoms and clusters anchored within micropores and channels of zeolite supports [DOI1, DOI2]. By correlating iDPC images showing the zeolite framework with HAADF-STEM imaging sensitive to heavier metal species, researchers precisely determined locations of sub-1 nm Pt species preferentially situated within 14-membered ring channels of zeolite UTL [DOI3]. 

Atomic-scale HAADF-STEM imaging has also elucidated the intermetallic structure of PtZn and PtY nanoparticles on supports, with images revealing alternating columns of Pt and promoter metals [DOI4]. Quantitative analysis of sequential images captured during treatment steps has allowed tracking of the atomic configuration from single atoms to larger clusters [DOI5]. Additional techniques like scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDX) mapping have uncovered strong metal-support anchoring, evidenced by isolated metal atoms and resistance to agglomeration after prolonged reaction [DOI6].

Overall, advanced microscopy has proven indispensable for fundamental understanding of active phase location, dispersion, metal-support bonding, and structural evolution under working conditions. Yet opportunities remain to explore in-situ, operando techniques [DOI7] to capture dynamic changes at critical interfaces, integrate with other structural probes like X-ray absorption spectroscopy [DOI8], and harness advanced data science for more automated quantification [DOI9]. Pushing such boundaries in multi-modal characterization will uncover further horizons related to tailored interface design and stabilization.

基于文件中提取的详细信息,先进的微观技术已经揭示了丙烷脱氢催化剂中金属载体界面上的关键见解。 具体来说,球差修正的扫描透射电子显微镜(AC-STEM)技术,如大角度环形暗场(HAADF)成像和集成差分相位对比(iDPC),使得直接观察到锚定在沸石载体的微孔和通道中的隔离金属原子和簇变为可能 [DOI1, DOI2]。 通过关联显示沸石框架的iDPC图像与对较重金属种类敏感的HAADF-STEM成像,研究人员精确地确定了优先位于沸石UTL的14元环通道中的亚1 nm Pt物种的位置 [DOI3]。

原子尺度的HAADF-STEM成像还阐明了载体上PtZn和PtY纳米粒子的金属间化合物结构,图像显示了Pt和助剂金属的交替列[DOI4]。 在处理步骤期间捕获的顺序图像的定量分析使跟踪从单个原子到更大簇团的原子配置成为可能[DOI5]。 额外的技术,如扫描电子显微镜(SEM)和能量色散X射线光谱(EDX)成像,发现了强大的金属载体锚定的证据,即隔离的金属原子和反应后不聚集[DOI6]。

总的来说,先进的显微镜技术在理解活性相位位置、分散度、金属载体键合以及工作条件下的结构演变方面至关重要。 然而,探索原位和操作条件下的技术[DOI7]以捕获关键界面的动态变化,将其与其他结构探针(如X射线吸收光谱[DOI8])集成,并利用先进的数据科学进行更自动化的定量[DOI9]仍有机会。 推进这种多模态表征的边界将揭示与定制界面设计和稳定化相关的进一步视野。
