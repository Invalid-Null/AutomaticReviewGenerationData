Based on the detailed analysis from the study [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548], larger Pt nanoparticles can accommodate more Sn species than smaller Pt clusters, leading to the formation of PtSn alloy nanoparticles in the former case and Pt-rich clusters with low Sn content in the latter case. These lessons could be further extended to other Pt-based bimetallic catalysts, though the choice of secondary metal (e.g. Cu, In, Zn, Ga instead of Sn) may influence the optimal size of the Pt sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548].

The article [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671] investigates the electronic and geometric effects of Sn as a secondary metal in supported PtSn bimetallic clusters. It demonstrates that Sn forms strong bonds with Al2O3 support oxygens at the cluster-support interface. Sn also exhibits a stabilization effect on the Pt clusters [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671].

Yes, the article [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667] investigates both electronic and geometric effects of phosphorus addition in Ru-P bimetallic catalysts. Increasing the P content is shown to reduce the Ru ensemble size, decreasing the hydrogenolysis rate (geometric effect). Ru-P bond formation also shifts the Ru XPS and XANES spectra to higher energies, suggesting decreased Ru 4d orbital energies that weaken metal-adsorbate bonds (electronic effect) [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667].

The article [10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5] investigates electronic and geometric effects of Ti and Nb as secondary metals in Pt-Ti and Pt-Nb bimetallic catalysts formed by reactive metal support interaction with MXene supports. It shows ordered intermetallic nanoparticles can be formed that are not possible with traditional oxide or carbide supports. The different MXene compositions allow tuning of the catalyst properties and performance [10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5]. 

Yes, the article [10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129] investigates electronic effects of the secondary metal Ga in GaPt bimetallic catalysts using XPS and IR data. The results indicate electron donation from Ga leading to a higher electron density on Pt sites due to GaPt alloy formation, improving catalytic performance [10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129].

[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]的文章研究了Sn作为PtSn双金属簇中次要金属的电子和几何效应。它表明Sn与Al2O3支持体氧原子在簇支撑体界面形成强键合。Sn还对Pt簇体现出稳定效应[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。

是的,文章[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]研究了磷在Ru-P双金属催化剂中的添加的电子和几何效应。结果表明,增加P含量可以减少Ru组分的大小,从而降低氢化裂解速率(几何效应)。 Ru-P键的形成也将Ru的XPS和XANES光谱转移到更高的能量,这表明Ru 4d轨道能量降低,从而削弱了金属吸附剂键(电子效应)[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。

[10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5]的文章调查了与MXene支撑体反应的Ti和Nb作为Pt-Ti和Pt-Nb双金属催化剂中的次要金属的电子和几何效应。它表明可以形成传统氧化物或碳化物支撑体所无法形成的有序中间相纳米粒子。不同的MXene组成允许调谐催化剂性能[10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5]。

是的,文章[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129] 使用XPS和红外数据研究了Ga作为GaPt双金属催化剂中次要金属的电子效应。结果表明,Ga的电子递赠导致Pt位点上的电子密度更高,这是由于GaPt合金的形成,从而提高了催化性能[10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129]。
