Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts require optimized active phase dispersion to achieve high selectivity and stability. The article investigates various catalyst preparation methods aimed at optimizing dispersion of active species. It prepared Co@Si-BEA catalysts with 0.1-0.5 wt% Co by vacuum impregnation, showing the low loading samples had best dispersion of isolated Co2+ sites and PDH performance [Placeholder_Of_DOI1]. Dealuminated Co@Si-BEA also produced less coke than standard Co@BEA, attributed to better metal dispersion [Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

Overall, common strategies include using low metal loadings [Placeholder_Of_DOI3], high surface area supports [Placeholder_Of_DOI4], and tuning parameters like pH [Placeholder_Of_DOI5] and calcination temperature [Placeholder_Of_DOI6] during impregnation or precipitation. Direct hydrothermal synthesis can incorporate isolated, stabilized metal sites into frameworks [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. Advanced characterization techniques like EXAFS, IR, and electron microscopy are crucial for understanding dispersion.

Achieving optimized dispersion requires systematically tuning preparation parameters and thoroughly characterizing the catalyst structure. Most reported catalysts still lack long-term stability. An opportunity exists to apply emerging characterization tools like machine learning to accelerate optimization and help translate lab-scale catalysts to commercial processes. Research could also explore more dynamic techniques like selective removal of aggregates to actively tailor dispersion based on reaction monitoring.
