**Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?**

Several studies have investigated the effects of alloying platinum (Pt) with secondary metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), and zinc (Zn) for improving the performance of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. 

**Pt-Sn Catalysts**

Multiple works have shown that adding Sn as a promoter to Pt-based catalysts supported on materials like alumina, silica, and zeolites leads to higher initial activity, propylene selectivity and stability compared to monometallic Pt catalysts [Placeholder_of_DOI1, Placeholder_of_DOI2, Placeholder_of_DOI3]. For example, one study on PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts achieved 40% propane conversion and >98% propylene selectivity at 570°C [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. 

The improved performance is attributed to Sn altering the geometric and electronic properties of Pt through ensemble and ligand effects. Sn helps disperse Pt sites, reducing unwanted hydrogenolysis and isomerization side reactions, while also modifying the adsorption properties and increasing the electron density on Pt sites to facilitate selective C-H bond activation [Placeholder_of_DOI5]. EXAFS characterization of Pt-Sn bonding suggests the formation of intermetallic phases like Pt3Sn during reaction [Placeholder_of_DOI6]. 

However, some studies note initial PtSn catalysts with ~1:1 Pt/Sn ratio show the best propane conversion, while excess Sn can suppress activity by diluting Pt sites [Placeholder_of_DOI7]. Thus, maintaining a Pt/Sn ratio >1:1 appears optimal for balancing high conversion and propylene selectivity.

**Pt-Ga Catalysts** 

Investigations on Pt-Ga bimetallic catalysts like Pt-Ga/Al2O3, Pt-Ga/zeolites demonstrate Ga as a promoter leads to improved activity, selectivity and stability over Pt catalysts for propane dehydrogenation [Placeholder_of_DOI8, Placeholder_of_DOI9]. One Pt-Ga catalyst achieved close to equilibrium propane conversion of 45.9% at 600°C with 92.1% propylene selectivity [Placeholder_of_DOI10]. 

The structure-performance insights indicate Ga enhances Pt dispersion while also tuning the geometric and electronic properties. Specifically, the Pt-Ga ensemble weakens side reactions like hydrogenolysis and suppresses coke formation pathways [Placeholder_of_DOI11].

**Pt-Zn Catalysts**

Pt-Zn catalyst studies demonstrate isolating Pt atoms within Zn-modified supports like silica and zeolite Beta allows maintaining small, highly selective Pt ensembles [Placeholder_of_DOI12, Placeholder_of_DOI13]. EXAFS analysis confirms the presence of Pt-Zn bonding with coordination numbers around 4, similar to intermetallic phases [Placeholder_of_DOI14]. A Pt-Zn dealuminated zeolite Beta catalyst achieved 30% propane conversion and 90% propylene selectivity close to equilibrium levels [Placeholder_of_DOI15].

The insights indicate Zn anchors and separates Pt sites while tuning the electronic structure. This limits unwanted hydrogenation/dehydrogenation pathways on Pt ensembles. Furthermore, the Zn isolates and stabilizes Pt against sintering during high temperature PDH conditions. An optimal Pt/Zn ratio > 1 helps balance conversion and selectivity.

**Integrative Understanding and Prospective Outlook**

The array of findings substantiate alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga, and Zn facilitates notable augmentation in conversion, selectivity and stability metrics for propane dehydrogenation catalysts over monometallic Pt benchmarks. 

While Sn, Ga and Zn modulate via disparate mechanisms, the overarching theme entails these secondary metals tailoring the electronic-geometric environment of Pt sites by ensemble dilution, ligand perturbations, metal-support anchoring and suppressed coking pathways. This offers opportunities to strategically tune the structure and composition of single-atom Pt sites decorated with promoters within zeolites and on supports for engendering optimal propane dehydrogenation performance.

Burgeoning research might explore ternary Pt-Sn-Zn, Pt-Ga-Zn combinations or alternative promoters like In, Cu, Fe, etc. High-throughput DFT screening of promoter motifs could identify novel binary/ternary mixtures and support architectures that offer thermodynamic downhill pathways for selective propylene formation. Operando spectroscopy and microscopy during propane dehydrogenation over these multifaceted catalysts can offer vital feedback for tuning promoter-metal-support synergy.

**双金属和合金催化剂 - 合金化Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?**

几项研究调查了用第二金属(例如锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn))合金化铂(Pt)来改善丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂性能的影响。

**Pt-Sn 催化剂**

多项工作表明,在氧化铝、硅酸盐和分子筛等载体上,向以Pt为基础的催化剂中添加Sn促进剂,可提高初始活性、丙烯选择性和稳定性,相比单金属Pt催化剂[Placeholder_of_DOI1, Placeholder_of_DOI2, Placeholder_of_DOI3]。例如,一项关于PtSn/Al2O3催化剂的研究在570°C下实现了40%的丙烷转化率和98%以上的丙烯选择性[Placeholder_of_DOI4]。

改善的性能归因于Sn通过配体效应改变Pt的几何和电子性质。Sn有助于分散Pt活性位点,减少不必要的加氢裂化和异构化副反应,同时也改变Pt的吸附性质,增加Pt位点上的电子密度,促进选择性C-H键激活[Placeholder_of_DOI5]。 Pt-Sn的EXAFS表征显示反应过程中形成Pt3Sn等互金属相[Placeholder_of_DOI6]。

然而,一些研究指出,Pt/Sn比约为1:1的初始PtSn催化剂显示了最佳的丙烷转化率,而过量的Sn会通过稀释Pt活性位点来抑制活性[Placeholder_of_DOI7]。因此,维持Pt/Sn比>1:1似乎是平衡高转化率和丙烯选择性的最佳选择。

**Pt-Ga 催化剂**

对Pt-Ga双金属催化剂(如Pt-Ga/Al2O3、Pt-Ga/分子筛)的研究表明,与Pt催化剂相比,Ga作为助剂可提高丙烷脱氢的活性、选择性和稳定性[Placeholder_of_DOI8, Placeholder_of_DOI9]。 一种Pt-Ga催化剂在600°C下实现了接近平衡的45.9%丙烷转化率,丙烯选择性为92.1% [Placeholder_of_DOI10]。

结构-性能结果显示Ga有助于分散Pt活性位点,同时调节几何和电子性质。 具体来说,Pt-Ga配合物削弱了加氢裂化等副反应,抑制了焦炭生成途径[Placeholder_of_DOI11]。

**Pt-Zn 催化剂** 

Pt-Zn催化剂研究表明,将Pt原子隔离在Zn修饰的硅酸盐和沸石Beta等载体内,可以维持小的、高选择性的Pt配合物[Placeholder_of_DOI12, Placeholder_of_DOI13]。 EXAFS分析确认了与互金属相类似的Pt-Zn键合存在,配位数约为4 [Placeholder_of_DOI14]。 一种Pt-Zn去离子Beta沸石催化剂实现了30%的丙烷转化率和90%的丙烯选择性,接近平衡水平[Placeholder_of_DOI15]。

这些发现表明,Zn锚定和分离Pt位点,同时调节电子结构。这限制了Pt配合物上的不必要的加氢/脱氢途径。 此外,Zn隔离和稳定Pt,抵抗高温PDH条件下的烧结。 Pt/Zn比>1有助于在转化率和选择性之间取得平衡。

※※※

**综合理解和前景展望**

诸多发现证实,与单金属Pt基准相比,用Sn、Ga和Zn等助剂合金化Pt可显著提高丙烷脱氢催化剂的转化率、选择性和稳定性指标。

尽管Sn、Ga和Zn通过不同的机制进行调制,但主题是这些第二金属通过配体稀释、配体扰动、金属载体锚定和抑制焦化途径等方式,调节Pt位点的电子-几何环境。 这为在分子筛和载体上策略性地调控掺杂助剂的单原子Pt位点的结构和组成,产生优化的丙烷脱氢性能,提供了机会。

新兴的研究可能会探索三元Pt-Sn-Zn、Pt-Ga-Zn组合或In、Cu、Fe等替代促进剂。 高通量DFT筛选促进剂结构可以识别新的二元/三元混合物和支持体系,为选择性丙烯形成提供热力学上利好的路径。 在这些复杂催化剂上进行原位光谱与显微表征,可以为调优助剂-金属-载体协同效应提供关键反馈。
