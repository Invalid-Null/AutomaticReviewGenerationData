    Based on the detailed analysis from the references provided [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, 10.101/j.jcis.2021.02.129, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.101/j.jechem.2020.08.058, 10.1126/science.abg7894, 10.101/S1872-2067(17)62750-5], bimetallic and alloy Pt catalysts with promoters like Sn, Ga, Zn show great promise for propane dehydrogenation. Adding these secondary metals helps modulate the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites to achieve higher activity, selectivity and stability compared to monometallic Pt catalysts. 
    
    Specifically, incorporating Sn into Pt catalysts is widely implemented, with PtSn intermetallic nanoparticles demonstrating exceptional propylene selectivity (>99%) without coking or sintering over extended periods [10.1126/science.abg7894]. The synergy is attributed to Sn altering the d-band center of Pt, improving activation of propane while suppressing hydrogenolysis and coke formation pathways [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]. Ga as a promoter also significantly enhances dehydrogenation selectivity and stability by modifying ensemble and ligand effects [10.101/j.jcis.2021.02.129]. A Pt-Ga catalyst achieved 92.1% propylene selectivity and 45.9% propane conversion at 600°C. Meanwhile, alloying Pt with Zn [10.101/j.jechem.2020.08.058] and In [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] increased propylene yield beyond equilibrium limitations.
    
    Overall, strategic addition of secondary metals creates optimized Pt bimetallic and alloy configurations for selective alkane activation [10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]. The compositional variations and geometric arrangements modulate critical descriptors governing activity and selectivity. Further explorations might focus on:

    - Fundamental studies into tuning metal-support interfaces and oxidation states to optimize ensemble requirements and ligand influences
    - Novel synthetic techniques to precisely control size, composition and structure of multimetallic clusters
    - Operando spectroscopic characterization to probe dynamic structural evolutions during working conditions
    - Kinetic and computational assessments into descriptor-performance relations directing rational catalyst optimization

    根据所提供参考文献的详细分析[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.101/j.cej.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, 10.101/j.jcis.2021.02.129, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.101/j.jechem.2020.08.058, 10.1126/science.abg7894, 10.101/S1872-2067(17)62750-5],掺杂Sn、Ga、Zn等助剂的双金属和合金Pt催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中展现出巨大的应用前景。添加这些辅助金属有助于调控Pt活性位点的几何和电子性质,相比单一Pt催化剂,实现更高的活性、选择性和稳定性。
    
    具体来说,掺杂Sn的Pt催化剂应用广泛,PtSn金属间化合物纳米粒子表现出卓越的丙�ylene选择性(>99%),在扩展的反应周期内不发生焦化和烧结[10.1126/science.abg7894]。这种协同效应归因于Sn改变了Pt的d带中心,提高了丙烷的活化同时抑制了氢裂解和焦化反应途径[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]。Ga作为助剂也明显提高了脱氢选择性和稳定性,通过调节配位数和配体效应实现[10.101/j.jcis.2021.02.129]。一种Pt-Ga催化剂在600°C下达到了92.1%的丙烯选择性和45.9%的丙烷转化率。与此同时,与Pt形成合金的Zn [10.101/j.jechem.2020.08.058] 和In [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]使丙烯收率超过了平衡限度。

    总的来说,策略性地添加辅助金属制备优化的Pt双金属和合金配置是选择性烷烃活化的关键[10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]。组成变化和几何排列调节了控制活性和选择性的关键描述符。未来的研究方向可能集中于:
    
    - 基础研究:调控金属-支持体界面和氧化态以优化配体组效应和配体影响
    - 新颖的合成技术:精确控制多金属簇的大小、组成和结构
    - 在线光谱表征:探测工作条件下的动态结构演变
    - 动力学和计算评估:确定描述符-性能关系指导合理的催化剂优化
