<English>
Chromium oxide based catalysts have emerged as promising options for propane dehydrogenation. Cr-based catalysts are reported to be among the most active options for CO2-assisted oxidative dehydrogenation reactions (Quotes 15, 16). CrOx forms a vital component of industrial CATOFIN catalysts employed for direct propane dehydrogenation to propylene (Quote 16). Among various metal oxides, Cr-based catalysts supported on MCM-41 exhibit the highest propane dehydrogenation activity compared to Ga, Ni, V, Fe, Mn, and Co (Quote 16). However, poor stability remains an issue with Cr-based catalysts that warrants further optimization. 

Gallium oxide polymorphs, especially β-Ga2O3, have also demonstrated considerable promise for propane dehydrogenation (Quotes 49, 50). This is attributed to their optimal surface acidity that correlates with high initial propane dehydrogenation rates (Quote 49). Unsupported nanocrystalline β-Ga2O3 and γ-Ga2O3 along with silica-supported β-Ga2O3/SiO2 and γ-Ga2O3/SiO2 display appreciable propane dehydrogenation activity, surpassing conventional amorphous Ga/SiO2 catalysts prepared by impregnation (Quote 50). Further research could focus on optimizing gallium oxide synthesis procedures and supporting architectures to boost stability and longevity.

Other promising options include bimetallic Pt-Sn catalysts which have been commercialized for propane dehydrogenation (Quotes 17, 34). The addition of Sn helps form intermetallic Pt-Sn sites that curtail over-hydrogenation and coking, enhancing propylene selectivity (Quotes 17, 34). Supported Pt-Sn catalysts synthesized by techniques like coimpregnation demonstrate high initial propane conversion up to 50% and propylene selectivity greater than 90% (Quote 34). Future efforts may concentrate on identifying optimal Pt:Sn ratios and tailoring synthesis protocols to maximize the population of bifunctional Pt-Sn active sites. 

In summary, Cr-oxides, Ga-oxides and Pt-Sn alloys highlight the most lucrative metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation thus far. However, long-term stability issues persist, warranting the need to unravel degradation mechanisms and alloy effects at the molecular level to inform rational design principles. Advanced in-situ spectroscopy techniques combined with computational modeling will likely spearhead these endeavors and illuminate optimal compositions and structures to unlock the full potential of metal oxide catalysts.

The synergistic integration of cutting-edge experimental and computational methodologies will likely catalyze transformative breakthroughs in optimized metal oxide catalyst design for propane dehydrogenation.

<Chinese>
以氧化铬为基础的催化剂已经成为丙烷脱氢的有希望选择。文献报道,铬基催化剂是CO2辅助氧化脱氢反应中活性最高的选择之一(引文15,16)。 CrOx形成了工业上采用的直接丙烷脱氢制丙烯的CATOFIN催化剂中的重要组成部分(引文16)。 在各种金属氧化物中,载于MCM-41上的铬基催化剂相比Ga、Ni、V、Fe、Mn和Co表现出最高的丙烷脱氢活性(引文16)。 然而,铬基催化剂的稳定性较差仍然是一个需要进一步优化的问题。

氧化镓的多晶体,尤其是β-Ga2O3,也展现出了可观的丙烷脱氢潜力(引文49,50)。这归因于其最佳的表面酸性,与高的初始丙烷脱氢率有关(引文49)。无载体的纳米晶β-Ga2O3和γ-Ga2O3以及硅胶载体上的β-Ga2O3/SiO2和γ-Ga2O3/SiO2均显示出显著的丙烷脱氢活性,优于常规的浸渍法制备的非晶Ga/SiO2催化剂(引文50)。进一步的研究可以关注优化氧化镓的合成方法和载体结构,以提高稳定性和使用寿命。

其它有希望的选择包括已商业化用于丙烷脱氢的双金属Pt-Sn催化剂(引文17,34)。 Sn的添加有助于形成铂锡互金属位点,抑制过度氢化和焦炭生成,提高丙烯选择性(引文17,34)。 采用共浸渍等技术合成的载体Pt-Sn催化剂表现出高达50%的初始丙烷转化率和90%以上的丙烯选择性(引文34)。未来的工作可能集中在确定最佳的Pt:Sn比例,并调整合成方案以最大程度地获得双功能Pt-Sn活性位点。

总之,至今为止Cr氧化物、Ga氧化物和Pt-Sn合金是最有前景的丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂。 然而,长期稳定性问题仍然存在,需要揭示降解机制和合金效应的分子水平细节,以指导合理的设计原则。先进的原位光谱技术结合计算建模可能会引领这些努力,阐明获得最佳组成和结构以释放金属氧化物催化剂全部潜力的关键。

综合理解与未来展望:考虑到文件中阐述的进展和发现,丙烷脱氢金属氧化物催化剂领域展现出巨大的前景。 尽管Cr、Ga和Pt-Sn氧化物至今表现出令人鼓舞的活性,但需要解决几个方面以进一步推进。阐明降解途径、实现理想的位点密度和最大化金属与载体的相互作用应该是未来追求的重点。 利用像原子层沉积这样的先进合成技术,可以实现带有可调组成的新型多功能单原子合金催化剂的合成。 使用原位光谱探针技术配合微动力学和DFT建模对揭示多面活性位点间的复杂机理互相关系至关重要。 先进的实验和计算方法ologies的协同整合可能会催生丙烷脱氢优化金属氧化物催化剂设计上的革命性突破。
