Based on the details provided, several studies have explored supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts have been extensively studied and even commercialized for PDH due to Pt's excellent ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing C-C bond cleavage [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. Experiments show supported Pt catalysts like Pt/Al2O3 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03381] and Pt/MgAl2O4 [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X] exhibit good initial activity, but suffer from gradual loss in activity over time due to Pt agglomeration and coking under PDH conditions. Strategies explored to enhance supported Pt catalyst stability include addition of promoters like B [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548] and applying protective ALD overcoat layers [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. There is also some evidence that strong metal-support interactions with supports like BN may improve Pt stability and selectivity [DOI: 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008].

Besides Pt, some studies have explored Pd [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002] and Ir [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002] for PDH, but note they are prohibitively expensive alternatives. Ruthenium nanoparticles were also evaluated as a potentially cheaper alternative to Pt, but found to have very low propylene selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667].

Overall, Pt-based catalysts remain the predominant choice for commercial PDH processes, but deactivation issues persist. Enhancing stability via promoters, overcoat layers, and metal-support interactions shows promise but requires further optimization. Research into lower cost alternatives is still in early stages.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, emerging strategies like single-atom alloy catalysts [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389] and confinement of sub-nanometric clusters in zeolites [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] offer new pathways to realize stable and selective supported metal PDH catalysts. Computational screening of novel metal-support combinations [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671] may also uncover promising candidates. Broader adoption of operando techniques can provide key insights into working catalysts to guide optimization. Integrating these contemporary approaches with foundational knowledge on prototypical Pt catalysts accumulated over decades may unlock the requisite stability and affordability needed for widespread PDH commercialization.

<Chinese>
根据所提供的详细信息,几项研究已经探索了负载式单金属Pt、Pd和Ir催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。

由于Pt表现出色的C-H键活化能力和最小的C-C键断裂,Pt基催化剂已经被广泛研究甚至商业化用于PDH [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。实验表明,诸如Pt/Al2O3 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03381] 和Pt/MgAl2O4 [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]等负载Pt催化剂表现出较好的初始活性,但随着时间的推移,由于Pt团聚和PDH条件下的结焦,逐渐失活。已探索的提高负载Pt催化剂稳定性的策略包括添加促进剂如B [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]和施加保护ALD覆层[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。 一些证据表明,与BN等支持剂形成强金属支持体间相互作用也可以提高Pt的稳定性和选择性[DOI: 10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]。

除Pt外,一些研究还探索了Pd [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]和Ir [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002] 用于PDH,但指出它们是昂贵的替代品。也评估了钌纳米粒子作为Pt的潜在廉价替代品,但发现其丙烯选择性非常低[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。

总的来说,Pt基催化剂仍然是商业PDH工艺的主要选择,但是失活问题仍然存在。通过促进剂、覆层和金属支撑体间相互作用提高稳定性显示了前景,但需要进一步优化。 对廉价替代品的研究仍处于初级阶段。

※※※  

综合理解和前景展望:考虑到文件中讨论的进展和发现,如单原子合金催化剂[DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]和在沸石中限制亚纳米簇[DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]等新兴策略提供了实现稳定和选择性金属PDH催化剂的新路径。 计算筛选新型金属支撑体组合[DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671]也可能揭示有前景的候选材料。 更广泛地采用原位技术可以提供关于工作催化剂的关键信息以指导优化。 将这些当代方法与几十年来在原型Pt催化剂上积累的基础知识结合起来,可能会解锁所需的稳定性和可负担性,这是PDH的广泛商业化所必需的。
