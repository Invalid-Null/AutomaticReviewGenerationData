 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the file, the article with DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636 employs integrated differential phase contrast STEM (iDPC-STEM) microscopy to directly visualize the location and coordination environment of incorporated cobalt (Co) species within the MFI zeolite framework. The imaging reveals that the isolated Co active sites with a distorted tetrahedral {(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} structure are homogeneously embedded at the T1(7) and T3(9) sites of the MFI framework [Quotes 1].

Additional insights into metal-support interfaces are provided by the article with DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7, which utilizes aberration corrected HAADF-STEM paired with integrated differential phase contrast (iDPC) imaging. This advanced microscopy approach enables precise localization of subnanometric platinum (Pt) clusters confined within the sinusoidal channels of the MFI zeolite structure [Quotes]. The microscopy also shows the Pt clusters are preferentially bonded to germanium-rich regions.

Furthermore, the article with DOI 10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4 employs HR HAADF-STEM and iDPC imaging in combination to identify the precise location of Pt nanoparticles in MFI zeolites. The paired images demonstrate the Pt clusters of 0.4-0.6 nm diameter are preferentially anchored in the sinusoidal channels over the linear channels [Quotes].

Overall, these studies showcase how cutting-edge electron microscopy techniques can offer unprecedented views into metal-support interfacial sites in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. The ability to visualize isolated metal atoms and clusters at exact locations within the microporous zeolite structures provides valuable insights to guide the design of selective and stable catalysts.

※※※

With the remarkable progress in advanced in-situ and operando microscopy, future investigations might capture real-time changes at metal-support interfaces during propane dehydrogenation reactions. By tracking dynamic transformations in metal coordination, dispersion, and mobility within zeolitic confines under working conditions, new structure-activity descriptors may emerge to optimize these multifunctional materials. 

Machine learning empowered image analysis could also quantify correlations between interfacial motifs and corresponding catalytic performance from the wealth of microscopy data. These structure-property relationships may uncover design principles to boost propane conversions and propylene selectivity while minimizing deleterious coking and sintering.

Atomic-scale observations might be paired with multi-scale simulations to achieve complete pictures of reaction mechanisms. The interplay between experimental and computational studies can validate existing theories and unlock novel pathways for rational catalyst improvement. Advancing along these directions could accelerate the deployment of robust and selective propane dehydrogenation processes.

根据从文件中提取的详细信息,DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636的文章采用集成差分相比STEM(iDPC-STEM)显微技术直接可视化了MFI分子筛框架中掺入的钴(Co)物种的位置和配位环境。 该成像技术显示,与扭曲的四面体{(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} 结构的隔离Co活性位点均匀地嵌入在MFI分子筛的T1(7)和T3(9)位点[引文1]。


DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7的文章提供了有关金属支撑体界面的额外见解,它采用了球差校正HAADF-STEM与集成差分相差成像技术。这种先进的显微技术可以精确定位嵌入在MFI分子筛的正弦通道中的亚纳米级铂簇[引文]。显微镜还显示,铂簇优先与锗富区域相连。

此外,DOI 10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4的文章采用HR HAADF-STEM和iDPC成像技术的组合来确定MFI分子筛中铂纳米颗粒的精确位置。这些成像技术显示,0.4-0.6纳米的Pt簇优先固定在正弦通道而不是直线通道中[引文]。

总体而言,这些研究展示了尖端电子显微技术如何提供前所未有的丙烷脱氢催化剂金属支撑体界面的视角。能够在微孔沸石结构的精确位置可视化隔离的金属原子和簇,为引导选择性和稳定的催化剂的设计提供了有价值的启示。

 ※※※

随着先进的原位和工作条件下显微技术的惊人进步,未来的研究可能会捕捉到丙烷脱氢反应过程中金属支撑体界面的实时变化。通过跟踪金属配位、分散和在分子筛环境下的运动性的动态转化,可能会出现新的结构-性能描述符来优化这些多功能材料。


机器学习支持的图像分析还可以从海量的显微数据中定量相关界面基元与相应的催化性能。这些结构-属性关系可能会揭示设计原理以提高丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性,同时最大限度地减少有害的结焦和烧结。


原子尺度的观察可能会与多尺度模拟相结合,以实现对反应机理的完整理解。实验与计算研究的相互作用可以验证现有的理论并开辟理性催化剂改进的新途径。沿着这些方向推进可以加速稳定和选择性丙烷脱氢工艺的部署。
