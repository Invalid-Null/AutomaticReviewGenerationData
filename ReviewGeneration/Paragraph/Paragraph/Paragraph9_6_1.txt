Based on the details extracted from the references, there is evidence showing that tuning oxidation states, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties of metal oxides can significantly impact propane dehydrogenation performance. 

Specifically, creating oxygen vacancies and generating coordinatively unsaturated metal sites via reduction have been shown to enhance activity for propane activation and dehydrogenation over several metal oxide catalysts. For example, the literature shows that for VOx-based catalysts, partly reduced V3+/V4+ species act as active sites under propane dehydrogenation conditions [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]. Reducing the electron density of bridge oxygen species in the VO bonds through weaker VOx-support interactions increases their capability for CH bond rupture [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]. For chromium oxide catalysts, the generation of Cr3+ species via reduction and maintaining their dispersion on supports is critical for achieving high propylene yield [10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014, 10.1006/jcat.1994.1183]. TiOx containing abundant surface oxygen vacancies and tetrahedral Ti4+ sites also shows significantly enhanced catalytic activity after H2 reduction treatment [10.1021/acscatal.3c00818].

In terms of acid-base properties, multiple studies have correlated the density and strength of surface acid sites on metal oxides with propane dehydrogenation activity and propylene selectivity [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454, 10.1016/j.jcat.2006.02.017]. For example, β-Ga2O3 with the highest acid site density demonstrated the best catalytic performance among gallium oxide polymorphs [10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]. DFT calculations have also revealed that the presence of Lewis acid-base interactions on Ir-doped Ga2O3 leads to superior activity compared to Pt-doped Ga2O3 by facilitating propane activation [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454].

Oxygen mobility has also been identified as an important descriptor influencing activity. Supporting metal oxides with weaker metal-oxygen bonds like LaZrOx intrinsically enhances oxygen mobility, creating coordinatively unsaturated sites even without reduction treatment [10.1002/anie.201508731]. For VOx-based catalysts, oxygen mobility plays a critical role, where lattice oxygen directly participates in propane activation but also leads to undesired overoxidation. Controlling VO mobility by interactions with the support or doping is important for optimizing selectivity [10.1021/jacs.9b09235].

Overall, through controlled manipulation of oxidation states, oxygen mobility, and acid-base properties of metal oxides, significant improvements in catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation can be achieved. Future research should focus on developing novel synthesis strategies to precisely tailor these descriptors. Operando spectroscopy techniques combined with kinetic and DFT studies would also help provide atomic-level insights to further advance this field.

根据参考文献中提取的详细信息,有证据表明调节金属氧化物的氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性会显著影响丙烷脱氢性能。

具体来说,通过还原产生氧空位和产生配位不饱和的金属活性位点已被证明可以增强几种金属氧化物催化剂对丙烷的活化和脱氢活性。例如,文献表明,对于基于VOx的催化剂,部分还原的V3+/V4+物种作为丙烷脱氢条件下的活性位点[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。 通过更弱的VOx与支撑体的相互作用降低桥氧物种中VO键的电子密度,从而提高其断裂CH键的能力[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。 对于铬氧化物催化剂,通过还原产生Cr3+物种并保持它们在支撑体上的分散度对于获得高丙烯收率至关重要[10.1016/j.jcat.2008.11.014,10.1006/jcat.1994.1183]。 经过H2还原处理后的TiOx表面存在大量的氧空位和四面体Ti4+活性位点也表现出了明显增强的催化活性 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00818]。

在酸碱性方面,多项研究已将金属氧化物表面的酸性位点的密度和强度与丙烷脱氢活性和丙烯选择性相关联[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001,10.1021/acscatal.0c05454,10.1016/j.jcat.2006.02.017]。 例如,具有最高酸性位点密度的β-Ga2O3在低价钙氧化物多形体中表现出了最好的催化性能[10.1016/j.jcat.2005.03.001]。 DFT计算也揭示了相比Pt掺杂的Ga2O3,Ir掺杂的Ga2O3上的路易斯酸碱作用的存在通过促进丙烷的活化而导致了更高的活性[10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]。

氧迁移能力也被确定为影响活性的一个重要描述符。 支持像LaZrOx这样金属氧键较弱的金属氧化物本质上增强了氧的迁移能力,即使在没有还原处理的条件下也可以产生配位不饱和的位点[10.1002/anie.201508731]。 对于VOx基催化剂,氧迁移能力发挥着关键作用,其中晶格氧直接参与丙烷的活化,但也会导致不需要的过氧化。通过与载体的相互作用或掺杂来控制VO的迁移能力对于优化选择性非常重要[10.1021/jacs.9b09235]。

总的来说,通过控制金属氧化物的氧化态、氧迁移能力和酸碱性,可以实现丙烷脱氢催化性能的显着提高。未来的研究应该关注开发新的合成策略来精确调节这些描述子。 同位素示踪技术结合动力学和DFT研究也将有助于提供原子水平的洞察力,以进一步推动这一领域的发展。
