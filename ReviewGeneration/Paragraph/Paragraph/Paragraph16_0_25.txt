Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Among the various strategies to enhance the performance of Pt-based catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH), alloying Pt with secondary metals has emerged as an effective approach. Several studies have evaluated Pt alloy catalysts with metals like Sn, Ga, and Zn for modulating the catalytic behavior in PDH [Placeholder_Of_DOI1, Placeholder_Of_DOI2].

Pt-Sn alloy catalysts are the most widely studied system for PDH. Sn addition helps increase propylene selectivity and stability while decreasing catalyst deactivation from coke deposition. For PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts, an optimal Sn/Pt ratio around 1.5-2 gives maximum propane conversion, beyond which excess Sn suppresses activity [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. The high activity of PtSn alloys is attributed to the formation of specific structures like Pt3Sn1 and Pt2Sn1 under PDH conditions [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. The Sn modifies the electronic properties of Pt, promoting selective C-H bond activation over C-C bond scission. It also causes a “drain-off” effect where Sn helps remove coke precursors from Pt sites [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. Besides γ-Al2O3, various supports like silica, zeolites, and zirconia have been evaluated for stabilizing highly dispersed PtSn alloy nanoparticles. Recently, strategies like confining PtSn clusters in zeolite pores or grafting isolated Sn sites in the support to anchor Pt have shown promise [Placeholder_Of_DOI6].   

Investigations on Pt-Ga alloys reveal similar activity and coke resistance benefits as PtSn catalysts. The geometric and electronic effect of Ga neighbors suppresses hydrogenolysis, isomerization and other side reactions over Pt-Ga catalysts. Pt-Ga intermetallic structures supported on zeolites have achieved ~40% propane conversion with >99% propylene selectivity [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. The thermal stability of such single-site Pt-Ga catalysts over prolong periods indicates promise as an alternate to PtSn alloys.

Although less studied than Sn and Ga promoted systems, Pt-Zn catalysts have also exhibited improved performance for PDH. Isolated Pt stabilized within Zn-modified zeolite frameworks display high conversion, selectivity and notable stability over 60 h time-on-stream. This points to tuning the Pt ensemble size andgeometric isolation of sites as key factors [Placeholder_Of_DOI8]. However, the intrinsic activity of Pt-Zn is lower than PtSn alloys. An optimal balance needs to be attained between maximizing stability from isolation and retaining Pt ensemble activity.

In summary, alloying Pt with secondary metals like Sn, Ga and Zn allows the modulation of ensemble, ligand and geometric effects. This helps enhance selectivity, stability and lifetime over Pt catalysts for propane dehydrogenation through electronic and structural alterations of the active sites. Each Pt alloy system has its niche – PtSn provides very high intrinsic activity, PtGa imparts resilience against deactivation, while PtZn grants site isolation and geometric stability benefits. Further research can focus on harnessing synergistic combinations integrated in a single catalyst system.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The insights gained from investigating Pt alloy catalysts open up broader horizons for innovation in PDH systems. With growing interest in bio-based propane feedstocks, developing catalysts resilient to impurities is vital. Approaches like selective poisoning of non-Pt sites could impart tolerance to sulfur compounds. Stabilization techniques can also enable low temperature operation with reduced energy loads. Low temperature PDH using Ga and In stabilized Pt nanoclusters may be worthwhile to explore from industrial viability and economic perspectives. Beyond conventional supports, integration with photocatalysts responsive to visible light is an emerging research direction. Exploring uncharted bimetallic combinations using high throughput computations and leveraging principles of single-atom catalysis could unravel new perspectives on tuning selectivity and stability. Integrating such innovations with engineering solutions like structured reactors can potentially push the boundaries achievable in PDH catalysis.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

在各种改善Pt基催化剂丙烷脱氢(PDH)性能的策略中,与第二金属合金化Pt已成为一种有效方法。几项研究评估了氧化锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn)等金属与Pt形成合金催化剂,以调控PDH中的催化行为[Placeholder_Of_DOI1,Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。

Pt-Sn合金催化剂是PDH中研究最广泛的体系。添加Sn有助于提高丙烯选择性和稳定性,同时减少来自焦炭沉积的催化剂失活。对于PtSn/Al2O3催化剂,Sn/Pt比率约为1.5-2时达到最大丙烷转化率,超过此比率,过量的Sn会抑制活性[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。 PtSn合金的高活性被归因于在PDH条件下形成Pt3Sn1和Pt2Sn1等特定结构[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。 Sn改变了Pt的电子性质,促进选择性C-H键活化而不是C-C键断裂。它还会引起“排放”效应,Sn有助于从Pt活性位点除去焦炭前体[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。 除了γ-Al2O3,也评价了二氧化硅、分子筛和二氧化锆等多种载体,以稳定高度分散的PtSn合金纳米粒子。 最近,将PtSn簇合金限制在分子筛孔内或将隔离的Sn位移植到载体上以锚定Pt等策略显示了前景[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。  

对Pt-Ga合金的研究表明,与PtSn催化剂类似的活性和抗焦炭沉积性。 Ga的几何和电子效应抑制Pt-Ga催化剂上的加氢裂解、异构化等副反应。 支持在分子筛上的Pt-Ga金属间化合物达到了约40%的丙烷转化率,丙烯选择性>99%[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。 这种单位点Pt-Ga催化剂的热稳定性在长时间运行中表明它是PtSn合金的替代选择。

尽管没有Sn和Ga促进体系研究得那么深入,但Pt-Zn催化剂在PDH中也表现出了性能的改善。 稳定在经Zn修饰的分子筛框架中的隔离Pt显示了高转换率、选择性和60小时异常稳定性。 这表明调节Pt集聚体的大小和位点的几何隔离是关键因素[Placeholder_Of_DOI8]。 然而,Pt-Zn的固有活性低于PtSn合金。必须在最大化稳定性和保留Pt配合体活性之间取得最佳平衡。

总之,与Sn、Ga和Zn等第二金属合金化允许调控配体、配体和几何效应。 这有助于通过电子和结构改变活性位点来增强Pt催化剂在丙烷脱氢反应中的选择性、稳定性和使用寿命。 每个Pt合金体系都有其利基——PtSn提供了非常高的固有活性,PtGa提供了抗失活的弹性,而PtZn提供了位点隔离和几何稳定性的好处。 进一步的研究可以关注利用协同组合集成在单一催化剂体系中。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 从研究Pt合金催化剂中获得的见解为PDH系统的创新打开了更广阔的视野。 随着对生物基丙烷原料的兴趣日益增长,开发抗杂质的催化剂至关重要。 选择性中和非Pt位点的方法可以提供耐硫化合物的容忍度。 稳定技术还可以实现低温操作,减少能量负荷。 使用Ga和In稳定的低温PDH Pt纳米簇可能值得从工业可行性和经济角度进行探索。 除了常规载体之外,与可见光响应的光催化剂的集成是一个新兴的研究方向。 利用高通量计算探索未开发的双金属组合并利用单原子催化原理可以揭示调谐选择性和稳定性的新视角。 将此类创新与工程解决方案(如固定反应器)相结合,可以潜在推进PDH催化的可达成界限。
