 <English>

Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) is an endothermic catalytic process that produces propylene, an important feedstock for the production of polypropylene plastics. Zeolites and mesoporous materials have emerged as promising supports to encapsulate and stabilize metal-based PDH catalysts, addressing key challenges like sintering and coking that lead to deactivation. This review analyzes recent advances in metal encapsulation strategies using zeolites and related porous structures to enhance PDH catalyst stability.

**Zeolites and Mesoporous Supports - How does the encapsulation of metals enhance stability?**

Metals and metal clusters are the active sites for catalyzing dehydrogenation reactions in PDH. However, unsupported metals are prone to agglomeration and growth into larger, less active nanoparticles during high temperature PDH conditions [\[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019\]](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019). Encapsulating these metal species inside zeolites and mesoporous hosts stabilizes the active sites. The microporous crystalline framework acts as a physical barrier that prevents diffusion and coalescence ([\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103\]](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.3c00103)). This confinement effect maintains high dispersion of sub-nanometric clusters and even isolated atoms. 

Various zeolite topologies have been utilized to encapsulate common PDH metals like Pt, Zn, and Sn. Strategies include hydrothermal synthesis with metal precursors to grow zeolites around templated metal sites ([\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\]](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.3c00816)), and post-synthetic techniques like solid-state ion exchange and impregnation for existing zeolites. PtSn nanoclusters confined in MFI ([\[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7\]](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7)), PtZn in S-1 ([\[10.1021/acscatal.1c01808\]](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.1c01808)), and Pt in UTL ([\[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7\]](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7)) exhibited high long-term stability under industrially relevant PDH conditions. No obvious deactivation was observed even after thousands of hours, unlike conventional supported catalysts. 

The zeolite framework additionally provides Brønsted acid sites that anchor partially reduced metal species during cycling, facilitating re-oxidation back to active cations ([\[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034\]](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034)). This regeneration capability maintains performance over repeated reaction-regeneration sequences. Stable metal-zeolite interaction is integral, with germanium playing an emerging role for strong metal anchoring ([\[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7\]](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7)). Overall, zeolite encapsulation addresses key stability issues in PDH catalyst design.

※※※

Metal encapsulation strategies in zeolites and related microporous hosts have shown tremendous promise for enhancing PDH catalyst stability against sintering, coking, and volatilization. However, aspects like scalable synthesis and diffusion limitations in bulk zeolites present challenges for commercial adoption. 

An integrative approach blending experimental techniques like high-resolution electron microscopy with computational modeling could offer insights into optimal active site locations balancing constraints like accessibility and confinement. There also lies untapped potential in emerging supports like hierarchical zeolites and mixed micropore-mesopore networks to address diffusion limitations. Exploring new zeolite compositions and multidimensional pore architectures that balance diffusion and encapsulation could uncover enhanced PDH catalyst designs.

<Chinese>

丙烷脱氢(PDH)是一种重要的催化端othermic过程,可产生丙烯,丙烯是生产聚丙烯塑料的重要原料。分子筛和介孔材料已成为承载和稳定PDH基于金属的催化剂的有前景的载体,解决诸如烧结和结焦导致的失活等关键挑战。本文分析了最近采用分子筛和相关多孔结构封装金属以增强PDH催化剂稳定性的策略的进展。 

**分子筛和介孔载体--封装金属如何增强稳定性?**

金属和金属团簇是催化脱氢反应在PDH中的活性位点。然而,未载金属易于聚集生长成为较大和较少活性的纳米粒子 在高温PDH条件下 [[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019]](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019)。将这些金属物种封装在分子筛和介孔载体中可以稳定活性位点。 微孔晶体框架起到物理屏障的作用,阻止扩散和结合 [[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.3c00103)。这种限域效应可以维持亚纳米团簇甚至单个原子的高分散度。

各种分子筛拓扑已被用于封装常见的PDH金属如Pt、Zn和Sn。 封装策略包括与金属前体一起的水热合成生长分子筛 [[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.3c00816))和后合成技术,如固态离子交换和浸渍用于现有分子筛。MFI [[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7)) 中限域的PtSn纳米团簇、S-1 [[10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.1c01808))中的PtZn以及UTL [[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7)) 中的Pt在工业相关的PDH条件下表现出高稳定性。即使在成千上万小时后,也没有观察到明显的失活,而传统的载体催化剂则不然。

 分子筛框架还提供Brønsted酸位点,可以锚定在循环期间部分还原的金属物种,促进氧化回活性阳离子[[10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034]](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jechem.2021.04.034))。这种再生能力可以在重复的反应-再生序列中保持性能。 金属与分子筛的稳定相互作用至关重要,其中锗的作用日益凸显,可实现强大的金属锚定作用([[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7))。总的来说,分子筛的封装解决了PDH催化剂设计中的关键稳定性问题。

 ※※※  

在分子筛和相关的微孔载体中封装金属的策略在增强PDH催化剂抵抗烧结、结焦和挥发方面具有巨大的前景。 然而,可扩展合成和体相分子筛中的扩散限制等方面仍存在挑战,有待商业化采用。 

 综合实验技术(如高分辨电镜)和计算建模的方法可以提供优化活性位点定位的见解,平衡易接近性和局限性的约束。 分层分子筛和微孔-中孔混合网络等新兴载体中也蕴含未被开发的潜力,可解决扩散限制。 探索新的分子筛成分和多维孔道结构以平衡扩散和封装可能会发现增强的PDH催化剂设计。
