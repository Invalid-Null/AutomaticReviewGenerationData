 <English>

Several studies have utilized density functional theory (DFT) calculations to elucidate propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and pathways over various catalysts. Gao et al. [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] demonstrated how DFT revealed that Zn-O-Zn sites in zinc-containing catalysts possess lower apparent activation barriers (1.81 eV) compared to single Zn2+ sites (2.14 eV), correlating with experimental observations. Additionally, their calculations showed the d-band center of electron-enriched Pt negatively correlates with propylene selectivity.  

DFT calculations have also elucidated fundamental scaling relationships guiding propane dehydrogenation on transition metal catalysts [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]. Microkinetic modeling based on the DFT results provided structure-activity-selectivity relationships. Moreover, Xu et al. [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889] applied spin-polarized DFT calculations in VASP to study propane dehydrogenation on 3N-coordinated single-atom catalysts, determining reaction energies and barriers. This offered insights into the dehydrogenation mechanisms and catalyst stability.

Comprehensive DFT studies by Liu et al. [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.018] probed the reaction pathway for propane dehydrogenation over Zn-exchanged mordenite. Various model chemistries evaluated how energies, entropy, and structures evolved along the coordinate, accentuating the role of dispersion interactions. Recent works have also analyzed trends in selectivity across metals using descriptors from scaling relations or microkinetic models [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916].

※※※

Considerable advances have been attained by applying DFT calculations toward elucidating propane dehydrogenation mechanisms and descriptors guiding catalyst optimization. However, most studies employ simplified models, and bridging model findings to real catalysts remains challenging. Emerging methodologies incorporating machine learning interatomic potentials with DFT data may construct accurate multiscale models. Computationally screening structures informed by these models could discover novel selective sites. Integrating such approaches with high-throughput DFT screening of realistic catalyst models offers pathways to accelerate development of enhanced propane dehydrogenation catalysts.

<Chinese>

几项研究运用了密度泛函理论(DFT)计算来阐明各种催化剂上的丙烷脱氢机理和路径。高等人[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]展示了DFT如何揭示了锌含量催化剂中的Zn-O-Zn位点相比单Zn2+位点(2.14 eV)具有更低的表观激活屏障(1.81 eV),与实验观察相关。此外,他们的计算表明,电子富集的Pt的d带中心与丙烯选择性负相关。

DFT计算也阐明了基本的缩放关系,指导过渡金属催化剂上的丙烷脱氢[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]。基于DFT结果的微观动力学建模提供了结构-活性-选择性关系。此外,徐等人[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]在VASP中应用自旋极化DFT计算来研究3N配位的单原子催化剂上的丙烷脱氢,确定反应能和屏障。这为脱氢机理和催化剂稳定性提供了见解。


刘等人[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.10.018]的全面DFT研究探讨了锌交换沸石上丙烷脱氢的反应途径。各种模型化学评估了坐标沿着坐标的能量、熵和结构的演变,突出了色散作用的作用。最近的工作还使用来自缩放关系或微观动力学模型的描述符分析了金属间的选择性趋势[10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]。


※※※

通过应用DFT计算来阐明丙烷脱氢机理和指导催化剂优化的描述符,已经取得了可观的进步。然而,大多数研究采用简化模型,将模型结果与实际催化剂桥接仍具有挑战性。整合机器学习原子间势与DFT数据的新兴方法论可以构建准确的多尺度模型。由这些模型启发的结构计算筛选可以发现新颖的选择性位点。将这种方法与DFT实时计算相结合,为加速开发增强丙烷脱氢催化剂提供了途径。
