 <English>
Based on the details extracted from the references, several studies have evaluated supported mono-metallic platinum (Pt), palladium (Pd), and iridium (Ir) catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most successful and widely used commercially for PDH, employed in the Oleflex process by UOP [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]. Studies have explored Pt supported on various metal oxides like alumina and silica, as well as zeolites. Key findings on supported Pt catalysts include:

- Pt/alumina catalysts modified with H2S or atomic layer deposited (ALD) Al2O3/TiO2 overcoats show improved yield and selectivity [DOIs: 10.1006/jcat.1994.1333, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]
- Sub-nanometric Pt clusters confined in Ge-UTL zeolite channels via Pt-O-Ge bonds give an ultrastable and selective catalyst [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]  
- Small Pt nanoparticles on boron-promoted alumina demonstrate over 98% propylene selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]
- Isolated Pt atoms and potentially small clusters on Zn-modified ZSM-5 are highly active [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]

Besides Pt, a few studies have explored Pd- and Ir-based catalysts. Supported RhCu single atom alloy sites show markedly higher activity and 100% propene selectivity versus 80% for Pt/Al2O3 [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]. However, many studies note Pd, Rh, and Ir catalysts remain expensive compared to Pt [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002].

In summary, Pt-based catalysts are the predominant supported metals studied for commercial PDH processes, with some emerging studies on other precious metals. Key descriptors of performance include yield, propylene selectivity, and stability.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and findings discussed in the file, further research into stabilizing precious metal sites and optimizing metal-support interactions appear promising to enhance catalytic performance. For instance, recent studies have shown promise in single atom alloy catalysts [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389], as well as zeolite confined metal clusters [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] to boost stability. Exploring lower cost metals that can match precious metal performance could also be impactful. Broader horizons may involve multifunctional catalyst-adsorbent systems that can limit coking, as well as integrated reactor-separator designs to enhance selectivity.

<Chinese>
根据参考文献中的详细信息,已经评价了几项研究支持的单金属铂(Pt)、钯(Pd)和铱(Ir)催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。

Pt基催化剂据报道是最成功和最广泛使用的商业PDH催化剂,在UOP的Oleflex工艺中使用[DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]。已经探索了在各种金属氧化物(如氧化铝和二氧化硅)以及沸石上支持的Pt。 支持Pt催化剂的关键发现包括:

- 用H2S或原子层沉积(ALD) Al2O3/TiO2涂层修饰的Pt/氧化铝催化剂显示出提高的产量和选择性[DOI:10.1006/jcat.1994.1333,10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]

- 通过Pt-O-Ge键限制在Ge-UTL沸石通道中的亚纳米Pt簇给出了超稳定和选择性催化剂 [DOI:10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]  

- 小Pt纳米粒子在硼掺杂的氧化铝上示出98%以上的丙烯选择性[DOI:10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]  

- Zn改性ZSM-5上的隔离Pt原子和潜在的小簇高活性[DOI:10.1021/acscatal.2c01631]

除Pt外,还探索了几种基于Pd和Ir的催化剂。 支持的RhCu单原子合金位点比Pt/Al2O3表现出明显更高的活性和100%丙烯选择性,而Pt/Al2O3为80% [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]。 然而,许多研究注意到,与Pt相比,Pd、Rh和Ir催化剂仍然昂贵[DOI:10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]。

总之,Pt基催化剂是研究商业PDH工艺的主要支持金属,并且出现了对其他贵金属的一些新兴研究。 催化剂性能的关键描述词包括产量、丙烯选择性和稳定性。

※※※  
综合理解与前景展望: 考虑到文件中讨论的进步和发现,进一步研究稳定贵金属位点和优化金属支持体相互作用似乎有望提高催化性能。 例如,最近的研究显示,单原子合金催化剂 [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]以及分子筛限制的金属簇 [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] 有望提高稳定性。 探索性能可与贵金属催化剂相媲美的低成本金属也可能产生重大影响。 更广阔的领域可能涉及具有抑制焦炭功能的多功能催化剂-吸附剂体系,以及集成反应器-分离器设计以提高选择性。
