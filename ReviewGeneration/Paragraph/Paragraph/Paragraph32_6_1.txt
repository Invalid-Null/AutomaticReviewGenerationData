Exploring alternative catalysts to replace expensive platinum-based systems is an ongoing pursuit [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-022-00885-1]. Definitive identification of the molecular structure and composition of active sites in promising catalysts like gallium-exchanged zeolites remains controversial and requires further study [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]. Fundamental understanding of how factors like oxygen vacancies influence catalytic performance needs to be improved [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c02621]. More work is required in tuning gold-based catalysts for selective propane dehydrogenation instead of combustion [DOI: 10.1016/j.jcat.2008.01.030]. Coke formation, deactivation mechanisms, and side reaction control need to be addressed [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.1c00331, DOI: 10.1021/cr5002436].
