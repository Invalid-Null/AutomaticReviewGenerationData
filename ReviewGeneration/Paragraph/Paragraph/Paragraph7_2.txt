Based on the analysis of the files provided, a few studies have utilized computational methods to reveal structure-activity relationships and identify descriptors correlating catalyst properties to propane dehydrogenation (PDH) performance [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]. 

One study established scaling relations between the formation energies of PDH intermediates on transition metal surfaces to recognize the ethyl and methylidyne adsorption energies as optimal reactivity descriptors [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]. Another work identified a volcano-type activity trend with respect to the propylene adsorption energy through microkinetic modeling of single-atom catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. Additionally, degree of rate control analysis on DFT-calculated transition states was leveraged to identify the rate-limiting PDH steps and correspondingly establish descriptors based on the energies of these steps [10.1021/acscatal.0c05454].

Beyond computational explorations, a few experimental studies have also delineated structure-activity correlations. The dispersion of active species, metal loading, and support nature were associated with catalytic performance [10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. Ensembles of Pt atoms were revealed to drive coke formation, while separated Pt single atoms led to enhanced stability [10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Taking into consideration the advancements and revelations discussed in the files, promising pathways can be charted for future investigations through synergistic experiment-computation methodologies. While linear scaling relations have identified simple descriptors thus far, exploring more complex and unconventional descriptors through high-throughput DFT screening and machine learning holds potential. Extracting such descriptors can further assist in rational catalyst design. On the experimental front, operando spectroscopic techniques can offer pivotal atomic-level insights into working catalysts. Overall, cross-pollination of computational modeling and spectroscopic characterization techniques promises to advance the identification of robust structure-activity relationships and design superior PDH catalysts.

根据所提供文件分析,一些研究已经利用了计算方法来揭示结构-活性关系,并确定与丙烷脱氢(PDH)性能相关的描述符 [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889, 10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]。

一项研究建立了过渡金属表面上的PDH中间体形成能量之间的规模关系,以识别乙基和甲基炔基吸附能量为最佳反应性描述符[10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]。另一项工作通过对单原子催化剂的微动力学建模,确定了丙烯吸附能量火山型活性趋势[10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。 此外,对DFT计算过渡态的速率控制分析被利用来识别限速PDH步骤,并相应地基于这些步骤的能量建立描述符[10.1021/acscatal.0c05454]。


除了计算探索之外,一些实验研究也已经描绘了结构-活性相关性。活性物种的分散、金属负载量和载体性质都与催化性能有关[10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]。 已经发现Pt原子集团促进焦炭形成,而分离的Pt单原子则导致稳定性增强[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:考虑到文件中讨论的先进成果和发现,通过实验-计算协同方法可以为未来调查勾画出有前途的途径。 尽管线性缩放关系至今仅确定了简单的描述符,但通过高通量DFT筛选和机器学习探索更复杂和非传统的描述符具有潜力。 提取这些描述符还可以进一步帮助合理的催化剂设计。 在实验方面,操作体光谱技术可以提供对工作催化剂的关键原子层次的见解。 总的来说,计算建模和光谱表征技术的交叉授粉有望推进稳健的结构-活性关系的鉴定和设计优良PDH催化剂。
