		Based on the detailed analysis from the references, bimetallic and alloy Pt catalysts with secondary metals like Sn, Ga, Zn show improved performance for propane dehydrogenation compared to monometallic Pt. Adding Sn as a promoter is found to increase propylene selectivity and stability by modifying the electronic properties of Pt, while slightly decreasing activity [References 1, 2, 3, 4]. The Pt:Sn ratio is an important factor, with a ratio around 2:1 to 3:1 considered optimal in multiple studies [References 5, 6].  

         Ga and Zn promoters also help disperse Pt sites and improve selectivity [References 7, 8]. Strong metal-support interactions are identified in several works to be important for stabilizing the alloy nanoparticles [References 9, 10]. Subnanometric PtM alloy clusters (M = Sn, Zn, etc.) confined within zeolites demonstrate exceptional productivity and stability owing to strict control over the active site [References 11, 12].

         ※※※

          With the remarkable progress elucidated in modulation of Pt-based catalysts by strategic incorporation of secondary metals, future horizons may encompass extending this approach using other promising host metals and zeolitic architectures. Advanced in-situ characterization techniques can offer deeper insights into the dynamic structural transformations. Machine learning methods could accelerate screening of ternary alloy compositions. The concepts developed around propane dehydrogenation may be transferable for upgrading other lower alkanes. Computational studies in synergy with experiments can unravel detailed reaction mechanisms and descriptor-activity relationships to realize truly customized catalysts.
