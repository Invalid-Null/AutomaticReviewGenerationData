Based on the details provided in the file, several studies have explored supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts appear to be the most widely studied and utilized. Multiple articles evaluate alumina-supported Pt catalysts, showing they can achieve relatively high propane conversion and propylene selectivity [10.1006/jcat.1994.1333, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. Adding promoters like boron [10.1021/acscatal.9b05548] or metal oxide overcoats [10.1021/acscatal.0c03391] can further enhance stability and selectivity. Other supports like boron nitride [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008] and germanosilicate zeolites [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7] have also been explored to optimize Pt dispersion and metal-support interactions. Pt-based catalysts are noted to have commercial relevance and wide use industrially [10.1016/j.apcatb.2012.11.005, 10.1002/anie.201507119, 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7].

Beyond Pt, there is some limited data on other Group VIII metals. Pd, Rh, and Ir are noted to have potential intrinsic activity, but high cost has hindered their adoption [10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]. Supported Ru nanoparticles were found to have very low 4.5% propylene selectivity, far below Pt and optimized Ru phosphides [10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]. An isolated study on RhCu single atoms supported on silica found superior performance to a Pt/Al2O3 benchmark, showing opportunities for advanced catalyst design [10.1126/science.abg8389].

Overall, Pt-based catalysts are the predominant choice for commercial propane dehydrogenation processes, with decades of research optimizing supports, promoters, and synthesis methods. Continued development of advanced materials like zeolites and single atom alloys may provide opportunities to further enhance catalytic performance.

※※※ 

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The collected insights spotlight Pt as the foremost viable mono-metallic dehydrogenation catalyst, buoyed by exhaustive optimization over extended timescales. However, singular evaluations of unconventional compositions like RhCu unveil remarkable prospective pathways to transcend inherent limitations of conventional catalysts. Delving into uncharted compositions guided by emerging principles of single atom alloy configurations could unravel breakthrough chemical efficacies. Analogously, interweaving zeolitic micropores with atomically dispersed motifs linked via metal-support conjugations engenders site-isolation-derived selectivities. Exploring frontiers of catalyst conceptualization guided by electronic and geometric orchestration of site-specific reactivities remains indispensable to providing next-generation dehydrogenation solutions tailored for propane feedstocks.

根据文件提供的详细信息,已经有几项研究探索了负载单金属Pt、Pd和Ir催化剂用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)。

Pt基催化剂似乎是研究和利用最广泛的。多篇文章评估了氧化铝支持的Pt催化剂,表明它们可以实现相对较高的丙烷转化率和丙烯选择性[10.1006/jcat.1994.1333, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。添加促进剂如硼[10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]或金属氧化物多层涂料[10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]可以进一步增强稳定性和选择性。boron nitride [10.1016/j.jechem.2021.10.008]和germanosilicate 分子筛[10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]也已被探索用于优化Pt 色散和金属支撑物交互作用。Pt基催化剂在商业上具有相关性,并在工业上被广泛使用[10.1016/j.apcatb.2012.11.005, 10.1002/anie.201507119, 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]。

除Pt外,还有一些其他VIII族金属的有限数据。文献指出Pd、Rh和Ir具有潜在的固有活性,但高成本阻碍了它们的采用[10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]。负载Ru纳米粒子的丙烯选择性�常低,仅为4.5%,远低于Pt 和优化的Ru磷化物[10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。 silica支持的 RhCu单原子合金的一项研究发现其性能优于Pt/Al2O3基准,这表明高级催化剂设计的机会[10.1126/science.abg8389]。

总的来说,Pt基催化剂是商业丙烷脱氢工艺的主要选择,几十年的研究优化了载体、促进剂和合成方法。 zeolites 和单原子合金等先进材料的持续开发可能为进一步提高催化性能提供机会。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 收集的见解将Pt视为首要的单金属脱氢催化剂,在长时间尺度上进行了大量优化。 然而,非常规组分(如RhCu)的独特评价揭示了突破传统催化剂固有局限性的卓越前景。遵循新出现的单原子合金配置原则,深入探索未知成分可能会揭示突破性的化学效能。 类似地,通过金属支持体共轭将原子分散的图案与分子筛微孔互相交织,产生源于位点隔离的选择性。 在电子和几何对特定位点反应性的调控指导下,探索催化剂概念化的前沿仍然是为丙烷供料提供下一代脱氢解决方案的必不可少部分。
