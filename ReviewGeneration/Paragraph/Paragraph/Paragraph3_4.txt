Based on the details provided in the file, several major catalyst classes have been explored for propane dehydrogenation, including metal oxides, supported metals, alloys, and zeolites. 

Specifically, metal oxides such as VOx/CrOx supported on MCM-41 and SBA-15 mesoporous silica [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816], Sn-incorporated hexagonal mesoporous silica (HMS) [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816], and Co-incorporated mesoporous silica [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] have been studied. Commercial catalysts utilize Pt- and Cr-based materials, with Pt showing excellent alkane C-H bond activation capability [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]. Intermetallic compounds and alloys containing Pt, Pd, Ni, and promoters like Sn and Zn have also demonstrated promise [10.1021/acscatal.1c01916]. Regarding zeolites, H-Beta [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019] and iron-silicate zeolites [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019] with isolated Fe sites have been explored.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The major catalyst classes studied for propane dehydrogenation encompass metal oxides, supported metals, alloys, and zeolites. As research progresses, there is potential to further tune these existing catalysts via strategies like selective doping and advanced nanoscale engineering, enabling enhanced activity, selectivity, and stability. Experimenting with novel catalyst supports and multifunctional composite systems could also stimulate innovation. Computational screening approaches may help identify promising candidate materials. Ultimately, the goal is to develop readily scalable and sustainable propane dehydrogenation catalyst technologies.
