Based on the details extracted from the references, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) faces considerable thermodynamic constraints due to being a highly endothermic process. Multiple studies note the positive reaction enthalpy of 124.3 kJ/mol, necessitating high reaction temperatures above 500°C to achieve reasonable equilibrium conversion \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01374\]. The endothermic nature limits conversion, with yields improved by decreasing propane partial pressures or increasing temperatures \[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331\]. 

To overcome these limitations, oxidative routes using O2 or CO2 co-feeds can lower the net reaction enthalpy, providing exothermic oxidation reactions to pull the equilibria towards desired products like propylene \[10.1002/advs.202207756\]. However, non-oxidative PDH still holds advantages, with molecular sieve supports helping anchor isolated metal sites and prevent sintering even at the high temperatures required \[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\].

Beyond bulk thermodynamic factors, the articles also note kinetic considerations around selectivity and side reactions. Steps like C-C or C-H bond activation control desired dehydrogenation rates, with apparent activation energies around 180-200 kJ/mol reported \[10.1021/jacs.2c03941\]. However, unavoidable cracking and coking pathways share similar transition states, making their kinetic discrimination difficult \[10.1021/acscatal.5b01423\]. Isolated single sites favor PDH while oligomeric ensembles promote cracking and coke formation \[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019\], posing a selectivity challenge.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements around thermodynamic enhancement via oxidative routes and kinetic stabilization of isolated active sites, future explorations might connect these threads by probing isolated metal sites in redox-active supports. Supports with lattice oxygen could reoxidize surface metal species, providing the exothermic oxidation potential to pull equilibria while still maintaining site isolation to minimize substrate over-activation. Kinetic probes could discern these oxidation events, enriching computational models to elucidate selectivity descriptors. Broader horizons might apply similar concepts towards other alkane dehydrogenations central to chemical production.

根据参考文献提取的细节,丙烷脱氢(PDH)由于是一个高度放热过程,面临相当大的热力学限制。 多个研究指出,反应焓为124.3 kJ/mol,需要高于500°C的反应温度来实现合理的平衡转化\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00103, 10.1021/acscatal.1c01916, 10.1021/acscatal.2c01374\]。内吸性质限制了转化率,通过降低丙烷分压或升高温度可以提高收率\[10.1021/acscatal.1c00331\]。

为了克服这些限制,使用O2或CO2共饲可以降低净反应焓,提供放热氧化反应来拉动期望产物(如丙烯)的平衡\[10.1002/advs.202207756\]。 然而,非氧化PDH仍然具有优势,分子筛支持体有助于锚定隔离的金属活性位,即使在所需的高温下也可以防止结焦\[10.1021/acscatal.3c00816\]。

除了整体热力学因素外,这些文章还指出了选择性和副反应的动力学考虑。诸如C-C或C-H键激活等步骤控制了所需的脱氢速率,报道的表观活化能约为180-200 kJ/mol \[10.1021/jacs.2c03941\]。 然而,不可避免的裂解和焦化途径共享相似的过渡态,使其动力学区分变得困难\[10.1021/acscatal.5b01423\]。 隔离的单一位点有利于PDH,而低聚体集合促进裂解和焦化\[10.1016/j.jechem.2023.04.019\],提出了选择性挑战。

※※※  

综合理解与未来展望:考虑到通过氧化途径实现热力学增强和稳定隔离活性位点的进展,未来的探索可能会通过探究具有氧化-还原活性的载体中的隔离金属位点来连接这些线索。 具有晶格氧的载体可以氧化表面金属种,提供放热氧化潜力来拉动平衡,同时仍能保持位点的隔离以最小化基质过度激活。 动力学探针可以鉴别这些氧化事件,丰富计算模型以阐明选择性描述子。 更广阔的视野可能会应用类似的概念用于化学生产中核心的其他烷烃脱氢。
