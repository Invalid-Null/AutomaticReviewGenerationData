		According to the file, some studies have evaluated zeolites like MFI and BEA as supports for propane dehydrogenation catalysts, showing promise compared to amorphous silica. One study on PtSn catalysts found that using dealuminated Beta zeolite instead of silica effectively decreased the PtSn nanoparticle size from 1.6 nm down to 0.9 nm [1]. Another work showed subnanometer PtSn clusters confined in the micropores of purely siliceous MFI zeolite, with sizes of 0.5-0.6 nm [3]. The rigid framework and pores of zeolites can anchor isolated metal atoms and nanoclusters, providing a confinement effect to stabilize small particle sizes that boost catalytic activity [2].
		
		In another investigation of propane dehydrogenation over Zn-exchanged H-ZSM-5, tuning the Si/Al ratio impacted the types of Zn species formed upon ion exchange [Reference1]. Lower Si/Al ratios favored the creation of more dehydrogenatively active Zn2+ paired with H+ sites. And chabazite zeolites exchanged with Ga were found to be exceptional propane dehydrogenation catalysts, with 96% propylene selectivity attributed to isolated Ga+ sites [Reference2]. So zeolite acidity and the resultant speciation of exchanged metal ions can profoundly influence catalytic performance.
