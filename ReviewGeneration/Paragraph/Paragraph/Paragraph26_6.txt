 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the file, the article with DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636 employs integrated differential phase contrast scanning transmission electron microscopy (iDPC-STEM) to characterize the Co-support interface in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. The imaging reveals that isolated Co atoms with a distorted tetrahedral coordination of {(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} are homogeneously embedded into the T1(7) and T3(9) sites of MFI zeolites [10.1021/jacs.2c02636].

Additional insights into metal-support interfaces are provided by the article with DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7, which utilizes aberration corrected high-angle annular dark field scanning transmission electron microscopy (HAADF-STEM) and integrated differential phase contrast (iDPC) imaging. This advanced microscopy approach enables precise localization of subnanometric Pt and PtSn clusters preferentially within the sinusoidal channels of the MFI zeolite structure [10.1038/s41563-019-0412-6].

Furthermore, the article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639 employs high resolution TEM (HRTEM) and scanning transmission electron microscopy coupled with energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (STEM/EDS) to characterize metal-support interfaces and distribution in Pt-Sn/SBA-15 propane dehydrogenation catalysts. This provides insights into nanoparticle size, composition, and structure [10.1021/acscatal.1c00639].

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The advanced microscopy techniques discussed in the articles have provided valuable insights into metal-support interfacial sites in propane dehydrogenation catalysts. Direct visualization of isolated metal atoms and clusters at specific locations within the support framework aids understanding of the active site requirements. 

However, further work could focus on studying changes at the metal-support interface under operating conditions. In situ microscopy approaches tracking the dynamic evolution of active sites during propane dehydrogenation could reveal additional details on stabilization mechanisms and deactivation pathways. Combined with computational modeling, such operando studies may guide designs of next-generation catalysts with optimized metal-support interactions resistant to sintering.

根据文件提取的详细信息,DOI 10.1021/jacs.2c02636的文章采用了集成差分相比扫描透射电镜(iDPC-STEM)来表征丙烷脱氢催化剂中的Co-支撑体界面。成像结果显示,MFI分子筛的T1(7)和T3(9)位点上均匀嵌入了配位环境为{(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2}的孤立Co原子[10.1021/jacs.2c02636]。  

DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7的文章提供了有关金属支撑体界面的进一步见解,它采用了球差校正的高角环形暗场扫描透射电镜(HAADF-STEM)和集成差分相比成像(iDPC)。这种先进的显微技术可以精确定位MFI分子筛结构正弦状通道中的亚纳米Pt和PtSn簇[10.1038/s41563-019-0412-6]。

此外,DOI 10.1021/acscatal.1c00639的文章采用了高分辨率透射电镜(HRTEM)和扫描透射电镜联合能谱显微分析(STEM/EDS)来表征Pt-Sn/SBA-15丙烷脱氢催化剂中的金属支撑体界面及其分布,这为纳米颗粒的尺寸、组成和结构提供了见解[10.1021/acscatal.1c00639]。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:文中讨论的各种先进显微技术为理解丙烷脱氢催化剂中金属支撑体界面的活性位点提供了有价值的信息。直接观察支撑体框架内特定位置的隔离金属原子和簇有助于理解活性位点的要求。

然而,进一步的工作可聚焦于研究催化剂在工作条件下金属支撑体界面的变化。原位显微技术可以跟踪金属支撑体界面活性位点在丙烷脱氢反应过程中的动态演变,揭示稳定机制和失活途径的更多细节。结合计算模型化,这种操作条件下的研究可指导下一代催化剂设计,获得优化的金属支撑体相互作用,抗烧结。
