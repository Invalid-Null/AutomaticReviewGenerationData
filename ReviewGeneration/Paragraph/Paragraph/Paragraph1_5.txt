	<English>
		Propylene is an essential raw material for producing chemicals like polypropylene, acrylonitrile, acetone, and propylene oxide, as substantiated by references [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816] and [10.1021/acscatal.0c01455]. Traditional production routes via naphtha and heavy oil cracking are unable to meet the growing demand, with projections estimating a supply-demand gap widening to 191 megatons by 2030 according to [10.1002/advs.202207756]. This renders propane dehydrogenation an economically favorable and ecologically benign alternative, evidenced by [10.1021/acscatal.3c00889].
		※※※
		Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the rising demand and process intensification challenges associated with conventional cracking methods, propane dehydrogenation serves as a stepping stone to shore up propylene supply. Further research could focus on enhancing conversion, selectivity and stability of PDH catalysts using novel nanostructures and catalytic promoters, potentially aided by in situ spectroscopy and machine learning. Advanced reactor designs may also boost productivity. Ultimately, a portfolio of technologies balancing economic and environmental factors will likely supply future propylene needs.
