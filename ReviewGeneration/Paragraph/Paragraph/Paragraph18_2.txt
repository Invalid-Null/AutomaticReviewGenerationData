The articles explored provide no insights into the use of subsurface promoters like Re or Mn in bimetallic and alloy catalysts to optimize propane dehydrogenation selectivity [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, 10.1021/acscatal.2c00642, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667, 10.1002/anie.201805150, 10.1021/es301323]. None of the studies delve into subsurface tuning mechanisms using Re, Mn or other promoters; the investigations are limited to bimetallic catalyst compositions without subsurface modification [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.12.019, 10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5, 10.1016/j.jcis.2021.02.129, 10.1021/acssuschemeng.1c03074, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05042].

While most works do not consider subsurface promoters, one study utilizes subsurface Mn in Pt3Mn bimetallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation [10.1021/jacs.8b08162]. The presence of subsurface Mn instead of Pt significantly improves selectivity by reducing hydrocarbon and hydrogen adsorption strengths. Models are developed to elucidate the role of distinct subsurface sites in modifying surface reactivity and selectivity. This underscores the potential of subsurface promoter tuning, via Re, Mn or other transition metals, as an emerging strategy to tailor catalytic performance.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The investigations surveyed showcase a research lacuna surrounding subsurface promoters in alloy dehydrogenation catalysts. Barring isolated efforts, the focus has centered on tuning bimetallic compositions without subsurface modification. Findings from the sole study evidencing enhanced propane dehydrogenation selectivity with subsurface Mn highlight the promise of subsurface tuning. Extending this approach by exploring subsurface Re, Mn and other promoters emerges as an uncharted territory holding immense potential to advance selectivity control and optimization. Research endeavors might productively shift toward developing novel synthetic routes to insert desired subsurface promoters, coupled with kinetic studies and microkinetic models to elicit subsurface tuning mechanisms. Broader horizons might entail expanding this strategy toward other catalytic systems and targeted chemical transformations.

尽管大多数作品不考虑亚表面促进剂,但有一项研究利用了Pt3Mn双金属催化剂中的亚表面Mn进行丙烷脱氢[10.1021/jacs.8b08162]。 相比Pt,亚表面Mn的存在显着提高了选择性,方法是减少了烃和氢的吸附强度。 开发了模型来阐明不同亚表面位点在修改表面反应性和选择性方面的作用。 这凸显了利用Re、Mn或其他过渡金属等亚表面促进剂调节的潜力,作为定制催化性能的新兴策略。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:所调查的研究突显了围绕合金脱氢催化剂中的亚表面促进剂的研究空白。 除了个别努力外,重点一直集中在不调整亚表面改变双金属组合上。 使用亚表面Mn增强丙烷脱氢选择性的唯一一项研究结果凸显了亚表面调谐的前景。 将这种方法扩展到探索亚表面Re、Mn和其他促进剂,作为一项未开发的领域具有巨大的选择性控制和优化潜力。 研究工作可能会对开发用于插入所需亚表面促进剂的新合成路线以及动力学研究和微观动力学模型以阐明亚表面调节机制转向更高产。 更广阔的前景可能涉及将这种策略扩展到其他催化系统和目标化学转化。
