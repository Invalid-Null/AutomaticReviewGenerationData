According to the details provided, several articles utilize in-situ characterization techniques to study working propane dehydrogenation catalysts and gain valuable insights.

One article uses in-situ temperature programmed surface reaction (TPSR) and diffuse reflectance infrared Fourier transform spectroscopy (DRIFTS) to monitor product formation and probe propylene adsorption/desorption during propane dehydrogenation over supported cobalt catalysts [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. Another combines in-situ X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), diffuse reflectance infrared spectroscopy (DRIFTS), and pulse titration experiments to determine the identity and composition of active gallium species in Ga/H-ZSM-5 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]. 

In-situ Raman spectroscopy reveals the in-situ transformation of inactive zinc hydroxide species to active zinc hydride species during the induction period over ZnOx catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.2c00928]. One article uses a combination of in-situ UV-Vis/Raman spectroscopy, XANES, EXAFS, and infrared spectroscopy to study structural changes in Pt/Al2O3 and PtSn/Al2O3 catalysts during repeated propane dehydrogenation and regeneration cycles [10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018].

Synchrotron-based operando XAS and EXAFS are utilized to detect changes in chromium oxidation states and coordination environments in Cr-MCM-41 catalysts during reaction [10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]. In-situ DRIFTS and online GC are combined to correlate catalytic performance with the presence of active sites in Ga/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.8b04578]. 

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The various in-situ techniques provide valuable atomic-level insights into working propane dehydrogenation catalysts. By elucidating structural changes, active site transformations, reaction intermediates and coke formation, they advance mechanistic understanding and guide rational catalyst design. 

However, most studies have examined single catalyst compositions under limited conditions. An opportunity exists to explore a broader set of catalytic materials using operando reactors with simultaneous spectroscopic/microscopic characterization under industrially-relevant conditions. Data-driven analysis of such multidimensional data could uncover completely new structure-activity relationships. Eventually, specialized reactors and automated ML workflows could accelerate catalyst development and optimize commercial dehydrogenation processes.

一篇��章使用了原位程序升温表面反应(TPSR)和漫反射红外傅立叶变换光谱(DRIFTS)来监测产物的形成和探测丙烯在氧化钴催化剂上的吸附/解吸过程[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。另一篇文章结合了原位X射线吸收光谱(XAS)、漫反射红外光谱(DRIFTS)和脉冲滴定实验来确定Ga/H-ZSM-5催化剂中活性镓种的身份和组成[10.1021/acscatal.1c05724]。

原位拉曼光谱揭示了非活性氧化锌种在感应期间原位转化为活性氢化锌种[10.1021/acscatal.2c00928]。一篇文章使用了原位紫外-可见光/拉曼光谱、XANES、EXAFS和红外光谱的组合来研究Pt/Al2O3和PtSn/Al2O3催化剂在反复的丙烷脱氢和再生循环期间的结构变化[10.1016/j.jcat.2010.09.018]。

同步辐射的原位XAS和EXAFS被利用来检测Cr-MCM-41催化剂在反应期间铬的氧化态和配位环境的变化[10.1016/j.jcat.2004.03.014]。原位DRIFTS和在线气相色谱(GC)被组合起来,以相关Ga/Al2O3催化剂的催化性能与活性位点的存在[10.1021/acscatal.8b04578]。

整体理解和前景展望:各种原位技术为工作状态下的丙烷脱氢催化剂提供��有价值的原子层次的信息。通过阐明结构变化、活性位点转变、反应中间体和焦炭形成,它们推进了机制理解,并指导合理的催化剂设计。

然而,大多数研究只在有限条件下检查了单一催化剂成分。 一个机会存在于利用工业相关条件下能同时进行光谱/显微表征的原位反应器来探索更广泛的催化材料。 对这种多维数据的基于数据的分析可以揭示完全新的结构-活性关系。 最终,专用反应器和自动化的机器学习工作流程可以加速催化剂的开发和商业脱氢工艺的优化。
