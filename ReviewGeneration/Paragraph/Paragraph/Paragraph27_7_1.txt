Based on the details extracted from the file, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts have been extensively studied using X-ray based techniques to elucidate oxidation state changes during catalysis. Specifically, X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS), X-ray absorption near edge structure (XANES), and extended X-ray absorption fine structure (EXAFS) spectroscopy have been instrumental in unraveling the intricacies of metal oxidation states and coordination environments in working catalysts. 

For instance, Co@Si-BEA catalysts analyzed by EXAFS showed the catalytic Co2+ sites retained structural stability against PDH with no discernible oxidation [DOI]. Another report harnessed in-situ XANES to confirm reduction of isolated Cr(VI) species in Cr-MCM-41 to less active Cr(III) octahedral sites under DHP conditions [DOI]. XPS examination of PtSn/Al2O3 revealed segregation of oxidized Sn from Pt nanoparticles during regeneration; subsequent H2 treatment restored the alloy structure [DOI]. Quantitative analysis of V 2p core levels by XPS demonstrated tunability of V4+/V5+ ratios in VSiBeta catalysts with varied V loadings [DOI].

Furthermore, tracking absorption edge energies and white-line intensities in XANES spectra has elucidated transitions between metallic, oxidized, and reduced states of active species like Fe, Zn, Ga, and Pt. Combined with Fourier transformed EXAFS data on interatomic distances and coordination numbers, intricate mechanistic comprehension of working catalysts has been accomplished. For example, the approach substantiated formation of metal-oxygen-support linkages responsible for stabilizing isolated Pt species in zeolites [DOI].

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The extensive application of synchrotron X-ray absorption techniques has advanced comprehension of propane dehydrogenation catalysts immensely. Building on these advancements, operando spectroscopy utilizing simultaneous X-ray and IR/Raman methodologies could illuminate oxidation state transformations concurrent to surface phenomena. Additionally, emerging capabilities in scanning transmission X-ray microscopy now permit direct visualization of individual active sites, potentially transforming PDH catalyst structural-functional correlations. Metal-organic framework and single atom catalysts also warrant thorough investigation. Overall, amalgamating cutting-edge in-situ X-ray innovations with computational modeling and kinetic experiments will usher rational design of robust PDH catalysts.

根据从文件中提取的详细信息,丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂已被广泛地研究,使用X射线技术来阐明催化过程中的氧化态变化。 具体来说,X射线光电子能谱(XPS)、X射线吸收近边结构(XANES)和延伸的X射线吸收细结构(EXAFS)光谱技术在揭示金属催化剂的氧化态和配位环境方面发挥了关键作用。  

例如, EXAFS分析表明, Co@Si-BEA催化剂中的催化活性Co2+位点在PDH反应中保持了结构稳定性,没有出现明显的氧化[DOI]。另一篇报道利用原位XANES确认了Cr-MCM-41中的离域Cr(VI)物种在DHP条件下被还原为较少活性的Cr(III)八面体位点[DOI]。 PtSn/Al2O3的XPS分析揭示了Sn从Pt纳米粒子中析出的氧化过程;后续的H2处理使合金结构复原[DOI]。V 2p核心水平的定量XPS分析证明,不同载V量的VSiBeta催化剂中V4+/V5+的比例是可调节的[DOI]。  

此外,跟踪XANES光谱中吸收边能量和白线强度的变化揭示了Fe、Zn、Ga和Pt等活性物种在金属性、氧化态和还原态之间的转换。 结合傅立叶变换EXAFS数据中原子间距离和配位数,已实现了对工作状态下催化剂的复杂机理认识。 例如,这种方法证实了负责稳定分子筛中隔离Pt物种的金属-氧-载体连接的形成[DOI]。

综合理解与未来展望: 同步辐射X射线吸收技术的广泛应用极大地推动了丙烷脱氢催化剂的理解。 在这些进展的基础上,使用同步的X射线与IR/Raman方法进行原位光谱学表征,可以揭示氧化还��状态的转变与表面现象之间的相互关系。 此外,扫描穿透X射线显微技术现在可以直接观察单个活性位点,这可能会改变PDH催化剂的结构-功能相关性。 金属有机框架和单原子催化剂也值得深入研究。 总的来说,将前沿的原位X射线创新与计算建模和动力学实验结合起来,将推动耐用PDH催化剂的合理设计。
