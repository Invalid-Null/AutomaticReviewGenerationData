The articles analyzed do not compare the selectivity profiles of metallic versus oxidized supported metal catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). Most studies focus solely on evaluating various metallic or bimetallic catalysts, without investigating differences in selectivity between metallic and oxidized surfaces [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548, 10.1016/j.cej.2014.02.084, 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671, 10.1021/acscatal.2c00642, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]. 

For example, one study synthesized PtZn alloy nanoparticles for PDH via co-impregnation of precursors on mesoporous MFI zeolite nanosheets [10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]. While showing outstanding stability, no comparisons were made to oxidized PtZn catalysts. Similarly, multiple works examined bimetallic PtSn catalysts on various supports like titanosilicate TS-1 and boron nitride without contrasting metallic and oxidized states [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024, 10.1016/j.cej.2007.11.009].

Overall, most literature has focused on evaluating promising formulations of metallic PDH catalysts rather than investigating the effects of oxidation state on selectivity profiles. No explanations were provided regarding potential selectivity modulation between metallic and oxidized catalyst surfaces. This represents an area for future research to understand how oxidation impacts product distributions for key reaction systems.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The presented analyses reveal metallic catalysts as predominant in PDH studies, with most efforts targeting improved stability and minimal attention to contrasting metallic versus oxidized selectivity. However, oxidation state can play a key role in modulating active site geometry and electronic properties, impacting catalytic performance. An opportunity exists to systematically evaluate PDH selectivity profiles on tuned model catalysts, correlating oxidation induced changes in catalyst surface properties to product distributions. Such fundamental structure-function insights could guide rational design of improved catalysts exploiting synergies between metallic and oxidized sites to optimize selectivity. Broader horizons may also emerge from extending such concepts to other important dehydrogenation reactions like propane aromatization.

例如,一项研究通过共浸渍金属前体于介孔MFI分子筛纳米片上合成了PtZn合金纳米粒子用于PDH [10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]。虽然显示出优异的稳定性,但没有与氧化PtZn催化剂进行比较。类似地,多项工作在titanosilicate TS-1和氮化硼等各种载体上检查了PtSn双金属催化剂,没有对比金属与氧化态 [10.1016/j.jcat.2017.05.024, 10.1016/j.cej.2007.11.009]。

总的来说,大多数文献侧重于评估有前途的金属PDH催化剂配方,而不是研究氧化态对选择性曲线的影响。没有提供金属和氧化催化剂表面之间的选择性调制方面的解释。这是一个未来研究领域,以了解氧化如何影响关键反应体系的产物分布。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:呈现的分析显示金属催化剂在PDH研究中占主导地位,大多数努力针对改进稳定性,很少注意对比金属与氧化选择性。然而,氧化态可以在调节活性位点几何形状和电子性质方面发挥关键作用,从而影响催化性能。通过在调谐模型催化剂上系统评估PDH选择性曲线存在机会,将氧化诱导的催化剂表面性质变化与产物分布联系起来。这种基本的结构-功能认识可以指导设计改进的催化剂,利用金属和氧化位点之间的协同效应来优化选择性。当扩展到丙烷萘化等其他重要脱氢反应时,也可能出现更广阔的前景。
