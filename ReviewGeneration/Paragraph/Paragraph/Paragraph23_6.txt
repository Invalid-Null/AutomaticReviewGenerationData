Based on the detailed analysis from the references, metal encapsulation in zeolite supports shows promise for enhancing the stability of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. Several studies demonstrate that encapsulating metal nanoparticles and clusters within zeolite frameworks like MFI, BEA, UTL, and S-1 prevents sintering and agglomeration during high temperature PDH conditions [10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019, 10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4, 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. The zeolite confinement stabilizes the active phase and leads to markedly improved catalyst lifetimes. 

In particular, Co@Si-BEA with encapsulated cobalt species exhibits excellent stability over 8 reaction-regeneration cycles [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]. Subnanometric PtSn clusters regioselectively positioned inside the 10R channels of MFI zeolite maintain the cluster size and high propane conversion for multiple PDH cycles [10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]. Ultrastable Pt clusters anchored to germanium sites in UTL zeolite via Pt-O-Ge linkages are effective for propane dehydrogenation for over 4000 hours without deactivation [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. The literature therefore demonstrates that zeolite encapsulation strategies impart remarkable improvements in stability compared to conventional supported metal catalysts that are prone to deactivation.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reveal new design principles and opportunities to synthesize superior PDH catalysts by deliberately localizing active metal species within zeolite micropores. While current efforts focus on proof-of-concept studies with model catalysts, translational research can explore more complex multimetallic compositions tailored for commercial reactors. For example, bimetallic combinations of late transition metals encapsulated in zeolites may offer advantages in cost and stability over precious metal catalysts. Future work might also apply in situ characterization and machine learning to gain atomic-level insights into the encapsulation process and derive structure-stability relationships to guide rational catalyst optimization.

<Chinese>

基于参考文献的详细分析,金属在分子筛载体中的包埋有助于增强丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂的稳定性。几项研究表明,在 MFI、BEA、UTL 和 S-1 等分子筛框架中包埋金属纳米粒子和簇可以防止高温 PDH 条件下的烧结和团聚[10.1016/j.jcat.2020.02.019,10.1038/s41563-019-0430-4,10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7,10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。分子筛限域稳定了活性相,显著改善了催化剂的使用寿命。


特别是,具有包埋钴物种的 Co@Si-BEA 在 8 次反应-再生循环中表现出优异的稳定性[10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726]。定域在 MFI 分子筛 10R 通道内的亚纳米 PtSn 簇在多个 PDH 循环中保持了簇的大小和高丙烷转化率[10.1038/s41929-020-0472-7]。通过 Pt-O-Ge 键锚定在 UTL 分子筛锗位点上的超稳定 Pt 簇可用于丙烷脱氢反应超过 4000 小时而不会 deactivate [10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。因此,文献证明,分子筛包埋策略相比于常规支持的金属催化剂(易发生失活)显著提高了稳定性。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望:研究结果揭示了新设计原则和机遇,通过有意将活性金属物种局限于分子筛微孔内来合成优良的 PDH 催化剂。尽管目前的研究侧重于用模式催化剂进行概念证明研究,但转化研究可以探索更复杂的多金属组合,这些组合可针对商业反应器进行优化。例如,包埋在分子筛中的晚过渡金属的双金属组合在成本和稳定性方面可能优于贵金属催化剂。未来的工作还可能应用基于原位表征和机器学习的方法来获得包埋过程的原子水平洞见,并推导结构稳定性关系以指导催化剂的合理优化。
