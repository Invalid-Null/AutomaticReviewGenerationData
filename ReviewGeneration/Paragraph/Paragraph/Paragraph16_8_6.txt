Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Pt-Sn Catalysts

Multiple studies have demonstrated that incorporating Sn as a promoter improves catalytic activity, propylene selectivity, and stability of Pt-based catalysts for PDH [Placeholder_of_DOI1], [Placeholder_of_DOI2]. The commercial Oleflex process by UOP uses a Pt-Sn/Al2O3 catalyst [Placeholder_of_DOI3]. DFT calculations suggest Sn modifies the electronic properties of Pt active sites, increasing electron density and altering ensemble requirements for selective dehydrogenation over undesired C-C bond cleavage [Placeholder_of_DOI4]. This “ligand effect” of Sn on Pt sites favors propylene formation while suppressing cracking and coking reactions. Additionally, Sn helps disperse Pt particles during synthesis, providing a "geometric effect" with more exposed Pt active sites initially. However, excess Sn can block sites and reduce activity [Placeholder_of_DOI5]. Multiple studies find optimal Sn/Pt ratios around 1-1.5 for balancing stability and activity [Placeholder_of_DOI6], [Placeholder_of_DOI7]. The decompsotion of Sn-rich clusters to Pt3Sn and Pt has also been suggested to improve activity over time on stream [Placeholder_of_DOI8].

Pt-Ga Catalysts  

Like Sn, studies show Ga as a promoter increases propylene selectivity and stability of Pt-based PDH catalysts [Placeholder_of_DOI9], [Placeholder_of_DOI10]. The electronic modification of Pt active sites by Ga enhances selectivity by suppressing hydrogenolysis and other side reactions. Additionally, Ga improves metal dispersion. The formation of Pt-Ga intermetallic compounds has been detected and linked with high activity and selectivity [Placeholder_of_DOI11]. 2% Si doped PtGa/Al2O3 achieved 24.5% equilibrium conversion at 450°C, exhibiting outstanding stability with negligible deactivation over 100 hours [Placeholder_of_DOI12].

Pt-Zn Catalysts

Several articles demonstrate Zn promotion also modulates Pt ensembles and electron density, improving PDH performance [Placeholder_of_DOI13], [Placeholder_of_DOI14]. Isolated, single-atom Pt stabilized by grafted SiOZn species in the framework of zeolite Beta showed remarkable activity (50% conversion) and stability (>500 hours) [Placeholder_of_DOI15]. EXAFS data confirms PtZn bonding for such isolated sites [Placeholder_of_DOI16].  PtZn intermetallic alloy NPs confined in mesoporous MFI zeolite exhibited high activity (17 molC3H6/molPt/s), stability, and propylene selectivity over a range of temperatures [Placeholder_of_DOI17].

In summary, alloying Pt with promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn has been widely demonstrated through experimental and computational studies to enhance PDH performance by modulating electron density, geometric structure and ensemble requirements of Pt active sites. each promoter provides unique synergistic effects with Pt. Key indicators of performance enhancement include increased propylene selectivity and stability, with optimally balanced activity tradeoffs.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: 

The presented analyses offer convincing evidence on how judicious alloying strategies can overcome limitations of monometallic Pt catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. By fine-tuning Pt ensemble requirements and electronic properties, promoters like Sn, Ga and Zn can restrict side reactions while activating desired C-H bonds with high selectivity. 

However, open questions remain regarding optimization of synthetic techniques and atomic-level characterization of different promoters and Pt alloy phases present. Future research could focus on novel characterization tools such as in situ spectroscopy and theoretical simulations to provide enhanced insights into the dynamic structure-performance interplay occurring for these complex bimetallic and alloy systems. Data-driven high-throughput computational screening of ternary and quaternary alloy compositions may also illuminate promising unexplored compositional spaces. 

On the applied side, there are opportunities to translate fundamental knowledge on model systems into industrial practice, adapting novel bimetallic combinations and synthetic methods to stabilize single sites and intermetallic nanoparticles under realistic operating environments. By bridging these gaps between fundamental science and implementation, next-generation PDH catalysts with revolutionary activity, selectivity and stability appear on the horizon.

双金属和合金催化剂——Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

Pt-Sn催化剂

多项研究已经证明,掺入Sn作为助剂可以改善Pt基PDH催化剂的活性、丙烯选择性和稳定性 [Placeholder_of_DOI1],[Placeholder_of_DOI2]。 UOP公司的商业Oleflex工艺使用Pt-Sn/Al2O3催化剂 [Placeholder_of_DOI3]。 DFT计算表明,Sn改变了Pt活性位点的电子性质,增加了电子密度并改变了选择性脱氢所需的配位环境,而不是不需要的C-C键断裂 [Placeholder_of_DOI4]。 Sn对Pt位点的这种“配体效应”有利于丙烯的形成,同时抑制裂解和结焦反应。 此外,Sn帮助分散Pt颗粒的合成,最初提供更多暴露的Pt活性位点的“几何效应”。 然而,过量的Sn会堵塞位点并降低活性[Placeholder_of_DOI5]。 多项研究发现Sn/Pt比率1-1.5左右可以在稳定性和活性之间取得平衡 [Placeholder_of_DOI6],[Placeholder_of_DOI7]。 也有研究表明,Sn-rich 簇解组成Pt3Sn 和Pt 可以提高活性[Placeholder_of_DOI8]。

Pt-Ga催化剂 

与Sn类似,研究表明Ga作为助剂可以增加Pt基PDH催化剂的丙烯选择性和稳定性 [Placeholder_of_DOI9],[Placeholder_of_DOI10]。 Ga对Pt活性位点的电子修饰抑制氢化反应和其他副反应,从而提高选择性。 此外,Ga还可以改善金属分散度。 已经检测到Pt-Ga金属间化合物的形成,并与高活性和选择性相关联[Placeholder_of_DOI11]。 2% Si掺杂的PtGa/Al2O3催化剂在450°C实现了24.5%的平衡转换量,在100小时内基本没有失活,展现了杰出的稳定性 [Placeholder_of_DOI12]。

Pt-Zn催化剂

几篇文章表明Zn掺杂也可以调节Pt组合和电子密度,从而提高PDH性能 [Placeholder_of_DOI13], [Placeholder_of_DOI14]。 Beta分子筛框架中与SiOZn基团连接的单原子隔离Pt显示出显着的活性(50%转化率)和稳定性(超过500小时) [ Placeholder_of_DOI15]。 EXAFS数据确认了这种隔离位点的PtZn键合存在 [Placeholder_of_DOI16]。 封装在介孔MFI沸石中的PtZn金属间化合物纳米颗粒显示了在广泛温度范围内都比较高的活性(17 molC3H6/molPt/s)、稳定性和丙烯选择性[Placeholder_of_DOI17]。

总之,通过实验和计算研究广泛证明了与Sn、Ga和Zn等助剂合金化可以通过调节Pt活性位点的电子密度、几何结构和配体要求来增强PDH性能。 每个助剂都与Pt有独特的协同效应。 性能提高的关键指标包括提高的丙烯选择性和稳定性,以及达到最佳活性权衡。
