Based on the details provided in the file, several studies have explored supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, and Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most widely used and commercially successful PDH catalysts. For example, Pt/Al2O3 is a vital PDH catalyst component due to Pt's ability to activate paraffinic C-H bonds while minimizing C-C bond cleavage [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201507119]. One study showed an alumina-supported Pt catalyst maintained high initial activity for PDH, before deactivating over multiple cycles due to Pt agglomeration accelerated by sulfur [DOI: 10.1016/0021-9517(86)90312-X]. Adding supports or promoters is a common strategy to enhance Pt catalyst stability and selectivity. Atomic layer deposition (ALD) Al2O3 or TiO2 overcoats on supported Pt increased PDH yield and selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391], while 1 wt% boron promotion of Pt/γ-Al2O3 boosted propylene selectivity to 98% and reduced coking 3-fold [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548]. Optimized dispersion of Pt as isolated atoms or sub-nanometric clusters in zeolites can also improve stability [DOI: 10.1038/s41467-018-07502-5, DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7].

Besides Pt, some studies have explored Pd- and Ir-based PDH catalysts. One article mentions Pd, Rh, and Ir show high intrinsic activity, but does not provide data [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.3c01002]. Another evaluates supported RhCu single atom alloy sites, showing higher activity, 100% propene selectivity, and coking resistance compared to a standard Pt/Al2O3 catalyst [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]. No studies were found evaluating supported Pd or Ir catalysts.

In summary, Pt-based catalysts, especially Pt/Al2O3, are the most widely studied and used supported metal catalysts for PDH, but modifiers and optimized dispersion can further enhance performance. Supported Rh and potentially other late transition metals also show promise. Further research is needed to fully assess and optimize supported Pd, Ir, and other metal catalysts for PDH.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements with supported mono-metallic Pt catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, there are opportunities to build upon this foundation through further materials development and optimization. One avenue is exploring novel supports and promoters that stabilize Pt sites, minimize coking, and tune selectivity. Compositional and electronic effects at the metal-support interface seem to play a key role, warranting expanded studies into how interactions with the support matrix modulate Pt’s PDH performance. 

Advancements with single atom alloy and atomically dispersed catalysts also highlight the importance of defined active sites, prompting research into synthesizing and stabilizing isolated Pt sites. Operando spectroscopy techniques combined with kinetic and DFT modeling will provide vital insights to guide this process.

Finally, while Pt dominates current PDH catalysts, emerging studies on Pd, Rh, Ir and other late transition metals suggest room for expanded catalyst screening and development. Identifying additional active metals, supports, and multifunctional sites could uncover more optimal PDH catalyst formulations.
