 <English>

Based on the detailed analysis from the study [10.1021/acscatal.3c00548], larger Pt nanoparticles can accommodate more Sn species compared to subnanometer Pt clusters, resulting in the formation of PtSn alloy nanoparticles. In contrast, small Pt clusters tend to form Pt-rich clusters with low Sn contribution. These insights could be translated to tuning other Pt-based bimetallic catalysts, where the choice of secondary metal influences the optimal Pt site size for maximizing performance.

Further investigations on the electronic and geometric effects are furnished by the work in [10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]. The geometry-isolated and electron-rich Pt sites in [PtZn4] surface ensembles enable facile desorption of propylene and enhanced stability over other [PtM4] configurations. Although the geometric structures were analogous between [PtZn4] and [PtM4] motifs, significant differences were discerned in the electronic state. 

Additional electronic structure modulation is imparted by Mn as the secondary metal in PtMn intermetallic nanoparticles, formed through reactive metal support interactions [10.1021/jacs.8b08162]. The introduction of a Pt3Mn intermetallic subsurface led to markedly higher propane dehydrogenation selectivity relative to monometallic Pt, attributed to the weakened hydrocarbon and hydrogen adsorption on the active sites. This exemplifies tuning selectivity via electronic effects induced by the secondary metal.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Considering the advancements elucidated in this study regarding secondary metal incorporation, future explorations might concentrate on expanding the library of bimetallic and multimetallic systems. Pursuing uncharted combinations by high-throughput DFT screening could unveil hitherto unknown motifs with unique selectivity patterns. Furthermore, recent progress with in situ spectroscopy and microscopy permits tracking dynamic restructuring phenomena, enabling better correlations with functioning. On the simulation front, developing multi-scale models incorporating realistic environments might reveal additional descriptors signifying performance.

<Chinese>

根据[10.1021/acscatal.3c00548]的详细分析,更大的Pt纳米粒子可以容纳更多的Sn物种,导致PtSn合金纳米粒子的形成。相比之下,亚纳米Pt簇倾向于形成Pt含量低的簇。这些见解可以转化为调节其他Pt基双金属催化剂,其中次要金属的选择影响最大化性能的Pt位点的最佳大小。


[10.1016/j.chempr.2020.10.008]的研究中提供了进一步的电子和几何效应的调查。 [PtZn4] 表面配位体中的几何隔离和富电子的Pt位点使丙烯易于解吸和增强的稳定性优于其他[PtM4] 配置。 尽管[PtZn4]和[PtM4]基团之间的几何结构是类似的,但在电子状态上观察到明显的差异。


通过与支撑体的反应性金属相互作用形成的PtMn金属间化合物纳米粒子中,[10.1021/jacs.8b08162]探究了Mn作为次要金属的额外电子结构调制。相比单金属Pt,Pt3Mn金属间化合物次表面导致丙烷脱氢显著提高,归因于活性位点对碳氢化合物和氢的吸附削弱。这证明了通过次要金属诱导的电子效应调节选择性。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 考虑到本研究中揭示的有关次要金属掺入的进展,未来的探索可能会集中在扩大双金属和多金属系统的库。 通过高通量DFT筛选追求未开发的组合可能会揭示迄今未知的具有独特选择性模式的基团。 此外,近期在位光谱和显微技术的进展允许跟踪动态重组现象,从而建立更好的相关性。 在模拟方面,开发结合现实环境的多尺度模型可能会揭示额外的描述子,这些描述子标志着性能。
