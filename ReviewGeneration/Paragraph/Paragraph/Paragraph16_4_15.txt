Bimetallic and Alloy Catalysts - How does alloying Pt with Sn, Ga, Zn affect propane dehydrogenation performance?

Several studies have evaluated the effect of alloying platinum with secondary metals like tin (Sn), gallium (Ga), and zinc (Zn) for enhancing the performance of propane dehydrogenation (PDH) catalysts. Adding these promoters helps modulate the geometric and electronic properties of Pt active sites. 

Pt-Sn alloys have been widely studied for PDH, with bimetallic PtSn nanoparticles supported on various oxides and zeolites investigated [Placeholder_Of_DOI1, Placeholder_Of_DOI2]. The addition of Sn serves to decrease Pt particle size and ensemble size, providing a "dilution effect", while also modifying the electronic properties and increasing electron density on Pt sites [Placeholder_Of_DOI3]. These ensemble and ligand effects help improve selectivity to propylene and stability by reducing hydrogenolysis, isomerization, and coking reactions [Placeholder_Of_DOI4]. DFT calculations suggest Sn addition increases PDH activation barriers on Pt but helps stabilize the active sites against deactivation [Placeholder_Of_DOI5]. Experimentally, PtSn catalysts have shown higher initial activity but faster deactivation than monometallic Pt catalysts [Placeholder_Of_DOI6]. An optimal Sn/Pt ratio around 1-1.5 is reported before excess Sn causes blocking of active sites [Placeholder_Of_DOI7]. 

Pt-Ga catalysts also demonstrate improved dispersion of Pt particles and selectivity in PDH reactions [Placeholder_Of_DOI8]. The electronic and geometric changes induced by Ga are suggested to suppress side reactions like cracking and coking. Pt-Zn catalysts have been less studied but show promise, with controlled PtZn intermetallic alloys achieving high propylene selectivity above 95% and stability over extended time-on-stream [Placeholder_Of_DOI9]. The ordered PtZn arrangement helps minimize the ensemble size. Overall, alloying modulates the adsorption properties and activation barriers on Pt sites. An activity versus stability tradeoff is often exhibited.

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Combining precious metals like Pt with abundant promoters like Sn, Ga, Zn has enabled notable advancements in propane dehydrogenation catalyst performance. However, fundamental insights into the active site requirements, tailored synthetic strategies, and stabilization mechanisms will be crucial to drive continued progress. Emerging operando spectroscopic and imaging techniques combined with computational modeling can offer unprecedented views into working bimetallic catalysts. Knowledge garnered could guide design of ordered intermetallic sites to optimize the Pt ensemble size, ligand environment, and stability. Transferring concepts to non-precious metal catalysts also holds promise if key descriptors are identified. Eventually, breakthroughs may enable commercially viable PDH catalysts operating under milder, more selective conditions.

双金属和合金催化剂 - Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn合金化如何影响丙烷脱氢性能?

几项研究评估了将铂与锡(Sn)、镓(Ga)和锌(Zn)等辅助金属合金化,以提高丙烷脱氢(PDH)催化剂性能的效果。 添加这些辅助剂有助于调控Pt活性位点的几何和电子性质。

Pt-Sn合金广泛用于PDH,对各种氧化物和分子筛支撑的PtSn双金属纳米粒子进行了研究 [Placeholder_Of_DOI1,Placeholder_Of_DOI2]。 添加Sn有助于减小Pt颗粒大小和集合体大小,提供“稀释效应”,同时也改变了电子性质,增加了Pt位点的电子密度[Placeholder_Of_DOI3]。 这些集合体效应和配体效应通过减少氢解、异构化和焦炭反应来帮助提高丙烯选择性和稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI4]。 DFT计算表明,Sn的加入会增加Pt上的PDH激活壁垒,但有助于稳定活性位点,防止其失活[Placeholder_Of_DOI5]。 从实验结果看,PtSn催化剂比单金属Pt催化剂具有更高的初始活性但更快的失活[Placeholder_Of_DOI6]。 当Sn/Pt比达到1-1.5左右时报道了最佳比例,过量的Sn会导致活性位点的阻塞[Placeholder_Of_DOI7]。

Pt-Ga催化剂也表现出Pt颗粒分散度提高,PDH反应选择性提高。 Ga引起的电子和几何变化被认为能抑制像裂解和焦化这样的副反应。 尽管Pt-Zn催化剂的研究较少,但它展示了前景,受控的PtZn金属间化合物取得了高达95%以上的丙烯选择性和长期稳定性[Placeholder_Of_DOI9]。 有序的PtZn排列有助于最小化集合体大小。 总的来说,合金化调控了Pt位点的吸附性质和激活壁垒。 通常体现出活性与稳定性的权衡。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望: 将贵金属Pt与Sn、Ga、Zn等廉价辅助剂相结合,使丙烷脱氢催化剂性能取得了显著进步。 然而,要推动持续进步,关于活性位点要求、定制合成策略和稳定机制的基本见解将至关重要。 新出现的原位光谱和成像技术结合计算模型可以前所未有地洞察工作中的双金属催化剂。 获得的知识可以指导设计有序的金属间化合物位点,以优化Pt的集合体大小、配体环境和稳定性。 如果确定了关键描述子,将这些概念转移到非贵金属催化剂也很有希望。 最终,突破可能实现在较温和、更选择性条件下商业上可行的PDH催化剂。
