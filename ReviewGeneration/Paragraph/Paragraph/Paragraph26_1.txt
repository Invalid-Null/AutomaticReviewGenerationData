Based on the details provided in the file, one article utilizes integrated differential phase contrast scanning transmission electron microscopy (iDPC-STEM) to reveal that isolated Co species with a distorted tetrahedral {(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} structure are homogeneously embedded into the T1(7) and T3(9) sites of MFI zeolites [10.1021/jacs.2c02636]. This directly images the Co coordination environment within the zeolite framework, providing insights into the metal-support interface.

Another study uses aberration corrected scanning transmission electron microscopy (AC-STEM) paired with spectroscopic methods to confirm Co species confined within hydroxyl nests of a SiBeta zeolite support after impregnation [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. The microscopy helps validate the location of the metal within the support.

One article also utilizes high-angle annular dark field scanning transmission electron microscopy (HAADF-STEM) to analyze Pt/Zn dispersion, distribution, and intermetallic nanoparticle formation on a mesoporous zeolite support in propane dehydrogenation catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]. This reveals details on the metal-support interactions.

※※※

The advanced microscopy techniques provide valuable nanoscale insights into metal coordination geometries and locations within zeolite frameworks and mesoporous supports. Future research could further exploit state-of-the-art, atomic-resolution electron microscopy and spectroscopy to uncover additional details related to synergies between metals and supports at an fundamental level. 

Machine learning-empowered image analysis may also help automate quantification of critical descriptors related to active site locations, metal cluster populations, dispersion uniformity, etc. Exploring correlative relationships between this microscopic data and macroscopic catalytic performance could unveil design principles to advance propane dehydrogenation systems.

<Chinese>

根据文件提供的详细信息,一篇文章利用集成差分��比扫描透射电镜(iDPC-STEM)揭示,取向扭曲的四面体 {(SiO)2Co(HOSi)2} 结构的隔离 Co 物种均匀地嵌入 MFI 分子筛的 T1(7) 和 T3(9) 位点 [10.1021/jacs.2c02636]。这直接成像了分子筛框架中的 Co 协调环境,提供了关于金属支撑体界面的数据。

另一项研究使用球差修正的扫描透射电镜(AC-STEM)配合光谱方法确认了Co物种限制在SiBeta分子筛支撑体的羟基巢内,这是在浸渍后 [10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。该显微技术有助于验证金属在支撑体中的位置。

一篇文章还利用高角环形暗场扫描透射电镜(HAADF-STEM)来分析丙烷脱氢催化剂中介孔分子筛支撑体上的Pt/Zn分散、分布和金属间化合物纳米粒子的形成 [10.1021/acscatal.1c01808]。这揭示了关于金属支撑体相互作用的细节。

※※※

先进的显微技术为分子筛框架和介孔支撑体中的金属配位几何和位置提供了宝贵的纳米尺度的见解。未来的研究可以进一步利用最先进的、原子分辨率的电子显微技术和光谱学,来揭示与金属和支撑体之间协同效应相关的额外细节。

同时,赋能了机器学习的图像分析也可以帮助自动量化与活性位点位置、金属簇体种群、均匀性分散等相关的关键指标。探索这些显微数据与宏观催化性能之间的相关性,可以揭示设计原理,以推进丙烷脱氢系统的发展。
