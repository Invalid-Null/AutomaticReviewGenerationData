 <English>

Based on the details extracted from the references, propane dehydrogenation (PDH) faces significant thermodynamic constraints due to being a highly endothermic reaction (ΔH ̊ = 124.3 kJ/mol). To drive the equilibrium conversion, high temperatures of 500-650°C are required [References placeholder]. However, this also kinetically facilitates undesired side reactions like coke formation and catalyst deactivation [References placeholder].

Some studies have calculated apparent activation energies in the range of 180-200 kJ/mol for the dehydrogenation step and 159-174 kJ/mol for cracking reactions over zeolite catalysts [References 10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. DFT studies have also computed barriers around 0.71 eV (68 kJ/mol) for the initial C-H bond activation [References 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. These kinetic factors determine the rates and selectivity.

Oxidative PDH coupling propane with O2 or CO2 feeds can partially alleviate the thermodynamic limitations, providing exothermic oxidation reactions to increase conversion [References 10.1002/advs.202207756]. However, the tradeoff with undesired overoxidation remains an inherent kinetic challenge.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Propane dehydrogenation faces complex tradeoffs between thermodynamic driving forces, kinetic selectivity, and catalyst stability. While recent studies have advanced mechanistic insights into these interconnected factors, further research should explore emerging computational, spectroscopic and in-situ characterization techniques (e.g. operando spectroscopy, microkinetic modeling) to obtain a dynamic multi-scale perspective. This could unveil optimization principles to tune active site geometries, oxidative conditions and other variables for targeted PDH performance. Broader horizons might entail explorations of novel reaction media (ionic liquids, membrane reactors etc.) and hybrid processes combining reaction and separation steps to push equilibrium limitations.

根据从参考文献中提取的细节,丙烷脱氢(PDH)由于是一种高度端othermic反应(ΔH ̊ = 124.3 kJ/mol),面临重大的热力学约束。 为了驱动平衡转换,需要500-650°C的高温[引用的占位符]。 然而,这在动力学上也有利于不desired副反应,如焦炭形成和催化剂脱活[引用占位符]。

一些研究计算了在分子筛催化剂上脱氢步骤的表观活化能在180-200 kJ/mol的范围内, 和用于裂解反应159-174 kJ/mol [引用10.1021/jacs.2c03941]。 DFT研究还计算了约0.71 eV(68 kJ/mol)的初始C-H键活化的障碍[引用10.1021/ acscatal.3c00889]。 这些动力学因素决定了速率和选择性。

氧化PDH将丙烷与O2或CO2反应物的偶联部分缓解了热力学限制,提供放热的氧化反应以增加转化率[引用10.1002/advs.202207756]。 然而,与不desired过氧化反应的权衡仍然是一个固有的动力学挑战。

※※※  

综合理解与未来展望:丙烷脱氢存在复杂的热力学驱动力,动力学选择性和催化剂稳定性之间的权衡。 尽管近期的研究对这些相互关联的因素取得了机制方面的进展,但进一步的研究应该探索出现的计算、光谱和原位表征技术(例如, operando光谱、微观动力学建模)来获得动态的多尺度视角。 这可以揭示优化催化剂活性位点几何、氧化条件和 other 变量以实现目标定向的PDH性能的原理。 更广阔的视野可能涉及探索新颖的反应介质(离子液体、膜反应器等)和 hybrid 将反应和分离步骤相结合以推动平衡限制的工艺。
