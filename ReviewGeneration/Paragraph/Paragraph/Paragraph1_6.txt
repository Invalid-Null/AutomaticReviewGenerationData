 <English>

Propylene is an essential raw material for producing a wide range of chemicals, with an estimated global market size exceeding $100 billion annually [10.1021/jacs.2c03941]. It is conventionally produced through steam cracking and fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) processes, but these routes face challenges in meeting rapidly rising demand [10.1021/acscatal.0c01580, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]. Propane dehydrogenation (PDH) has emerged as an attractive, largescale production method for propylene, exploiting abundant and low-cost supplies of propane from shale gas [10.1021/acscatal.0c01455, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]. 

Propylene serves as a feedstock for manufacturing commodity chemicals and polymers critical to sectors like construction, packaging, transportation, and consumer products. Key derivatives include polypropylene, propylene oxide, acrylonitrile, acetone, and cumene [10.1021/acscatal.2c01374, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]. Global propylene demand was around 130 megatons in 2019 and continues to grow over 5% annually, expected to reach 191 megatons by 2030 [10.1002/advs.202207756]. PDH technology has rapidly expanded and now accounts for approximately 10% of total propylene output to help close projected supply shortfalls [10.1002/advs.202207756].

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: Propane dehydrogenation processes will likely play an increasingly vital role in meeting the world's growing appetite for propylene and subsequent derivative chemicals. While traditional production routes face structural limitations, propane feedstocks are abundant and the underlying PDH chemistry is well-understood. Future research and development efforts may focus on raising catalyst performance to boost propylene yields, exploring alternative co-catalysts to improve selectivity, or incorporating renewable hydrogen sources. Broader adoption of PDH could support greener propylene production with lowered greenhouse gas emissions compared to conventional fossil fuel-based cracking technologies.

丙烯是生产广泛化学品的关键原料,其估计的全球市场规模超过1000亿美元每年[10.1021/jacs.2c03941]。它通常通过蒸汽裂解和流化催化裂解(FCC)工艺生产,但这些途径在满足快速增长的需求方面面临挑战[10.1021/acscatal.0c01580, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00816]。丙烷脱氢(PDH)已成为大规模生产丙烯的有吸引力的方法,利用来自页岩气的丰富和低成本的丙烷供应[10.1021/acscatal.0c01455, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00889]。

丙烯用于制造商品化学品和聚合物,这些产品在建筑、包装、运输和消费品等部门至关重要。关键衍生物包括聚丙烯、丙烯醇、丙烯腈、丙酮和邻甲苯[10.1021/acscatal.2c01374, 10.1021/acscatal.3c00103]。2019年全球丙烯需求约为1.3亿吨,并继续以每年5%以上的速度增长,预计到2030年将达到1.91亿吨[10.1002/advs.202207756]。PDH技术已经快速扩张,现在约占总丙烯产量的10%,以帮助弥合预计的供应短缺[10.1002/advs.202207756]。
 
※※※
综合理解与未来展望:丙烷脱氢工艺可能在满足世界对丙烯及其后续化学品日益增长的需求中发挥越来越重要的作用。虽然传统的生产途径存在结构性限制,但丙烷原料充足,PDH化学反应也得到很好的理解。未来的研发工作可能会关注提高催化剂性能以增加丙烯产量,探索替代共催化剂以改善选择性,或者结合可再生氢源。PDH技术的更广泛采用可以比常规的化石燃料裂解技术大大降低温室气体排放,支持更环保的丙烯生产。
