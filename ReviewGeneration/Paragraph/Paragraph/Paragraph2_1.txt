	<English>
		Catalysts enable propane dehydrogenation by providing alternative pathways and active sites that lower the activation energy barriers compared to the uncatalyzed thermal process [10.1016/j.jcat.2021.09.008]. Industrial PDH utilizes supported metal catalysts like Pt/Al2O3 and CrOx/Al2O3, where the metal sites activate C-H bonds in propane [10.1016/j.jcat.2022.02.025, 10.1021/acscatal.0c01455]. DFT calculations have elucidated how two neighboring Zr cations on ZrO2 catalysts also create active sites [10.1021/acscatal.0c01580]. Besides metals, zeolites and mesoporous silicas provide confinement effects that stabilize active sites [10.1021/acscatal.3c00816, 10.1021/acscatal.0c03999]. Overall, catalyst design involves optimizing active site formation while balancing selectivity over undesired cracking reactions [10.1021/acscatal.5b01423].
		※※※
		With expanding propylene demand and shale gas resources, PDH technology holds strong potential. Further explorations could uncover novel single-atom catalysts or bimetallic systems with optimized activation barriers. Operando spectroscopy and microkinetic modeling may provide deeper insights to guide rational catalyst optimization. Broader adoption of PDH also relies on addressingstability and environmental challenges.

		※※※
		随着丙烯需求的扩大和页岩气资源的增加,PDH技术具有很强的潜力。进一步的探索可能会发现新的单原子催化剂或双金属系统,具有优化的激活屏障。 Operando光谱和微动力学建模可以提供更深入的见解来指导催化剂的合理优化。 PDH的更广泛采用也依赖于解决稳定性和环境问题。
