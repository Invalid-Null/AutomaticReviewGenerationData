Based on the details provided in the file, there are a few studies that explore metal-support interfacial effects in metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation and provide insights into activity and selectivity modulation.

One example is the study on phosphine-modified VOx/Al2O3 catalysts [10.1016/j.apcatb.2020.119089]. Phosphine treatment is found to weaken the interaction between vanadium species and the alumina support. This facilitates desorption of propylene intermediates/products from the catalyst surface, enhancing selectivity and stability.

Another relevant study investigates the impact of different oxide supports like ZrO2 and Al2O3 on VOx catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]. Density functional theory calculations reveal weaker VOx-ZrO2 interactions lead to lower electron density on bridge oxygen atoms, enhancing their capability to rupture C-H bonds in propane and boosting activity. 

The role of strong metal-support interactions is highlighted in Pt/Sn-Beta zeolite catalysts [10.1021/acscatal.9b03527], where isolated Sn sites strongly anchor Pt clusters, resulting in high Pt dispersion and thermal stability. This enhances catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation.

Overall, these studies demonstrate that judicious tuning of metal-support interfaces, by modifiers or choice of support, can effectively modulate the microenvironment and electronic properties of active sites. This provides opportunities to optimize activity, selectivity and stability of metal oxide catalysts for the crucial propane dehydrogenation reaction.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings reveal metal-support interplay can profoundly influence propane dehydrogenation over metal oxides by altering active site isolation, electron density, anchoring effects and desorption kinetics. However, most studies have focused on few conventional supports like alumina and silica. Exploring emerging supports with tailored interfaces (e.g. metal-organic frameworks, covalent organic frameworks) could uncover further structure-performance correlations. Operando spectroscopies probing working catalysts can also help advance molecular-level mechanistic comprehending of interfacial phenomena governing selectivity and deactivation. Such fundamental insights can guide the design of robust, multifunctional metal oxide catalysts for efficient on-purpose propylene production.

根据文件提供的详细信息,有几项研究探索了金属氧化物催化剂中金属载体界面效应对丙烷脱氢反应的活性和选择性调控。

一个例子是研究磷化氢处理过的 VOx/Al2O3 催化剂 [10.1016/j.apcatb.2020.119089]。发现磷化氢处理削弱了钒种与氧化铝载体之间的相互作用。这促进了丙烯中间体/产物从催化剂表面的解吸,提高了选择性和稳定性。

另一项相关研究调查了不同的氧化物载体如 ZrO2 和 Al2O3 对 VOx 催化剂的影响 [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]。密度泛函理论计算揭示较弱的 VOx-ZrO2 相互作用导致桥氧原子上的电子密度更低,增强了它们断裂丙烷中的 C-H 键的能力,从而提高了活性。

Pt/Sn-Beta 分子筛催化剂中观察到强的金属载体相互作用 [10.1021/acscatal.9b03527], 其中孤立的 Sn 位点牢固锚定 Pt 簇,导致 Pt 分散度高和热稳定性好。这增强了丙烷脱氢反应的催化性能。

总的来说,这些研究表明,通过修饰剂或载体的选择来精确调控金属载体界面,可以有效调控活性位点的微环境和电子性质。这为优化金属氧化物催化剂在关键的丙烷脱氢反应中的活性、选择性和稳定性提供了机会。


※※※

综合理解与未来展望:研究结果揭示金属载体相互作用可以通过改变活性位点的隔离性、电子密度、锚定效应和解吸动力学等途径深刻影响金属氧化物上的丙烷脱氢反应。然而,大多数研究仅关注几种常规载体如氧化铝和二氧化硅。探索新兴的带有定制界面(例如金属有机框架,共价有机框架)的载体可能会发现更多的结构性能关系。同时,对工作状态下的催化剂进行原位光谱研究可以帮助我们在分子水平上理解控制选择性和失活的界面的现象。这种基本见解可以指导设计稳定的、多功能的金属氧化物催化剂,用于高效的丙烯定向生产。
