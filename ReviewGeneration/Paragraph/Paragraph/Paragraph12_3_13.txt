Based on the details extracted from the file, several articles evaluate supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, or Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. 

One study investigates adding sulfur to Pt/alumina catalysts during preparation and in the gas phase during reaction [DOI1]. Predosing the catalyst with H2S greatly improves selectivity to propene, and sulfur incorporation during preparation is more effective than during reaction.

Another article shows isolating single Pt sites in the pores of a Ge-UTL zeolite via Pt-O-Ge bonds leads to a highly stable and selective catalyst [DOI2]. The Pt sites demonstrate excellent catalytic performance for propane dehydrogenation.  

Additional work probes supported Pt clusters on alumina and finds atomic layer deposition Al2O3 or TiO2 overcoats can increase propane dehydrogenation yield and selectivity [DOI3]. The overcoats are implemented throughout the length and width of extruded Pt/alumina catalysts.

Beyond Pt, one study evaluates dealuminated zeolites containing isolated Co2+ active sites at low Co loadings [DOI4]. A linear relationship confirms the cations are the primary active sites. Higher loadings lead to inactive Co3O4 particles.

Overall, mono-metallic Pt catalysts are the predominant focus, with some exploration of Pd and Ir noted as well [DOI5]. Pt demonstrates superior capability for alkane dehydrogenation compared to other metals. Modifications via addition of other metals, overcoats, and supports aim to further augment stability and selectivity.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The analyzed articles provide valuable insights on advancing supported mono-metallic catalysts for propane dehydrogenation, especially focused on Pt. Key enhancements come from metal-support interactions to isolate active sites and prevent agglomeration. This may inspire work on custom supports like zeolites or metal organic frameworks to strictly control metal dispersion. Expanding beyond Pt to thoroughly investigate other metals could uncover additional opportunities to tune catalytic parameters. Modeling varying active site surroundings may also elucidateDescriptor descriptors enabling rational design. Leveraging these multidisciplinary Directions directions could catalyze development of superior catalysts.

根据文件提取的详细信息,几篇文章评估了应用于丙烷脱氢的载体单金属Pt、Pd或Ir催化剂。

一项研究调查了在制备Pt/氧化铝催化剂期间和反应气氛中添加硫的效果[DOI1]。用H2S预处理催化剂大大提高了丙烯选择性,而在制备中掺入硫比在反应过程中添加硫更有效。

另一篇文章显示,通过Pt-O-Ge键将单个Pt活性位点隔离在Ge-UTL分子筛的孔内,可以获得高度稳定和选择性的催化剂[DOI2]。Pt活性位点展示出卓越的丙烷脱氢催化性能。

额外的工作研究了氧化铝载体上的受支持Pt簇,发现原子层沉积Al2O3或TiO2涂层可以提高丙烷脱氢的收率和选择性[DOI3]。这些涂层贯穿压制Pt/氧化铝催化剂的整个长度和宽度。

除Pt之外,一项研究评估了具有低Co负载的无醇化沸石中隔离的Co2+活性位点[DOI4]。线性关系证实这些阳离子是主要活性位点。更高的负载导致无活性的Co3O4颗粒的形成。

总的来说,单金属Pt催化剂是主要关注点,也有些对Pd和Ir的探索[DOI5]。与其他金属相比,Pt表现出卓越的烷烃脱氢能力。通过添加其他金属、涂层和载体进行修改,旨在进一步增强稳定性和选择性。

※※※
综合理解与未来展望:分析的文章为改进丙烷脱氢用的载体单金属催化剂,特别是Pt类,提供了有价值的见解。关键的改进来自金属与载体之间的相互作用,以隔离活性位点并防止团聚。这可能会激发对分子筛或金属有机框架等定制载体的研究,以严格控制金属分散。除Pt之外深入研究其他金属可能会发现调节催化性能的额外机遇。建模不同活性位点周围环境也可能阐明方向描述符,使理性设计成为可能。利用这些多学科方向的驱动力可能会催生卓越催化剂的开发。
