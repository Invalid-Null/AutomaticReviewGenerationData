Based on the detailed analysis of the references provided, several studies evaluate supported mono-metallic Pt, Pd, or Ir catalysts for propane dehydrogenation (PDH). 

Pt-based catalysts are reported to be among the most widely used and commercially successful PDH catalysts [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]. For example, Pt/Al2O3 is considered vital for its ability to activate C-H bonds while minimizing C-C bond cleavage [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201507119]. Adding promoters such as B or metal oxide overcoats via ALD can enhance Pt/Al2O3 performance by decreasing coking or increasing yield and selectivity [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548, DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]. Studies also show small, sub-nanometric Pt clusters stabilized in zeolites demonstrate high intrinsic activity and propylene selectivity above 99% for extended periods [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]. 

Besides Pt, Ru and Rh have been explored as alternatives. Monometallic Ru nanoparticles were found to have very low propylene selectivity around 4.5%, much worse than Pt or optimized Ru phosphides [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]. In contrast, an RhCu single atom alloy (SAA) catalyst showed higher activity, lower light-off temperature, 100% propene selectivity and coking resistance compared to a standard Pt/Al2O3 catalyst [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]. This highlights the potential of SAA catalysts as more selective alternatives to conventional metals.

In summary, Pt-based catalysts, especially Pt/Al2O3, remain the predominant choice for commercial PDH processes owing to their balanced capability for C-H activation and resistance to overcracking. However, emerging catalysts based on stabilized single metal sites or SAAs demonstrate opportunities to tune selectivity while maintaining activity. Further research into the synergistic effects between metals and tailored supports is critical to unlock enhanced catalytic performance.

Future research endeavors might focus on elucidating and exploiting fundamental metal-support interactions to stabilize isolated metal sites and strategically tailor coke/coking resistance. Combined theoretical and experimental approaches can offer mechanistic insights to guide rational design principles for next-generation PDH catalysts.

<Chinese>
基于所提供参考文献的详细分析,一些研究评估了用于丙烷脱氢(PDH)的载体单金属Pt、Pd或Ir催化剂。

Pt基催化剂被报道是最广泛使用和商业上最成功的PDH催化剂之一 [DOI: 10.1016/S1872-2067(19)63360-7]。例如,Pt/Al2O3因其能激活C-H键而最小化C-C键的断裂被认为是至关重要的 [DOI: 10.1002/anie.201507119]。 添加促进剂,如B或通过ALD的金属氧化物涂层,可以通过减少焦炭生成或提高产率和选择性来增强Pt/Al2O3的性能 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.9b05548,DOI:10.1021/acscatal.0c03391]。研究还显示,稳定在沸石中的亚纳米Pt簇展示了高的固有活性和丙烯选择性,在延长的时间内均高于99% [DOI: 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7]。

除了Pt之外,也探索了Ru和Rh作为替代品。 发现单金属Ru纳米粒子的丙烯选择性非常低,约为4.5%,远低于Pt或优化后的Ru磷化物 [DOI: 10.1021/acscatal.0c01667]。相比之下,一种RhCu单原子合金(SAA)催化剂显示出比标准Pt/Al2O3催化剂更高的活性、更低的起始温度、100%的丙烯选择性和抗焦炭性 [DOI: 10.1126/science.abg8389]。这突出了SAA催化剂作为传统金属更具选择性的替代品的潜力。

总之,Pt基催化剂,特别是Pt/Al2O3,由于其平衡的C-H激活能力和抵抗过度裂化的能力,仍然是商业PDH工艺的首选。但是,基于稳定的单金属位点或SAA的新兴催化剂展示了在维持活性的同时调节选择性的机会。深入研究金属和定制载体之间的协同效应对于释放增强的催化性能至关重要。
