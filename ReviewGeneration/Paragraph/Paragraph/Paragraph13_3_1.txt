 <English>
The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631 investigates how properties like Pt particle size, shape, and support interactions affect the performance of supported Pt catalysts. Using HAADF-STEM, it shows that ultra-small Pt species down to single atoms are stabilized on the zeolite support even after pretreatment and reaction. EXAFS data also suggests changes in Pt coordination during reaction, indicating metal-support interactions. The ultra-small Pt species demonstrate high propane dehydrogenation activity.

The article with DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671 studies how properties like Pt cluster size, Sn addition, and metal-support interactions affect propane dehydrogenation over supported Pt-Sn catalysts. Results show increasing size and Sn content reduce activity by increasing barriers and reducing intermediate stability. Sn addition enhances stability.

The article with DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7 reveals isolating sub-nanometric Pt clusters confined in 14-MR pores via Pt-O-Ge bonds with Ge-enriched units in a Ge-UTL zeolite provides high activity and stability for propane dehydrogenation. The Pt4-Ge2-d4r@UTL structure demonstrates the best performance.

※※※
Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The analyzed studies offer valuable insights into how judicious tuning of critical parameters like metal cluster size, composition, support interactions etc. can optimize propane dehydrogenation performance over supported metal catalysts. Key descriptors like particle size distribution, ensemble composition, metal-support interface chemistry etc. merit continued scrutiny. Future explorations might focus on expanding the combinatorial matrix of metal cluster architectures and support materials to unlock additional activity-selectivity-stability improvements. Operando spectroscopic techniques could illuminate catalyst structure-function interdependencies over a multitude of length and time scales during working conditions. Machine learning algorithms may also be employed for high-throughput screening and predictive catalyst design.

<Chinese>
DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c01631的文章研究了Pt颗粒大小、形状和支撑剂相互作用等属性如何影响负载Pt催化剂的性能。 HAADF-STEM显示即使在预处理和反应后,超小Pt物种一直稳定在沸石支撑上,直到单原子。 EXAFS数据也表明反应过程中Pt的配位发生了变化,这表明存在金属支撑相互作用。 超小Pt物种表现出高丙烷脱氢活性。

DOI 10.1021/acscatal.2c05671的文章研究了Pt簇大小、Sn添加和金属支撑相互作用等属性如何影响负载Pt-Sn催化剂上的丙烷脱氢反应。 结果表明,随着大小和Sn含量的增加,活性通过增加屏障并减少中间体的稳定性而降低。 Sn添加增强了稳定性。

DOI 10.1038/s41929-023-00968-7的文章显示,通过Pt-O-Ge键将亚纳米Pt簇限制在富Ge单元Ge-UTL沸石14-MR孔道中,可实现高活性和高稳定性丙烷脱氢。 Pt4-Ge2-d4r@UTL结构表现出最佳性能。

※※※
综合理解和未来展望:分析的研究通过精心调谐如金属簇大小、组成、支撑相互作用等关键参数,为优化负载金属催化剂上的丙烷脱氢性能提供了有价值的见解。 如颗粒大小分布、配合物组成、金属支撑界面化学等关键描述子值得进一步关注。 未来的研究可能会关注扩大金属簇体系结构和支撑材料的组合矩阵,以释放额外的活性选择性稳定性改进。 当工作条件下的原位光谱技术可以阐明多种尺度长度和时间尺度上的催化剂结构功能依赖性。 机器学习算法也可以用于高通量筛选和预测催化剂设计。
