 <English>

The article by Wang et al. [10.1021/acscatal.2c00878] explores metal-support interfacial effects in VOx catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. It systematically investigates the effects of different oxide supports on PDH activity over VOx catalysts, finding VOx/ZrO2 to be 4 times more active. DFT calculations reveal this is due to weaker VOx-ZrO2 interactions leading to lower electron density on bridge oxygen atoms, enhancing their capability to rupture C-H bonds in propane. The principles established aim to help tune the VO active site microenvironment.

Additionally, the study by Wang et al. [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726] investigates how dealumination of the BEA zeolite support affects the structure and catalytic performance of supported Co catalysts. Dealumination generates silanol groups that anchor isolated Co2+ cations in the zeolite micropores. These stable Co2+ sites selectively activate C-H bonds over C-C bonds in propane, leading to high propylene selectivity.

※※※

Integrative understanding and prospective outlook: The findings from these studies exemplify how systematic modulation of metal-support interfacial interactions can effectively tune the microenvironment and electronic properties of active sites in supported metal oxide catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. This knowledge opens exciting opportunities to rationally design optimized interfaces that maximize desired activity and selectivity. 

Future efforts might explore high-throughput computational screening of metal-support combinations to identify promising interfaces for selective dehydrogenation. There is also potential to extend this concept to designing multifunctional single sites at the interface, combining protonic, redox, and metallic functionalities to enable new reaction mechanisms. Advances in in situ characterization could provide further insights to guide these design endeavors. Overall, strategic manipulation of interfacial effects promises to be a fruitful strategy for developing efficient and sustainable catalysts.

<Chinese>

Wang等人的文章[10.1021/acscatal.2c00878]探讨了丙烷脱氢反应中VOx催化剂的金属载体界面效应。该文系统地研究了不同氧化物载体对VOx催化剂丙烷脱氢活性的影响,发现VOx/ZrO2的活性是其他样本的4倍。DFT计算显示,这是由于VOx与ZrO2的较弱相互作用导致桥氧原子上的电子密度降低,从而增强了它们断裂丙烷中C-H键的能力。该研究旨在帮助调谐VO活性部位的微环境。

此外,Wang等人的研究 [10.1016/j.cej.2022.140726] 探究了BEA沸石载体脱铝处理对Co催化剂结构和催化性能的影响。脱铝处理生成硅醇基团,固定Co2+阳离子在沸石微孔中。这些稳定的Co2+位点选择性活化丙烷中的C-H键而非C-C键,导致高丙烯选择性。

※※※

综合理解与未来展望:这些研究发现表明,系统调控金属氧化物载体催化剂中金属-载体界面相互作用,可以有效调谐活性位点的微环境和电子性质,为丙烷选择性脱氢反应提供了新思路。这为合理设计优化界面以最大化目标活性和选择性提供了令人兴奋的机遇。

未来的研究可能会利用高通量计算筛选金属-载体组合以识别脱氢反应的有前景的界面。也有可能在界面处设计结合质子、氧化还原和金属性功能的单一活性位点,启用新的反应机制。原位表征的进展也将为这些设计努力提供进一步指导。总体而言,战略性操纵界面效应有望成为开发高效和可持续催化剂的有果实的策略。
