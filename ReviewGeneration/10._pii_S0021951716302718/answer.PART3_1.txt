<English version answer>  

3. Answer: The article investigates catalyst preparation methods aimed at optimizing active phase dispersion. Specifically, it studies the addition of 3-mercaptopropyltrimethoxysilane (MPS) during the hydrothermal synthesis of gallosilicate MFI zeolites. MPS addition is found to increase the number of strong Lewis acid sites and decrease the ratio of Brønsted to Lewis acid sites. The concentration of strong Lewis acid sites is known to strongly influence propane dehydrogenation selectivity, with the most selective catalysts having small Brønsted acidity and strong Lewis acidity. Thus, the MPS addition tunes the acid site distribution to favor propane dehydrogenation over cracking reactions.

</English version answer>
