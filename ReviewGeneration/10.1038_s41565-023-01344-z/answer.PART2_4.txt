<English version answer>
3. Yes, the article investigates electronic and geometric effects of the secondary metal in bimetallic and alloy catalysts. It introduces a methodology using DFT calculations to separately identify the electronic regulation and geometric modulation of the Pt site when alloyed with a secondary metal M. A simple descriptor called "degree-of-isolation" is defined that depicts the degree of Pt-C repulsion, corresponding to an isolation effect. PtZn IMA manifests a moderate degree of isolation and is found to be the optimal PDH catalyst. For IMAs with high degree of isolation like PtMn, alternation of the active center has a big impact on tuning selectivity. PtMn IMA achieved >93% propylene selectivity at 49% propane conversion at 600C.
</English version answer>
