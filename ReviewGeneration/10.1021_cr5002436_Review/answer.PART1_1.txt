1. The article evaluates CrOx, Pt-Sn, and GaOx catalysts for propane dehydrogenation. It provides data on conversion, selectivity, specific activity, and stability for various catalyst formulations (Tables 3-7). The CrOx/Al2O3 catalyst shows moderate activity but deactivates rapidly. Pt-Sn/Al2O3 is very active and stable but expensive. Promising results are reported for GaOx, with high initial activity similar to Pt-Sn, but faster deactivation. 
</English version answer>

2. The article investigates how oxidation state affects propane dehydrogenation performance over CrOx catalysts. It states that Cr3+ species are the most active, while Cr6+ and Cr5+ need to be reduced to Cr3+ under reaction conditions to show activity. Insights into the effects of acid-base properties are also provided. Basic promoters like alkali metals can enhance CrOx catalysts by stabilizing the alumina support and reducing surface acidity.
</English version answer>
