It screens different metal oxide dopants (In, Ce, Mo, Cr) within 3D printed bifunctional catalyst-adsorbent (BFM) monoliths and characterizes their dispersion using XRD and EDS mapping. The data shows differences in dopant dispersion depending on the metal, with In showing large agglomerations and poorer dispersion compared to Ce, Mo, and Cr. The article notes that optimizing dispersion is important for ensuring sufficient contact between the metal dopant and the primary ZSM-5 catalyst phase.
