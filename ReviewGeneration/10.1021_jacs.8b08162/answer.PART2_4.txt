<English version answer>
Answer: Yes, the article investigates electronic and geometric effects of the secondary metal Mn in the bimetallic Pt-Mn catalysts. It shows that manipulating the amount of Mn precursor allows tuning the extent of intermetallic formation, from core-shell to full body Pt3Mn nanoparticles. With the same Pt3Mn surface ordering, having a Pt3Mn intermetallic subsurface instead of Pt metal significantly improved propane dehydrogenation selectivity by lowering hydrocarbon and hydrogen adsorption strengths. This is attributed to an electronic effect related to downward shift of the Pt d-states due to Mn incorporation, as explained by d-band theory.
