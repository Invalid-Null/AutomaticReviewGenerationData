<English version answer>  
3. Yes, the article investigates electronic and geometric effects of the secondary metal in bimetallic and alloy catalysts. It utilizes colloidal synthesis to control the composition, morphology, and phase of Pt-In and Pt-Ga bimetallic nanoparticles. It shows that while the selectivity profiles look similar, the kinetics of alkane dehydrogenation are strongly impacted by the composition, with PtIn2 showing the highest turnover rate. This is attributed to both electronic effects from the In promoting orbital hybridization with Pt, as evidenced by shifts in the Pt XANES spectrum, as well as geometric effects from the change in surface ensemble upon forming the CaF2 crystal structure in PtIn2.
